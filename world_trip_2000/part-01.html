Part 1 - Malaysia

  • Diary Index

Saturday 22nd - Sunday 23rd July We left Heathrow on time at midday and had a fairly uneventful flight to K.L. Arrived in K.L. at 7ish and had a three hour wait for our flight to Kuching. This flight was also on time and by 3:30 we had arrived at the Grand Continental Hotel, Kuching.

We were all completely knackered but after a brief visit to the pool and much needed showers we went out to see the sights of Kuching.

The sight-seeing trip consisted mainly of visits to Chinese restaurants which are the only establishments that seem to sell beer. We carried on drinking (mainly cans of Carlsberg) up until around midnight but no-one is sure exactly what time we got back to the hotel.

Monday 24th July We got up for breakfast at 10 but then returned to bed and slept until just after 1. There were no serious hangovers , so once we were up, we walked to the National Park office on the other side of town. We had hoped to book accommodation at three different parks. The person in the office could not make a booking for us but gave us a number which Roz rang to book all three. We stopped for lunch on the way back to the hotel and then spent an hour by the pool.

Before going out to tea in the evening, we visited the Big Cat statue, the first sight we have made any effort to see.

Tuesday 25th July After breakfast, we got a taxi to the airport and got the 9:10 flight to Bintulu. From Bintulu we had planned to travel to Similajau, a beach national park. However, it had been raining steadily all morning so we had a change of plan and decided to go to the Niah Caves a day early. Roz phoned up from the airport to alter the accommodation booking and we eventually got in a minibus for the two hour journey to Batu Niah. From Batu Niah, it was a short taxi ride to the national park where we checked into a four bed hostel room.

The rain had not really stopped but at 2:30, we headed off to look at the caves. It took about an hour in each direction to look at the caves and we spent a couple of hours walking around them. The caves were more impressive than I had expected them to be and it was an enjoyable afternoon despite the rain.

There were not too much in the way of facilities at the place we were staying so we had tea in the restaurant and returned to the room.

Wednesday 26th July We decided against a second day at Niah and headed instead to Miri. We got a boat up the river to Batu Niah and then the 9:00 bus to Miri. The journey was about two hours and by lunchtime we had checked into a hotel, a short walk from the bus station. As the weather was good, we decided to go to "Hawaii Beach" about 45 minutes from Miri. During the bus journey out to the beach the weather deteriorated rapidly and by the time we were there, it was wet, windy and generally unsuitable for lying in the sun. We spent about ten minutes standing around doing nothing and then got the same bus back to Miri.

As we had a spare day and there was not much to do in Miri, we started drinking as soon as we were back in the hotel at about 2:30. We got through most of a bottle of Bacardi before going out drinking in the Chinese restaurants. Had an excellent curry for tea as well as a couple of other meals during the session. Got back to the hotel at around 12.

Thursday 27th July Didn't do a lot all day apart from eat, sleep and watch videos. We did manage to phone up and book accommodation for the walk up Kinabalu next week.

Friday 28th July We got up at 7 and walked to the bus station stopping for breakfast on the way. From the bus station, we got a taxi out to Miri aiport for the 9:20 flight to Mulu. The flight was in a Twin Otter plane which only held about 20 people. The flight was only 35 minutes but due to the low altitude was quite spectacular.

We had checked into a room in the national park by midday and at 1:30 set off to see a couple of the show caves. We walked to Deer Cave and Long Cave both of which were pretty spectacular. We had beaten the tour groups up there so had both of them to ourselves for most of the time we were there. By 3:30 we were out of the caves and had a two hour wait to see the millions of bats that live in the cave exit for their nights feeding. This was an incredible sight and we spent about 4 5 minutes watching the swarms (?) of bats leaving the cave.

It was a two mile walk back to our rooms. As with Niah, there was not much else to do so we had an early night.

Saturday 29th July Had breakfast at 8 and then set off to see the remaining two show caves in the national park. the walk to the first was just over 3km but was a much harder walk than yesterday with a lot more steps to climb. The walk was quite enjoyable but we were all soaked in sweat by the time we reached Wind Cave. Wind Cave was the best of the caves we have seen in Borneo with plenty of typical cave features all brightly lit up. From there, we walked to Clear Water Cavewhich was also pretty solid but none of us took the option of going in the swimming pool at the foot of the steps to the cave. We had managed to keep ahead of most of the tour groups again and left Clear Water as most of them were setting up their picnic lunches.

We walked back to the park H.Q. getting totally soaked on the way back due to a tropical downpour. The rain if anything made the walk back easier but it did not do a lot for the washing that we had hung out prior to our walk.

We had a brief break in the rain during the afternoon during which time we managed to sun-bathe for about 30 minutes but spent most of the rest of the day reading and trying to work out how to dry all our clothes before we leave tomorrow.

Sunday 30th July Had breakfast at the park canteen again and then got in a minibus for the short trip to Mulu airport. We were due to fly to Miri at 10:40 and arrived at the airport with about an hour to spare. An "extra flight" left for Miri at about 10:30 and at 10:45 we were told that our flight was canceled for technical reasons. It was obvious that our plane had been used to carry a group of people to Miri (whose flight had been canceled yesterday) but the Malaysia Airlines bloke would not admit this. We were told that a later flight was also canceled and at this point had no idea whether or not we were going to get away from Mulu today.

After a lot of discussion and phone calls to Miri, another "extra flight" was announced and we were told that we could leave at 3:40. We had no option but to accept this and sat around waiting. At 1:30, we were told that the 14:05, which had been canceled, was now leaving on time and we could have the last three seats.

We eventually landed in Miri just after 3 but had missed our connecting flight to Lawas (we did not know if this had been canceled as well but either way, we could not catch it). There was more standing around in front of the Malaysian Airlines check-in counter until someone told us we could have seats to Lawas in 15 minutes time on the 3:45 flight.

We eventually arrived in Lawas at about 4:30. The fact that we were only about an hour late was incredible considering the confusion earlier in the day.

From the airport at Lawas we got quite a good deal on a taxi to Sipitang in Sabah, our intended destination for the day. On arrival in Sipitang we got anther taxi and managed to get all the way to Beaufort. We checked into a hotel for the night and had another relatively quiet night.

Monday 31st July We were up at 7, had breakfast and then walked the 300m to Beaufort railway atation. We had reserved seats on teh Beaufort - Tenom scenic railway journey. The journey was in a 13 seat "railcar" which was not much bigger than a mini-bus. The journey was quite interesting without being as spectacular as Lonely Planet made out, The highlight was probably the railcar itself.

We arrived in Tenom at 10:00 and got straight into a minibus bound for Keningau which was on the way to Kota Kinabalu, today's destination. At Keningau we got straight onto another bus but had to sit watching the usual selection of crap videos for two hours until the bus finally departed for KK.

The journey to KK went through a mountain range where the road was over 6000 ft above sea level in places. It was at least as spectacular as the train journey.

After checking into a hotel, we checked on buses to the Kinabalu National Park for tomorrow and then spent an hour or so in an internet cafe checking e-mail, etc.

Tuesday 1st August We did not have a lot to do this morning as we were waiting for the 2pm bus to Kinabalu National Park. We walked out to the HSBC Bank and spent the rest of the morning planning what to do in our last week in Borneo and making the most of the hotel's air-conditioning. We killed a couple of hours in a restaurant at lunchtime and then boarded the bus.

We arrived at the park at 4ish and spent about 20 minutes checking in and sorting out climbing permits, etc. Once in the hostel we had the fun of sorting out rucksacks for tomorrow's walk. Not much else to do in the park apart from lying around waiting for the walk to start. Stocked up on Cadbury's Dairy Milk and peanuts.

Wednesday 2nd August We were up before 7 and headed to the park canteen for breakfast. We put our luggage in the luggage room at park HQ and then managed to get five other people together to form a group of eight. We hired a guide between us and set off by bus to "the power station" which is at the start of the walk. As well as us in the group was an English couple, an English bloke on his own and a German with a Chinese girlfriend.

We started walking at 8:15. The group all seemed to be similarly matched in walking ability apart from the English bloke who was obviously fitter than everyone else but seemed content to walk at the back. The walk was quite enjoyable but reasonably hard work. Most of the climbing was done by walking up steps - 2,500 in total. Where there were no steps, we had to climb up the path of rocks but there was nothing too difficult. There were "pondoks" every 30 minutes or so with drinking water which made things a lot easier. In total, we climbed from 1,800m to Laban Rata at 3,300m. We arrived at Laban Rata just before 1, had lunch and then continued to our accommodation a few minutes further up the mountain.

The accommodation was basic and had no heating or hot water. After cold showers, we spent the rest of the day sat around looking at the rain, drinking boiling water to try and get warm. We did not want to walk down to the restaurant at Laban Rata as we could not risk getting our only sets of clothes wet so we survived the afternoon on crisps, biscuits and hula hoops. The hostel filled up gradually during the afternoon and by 8pm, most of our group was tucked up in bed.

Thursday 3rd August People in the hostel started getting up at about 1:20 am and our group was all up about an hour later. At 2:50, the eight of us plus Gearoge, our guide set off to walk to the summit.

The walking today was much more difficult than it had been yesterday. The steps were steeper, it was totally dark and the altitude made breathing difficult. We spent about 45 minutes going up steps before we came to the ropes. The next 45 minutes were spent mainly hauling ourselves up the mountain with the aid of ropes which are fixed to the granite slopes to help climbers. This was probably the hardest part of the whole climb as two hands were needed on the ropes which made holding torches just about impossible. We reached Sayat Sayat by 4:20 with supposedly just an hours walk ahead of us.

The last part of the walk saw the rest of the group go on ahead of us. Dave was having increasing breathing difficulties and Roz was also finding the going tough. There were still ropes guiding us towards the summit but these were mainly to keep us going in the right direction as opposed to being essential to get up the slopes. The aim of the walk was to get to the summit by sun-rise and we all just about made it, getting to the top at exactly 6am

There were about 50 people huddled together at the top and the next half hour was spent taking pictures and generally being relieved that the climb was over. The sky was almost totally clear by the time the sun rose and the views in all directions were superb. We had been lucky with the weather all the way. Although it was cold at the top, the wind was no-where near as strong as we had been led to believe it would be and it had also been dry for most of the time we were walking.

We started our descent at 6:30. We had an hour stop at Laban Rata to collect our rucksacks and eat breakfast. We left Laban Rata by 9:30 and were back at the power station by about 12:30. The walk down was easier but by the time we were at the bottom, all our knees were beginning to suffer from the constant steps.

We were picked up by bus and driven back to park HQ. By this time, any leg movement was difficult and going down steps was almost impossible.

We collected our certificates and together with Claire and Andy, the English couple, we got a minibus to Poring for a visit to the hot springs.

We were in dormitory accommodation for the third night running. We put our stuff in the dorm and went straight into the hot baths. The baths were for 1 or 2 people and were filled with sulpherous hot water. We spent a couple of hours recovering either in or just outside the baths. The baths were not brilliant but it was an ideal way of spending the afternoon following the climb

All eight people in our dorm had just done the walk and by about 9pm everyone was asleep.

Friday 4th August We got up at 7:30 (having decided not to visit the canopy walkway at 6:30) and got a minibus into Ranau, still with Claire and Andy. We had breakfast in Ranau and then got another minibus to Sepilok, a journey that lasted three and a half hours. At Sepilok, the bus dropped us at The Jungle Resort just outside the Orangutan Rehabilitation Centre. We could not walk any great distance as our legs had all stiffened up so we checked into a room for three at the resort.

At 2, we walked to the rehabilitation centre to see the afternoon feeding session. We were all having serious problems walking up or down steps but could just about cope with walking on flat roads. The feeding session was superb, probably better than the ones we had seen in Bukit Lawang in Indonesia. There were 7 - 8 orangutans on the feeding platform and three of them walked across the viewing platform and happily posed for pictures along the way. When the orangutans had finished, a group of wild monkeys moved in to eat the leftovers.

We watched a thirty minute video about the centre and then walked back to our hotel.

Saturday 5th August After an hour or so of sitting in the sun, we caught the bus to Sandakan, 25km away. There is not a lot to do in Sandakan but it had been a while since the last session and Sandakan seemed as good a place as any.

We started off in Chinese restaurants at about 4. We had a few pints by the time we ate at 7 but then all the Chinese restaurants shut for the night and we were left with no-where to drink. We found an open supermarket and bought orange juice, soft drinks and ice and returned to the room where we consumed another litre of duty free spirits. It worked out quite well in the end as we could drink, play cards and listen to the sport program on World Service.

Sunday 6th August We had breakfast and then returned to bed until checkout at 12:00. We got a taxi out to the bus station and sat around waiting for the 2pm bus back to KK (we had booked tickets yesterday). The bus journey back took six hours but was not too bad as it was a big comfortable air-conditioned bus with plenty of leg room.

We arrived in KK at 8, checked into a hotel and then went for our first McDonalds of the trip.

Monday 7th August We checked out of our hotel at 8:30 and walked down to the waterfront where we had planned to get the 9am ferry to Manukan island. When we got to the ferry office, we found that not only was there not a 9am ferry, but all of todays ferries had been canceled for no apparent reason. We chartered a boat to take us out to the island for RM10 each, which is what we had expected to pay anyway, and were at the island at 9:15.

We had pre-booked accommodation on the island for two nights. It is the most expensive place we have stayed so far but for RM161 we have got a two storey "chalet" with two bedrooms and a large living area all within a couple of minutes walk from an excellent beach. We spent just over an hour lying in the sun and swimming before returning to the chalet for most of the afternoon.

Tuesday 8th August For the first time on the trip, we had a whole day with absolutely nothing to do. It was also the first day since climbing Kinabalu that our legs had returned to normal. There is nothing to do on Manukan so we spent the morning and part of the afternoon lying on the beach and swimming and spent the rest of the day lazing around the chalet reading, etc.

All very enjoyable!

Wednesday 9th August Another day without a lot to do. We left Manukan island by charter boat and returned to KK as we fly from there to Kota Bharu tomorrow. We were in our hotel by 11:30 and spent the day checking e-mail, writing postcards, reading, etc, etc.

Thursday 10th August We checked out at 9:30 and got a taxi to KK airport. We flew first to KL and then a 50 minute flight to Kota Bharu where we are due to meet Regan tomorrow. We arrived in Kota Bharu at 4:20 and got a taxi to the Perdana Hotel where we had arranged to meet. We had an hour or so by the pool and then went out to find places selling beer for tomorrow night. We found one or two but there were not too many places around

We had a couple of beers and ate at the night market.

Friday 11th August Spent most of the day lying by the pool. Regan and Annette turned up at about 6:30 by which time the three of us had already started on our next bottle of duty free. We found an excellent Chinese restaurant for beer drinking and remained there until 1am. We continued with the Bacardi when we got back to the room and fell asleep at about 2.

Saturday 12th August At midday, we were picked up by a share taxi from our hotel for the two hour drive to Merang (Dave, Roz and I in this taxi - Regan and Annette followed later.

I felt pretty rough in the taxi and by the time we got to Merang I just felt like lying down on my bed for the rest of the day. We got two rooms in the Kembara Resort and I did spend most of the remainder of the day laid on the bed. We listened to the first day of the football season on World Service from 10pm until the results at midnight.

Sunday 13th August I still felt pretty rough all day with a stomach upset and slight temperature. I did make it to the beach for the first time which was OK but nothing spectacular.

A session was called for the evening as yesterdays planned session was postponed due to me not feeling well. We started on the duty free at 5:30, ate food from the night market and then carried on drinking until about 3am. The whole session took place in Regan and Annette's room as there is nowhere at all in Merang where we could drink our own alcohol let alone buy any more. I was still not feeling at my best so did not drink as much as the others but we still managed to drink 3½ litres of spirits between the five of us.

Regan was unconscious three hours before everyone else!

Monday 14th August Bad hangovers all round - particularly Annette. I spent most of the day on my bed again and hardly managed to eat a thing.

Tuesday 15th August Woke up feeling better than I had done since leaving Kota Bharu.

We checked out early and had booked a minibus to collect us at 6:45. It turned up at about 7:20 and got us to Marang by about 8:30. We were dropped at the ferry pier and got a fast ferry across to the island of Kapas where we plan to spend the next three days.

We spent about an hour looking for accommodation as everywhere was a lot more expensive than other places we have stayed. We eventually ended up taking the more upmarket option of alarge air-con room (about four times bigger than the one we had in Merang) at The Kapas Island Resort. Spent most of the day on the beach.

Wednesday 16th August Another day spent mainly on the beach. We sunbathed on the island's main beach in front of or hotel and walked round to a couple of smaller bays for snokeling which is pretty good here.

After tea, we finihsd off our supplies of alcohol by drinking the two bottles of "James Bond Brandy" that Regan had bought with him.

Thursday 17th August Slight hangovers but nothing too serious. Most of the day spent either on the beach or reading in the room.

Friday 18th August We said our goodbyes to Regan and Annette and left Kapas by boat at midday. The boat took us across to Marang where we had a ten minute walk to the bus stop for the Rantau Abang bus. We had about 30 minutes to wait for the bus and the journey took about an hour. By 2 we had checked into a hotel.

We spent what was left of the afternoon lying on the beach. In the evening Roz and I visited the Turtle Information Centre. We had stopped at Rantau Abang on the off chance that we would see one of the giant turtles coming ashore to lay eggs. However, no turtles appeared while we were there.

Saturday 19th August We left straight after breakfast and managed to get a lift in a "private" bus which took us all the way to Cherating. We were in Cherating by midday and checked into an excellent room with a balcony overlooking an artificial lake.

During the afternoon, we managed to meet up with Colin Juett who is also on a round the world trip and is staying nearby.

In the evening, we all went out for a few beers but were unsuccessful in our attempt to find anywhere showing English football. In the end, we bought a few cans and returned to our room to listen to World Service.

Sunday 20th August Again, we all had slight hangovers so did not do much all day. Made it to the beach by about 4.

Monday 21st August We decided against the beach today as not only was the weather bad but we all seem to be covered in sand-fly bites from yesterday. At midday we got the bus into Kuantan with Colin who is departing for Penang. We got out money, stocked up at the supermarket and said or goodbyes to Colin. We also looked at a couple of hotels as we are not really sure of our plans for the next week and staying a few nights in Kuantan is one of our options. We had planned to stay at Cherating until next Monday but the poor beach (sand-flies and jellyfish) together with the fact that the nightlife is not all that Lonely Planet makes it out to be, means that we could do with leaving sooner and doing something else for a week.

In the evening, we drank the brandy we had bought in Kuantan and also spent about four hours drinking beer in a Chinese restaurant. (I was sick towards the end of the evening, the second time in just over a week) Most of the evening was spent discussing possible itineraries for the next week. A short trip to Thailand was a popular option.

Tuesday 22nd August Spent most of the day lying around in the room. Thailand became a less popular option with soberness and we instead decided on three days in Melaka before heading to KL. We will get the early bus from Kuantan tomorrow.

Wednesday 23rd August We got up at 5:45 and walked out to the bus stop on the main road. We got on the bus to Kuantan at 6:20 and arrived in Kuantan at 7:50, ten minutes before the Melaka bus was due to leave. We got straight in a taxi which got us to the express bus station with a couple of minutes to spare. The journey to Melaka was fairly uninteresting and took six and a half hours.

We checked into a hotel, had lunch and just made it to the pool by the time the rain started. In the evening, we wandered round some of Melaka's sights and had a relatively early evening.

Thursday 24th August Spent the morning by the pool. Excellent weather and no sand-flies! At lunchtime we went on a one hour river trip and saw the remaining sights.

Spent the evening drinking again. We had bought bottles of rum/vodka from the supermarket and also went for a few beers in a couple of restaurants.

Friday 25th August Most of the day was spent recovering either sleeping or lying by the pool. Went to Pizza Hut for tea.

Saturday 26th August We got the midday bus to KL, a journey of just under two hours. From the bus station we got the LRT out to our second Grand Continental Hotel of the trip. At 4, we walked inot central KL and revisited The Bull's Head where we had got pissed ten years ago with Dave and had also been to six years ago with Hoggy. Fortunately, they still had their 12 - 7 Saturday happy hour so we stayed there for a couple of hours drinking jugs of Carlsberg.

At 7 we found an internet cafe and managed to "chat" to Cathy and Hoggy in Swansea. After eating, we spent an hour or so drinking in the Chinese bottle shop, had another couple of beers and then returned to the room to listen to the football on World Service.

Sunday 27th August Didn't do a lot all day. We had a couple of trips out for meals and to use the Internet but could not be bothered with any sight-seeing.

Monday 28th August After a couple of hours by the pool, we walked out to the KL Tower, the 4th largest telecoms tower in the world (although it was becoming the third largest as we visited due to a fire in the one on Moscow). We went up to the viewing tower platform where there were excellent vies of KL. We walked from there to the Petronas Towers which is according to the Malaysians, the tallest building in the world. We could not go up this one but it was an amazing building to look at anyway.

We spent an hour or so in the shopping complex at the base of the towers before returning to the hotel.

Dave left us at 5:30. He flies to Australia for three days before flying home by next week. It is now just me and Roz (and Paddy and Patty) until we meet up with everyone at Christmas.

Tuesday 29th August After an hour or two by the pool, we set off for another walk around KL. We went out to the MAS office where we attempted to move our flights to Cairns forward by thee days (we got wait-listed as the flight was full). We walked from there to the centre, used the Internet, had lunch and wandered round a few more sights.

We had checked out of the hotel before setting off but we returned there to spend a couple more hours by the pool (despite the rain).

After tea, we collected our luggage from the hotel and got a taxi to the railwy station. We spent an hour or so sitting around at the station before catching the 10:30 train boound for Singapore. We had pre-booked sleeping berths and with nothing else to do, went to sleep soon after boarding.

Wednesday 30th August We were woken at 6 to go through Malaysia/Singapore border control and arrived in Singapore on time at 7:30. We changed money, walked to the MRT station and got the underground to within 100m of our hotel. We checked in and were given a room straight away. After breakfast, we slept until about midday. MOst of the rest of the day was spent by the pool. We did spend some time making phone calls as it only costs 12p a minute to phone home from Singapore.

In the evening we wlaked round a few of the sights before eating at The Satay Club, a collection of stalls serving "the best satay in Singapore" according to Lonely Planet.

Thursday 31st August Most of the day was spent lying by the pool. At lunchtime we bought Cheddar cheese and Franch bread and ate cheese sandwiches in our hotel room. This seemed quite a treat after nearly six weeks away from home. In the evening, we wandered round the city centre again before eating at an Indian restaurant near the hotel - probably the nicest restaurant of the trip so far.

Friday 1st September We checked out at midday and got a bus from outside the hotel to Changi Prison. We spent about an hour looking round the chapel and small museum which was reasonably interesting.

At 2:30 we left the museum and got a taxi to the airport, a couple of miles away. We checked in immedeatly and then had a three and a half to four hour wait for our flight to Bali. We spent our time eating, buying duty free and playing with the Internet.

The three hour flight was fairly uneventful but we did manage to consume quite a few beers. We arrived in Bali on time at about 9:15. We had pre-booked a hotel that was supposed to pick us up at the airport. However, there was no-one to meet us and it took about 45 minutes and a phone call from tourist information before someone turned up for us. We were not happy by this time and the fact that the hotel was not very good or ideally located did not make the matters any better! We had booked to stay at the hotel for three nights but decided that we would leave in the morning.

We went for a walk around Kuta looking at a couple of other hotels and returned to our hotel by about 12.
