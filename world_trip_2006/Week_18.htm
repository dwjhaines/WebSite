World Trip 2006/7 - Week 18

Monday, 9th October 2006 We were up at 3:40am and were on our way to Luton Airport by 4. It was an un eventful drive down and we were parked at the off-site parking by about 5:30. We got the shuttle bus to the airport and were checked in with well over an hour to spare. We had been concerned about WizzAir who we were flying with but everything seemed reasonably efficient at check-in and the plane departed just about on time. The plane was not full so it was a comfortable flight and we landed in Zagreb at about 11:40. We only had hand luggage with us so were straight through the airport in about 10 minutes and were able to get the 12:00 shuttle bus into Zagreb. We were at the bus station at 12:30. We had no accommodation booked for the first two nights of our stay here and were not 100% sure of what we were going to do. At the bus station, we found that we could get a bus at 1pm to Varaždin which had a pretty good write-up in Lonely Planet. It took just under two hours to get to Varaždin and by just after 3, we were checked into a hotel near the bus station.

We spent most of the rest of the afternoon catching up on sleep although I did go for a short walk around the old town which seemed quite pleasant. We were both still tired when we went out to eat. We were not bothered about drinking too much and as most of the bars seemed a bit trendy for us, we settled on a pizza and an early night.

Tuesday, 10th October 2006 After a week with a few early starts and quite a bit of travelling, we slept for about 10 hours and woke up feeling better for it. We had breakfast in the hotel and then headed off to see the sights of Varaždin. It was a nice enough place to wander around, similar to small towns in Austria or Slovenia, neither of which are far away. Lonely Planet describe it as a mini Prague - again, a bit of an exaggeration but an enjoyable place to wander around nevertheless. After a couple of hours looking at old buildings, we spent an hour in the library looking at the internet before getting takeaways for lunch. Spent most of the afternoon back in the hotel not doing a lot.

We drank a litre of red wine in the room before going out for tea. We returned to the same restaurants we had eaten in last night and had another couple of litres of wine with our pizzas. Only vaguely remember returning to the hotel.

Wednesday, 11th October 2006 Woke with slight hangovers, had breakfast and walked to the bus station for the 8:50 bus back to Zagreb. The bus got in at about 10:20 and we walked from the bus station to our hotel. We checked in straight away and were in our room before 11. While waiting for Mark and Steve to turn up, we walked out to the hotel where the FA were based to register our proof of travel.

Mark and Steve appeared at about 12:30 and we left the hotel about 45 minutes later. We walked up to the main square where we met a couple of Steve's friends and then went to one of the bars in the Upper Town. Totally by chance, we managed to find a bar that had an excellent range of good, cheap beers and spent the remainder of the afternoon there.

At 5 o'clock, two hours before kick-off, we started making our way towards the ground. Me, Roz and Mark managed to get separated from the others in the main square but the three of us got on a tram fairly quickly for the 20 minute journey to the ground. We were at the ground by about 5:45 so went for another pint before going in (Steve and his mates got there after us and went straight into the ground).

We left for the ground at about 6:15. There were big queues at the first checkpoint but the queues were moving and we probably would have made kick-off without any problems. However, with about 20 minutes to go until kick-off, the stewards decided to throw open the gates at the checkpoint and let everyone in without searches or ticket checks. This seemed like a good idea, but instead it just caused a huge bottleneck at the next checkpoint. This was a single gateway for all the England fans to enter their terrace and was not big enough to cope with the number of fans trying to get in. Their was the usual pushing and shoving and eventually, the riot police waded in swinging batons in an attempt to calm things down. This inevitably made matters worse and things were not helped by the fact that they had stopped letting in any fans at all. It was a very unpleasant 20 minutes and it looked as it could of all turned very nasty. We stayed back from the trouble and eventually got pushed through some barriers into a no-man's land between our entrance and the media entrance. We could not get into the ground but we were away from the trouble and safe. Although we were not supposed to be there, the stewards saw some sense and let us and a handful of other fans remain where we were until the trouble had calmed down. Eventually they started letting us into the ground. We remained at the back to avoid the crowds and got into the ground about 20 minutes into the first half. We found Steve and his mates about 10 minutes later and were reunited with Mark at half-time.

The game itself was pretty poor and the 2 - 0 score-line was a fair result. England could easily of lost by more. We were kept in the ground for about 40 minutes after the game. By the time we were allowed out, there were hardly any trams running and those that were about were, were already full by the time they had reached our stop. We gave up and went for a drink and got a later tram after the crowds had died down. We had to put up with the Croat celebrations all the way back and in the main square but it was all fairly good-natured. We went for one more drink in the bar we had been in earlier but the whole place seemed to close down around midnight so we returned to the hotel.

Thursday, 12th October 2006 There was not too much that any of us wanted to do in Zagreb so we decided on a day trip to Samobor. We all walked to the bus station and got tickets for the 11:55 bus. We were told that the journey was 30 minutes but it took well over an hour and we did not get to Samobor until after 1. Samobor was another picturesque little town near the Slovenian border. We spent an hour or so wandering round the centre of the town and another hour or so sitting in bars with a couple of beers. It was another place that was not overly spectacular but a nice enough way of spending the day. We got a bus back to Zagreb just before 5 and after showers at the hotel returned to where we had been drinking yesterday. We spent most of the evening in the same bar as yesterday apart from a trip to a pizza restaurant where we had eaten on our trip to Zagreb five years ago.

Friday, 13th October 2006 The four of us got a taxi from the hotel to the airport at 10am. We checked in for the 11:55 flight without any problems but then had to wait in the departure lounge for a few hours as the flight was nearly two hours late leaving. Eventually got back into Luton at 2:45. We said goodbye to Mark and Steve and got straight on the bus to collect the car. Had a better than expected journey North and were Hoggy's house in Sheffield just after 6. Regan was already there and after a couple of beers in the house, spent the rest of the night in the local pub.

Saturday, 14th October 2006 I went to Chesterfield by train to watch Swansea win 3 - 2. Roz drove to Preston to see Sunderland lose 4 - 1. Met up with Hoggy, Sarah and Regan in Sheffield for the session.

Sunday, 15th October 2006 Took Regan back to Wheatley and drove from there to Aleks and Andi's. Had another curry and got through about 6 or 7 bottles of wine!
