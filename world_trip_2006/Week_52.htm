World Trip 2006/7 - Week 52

Monday, 4th June 2007 Got up at about 10 despite the fact that it had been light since about an hour after we went to bed. We had a trip out to a supermarket to buy food for breakfast and then I spent the rest of the morning sorting out my CV. I applied for a contract position at Ericsson by e-mail and got told by Andy that I should also apply for a permanent position in another department.

The old town of Tallinn seems a really nice place and we could not be much better located right in the middle of it. Loads of medieval buildings and good looking restaurants and bars. We met up with Mark and Sophie in the main square and went for a walk around the old town before settling in to The Beer House for the rest of the afternoon. We returned to the apartment for showers and I had more e-mails regarding jobs from Ericsson, the outcome of which will mean two phone calls tomorrow, one regarding the permanent position and the other the contract job.

We all met up at 7 at The Beer Hose again for a drink before eating and going for a drink in a Depeche Mode theme pub. We only had one drink there before finding an excellent pub (Hell Hunt) where I spent the remainder of the evening drinking Young's Double Chocolate Stout! A good day out without anyone getting too pissed. Roz probably more drunk than me as I did not want to be too hung-over for my phone calls tomorrow!

Tuesday, 5th June 2007 Had an e-mail from Christina saying that my phone call re the permanent job would be tomorrow morning instead of today. Did speak to someone re the contract position but that conversation was a bit of a waste of time!

We met up with the others for the end of the Beer House's happy hour just before 2. We had a drink there before heading off in our different directions for more sightseeing. Roz and I walked around the castle area and took in some of the view points of the old town before heading back to the apartment. We met up with everyone again at 7ish and had a similar evening to yesterday. We went for a meal and had a few drinks before ending up at Hell Hunt again. All left just after midnight.

Wednesday, 6th June 2007 We met up with Mark and Steve at 11 and walked out to collect our match tickets from the FA's hotel. This was all very straightforward and we were back in the apartment before midday. I had my phone call from Christina just after 1. The job on offer all sounded quite interesting and I am fairly hopeful of being offered the job, hopefully without any further interview!

We met up with the others in The Beer House again and spent most of the rest of the afternoon drinking either in there or in Hell Hunt. The game did not kick of until 9:30. We left Sophie in the pub at 7:30ish and walked to the ground stopping for one more drink on the way. The game was a very enjoyable experience compared to some trips. It was easy to get in, we had good views, the game was OK to watch and we won 3 - 0. Reasonably quick getting away from the ground after  the final whistle as well and we were back in the centre of Tallinn by midnight.

We had talked about going for another drink but everyone was knackered by this stage so Mark and Steve came back to our apartment for half an hour before returning to their hotel.

Tallinn has been a superb place to visit. As well as the fact that the football was good, it is a very attractive old town with plenty to see, good beer and food, and excellent places to sit drinking. Definitely one of the best England away trips.

Thursday, 7th June 2007 Left our apartment after breakfast and walked the two km out to the bus station. We caught the 11:45 bus to Kuressaare on the island of Saaremaa. It was  a four hour journey of which about 35 minutes was spent on a ferry. This had seemed a good option as a place to spend a couple of days en-route to Riga from where we fly home next week. However, as soon as we arrived there just before 4pm, neither of us were overly impressed. It was a nice enough place but no-where near as attractive as we had both expected. We have seen a lot of far nicer medieval towns in the last year! Also, the couple of hotels we tried were either full or expensive for what we were getting and most of the bars/restaurants seemed to be stretched out along a main road.

We checked into a hotel for the night and sent a few e-mails to hotels in other places that we could go to for a couple of days. We walked around the town and saw the main sight, the not particularly impressive castle, before walking back to the hotel via the bus station. We had not seen anything that made us want to stay here longer than one night so booked bus tickets for the 7:45 bus to Pärnu tomorrow morning. We will probably stay there one night there before heading for Latvia and a national park that looks quite good.

We ate in a pub type restaurant next to our hotel and had an early night.

Friday, 8th June 2007 We got up at 7 and after grabbing a couple of rolls from breakfast, walked to the bus station. We were on a large mini-bus for the three hour journey to Pärnu where we arrived at about 10:30. The hostel we had booked was just round the corner from the bus station in Pärnu and was the third place in a row that we have stayed in in Estonia with free internet access in the room.

We went straight out for a walk around the town which was a lot nicer than Kuressaare. We had hoped to spend the afternoon having "mud treatment", whatever that entailed, but the main mud baths was closed and did not look like re-opening for quite some time. We wandered along the beach which looked OK. The water was not quite so appealing as at some of the beaches we have been to recently but the beach-volleyball tournament that was taking place was good to watch! We looked at a hotel that did all the spa treatments but it all looked over priced and we decided that we could not be bothered.

We walked back into the town and bought lunch from a supermarket before returning to our room. We had planned to go to the beach for the afternoon but after eating neither of us could be bothered, not least because we did not have beach towels with us. Spent the afternoon lying around in the room instead.

We went out for a couple of drinks at 5ish before heading back to the room for a couple more. I drank two bottles of A Le Coq Porter, the only Estonian beer on my beer list and was fairly pissed by the time we went out to eat. We had a few more drinks in a Russian Tavern type place and then went for a pizza that I only vaguely remember. Got through two bottles of wine with the meal.

Saturday, 9th June 2007 Up at 8 for the 8:50 bus to Riga. It was a three hour journey, crossing the border into Latvia after about an hour. In Riga, we got cash out and got straight on to the 12:15 bus to Sigulda. We arrived in Sigulda at about 1:30 and checked into the hotel we had booked which was just round the corner from the bus station. We went out for lunch and then fell asleep back at the hotel. At 4ish we made it out of the hotel and went for a walk. We did not complete the planned route due to Roz being bitten by insects but still managed to walk for a couple of hours.

We were back at the hotel by 6 and went out for an early tea - pizza again but without the alcohol this time!

Sunday, 10th June 2007 Spent the morning doing the longer of the two walks that were recommended in our Sigulda walking guide. This was a total of 10.5km and took just over three hours. We saw the main sights of Sigulda at the end of this walk - old castle, new castle and Sigulda church. Both walks we have done here have been OK without being too spectacular. The same could probably be said about Sigulda itself!

We ate the remains of last nights pizza for lunch and did not really do a lot for the rest of the day.

Had a change from pizza in the evening and ate in a cafeteria type place near the hotel. Pretty good food with chocolate éclairs for dessert. Back in the hotel by 8ish.
