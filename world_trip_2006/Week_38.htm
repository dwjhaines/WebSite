World Trip 2006/7 - Week 38

Monday, 26th February 2007 A days diving with Forra Divers which was a bit like a cross between diving in Zanzibar and diving in Koh Chang. We arrived at the dive just after 9:30 and were told that due to problems with boat/compressor we would only be able to do two dives today. The boat was going to be going to a different place to where we had been told it would be going and we would have to sit out one of the dives due to a shortage of full tanks. We were not overjoyed with this but said we would miss out on the first wreck dive and do the other two. This seemed to cause them more problems for some reason as they expected us to do the wreck dive and one other.

After about an hour of sitting around waiting we were taken by long-tail to the dive boat which appeared too small for the amount of divers on it. We were told however that we would now be able to do all three dives. We dived with a French instructor and one French customer at the first site. This was a wreck sitting on its side at about 40m. This was the deepest dive we had ever done and the vis was crap. I almost panicked completely as we went down through 31 - 32m and lost sight of the others in the group, but managed to sort myself to continue going down. We went down to 39m and swam through parts of the wreck for 10 minutes or so and then along the side at 30m for another 10 minutes. We then spent 10 minutes going up the buoy line and doing a safety stop.

The second and third dives were more normal dives but we were joined by two divers completing their open water courses. Both these dives were OK without being too spectacular. Saw plenty of small stuff - nudibranchs, ghost pipe fish, etc.

We were back on Lipe by 5, returned to our room and tried to decide our route to Penang where we have to be on Wednesday afternoon. Option 1 was to spend the day on the beach tomorrow and get the 4pm ferry to Langkawi, from where we could get another ferry direct to Penang on Wednesday. Option 2 was a return to Pak Bara and a night in Hat Yai with a bus journey over the border and down to Penang. We eventually chose the second option as it means one more night in Thailand and should work out slightly cheaper.

We went out and bought our boat/mini-bus tickets for tomorrow and ate out at our resorts restaurant again.

Tuesday, 27th February 2007 Our journey to Hat Yai went about as well as we could of hoped for. We were taken by long-tail to the ferry at 9:30. The ferry left just after 10 and got to Pak Bara three hours later. We got straight onto the waiting minibus at Pak Bara and were in Hat Yai by 4. We checked in to a Guest House near the train station as this was where we were dropped and train was one of our options for getting to Penang tomorrow. The train times were not ideal so we walked out to the bus station to see if there were any direct buses. There was nothing of much use so reluctantly, we booked ourselves onto the 9am minibus direct to Georgetown.

We went out to eat at 7 and ended up in an air-con English type pub due to the lack of other options. The food here was surprisingly good and we ended up drinking a lot more than we had intended to. There was a happy hour when we arrived with cheap litre jugs of Chang. By the time we had got through three of these, happy hour had finished and we moved on to the Sang Som for a final time. We had a couple of bottles before staggering back to the guest house with Roz in particular fairly pissed.

Wednesday, 28th February 2007 Another day when the travelling went surprisingly smoothly. We were up at about 8 feeling decidedly rough after too much alcohol and not enough sleep. The mini-bus office was just round the corner form the guest house so we walked there and had breakfast next door while we waited to be picked up. We left just after 9 and left crossed into Malaysia about 90 minutes later. By 2pm Malaysian time, we had arrived in Georgetown on Penang. We walked from where the mini-bus dropped us to our hotel where we checked into a huge room with an excellent view of the sea. It all felt very luxurious after the last few days.

We walked from the hotel to the Komtar Tower and found the travel agent that we had booked our Penang - Medan flights with and collected our tickets with no problems. We were both still not feeling 100% so went to McDonalds for lunch which just about sorted us out! We walked back via the main backpacker area of town and spent a bit of time looking at second hand books and photocopying the documents that we need for our Indonesian visas tomorrow.

Back at the hotel we paid for 24 hours of internet access so we could start sorting out flights, etc for the last part of our trip. We had found reasonably priced flights back to Germany from Bangkok. This means that we will not use our return tickets with Royal Jordanian and will have an overnight stopover in Düsseldorf instead of 8 hours waiting around in Amman. The main reason for doing this is that it gives us an extra week in SE Asia before flying home.

We ate out in Little India at a restaurant recommend by Anjali and were back in the hotel by 9ish.

Thursday, 1st March 2007 After breakfast, we got a taxi out to the Indonesian Consulate. Applying for the visa was very straightforward. We filled out the form (twice), and handed over our photocopies, RM135 (about £20) each, and our passports. In theory we should be able to collect the passports with visas tomorrow at 3.

We got the bus back to the Komtar but most shops were still closed so we walked back to the hotel. We booked a few more things on the internet - flights home from Düsseldorf, accommodation in Düsseldorf, car hire, flights home from Latvia, etc. I left Roz using the laptop while I went off to get my haircut and look round the shops. We went out for roti chani for lunch and then Roz went to the pool while I had my turn with the internet.

At 5ish, Roz got an e-mail which was to cause another change to our plans. She had enquired about staying at the Borneo Divers resort on Mabul Island near Sipidan at the end of May. We had intended that our visit to Sipidan would be the last thing that we would do before flying back to England and Borneo Divers are the operator with by far the best reputation for diving there. The e-mail told us that they were full on the 27th May meaning that we would have to leave earlier than we had hoped. This would waste a lot of the week we had gained by buying our flights back to Germany. We sent an e-mail enquiring about other dates and also e-mailed a couple of other operators and Roz considered missing out on Sipidan altogether.

We continued working our way through the shopping list we had for things we needed for Palau Weh and ate out at the same restaurant as last night. We bought a Malaysian SIM card that allowed phone calls home for 3p a minute!

Friday, 2nd March 2007 Spent most of the morning trying to sort out the last month of our time in S.E. Asia. Roz spoke to Borneo Divers and found that they had spaces at the end of April from 24th to 29th. This would mean that we would have to leave Pulau Weh on the 22nd giving us seven weeks to complete our DM training. I had hoped to have the option of staying until our visa runs out if we really liked it there but this only shortens our stay by a few days and means that we can go to Sipidan and still have a month for Bali and the rest of Indonesia.

We contacted Borneo Divers again and confirmed that we would go on the dates that they had offered. Roz spent the rest of the morning looking at flight options for getting from Penang to Sipidan and then to Bali while I walked to the post office to send home postcards, CDs, etc. When I got back to the room, we booked the four flights required and also sorted out car hire for when we get back to the UK. We had certainly got our moneys worth from the 48 hours internet access we had paid for.

We went out for lunch (roti chani again) and then got a bus out to the Indonesian Consulate to collect our passports. There were loads of people there when we arrived but as with yesterday, we went to the front of a different queue and got our passports with visas in a few minutes. We finished our shopping and returned to the hotel.

We made the most of the last few hours of our internet access and went out to eat at Sri Ananda Bahwan for the third night in a row.

Saturday, 3rd March 2007 Checked out of the hotel at 7:30 and got a taxi to the airport. The 9:30 Adam Air flight to Medan was on time and we arrived in Medan at 9:25 Indonesian time. As soon as we were through immigration we were met by Mr Nadjri with our onward tickets to Banda Aceh. We walked to the domestic terminal with him so we could get money from the ATM to pay him and than sat around waiting for the 1:10 flight. This eventually left one hour late but we still got out of the airport in Banda Aceh with our luggage by 3:10, giving us just enough time to get the last fast boat of the day to Pulau Weh. We were in Pulau Weh by 5 and had an hour in a minibus to our final destination at Gapang Beach.

We were dropped near the Lumba Lumba dive centre where we met some of the staff and were shown around. We got into one of the bungalows near the dive centre but can only stay a few nights as it is booked later in the week. We will look for somewhere else tomorrow. Back at the dive centre we booked ourselves onto our first dive trip tomorrow which we do as customers and met David who I think will be our instructor for most of the course.

We ate at a restaurant at the beach and had a pretty good meal before returning to our room to listen to World Service.

Neither of us are too sure of what to make of Gapang from our first impression. The beach is smaller than we expected. We were not expecting much development here but there is hardly anything here at all. The restaurants seen very basic (although the food was very good tonight). Our room is OK and has a very nice balcony but the downstairs outside bathroom is certainly not ideal. We will certainly be looking for somewhere else to stay for the remainder of our stay.

Sunday, 4th March 2007 The first day of our dive master training. Today was just doing fun dives as we need to start learning the dive sites. Both dives were very enjoyable. The visibility was a lot better than we have got used to in Thailand and the currents were not too bad. We dived with Silke who has just finished her DM training and on the second dive also with Daniel, an Indonesian DMT. On the first dive we saw the biggest Napoleon wrasse we have ever seen and on the second a turtle sheltering from the current.

In between dives, we went and had a look at a couple of rooms at a nearby resort. We found a much better room than the one we are in at the moment and should move in tomorrow. It has loads more space, an attached bathroom and still has the views of the sea from the balcony. Hopefully we will be there for the rest of our time here.

I was a lot happier with everything here today. We have found a nicer place to stay, the food that we have eaten has all been OK and the diving has been excellent. Roz still not really sure!
