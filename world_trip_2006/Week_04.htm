World Trip 2006/7 - Week 4

Day 22 - 28th June 2006 Checked out of the hotel by about 10 and had an uneventful 4 hour drive up to Münster where we had booked four nights in another Ibis. We had found Münster due to it having a brewery that produces two of the beers in my 300 Beers book and it is also convenient for Gelsenkirchen, the venue of England's quarter final against Portugal.

We wandered round the sights of the old town for an hour or so and found out where we would be able to hire bikes for tomorrow. In the evening we went out to the Pinkus Müller Brewery for a couple of drinks and had a kebab before a relatively early night (the first day since June 9th with no football)

Day 23 - 29th June 2006 As well as the beer, Münster was a good place to come due to the number of cycle paths in the area which is famous for its castles and palaces. We hired bikes at the train station and headed off on what turned out to be a 55 mile trip. There were disappointingly few castles on the trip but the one that we did get to, Schloss Nordkirchen, was quite impressive and worth the trip. We got back into Münster at about 5:30, went out for a few drinks and another relatively quiet night.

Day 24 - 30th June 2006 We had planned to do another days cycling but had gone further than expected yesterday so decided to have a day off today. Also, Mark had decided to join us in Münster and would be arriving at about 3:30 in time to go and watch the Germany quarter final. After Mark had checked into his hotel, we headed up to the student area of town and found a pub to watch the game. We were the only English people in the bar and once again the atmosphere was excellent, as the Germans went through on penalties. We went for a meal after the game and then found another bar for the Italy v Ukraine game. Not too pissed by the time we got to bed at about mid-night.

Day 25 - 1st July 2006 Quarter Final - England v Portugal. We met Mark at the railway station and got the 9:11 to Gelsenkirchen. Picking up our tickets was relatively painless and after a quick look around the town, we headed out to Gelsenkirchen-Baur. We had chosen this are as it was a few tram stops the other side of the ground meaning that it should be easier to get to and form the game. Gelsenkirchen-Baur was also an area recommended for eating and drinking by Free Lions. After ticking off another of my 300 beers at a street stall, we found an excellent pub where we stayed for a few hours. We were joined by Dave and Glen and stayed in the pub until 3ish before getting the tram to the ground. Met Steve in the ground - he had had a few problems getting his ticket due to his late arrival - and had another couple of drinks before taking our seats.

It was another impressive ground with excellent views. It was by far the most exciting game we have seen so far but once again, England missed out on an excellent opportunity to get to a semi-final. Although they had played reasonably well, once Rooney was sent off our best chance of winning was gone. Once it went to penalties, the result seemed almost inevitable although this was a poor shoot-out even by England's standards. I think this defeat felt particularly painful as if just one or two of our players had played to the best of their ability, beating Portugal should have been easy. The fact that Brazil were to get knocked out later in the evening, leaving the whole tournament wide open just made things worse.

Rather dejectedly we made our way back to Gelsenkirchen-Baur as soon as the last penalty went in. Surprisingly, we had a reasonably enjoyable evening in the same bar we had been in before the game. We were joined again by Dave and Glenn as well as Roy and Fred, Steve's friends. Watched the Brazil game and then made our way back to Gelsenkirchen for the 23:56 train back to Münster.

Day 26 - 2nd July 2006 Woke up feeling depressed thinking of what might of been. I had been really looking forward to a semi-final in Munich but it was not to be. Time to change over to our non-football plan. We cancelled various hotels that were booked for the latter stages and went off to say goodbye to Mark. We wanted to head towards Regensburg to cycle down the Danube again. We decided that as we were going to be pissed off all day anyway, we may as well get most of the driving out of the way in one go. We spent about five and a half hours driving down to Dinckelsbühl, just South of Rothenburg on the Romantic Road. We found the campsite and pitched the tent in what is probably the nicest spot we have camped at so far, at the side of a small lake with very few other campers around us. We walked into Dinckelsbühl for our evening meal. It seemed a very nice place but we were both tired and still not too enthusiastic about doing anything so we had a meal and a couple of drinks before returning to the tent for an early night.

Day 27 - 3rd July 2006 We had planned to hire bikes today and cycle to one or two of the other towns on the Romantic Road but the only place in Dinckelsbühl that hired bikes, the tourist information, had none available all week. This did not turn out too badly. We spent a couple of hours walking around the town which was all very scenic and then spent the rest of the day lazing around in the sun by our tent.

We had found a shop that sold another 4 of my 300 beers as well as a garage selling ice so we had a few beers at the camp site before heading into Dinckelsbühl for tea. Had a few more beers and we were both pretty pissed by the time we walked back to the campsite.

Day 28 - 4th July 2006 Had slight hangovers and decided to head up to Regensburg. After packing up the tent, we had about 3 hours of driving and another hour of finding our way into the town and trying to find accommodation, before settling on another Ibis. We found out details of bike hire for tomorrow and had a walk around some of the sights of the old town. In the evening, we went out for a drink but did not fancy watching the Germany semi-final with hundreds of Germans so went back to the hotel with a take-away.
