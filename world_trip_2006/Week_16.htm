World Trip 2006/7 - Week 16

Monday, 25th September 2006 Day one of our Rescue Diver course. We started the day by completing the EFR course. This involved marking our knowledge reviews, taking an exam and then the skills. We did the skills with Sabry who we had dived with last year. The skills took about an hour and included such things as putting on and removing gloves as well as more complicated procedures such as CPR. We both successfully completed everything meaning that we are qualified to perform first-aid and can now go on to the rescue diver course.

We had to watch the PADI videos for the first two sections of the course and then headed off to the lighthouse dive site for the first of the practical exercises. We started with a dive:

Dive 217: Lighthouse
First dive of our rescue diver course. A normal dive for the first 30 minutes or so then Roz and I had to pretend to be out of air and surface using each others alternate air source.

Following the dive, we went through knowledge reviews in one of the restaurants, had lunch and then began the confined water exercises. This mainly involved spending an hour or so rescuing tired or panicked divers (played by Hywel, the trainee DM from yesterday) under the supervision of our instructor for the course, JP. It was all quite good fun and we seemed to be able to do everything that was required. Back at the hotel we spent another 20 minutes watching section 3 of the video and spent the rest of the evening working our way through the next two sections of the book.

Tuesday, 26th September 2006 Day two of our course. Spent the whole day at the lighthouse. We were based in the restaurant with Hywel and JP again. We started with knowledge reviews and practised a few skills for shore rescues. We had a couple of scenarios where we had to rescue Hywel from the shore and then did two dives:

Dive 218: Lighthouse
We were told it was to be a fun dive but spent most of the dive sorting out problems with our instructors equipment e.g. loose tank straps, upside down mask, fins falling off, BCD hose disconnected, cramp, panic, etc, etc.

Dive 219: Lighthouse
Had to try and find a weight belt some-where in the site we had dived this morning. It did not go too well, mainly due to me swimming too quickly with Roz getting left behind. We swam past it without seeing it but did get to it in the end.

Totally knackered by the end of the day. We did read some more of the book and started the next knowledge review but gave up at about 7 and walked into Dahab for a few drinks and a meal at El Fanar.

Wednesday, 27th September 2006 A day off from the course as we wanted to do the boat trip that Reef 2000 organise once a week. We were up by 7 and got our equipment ready before going for breakfast. We were driven to the jetty and helped load up the boat - enough equipment and tanks for 27 divers, including the staff. The boat left at about 8:30 and took an about an hour to get to the dive sites at Gabr el Bint - first dive at 9:44. We were led by Dom and were in a group with 4 other divers - Suzanne, Rachel, Tim and Matt.

Dive 220: Gabr el Bint
First dive of a two dive boat trip from Dahab to Gabr el Bint. Dived with Dom, Suzanne, Rachel, Matt and Tim. Started with a drift dive along a coral wall and swam back through a sandy lagoon area. Very nice dive!

Dive 221: Gabr el Bint
.Similar dive to the first one. Saw a couple of crocodile fish in the lagoon and a devil scorpion fish while doing the safety stop.

This was the same trip that we did last year and once again, it was an excellent day out. Back at the hotel, we finished off all of our reading for the rescue course and completed most of our knowledge reviews. We then walked into Dahab and had a couple of beers in Adam's Bar and a burger at "Kwickies".

Thursday, 28th September 2006 Back to our course. We spent the whole day at the lighthouse again. We did not log any dives today but had a couple of sessions practising the skills that we will need for tomorrows scenarios. Most of the morning was spent practicing getting an unconscious, non-breathing diver out of the water while giving rescue breaths, and in the afternoon, we learnt how to get an unconscious diver from underwater up to the surface.

When we got back to the dive centre, we joined JP, Hywel and Rachel for a couple of beers and ended up spending most of the evening with them. We ate in the hotel restaurant by the pool and stayed there until about 10:30 . The others all went to a party and we had a beer on the balcony before going to bed. We had not planned to drink tonight but it turned out to be a very enjoyable evening.

Friday, 29th September 2006 The final day of our rescue course. We were joined by Rachel who had volunteered to become a second victim and the five of us headed of to the Southern sites that we had dived last Friday. The day involved completing four scenarios. The first two occurred at the end of the first dive there and the next two involved us looking for a missing diver.

Dive 222: Moray Gardens
Scenario 1 - Distressed diver underwater, diver out of air, assist injured diver on the surface
Scenario 2 - Calm and control panicked diver at the surface, tow to safety.
I had to rescue Hywel who had been "attacked by a shark" while Roz rescued Rachel who was "out of air". Did not go perfectly but we all seemed to survive! Saw at least one turtle at the start of the dive (possibly two or the same one twice)

Dive 223: Moray Gardens
Scenario 3 - Search for missing diver, surface unresponsive diver
Scenario 4 - Non-breathing diver at the surface
We found Rachel pretty quickly. The vis was excellent and we could see her bubbles without paying too much attention to search patterns. Made a mess of getting her to safety and had to do that bit again but we are now both Rescue Divers!

Everyone had told us before the course that it would be good fun, hard work and that we would learn a lot. This turned out to be pretty accurate. While it had been tiring, the whole thing has been very enjoyable and well worth doing.

The five of us who had been diving today had planned on doing a night dive, but by the time we got back to the dive centre, everyone was more interested in starting on the beer again. We spent a couple of hours drinking and playing a dice game that Hywel had and then continued drinking while we ate at the restaurant by the pool. Got to bed sometime around 11:30 I think. Another very good evening.

Saturday, 30th September 2006 We were back to fun diving today.

Dive 224: Abu Hilal
First time we had dived this site. Dived with Matt (who we had dived with last year) and two Germans. Another very nice dive site with loads of coral. Saw a large Napoleon wrasse.

Dive 225: Islands
A superb dive at one of my favourite sites in Dahab. Spent more time swimming around the islands than when we were here last week and it was a much more enjoyable dive because of that.

The second dive in particular was excellent. The sea was totally flat today so it was a very good dive for photographs with plenty of light. We were back at Reef 2000 by 3ish so had time to have lunch by the pool before listening to the football (Forest 3, Swansea 1).

After the football, we walked into Dahab to eat. We had invited Rachel to join us and met her in Adams Bar. We had a few beers there and ate at Al Fanar again.

Sunday, 1st October 2006 Two more fun dives with Matt.

Dive 226: Bells → Blue Hole
A really nice dive again. Dived with Matt, Hywel and Martin, one of the Germans from yesterday.

Dive 227: Blue Hole
Dived the right side of the Blue Hole i.e. we crossed the hole and descended to 20m before turning right. Kept going for 30 minutes before turning round and coming back at about 10m.

Martin complained about a headache at the end of the first dive and was ill in the restaurant when we were waiting between dives. We left him sleeping while we did the second dive but he had deteriorated quite dramatically by the time we got back. He had to be just about carried back to the car and was looking very unhealthy by the time we got back to Reef 2000. We had called out the doctor but when he started complaining about numbness in his hands, Matt and Hywel took him directly to the doctor at the chamber in Dahab. It turned out that he had gastroenteritis and was able to walk on his own when he returned a couple of hours later but it had been quite worrying at one point.

Back at the hotel, we had a late lunch and spent an hour at the pool before saying goodbye to Rachel (and Tim!) who was flying home this evening. After showers, we walked into Dahab for our usual beers at Adams bar and meal at Al Fanar.
