World Trip 2006/7 - Week 47

Monday, 30th April 2007 We were up at 7:30 and after our final roti canai for breakfast, we began our journey to Bali. Both of our colds seemed to have cleared up but Roz still had problems with one of her ears and we were still both feeling tired despite the amount of sleep we had had in Tawau.

We got a taxi from the hotel to the airport at about 8:30 and checked in for the Air Asia flight to KL. This left slightly early just before 10:30 and landed in KL by 1. We had a few hours to kill at the airport which went fairly quickly - McDonalds, internet, postcards, duty free, etc - and we departed on our second Air Asia flight of the day at about 5:30. This flight was also slightly early and we arrived on Bali at 7:30.

We were met at the airport by two taxis! Roz had made enquiries with a couple of places to stay before booking one of them. However, both of them turned up to collect us. After sorting out that confusion, I waited in the car while Roz went to collect tickets from the Garuda office for our flights to Sulawesi in two weeks time. We left the airport at about 8:40 and had a journey of just over two hours to get to our accommodation at Amed.

By the time we arrived in Amed, we were both totally knackered. The room we had booked however, was excellent. It was huge, really nicely decorated, had a fridge and a superb balcony. The balcony was also huge with comfortable chairs, a platform area for lying down and was only a couple of metres from the sea. We had quick showers in the outdoor bathroom which was also very nice and were asleep by 11:30.

Tuesday, 1st May 2007 A very enjoyable day doing not very much except lazing around on our balcony. We had breakfast served on the balcony when we got up at about 8:30 having finally caught up on our sleep. We wandered over to Eco Divers which is only a couple of hundred metres away and booked ourselves onto a trip to Tulamben for tomorrow. Roz had contacted them by e-mail and we are getting good discounts as we are now dive masters. It is only going to cost us US$45 each for two dives tomorrow which further increases the chances of us staying here longer than the week we had originally booked.

The area where we are staying is ideal for us. We are very happy with the room, we are five minutes walk from the dive centre and there are a few really nice restaurants within walking distance as well. You also get excellent views of the three volcanoes Agung,  Abang and Batur from the beach and several places around the village.

After a lazy afternoon, we had a couple of drinks in the evening. We had a couple of beers with our meal and then a couple more on the balcony. We are just about back to full fitness!

Wednesday, 2nd May 2007 Supposed to be picked up by Eco Divers at 8:30 but ended up walking down there instead. We sorted out our equipment and headed off to Tulamben at 9. We did two dives at Tulamben. The first was at the Liberty wreck, a dive we did just after completing open water in 2000. The second was a wall dive a bit further along the beach. Although not quite up to the standards of Sipidan, both dives were very good. On the way back from Tulamben, our guide Gusty and Michelle, an instructor who had also been guiding, had an accident on their moped and ended up with quite a few nasty looking cuts. They were reasonably lucky to avoid more serious injury but were still looking quite shaken when we left the dive centre.

We had an hour or so back at our room before spending half the evening sending a couple of e-mails. We walked for about an hour before finding an internet cafe with a working computer and then had to wait for ages for someone else who was using it. We did get confirmation of our trip to Sulawesi and have hopefully confirmed our booking at Poppies for when we return to Bali.

Thursday, 3rd May 2007 Two local dives, diving from a small fishing boat. Both dives very good again. Another lazy afternoon on our balcony. Had a few drinks again in the evening. Ended up getting more pissed than we had planned to but an enjoyable evening anyway.

Friday, 4th May 2007 We returned to Tulamben for another couple of dives. This time we dived about 3km North of the Liberty at Kubu. Very relaxing dives with loads of small fish and very pretty coral.

Had a similar evening to yesterday and drank a similar amount. We decided during the evening that it may be a good idea to buy BCD's and regulators in Bali before flying to Sulawesi. We have talked about getting them at home before going to Egypt but it may be cheaper to buy them here and we would save a further $125 each on the diving at Bunaken. It certainly seemed a good idea after a few beers!

Saturday, 5th May 2007 We did a repeat of the dives that we did here on Wednesday today, i.e. one dive at the Liberty and the second at the Tulamben wall. We did the dives without a guide today as both dives are fairly straightforward and diving on our own reduces the cost to $40 each for two dives. Both dives were very enjoyable particularly the first.

We had planned a session for tonight while listening to the football. We had a couple of drinks before going out to eat at Divers Cafe. A couple more drinks with the meal and then we continued drinking while listening to World Service. Swansea missed out on the play-offs by losing 3 - 6 at home to Blackpool. Ended up very pissed and do not remember the latter stages of the night.

Sunday, 6th May 2007 Did not do a lot all day apart from sleeping off the affects of last nights alcohol
