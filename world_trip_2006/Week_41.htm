World Trip 2006/7 - Week 41

Monday, 19th March 2007 The first dive of the day was very busy again due to there still being a lot of people around following the long weekend. Both Roz and I had short dives again due to members of our group using their air very quickly. Things did get a lot better after this however. Most of the people staying in Gapang headed back to Banda Aceh and the dive shop was quiet for the first time in ages. We both did the afternoon dive with only two customers meaning we got longer dives (and saw a turtle)

We also managed to pass the Physiology exam which was good news as I had really struggled with this part of the studying. We cheated slightly to get to get through the exam but did not feel too guilty about this as we have not had a lot of time to revise in the last week or so.

Tuesday, 20th March 2007 A relatively easy day. We both did the two boat trips - in the morning we went to Batee Tokong and in the afternoon to Rubiah Sea Garden. The first dive was one of the best we have done here - good vis and two black tip reef sharks swimming around on the plateau. The second, we dived on our own and got dragged away from the dive site by the current. There was still plenty to see and we were fortunate that the current turned and took us back to where we started. Everyone else on the boat aborted their dives and got dropped somewhere else!

Wednesday, 21st March 2007 I decided that I was in need of a day off diving as we have dived every day for the last 8 days. Roz kept going and did both the boat trips again. In between the dives, we completed the equipment exam so we are 75% of the way through the theory and the hardest ones are certainly out of the way. Ten Indonesians started their open water course today. So far we have not had any involvement with them and hopefully that will continue. It is very nice not having a lot to do and only having 2 - 4 customers to deal with is ideal.

We left the dive centre relatively early this evening and went to eat up the hill from our resort for a change. Returned to our room by 7:30 to start revision for the next exam.

Thursday, 22nd March 2007 Two good dives today. We both dived separately at The Canyon and then on our own again at Rubiah Sea Garden. Visibility at The Canyon was the best since we arrived here. The water temperature, however, was the lowest since getting here with most peoples computers showing 20°c. This was not a big problem on this dive as we were only in the water for about 30 minutes. At Rubiah Sea Garden we were in for 59 minutes and were completely frozen by the end of the dive partly due to being the only people diving in shorties. This dive went far more to plan than the one two days ago and we are both now confident that we could lead dives here. We completed exam number 7 after the second dive - just one more to go!

Today was the last day of diving for Ralf and Christian who have been diving here for the last two weeks and leave in the morning. During the evening, Cedric and Dianne turned up. They are our next door neighbours at Laguna and will be diving here for the next month.

Friday, 23rd March 2007 No boat trip today as there were no customers apart from Cedric and Dianne who were diving from the beach. We got a few more things ticked off from our course. Firstly we spent a few hours with David completing some of the twenty skills that we are supposed to be able to perform to demonstration standard. This all went pretty well although Roz is going to have to perform mask removal and fin pivot again. Each skill is marked from 1 to 5 we have to get at least 3 points for each skill except mask removal which must be a 4. We both got enough 4's and 5's to ensure that we just need 3's in the remaining skills to get the total of 68 points that is required.

We spent the remainder of the afternoon studying dive tables and the wheel and were able to complete our final exam, both getting 100%. It was really good to have completed the theory part of the course already and we should now have a bit more spare time without having to be constantly studying.

Peter turned up in the evening to begin his DM training.

Saturday, 24th March 2007 We both did three dives today including our first attempt at guiding customers. As it is the weekend, there were a few more people around but it was nowhere near as busy as last weekend. The first dive was to Batee Tokong which was a nice enough dive in a group of five led by Collet. For the second at the Canyon, me and Roz were at the back of different groups although it all got a bit messy after about 20 minutes when the current picked up, with customers getting confused about which group they should be in. During this dive, a few people, including Roz, saw a mola mola or sun fish which is quite a rare sight.

For the third dive, Roz did her first guided dive while being supervised by Mette. She led one student completing her 4th OW dive at Rubiah Sea Garden. I led two customers at Arus Paleh. This was supposed to be under the supervision of Collet but he disappeared with other customers leaving me on my own with Daniel at the back. Both dives went OK without any major incident.

We did not bother listening to the sport program on World Service but did get up at 1:15 am to try and find somewhere showing Israel v England. We knew of two places that may of been showing it but both were closed so we were back in bed not long after 1:30.

Sunday, 25th March 2007 First dive was at 8:30. We were down to three customers due to the low water temperature. We returned to the canyon in the hope that we would see the mola mola again. Collet and Daniel saw it at 43m and Roz thought she saw it from about 10m higher but I missed out again. Roz guided the second dive to Rubiah Sea Garden with three customers including Anne who had just finished open water.

Did not do a lot for the rest of the day apart from laze around the dive centre. We ate at Mama Jungle's with Peter, Mette and a few of our remaining customers.
