World Trip 2006/7 - Week 32

Monday, 15th January 2007 We were up at 4 for the 11 hour bus journey to Kalaw. Our guesthouse gave us breakfast before leaving, we were picked up by the bus at 4:30 and departed from Nyaung U at 5. The bus was not particularly comfortable particularly towards the end of the journey but it was an interesting enough journey and the day passed fairly quickly.

We had considered staying on the bus through to Inle Lake but by the time we reached Kalaw at 3:30, we were both happy to get off. We will complete the last few hours of the journey to Inle Lake tomorrow. We checked into a budget hotel which was fairly basic but had an excellent balcony overlooking the whole town.

Kalaw is a bit of a backpacker centre mainly as it is used for a base for trekking in the area, but there is not too much to see and do in the town itself. We wandered round the centre stopping for tea and pancakes at one of the tea shops before returning to the room and sitting on the balcony until it got dark.

Due to its altitude, Kalaw is the coldest place we have been to on this trip so far. We chose a restaurant based on the fact that it looked warmer than our other choice, had a pretty good curry again and were back in the room not long after 8. We were both knackered after our early start and were asleep by 9.

Tuesday, 16th January 2007 We had thought about getting a share taxi to Inle Lake via a set of caves that had sounded reasonably interesting, but we found out that there was a bus we could get at 7:45 and we took that option instead. The bus arrived at Shwenyaung at about 9:30. From there a taxi took us and the three other Westerners on the bus the 11km to Nyaungshwe. Nyaungshwe is the main base for visiting the lake and is about 3km North of the lake. The taxi took us round a couple of places and we eventually got a room at the Bright Hotel for $18 a night.

We made the most of the sun on our balcony for an hour or so and went for a look around the town at about midday. Before going out, we booked a boat trip round the lake for tomorrow. The town was nice enough without having too much of interest to look at. We had lunch at a stall in the market and then walked out to a monastery about a km North of the town. Did not do a lot for the rest of the day.

We went out to an Italian restaurant for tea - once again chosen mainly because it was reasonably warm. We both had tagliatelle with meatballs. Not very Burmese but very good anyway.

Wednesday, 17th January 2007 We were served breakfast on our balcony at 8 and departed for our day trip around Inle Lake at 8:30. We were joined by Matteus, who had been on our bus and shared the taxi yesterday. We all walked down to the jetty and boarded the boat, a long canoe type thing with a lawn-mower engine stuck on the back. As well as the driver, there was a guide who spoke reasonable English.

The lake itself was very scenic. We headed towards the Southern part of the lake passing fishing boats many with the "leg rowers" that the lake is famous for. We stopped at the floating garden and a fishing village for pictures and continued on to the market at Nam Pan. This was an excellent place with mainly fruit and vegetables being sold as well as fish, meat, clothing, etc etc. There were a few other tourists around but it was catering mainly for locals and was a very colourful and interesting place to wander around.

We left the market and visited a few very touristy places to view such skills as silk weaving, cigar rolling and boat building before stopping at a pagoda and visiting a restaurant for lunch. After lunch we visited a silver-smiths but turned down the option of going to see umbrellas being made. We chose to go to Indein which meant an enjoyable excursion up a river from the lake and a 1km walk to another pagoda. The temple itself was nothing special but the area was filled with ruined stupas which made it worth visiting.

There were just two more places remaining to visit. Firstly we went to a particularly tacky tourist spot to photograph a few "long-necked" women who were posing in traditional costume. None of us were particularly impressed with this but we all got our photos! Our Inle Lake experience was completed at the "Jumping Cat Monastery" where the monks have trained cats to jump through hoops. The monastery itself was worth seeing and we did get to see a few of the 25 cats that live there jumping through a hoop. The cats all seemed very well looked after and the monks were all quite friendly and keen to talk to us.

The whole trip had been excellent although I think that we enjoyed it more than Matteus who found it all a bit too commercialised. Parts of it were commercialised but the good parts more than made up for that. It was also remarkably good value as the whole thing including tips for driver and guide came to 18,000 Kyats or £2.50 each which seemed ridiculously cheap considering the trip lasted over eight hours.

We were a bit more adventurous with our evening meal tonight, visiting a traditional Shan restaurant for a couple of curries with plenty of side dishes some of which were more traditional than others. Back in the hotel by 9.

Thursday, 18th January 2007 Had breakfast on our balcony at 7 before getting into a taxi for the 1 hour drive to Heho airport. Our flight to Yangon was due to leave at 9:35 and eventually took off about an hour late. The flight took just over an hour and by 12:45 we had checked back in to the Traders Hotel. There was not too much left in Yangon that we still wanted to see so spent most of the remainder of the afternoon by the pool.

At 4:30 we got a taxi out to Chaukhtatgyi Paya to see the huge reclining Buddha. we walked from there to Kandawgyi Lake, hoping to see the sunset. However, the walk to the lake took longer than we had expected and we did not realise that you had to walk round to one of the official entrances to get in. By the time we had got to the entrance, it was not worth paying the entrance fee and camera fee so we got a taxi back to the hotel instead.

We ate at the same restaurant that we had eaten in two weeks ago. We had the same meal but both thought it was better than when we were here the first time. We returned to the hotel and had our complimentary (non-alcoholic) drink in the lobby bar area before returning to the room by about 8:30.

Friday, 19th January 2007 Up at 8 for breakfast which was one of the best we have ever had. Curries and Indian breads to start followed by French Bread and real cheddar cheese! The range of food is superb and one of the reasons we were keen to return to Traders.

After eating we went for a walk, firstly to the post office to send home our postcards and then on to Botataung Paya, the only one of Yangon's "big three" pagodas that we had not yet seen. The pagoda itself was nothing spectacular after what we have seen in the last two weeks. However, it had a pool of turtles that Roz could feed, keeping her entertained for about 20 minutes. It also featured something that resembled a fairground game for donating money by throwing it at a Buddha in a moving glass case with a metal bucket in front of it which was quite entertaining for its sheer tackiness.

This marked the end of our sight-seeing in Myanmar and we got a taxi back to the hotel for a couple of hours by the pool. Did not do a lot for the rest of the day. We had a few biscuits for lunch before returning to the pool again. I spent over an hour in the gym in the evening - it looks as if my toe is now 100% healed as I managed a 5km run with no problems.

On our previous visit to this hotel two weeks ago, Roz had completed a customer service questionnaire and been rewarded with a US$14 voucher which could be used in any of the hotels bars and restaurants. We had another questionnaire put under our door today. I filled it in and we got another voucher for the same amount meaning that we had $28 to spend. We had a couple of beers in our room while watching a preview of this weekends premiership games. before going down to eat.

The hotel restaurant has a "theme night" each evening and today's was an Indian buffet for $16 each. We both had the buffet and a bottle of beer which came to a total of $38 - almost as much as we had spent on all our evening meals since getting to Myanmar ($48 in total for the previous 15 nights). With our vouchers, this came to $10, still more than we have spent on any meal since getting here but excellent value. There must of been 25 - 30 different meat, fish and vegetable dishes as well as a selection of rice and Indian breads. When we were both totally full of curry, especially the excellent prawn curry, there was an equally impressive selection of desserts and ice-cream. A very enjoyable way of spending our last evening here.

Saturday, 20th January 2007 Another excellent breakfast followed by a couple of hours by the pool before checking out at 11:45. We got a taxi to the airport and departed about 25 minutes late at 2:10, arriving in Bangkok at about 3:45, Thai time (30 minutes ahead). It was a more eventful arrival than usual in Bangkok. Firstly, the immigration officer only gave Roz a 30 day stay in her passport instead of 60 days. There was a bit of an argument about this before a supervisor resolved the issue. Then our taxi crashed into another taxi before we had got out of the grounds of the airport. We were not travelling with any great speed so there were no injuries but we had to wait while a policeman went off to get us another taxi. By 5:15 we were checked back into the Asia Hotel.

We both went out for about an hour - Roz to use the internet and catch up on her e-mail while I began looking for birthday presents. I went to Panthip Plaza hoping that I would be able to find a camera and underwater case for a reasonable price. I found two shops which sold the Canon Ixy80, which is the equivalent of the Ixus65, together with the underwater case for about Baht16,500 which seemed a pretty good deal - less than I was expecting to pay. I wanted to check the reviews of the camera before buying but just about decided that I would come back tomorrow and buy the camera and case.

We had a few drinks in the room after showering and stayed in the room for the first half of the Liverpool v Chelsea game. We decided not to go to our usual restaurant for tea but went to a place just round the corner that was showing the second half. We got through a bottle of Sang Som with our meal and then returned to the room for another while watching Spurs v Fulham and listening to World Service (Swansea 0, Forest 0). Don't really remember final score too clearly!

Regan and Annette arrived in Bangkok on their way home from New Zealand and checked into the hotel at about 10:30. They did not join us for a drink due to being too tired!

Sunday, 21st January 2007 Woke early and could not get back to sleep, probably due to too much diet coke yesterday evening. We got up at about 9 and went to McDonald's for breakfast. Neither of us was too badly hung-over although I was not feeling 100% for most of the day. Roz went to the pool while I went to Panthip Plaza. I went onto the internet and checked the camera which all seemed OK. I returned to one of the shops I had been to yesterday and managed to get camera and underwater case for Baht16,200 with a 1GB memory card and a case for my camera thrown in as well. I had not really expected to get this so easily so was very pleased with my purchases. I had also been considering upgrading the laptop's hard drive - something about which I remained undecided but did not actually do anything about. The effects of last nights Sang Som made this decision a bit too difficult to make!

We met Regan and Annette for lunch in the MBK food centre, did some more shopping and returned to the hotel. We started drinking again at 6:30. Regan and Annette came down to our room and we had a couple of beers before going out to eat. We ate at Ban Khun Mae and then got a taxi back to the place we ate in last night so we could watch Arsenal v Man Utd. We were still there at about 1:30 and then returned to our room to finish the Sang Som we had left over from yesterday. Only vaguely remember the latter stages of the night.
