World Trip 2006/7 - Week 53

Monday, 11th June 2007 Checked out of the hotel at 7:45 and caught the 8:11 train into Riga. We were in Riga by 9:30 and walked to the hotel we had booked in the old town. Our room was not ready when we arrived so we left our luggage at the hotel and went off to see the sights. We started doing the Lonely Planet walking tour as the first part of this took in most of what we wanted to see.

We walked round a few churches and other old buildings which were all quite impressive. Also went to the Freedom Monument and managed to time our visit with a visit from the Estonian president. We sampled the local pancakes before returning to the hotel and checking into our room. We went out and started drinking at 3ish. We had planned to have a couple of drinks and return to the hotel for showers but the discovery of a happy hour with 90p a pint Franziskaner put an end to that! Ended the evening in a Belgium Beer Cafe across the road from our hotel and inevitably do not remember getting home.

Tuesday, 12th June 2007 Apart from getting up for breakfast, we did not leave the room until 1ish. We continued our sightseeing starting off with a trip to the top of a church tower for good views of the city. Apart from that, we spent a couple of hours just wandering around the old city again.

We had a few more drinks again in the evening. Started off at the happy hour again and continued drinking until about 11 before heading back to the hotel.

Wednesday, 13th June 2007 Checked out of the hotel and got a local bus out to the airport. Plane left just about on time at 11:10 and got into Stanstead at 11:40 local time. We picked up a hire car and joined the traffic jams on the M25 again.

Got back to Kingsclere at 3ish and spent most of the rest of the day trying to get our phone reconnected!

Thursday, 14th June 2007 Got up and drove down to Swansea. Dropped the car off at Forrestfach where we met Mum before driving down to Llansteffan. We were joined by Jane and Ayla for tea and ended up getting through five bottles of wine between us. Got to bed by about 1am.

Friday, 15th June 2007 Had to get back to Kingsclere by 1 to wait for a BT engineer to come and look at our phone socket. When he did turn up, he could not fix the problem but said someone else would have to come out and reconnect us. Still no phone line so no internet.

Spoke to Christina in the afternoon and found out that I have a job at Ericsson to go back to. This could not have worked out a lot better for me as I do not even have to go to an interview. Very good news. Started preparing for redecorating the house - took all office furniture to the tip and bought paint, etc but did not actually start doing any painting.

Went out for a couple of pints at Aldermaston after all the thought about decorating. Had a couple more in the house with tea and a couple more down The Swan. It did not seem to have changed a lot - same people, same beer - quite an enjoyable evening.

Saturday, 16th June 2007 Drove down to Bognor to go for lunch with Dad and Doris. Dad not up to going out due to COPD so had pizza in the bungalow instead. As cheerful a visit as ever but we were away by 3 and back in Kingsclere by 4:30. Went out on our bikes for an hour or so but the weather was still not really hot enough for beer gardens so we just cycled 11 - 12 miles. Had our second meal in a row from Domino's while watching tele and had an early night.

Sunday, 17th June 2007 Up at 6:30 to drive to Gatwick for the 10:45 flight to Sharm. We parked very close to the airport and checked in after about 45 minutes of queuing. Flight left just about on time and arrived in Sharm at 6ish local time. We sorted out our visas and got our luggage without any problems but then things started to go wrong!

We were supposed to be collected at the airport and be transferred to the MV Orchid for a six night live-aboard. There was no-one there to meet us which was slightly concerning. Sharm airport has free wifi so we went onto the internet to try and find contact details of someone we could talk to and found that the MV Orchid now departs every Saturday and left yesterday. We tried to contact Dive Tours who we had booked the whole trip with but were unable to get through to them. We had no real option but to get a taxi into Sharm and try and find a room for the night. Once in Sharm, we managed to get through to Graham from Dive Tours but he was out of the office and could not do a lot. We checked into The Camel Hotel for the night and started to look for other possible liveaboards.

We were both knackered by this time and could not be bothered to go out to eat. Finished off our sandwiches with a couple of bottles of beer and our duty free Bacardi in the room instead.
