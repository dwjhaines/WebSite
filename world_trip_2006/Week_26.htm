World Trip 2006/7 - Week 26

Monday, 4th December 2006 Got up at about 9 for breakfast but did not leave the room again until after midday. We were nowhere near as badly hung-over as we had been in Bangkok but slept for most of the morning anyway. We spent the afternoon on the beach apart from a trip out for lunch at 2ish. Ran for 20 minutes before tea at our hotel restaurant.

Tuesday, 5th December 2006 Another day of not doing a lot apart from reading on the beach and trying to sort out hotels/flights for later in the trip. Booked another days diving for tomorrow. Roz phoned both her mum and Cathy. I ran for about 26 minutes.

Wednesday, 6th December 2006 Dived again with Wing Divers. When we go the boat, we discovered that Ray, who we had dived with previously, was ill and there were apparently no other dive masters available. This meant that we would be diving with an instructor (Dave) and two students who were completing their open water course. This did not seem ideal but it did not turn out too badly.

We spent about 30 minutes snorkelling at the first site while waiting for the students to complete some of their skills. We all dived almost as soon as they had finished and we were able to continue the dive with Dave after the students were low on air - ending up with a 73 minute dive. We did the second dive with them again and although they did one or two skills during the dive, it did not affect us too much. Neither dive was overly spectacular although both were still quite enjoyable with enough to see. Both dives were very shallow, partly due to who we were diving with and partly due to the visibility not being that good anyway. Got back to the hotel at about 6:45.

Thursday, 7th December 2006 Did not do much all day again. Due to the high tides at the moment, there was hardly any beach to lie on for most of the day so had to sunbathe in the hotel garden in front of our balcony. Spent an hour or two there in the morning, checked e-mail, had lunch, a couple more hours in the sun, etc, etc. We still do not appear to be getting much closer to either sorting out accommodation in Chiang Mai or getting anything booked in Myanmar.

Ate out at the same restaurant on the beach that we ate at last night and were back in the room by 9.

Friday, 8th December 2006 We had to change rooms this morning as we wanted to stay one night longer than we had planned to and our existing room was unavailable for the extra night. We spent an hour or so lying in the garden (no beach again) and moved at about 11. The new room was slightly better due to having an excellent balcony overlooking the beach (or the sea when we moved in!). We sunbathed on the balcony for a while before heading off to use the internet. We found that we had finally managed to book a room in Chiang Mai for Monday night and things seem to be progressing with our Myanmar booking.

We also tried to book Nok Air flights from Mae Hong Son back to Chiang Mai for 9 days time as it is a cheap flight that will save us a days bus travel. When we tried to book this on-line, we could not do it due to not having a Thai credit card. We attempted to book over the phone and Roz was told that we had 24 hours to make the payment at either a Nok Air office, a bank or at a 7-11. The first 7-11 we tried did not know what we were talking about but after lunch we tried the other one and it went remarkably smoothly. We gave the cashier a reference number, paid about Baht 1800 for our two tickets and got a till receipt with our names and the flight details.

 We faxed all the required details to the travel agent that is sorting out our Myanmar flights so hopefully that is getting close to being sorted out. With lunch, this had taken about three hours to sort out.

We had about an hour on the beach after sorting everything out and it was then time to watch the sun setting from our balcony while starting on the beer again. It was a very similar session to the one five nights ago with the same end result, i.e. we do not remember getting back to the room!

Saturday, 9th December 2006 Once again, our hangovers were not too bad but we still spent most of the morning in our room. Did not really do much for the rest of the day. We had lunch in the same place we have eaten in almost every day since we have been here and had a couple of hours on the beach during the afternoon.

As it was our last night on Koh Chang, we both felt like having a few beers. Roz suggested getting a few bottles for listening to the football and I was keen on having a couple on the balcony while watching the sun set (did not feel like running). We had two bottles of Leo for watching the sunset, had two bottles of Chang with our meal and bought 4 more bottles of Leo to consume while listening to World Service (Blackpool 1, Swansea 1). I was just about asleep by final whistle at midnight.

Sunday, 10th December 2006 We checked out of the hotel at 8 and began what was to become a 29 hour journey to Chiang Mai via Bangkok. Getting to Bangkok all went as smoothly as we could of possibly hoped for. We got a songthaew from outside our hotel to the ferry pier in time for the 9 o'clock ferry. On the other side there was another songthaew waiting to take people to the bus station so we were able to be on a bus heading for Bangkok by about 10:45. The journey to the Eastern Bus Station in Bangkok took slightly longer than going the other way due to a lunch stop but we we were still there by about 4:30. We got the sky train and then MRT to the train station and left our rucksacks in left luggage as we still had 5 hours to kill before our train.

We returned (via MRT and sky train again) to the MBK shopping centre to kill time for a couple of hours. We bought ourselves a "Happy SIM" to give us cheap phone calls and used it straight away to book another Nok Air flight - from Bangkok to Krabi on 22nd January. We ate in the food hall and then returned to the railway station stopping at another 7-11 to pay for the flights on the way.

We had an hour or so to kill at the station but the train was in by 8:45 so we could get our luggage in place and hung around on the platform until departure. We left just about on time at 10 and were in our beds asleep about an hour later.
