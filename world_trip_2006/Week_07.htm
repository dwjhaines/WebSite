World Trip 2006/7 - Week 7

Monday, 24th July 2006 A day spent almost entirely on buses. We got the 8:45 from Rovinj to Rijeka getting in at about 12:10 and then straight onto another at 12:30 taking us down the coast to Zadar where we arrived slightly earlier than expected at 5:15. The journey was not too bad as the scenery was pretty spectacular most of the way down. We had expected problems finding accommodation in Zadar's old town and there were not too many options, but an agency found us a room for about £24. It was not ideal but was OK for one night. We walked round the old town which was pleasant enough and had a meal involving neither alcohol or pizzas. We had returned to our room when we decided that we may be better off getting an early bus tomorrow so I went back to the agency we had used before and got two tickets for tomorrow's 7 am bus to Trogir.

The room turned out to be one of the worst we had stayed in - either too hot or too noisy depending on the window being open or closed. Did not sleep well.

Tuesday, 25th July 2006 We got up at 6 and walked to the bus station to get on the 7am bus. We arrived in Trogir about three hours later. This was another place that we did not have any accommodation booked for despite our attempts to get something sorted out on the internet. We were not sure what we wanted to do but eventually, an agency found us a "studio apartment", about 1500m from Trogir (and 100m up a steep hill from a beach) on another island called Čiovo. We were collected by the owner of the apartment from the agency and driven back to the apartment. The room was ideal for a couple of days with a nice terrace and was handy for shop, restaurant etc.

We did not bother going back to see Trogir's sights but had a lazy day with a couple of hours on the beach in the afternoon and an evening meal in the nearby restaurant.

Wednesday, 26th July 2006 We had breakfast on our terrace before walking the 1500m back to have a look around Trogir. It was another very nice place, the entire island consisting of the old town. We went into the cathedral and up the tower for good views off the whole island as well as Čiovo. We spent another hour or so wandering around the town before heading back to the apartment. Another afternoon of not doing a lot. I went down to the beach for a swim but apart from that, we spent the whole afternoon on the terrace outside our apartment. Ate in the same place as yesterday.

Thursday, 27th July 2006 We were given a lift back to Trogir by the apartment owner and just got there in time to get the 7:40 bus to Split. The journey took an hour and we headed down to the port area. We bought tickets for the 10pm ferry to Vela Luka on Korčula and were on the ferry by about 9:45. The ferry journey was quite enjoyable stopping in the centre of Hvar  on the way. We were in Vela Luka by about 1:40 and then had a 30 minute wait for the water taxi across to Croatia Divers on the other side of the bay.

It took a couple of hours to get ourselves sorted out at Croatia Divers. We had to fill in all the paperwork and then try on full length 5mm wet suits. We were both given wet suits that were far too small for us so this took some considerable time. We were eventually shown to our room, just behind the dive shop, sometime after 4, having booked ourselves on to dive trips for the next two days.

We walked around the bay to the main part of Vela Luka in the evening for our evening meal and a walk round the town which was another very nice little place. Back in the room by about 10:30.

Day 5 with no alcohol and feeling a lot better for it!

Friday, 28th July 2006 We were not due to dive until this afternoon so wandered into town again stopping to buy breakfast from a supermarket on the way. We found out details of buses for Sunday and had another look around and were at our room for 12:30. Roz was feeling faint on the way back and had to stop to sit down, but after lying in the room for 20 minutes, decided that she was OK to dive.

We were at Croatia Divers by 1pm and after about an hour of messing about, we were in our full length wet suits and boarded the "rib" to take us to the dive sites. We dived the Blue Hole and one other site. We both had problems getting under the water on the first dive and the boat driver had to keep handing us more and more weight until we could sink. I ended up diving with 12kg, 2kg of which I carried for the entire dive. Getting into all our gear in thick wet suits on a crowded boat did not help as we were not as relaxed as we should of been but we got there eventually. The dive sites were both OK without being overly spectacular. There was no comparison between diving here and in places like the Red Sea or Thailand but it was still a reasonably enjoyable way of spending the afternoon.

We were booked on the morning dive trip for tomorrow so had a pizza and an early night.

Saturday, 29th July 2006 We arrived at the dive centre at 8am and then sat around waiting for 2 hours. This was due mainly to the late arrival of other divers but also a general lack of organisation. We did not have too much else to do today so it was not too important but not the best of starts to the day. When we finally did get to the dive sites, they were once again quite enjoyable dives. The first through a series of caves and the second was allegedly a drift dive but as there was no current at all, this was not a wholly accurate description. We were both pleased to have done the diving but would not rush back to dive here again and would certainly not consider a holiday in Croatia specifically for the diving.

By the time we had got back to the dive centre and sorted out our gear, it was almost 2pm. We walked round the bay and had lunch and did not do to much else for the rest of the day. Roz spent an hour on the internet; I spent most of the rest of the day lying in our room reading.

The weather the last few days has been getting increasingly hot and humid. The rooms we have been staying in since leaving Rovinj, particularly here in Vela Luka and the one in Zadar, have not been designed for such temperatures so have been constantly too hot and sleeping has been a problem. Tonight we finally had the storm which hopefully will clear the air a bit. The was quite a lot of thunder and lightening in the distance during the evening but it did not really reach us until about 3am. When it did, it was one of the most spectacular displays of lightening I have ever seen.

Sunday, 29th July 2006 We got the water taxi back across the bay in time for the 10am bus to Korčula Town. At mid day, we were collected from the Korčula Town bus station by the son of the owners of the apartment we were booked into in Lumbardo. The apartment is the nicest we have had in Croatia with a kitchen and a terrace and great views of the sea and the Croatian mainland. It is on a hill with plenty of windows so is considerably cooler than other places we have stayed in the last week.

We had a walk around Lumbardo and then lunch on our terrace before heading out to the beach. The beach is about 15 minutes walk from where we are staying and is the first sandy beach we have had in Croatia.

In the evening, after seven alcohol-free days, we started on the wine again. The family that own the apartments (four generations of them) all live in the same place and are very friendly. They had already given us half a litre of home made wine and after we had finished that on our terrace, they bought us another. They also seem quite keen on feeding us so we had some sort of deep fried vegetable things to accompany our wine. We went out to the pizza place recommended to us by Regan and got through another litre of wine with our meal. We ended the evening with a few more glasses back at the apartment.
