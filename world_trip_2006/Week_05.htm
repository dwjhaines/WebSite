World Trip 2006/7 - Week 5

Day 29 - 5th July 2006 We picked up our bikes from the station and, after sorting out a couple of problems with the panniers and Roz's bike, we headed out of Regensburg along the Donauradweg about an hour later. We stopped in Straubing for lunch and then continued on to Deggendorf where we arrived at about 6ish. This meant that we had done 88 of the 152km to Passau, leaving 64km for tomorrow.

Day 30 - 6th July 2006 We set off after breakfast at about 9 and things were going quite smoothly until Roz fell off her bike while trying to get from the road onto the pavement. She was quite shaken by the fall and had several grazes as well as hurting her wrist, shoulder and getting a bump on the head. We had about a 20 minute wait until she was able to get on her bike again and it did not look as if we were going to make it all the way to Passau. After another hour or so, she had just about recovered and we were able to complete the trip by about 2pm. The last 30 - 40 km was probably the best part of the whole journey as the cycle path was alongside the river so we had spectacular views as we cycled.

After finding a hotel room and having much needed showers, we combined some sight-seeing with some visits to beer gardens. Passau was a really nice place with plenty to see and some excellent places to drink. Although we had intended to go back to the hotel before going out for the evening, we ended up staying out until about 11.

Roz now has a swollen black eye from her accident.

Day 31 - 7th July 2006 We had another walk around the sights after breakfast, this time going into the cathedral which was particularly impressive. We got the 11:13 train back to Regensburg and returned our bikes before checking back into the Ibis near the station. After a few hours sleep, we headed off to a couple of beer gardens again. After we had had a few drinks, the idea of visiting Munich on the way home started to appeal. We stayed in beer gardens until it started to rain and then revisited a place that we had eaten in a couple of times when we visited Regensburg three years ago. Don't really remember getting back to the hotel.

Day 32 - 8th July 2006 We were still keen on Munich when we woke up. We phoned the Jedermann and found that they had returned to normal booking conditions following the world cup, so we booked a double room, had breakfast and headed South. The journey took about two hours including a stop for photographs at The Allianz Arena which is right next to the motorway.

We checked into the hotel and headed off on a tour of some of our favourite beer gardens and beer halls from previous visits. We started by walking to the Viktualienmarkt before heading back to the Augustiner beer garden via the Augustiner-Grossgaststätt. We returned to the hotel for showers before going out again to four beer halls, spending most of our time just round the corner from the Marianplatz due to them showing the third place play-off on a big screen. Things are very vague in my memory after leaving there but we went for one in the Schneider Weisse Hall (empty due to no TV) and one more over the road from there before staggering back to the hotel.

Day 33 - 9th July 2006 Our trip to Munich meant that we had slightly more driving to do to get back to Belgium and we got most of it out of the way today. It was a long day in the car - about six and a half hours including a lunch stop - and we arrived in Bonn at about 5:30.

We had a quick look round the sights and then went to an Italian pizza restaurant, across the road from our hotel to watch the World Cup Final. The atmosphere in there was excellent and although I was totally knackered and desperately in need of sleep, it turned into an enjoyable evening.

Day 34 - 10th July 2006 It took us a couple of hours to drive to Ghent and we found the Ibis remarkably easily. We managed to not start drinking until about 4ish when we made our way to Het Waterhuis. We had a couple in there before going to t Galgenhuisje, ticking off plenty more of my 300 beers. We were already getting quite drunk when we returned to the hotel for showers and we then went out to The Trappistenhuis where we stayed for the remainder of the evening.

Day 35 - 11th July 2006 We got a train to Bruges at about midday and arrived there by 12:30. We started doing part of the Lonely Planet walking tour and soon found ourselves at the "Straffen Hendrik" brewery. We did the tour which was reasonably interesting and had our first beer of the day in the tasting room. We continued walking round the sights. We spent a couple of hours in De Garre, another excellent bar, recommended by Lonely Planet and wandered around some of the sights before heading back to Ghent on the 18:03 train. Once in Ghent, we walked to the Hop Duvel and then back to our usual pizza restaurant. Stopped drinking relatively early at about 11pm.

Day 36 - 12th July 2006 Left the hotel at 10 and drove back to Calais making two stops along the way. We called in to the Hop Duvel bottle shop and also at the Sint Sixtus Abbey, the only place in the world where you can purchase Westveleteren Trappist beer. Got to Calais in time for the 14:00 ferry and where back in Dover by 14:30.
