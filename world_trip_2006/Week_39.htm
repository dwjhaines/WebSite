World Trip 2006/7 - Week 39

Monday, 5th March 2007 Two more fun dives - the first in particular was excellent. We sat down with David, the instructor here, between dives and were given more of an idea of what we are going to be doing for the next few weeks. We were also given a load of books to start working our way through and were told to finish the "assisting instructors" section by the end of the week due to the arrival of 19 Norwegian open water students on Friday.

We moved to our new room after the second dive. It seems huge compared to where we have moved from! We had wanted to do a night dive but there was a shortage of torches so as DMT's we missed out.

Someone at the dive centre had organised a fish bar-b-q for the evening. Just about everyone at Gapang, including staff and customers were there - a total of about 15 - 20 people. The meal was OK and the evening as a while quite enjoyable.

Tuesday, 6th March 2007 Two more dives and the first time either Roz or I had ever dived without the other one. At the first dive, I accompanied David while he took Fleur and Wout for the deep dive from their advanced course. Roz did a fun dive during which she saw a manta and a turtle. For the afternoon dive, Roz accompanied the advanced class for their navigation dive while I went out on the boat. I dived with Daniel again and Luka plus two customers. There was strong currents on the dive leading to a lot of confusion and us having to abort the dive after 34 minutes. During the day we started working through our manuals and made a start on the knowledge reviews.

We all for a "pizza evening" at Iboih, about 15 minutes drive away. It was the same crowd as yesterday give or take a few different faces. Excellent food but totally knackered by the end of the evening. Got back to Gapang by about 10:30.

Wednesday, 7th March 2007 We were back diving together today as we both accompanied Fleur and Wout with the last two dives of their advanced course (Naturalist and Fish ID). We also had our first attempt at going off on our own and diving the house reef outside the dive centre. This did not go quite according to plan but we made it back safely and we did have a green turtle to follow for a few minutes of the dive.

We had our third organised evening meal in a row tonight (quite unusual apparently) as it was Torsten and Silke's "snorkel test". They have recently become DM's and leave the day after tomorrow. The test itself consisted of a can of lager being poured down a snorkel and appeared to be a relatively easy part of the course! It was a pretty good meal (at Ohana's) and an enjoyable evening. Nothing goes on too late here however and we were still back in the room not long after 10.

Thursday, 8th March 2007 Not too many customers today and the only boat dive was to the deep wreck so it was a day spent mainly working through the dive master manuals. We completed the sections that we were required to get done before the arrival of the Norwegians and also got through the first three of the eight "final exams". We were working today with Mette, the second of the two instructors here. The exams were fairly straightforward but it was good to get at least some of the course completed.

This all took up most of the day but we had finished by 4 so went out for our second attempt at the house reef. Our navigation was slightly better than it was yesterday and we managed to find one of the three hawksbill turtles that lives on the reef. We sat around at the dive centre for a couple of hours before heading back to our bungalow at about 8. Finished a couple more knowledge reviews before going to bed.

Friday, 9th March 2007 Two more dives today, both with Maria. Several people left today including Torsten and Silve so the place seemed very quiet.

Saturday, 10th March 2007 Our busiest day here yet. We had 17 Dutch snorkellers arrive at about 9:30. Three of them (2 Open Water) ended up doing the morning dive. Roz and I had to help them all out underwater and then lead two of the girls back to the boat when they were low on air - our biggest responsibility yet! Roz's computer stopped working today almost certainly due to me changing the battery yesterday.

At about 2:30 the Norwegians arrived and most of the rest of the day was spent assisting with getting them sorted out with their equipment and paperwork, etc. Things were not helped by a power cut at 6pm and we did not leave the dive centre until after 9. We ate at Limbo's with Maria and were back in the room by 10 to listen to World Service.

Sunday, 11th March 2007 Another busy day. Roz and I were both assisting different groups of Norwegian students. Me and Luca were with David's two groups and Roz and Maria with Mette's. We both assisted with the first confined water session and the first open water dive for each of our two groups.
