World Trip 2006/7 - Week 19

Monday, 16th October 2006 Woke at 7 and left immediately for Heathrow. We left the car (containing Paddy, Patty and Lee) at Economy Parking and were driven to Terminal 4. We had already checked in on-line so just had to collect our boarding passes and give in our luggage.

We had breakfast once through security, bought a few things that we needed for the trip and boarded the plane for Nairobi just after the scheduled departure time of 10:15.

The flight was uneventful and we arrived in Nairobi at 8:55pm (two hours ahead of UK). We bought our visas and collected our luggage fairly quickly and were then relieved to find someone waiting for us as we came through arrivals. Roz had pre-booked our first couple of nights accommodation with the same company that we will use for our safari and they were at the airport to drive us to the hotel. We had checked into our hotel by 10 and after a quick look around, had an early night.

Tuesday, 17th October 2006 After breakfast we ventured outside our hotel for the first time. We got a taxi into the centre of Nairobi. It was not far to walk but we had US$1200 to pay our safari company in cash. We were in the safari office for quite a while sorting out what we are doing and eventually paying for it all. We were shown a couple for choices for accommodation for when we return to Nairobi and eventually chose a more expensive option than the one we had originally booked.

We spent another couple of hours in the centre. There was not a lot to see but we had a few things to sort out such as postcards, checking e-mail, etc. We had lunch, bought what we needed for the next couple of days and walked back to the hotel.

We ate our evening meal (chicken curries with varying amounts of meat) in the hotel restaurant due to it not being safe to leave the hotel on foot after about 6:30.

Wednesday, 18th October 2006 We were picked up from our hotel by Best Camping at 9 and were driven to their office again. We were given a brief about what we may see in the next few days and, after loading the mini-bus with food, we headed off for the Maasai Mara. We were fortunate that there was just me and Roz in the minibus together with our guide/driver - Mustafa.

We drove North of Nairobi and along one side of the Rift Valley before dropping down and crossing the valley floor. Her we saw our first giraffe of the day, fairly close to the road.

A lot of the afternoon was fairly boring, driving along a pot-holed, dusty road. The scenery got more interesting as we got closer to the Maasai Mara and we finally entered the park gates at 4:45.

After our first encounter with the Maasai souvenir sellers, we started driving towards our camp and were instantly impressed. Within the first few minutes, we had seen wildebeest, buffalo, gazelle and came very close to a couple of elephants (mother and 14 year old "baby") We continued to see plenty of animals - more than we had both expected - but the highlight was a herd of six elephants. We saw them from a distance and drove closer to them until we eventually blocked their path. They walked to within a couple of metres of us until we drove out of their way.

We continued to the campsite spotting about 10 - 15 hyenas on the way. The campsite was basic but fine for a couple of nights. We were shown to our two-man tent and given blankets. We had showers (hot) and then went to the "eating hut" for tea. There were only us and four French people staying at the campsite. Tea was surprisingly good but there was not a lot to do once we had eaten so we were in our tent by 9.

Thursday, 19th October 2006 We were up at 6 for the first game drive of the day at 6:30. The drive started in the same way as yesterdays - plenty of animals including a group of giraffe that we were very close to.

After about 10 minutes, our driver found out that cheetahs had been spotted and we headed off in that direction. When we found the cheetahs, there were three of them and once again we were able to get very close to them. This was fairly spectacular but we watched as they spotted a group of wildebeest and spent about 20 minutes stalking them before moving in for the kill. Apparently this is quite a rare sight and we were lucky to be able to witness it. The three cheetah then set about eating their prey while being watched by about 20 minibuses. They were not bothered by our presence and it made for some excellent photos.

We returned to the scene later in the drive when the carcass had been almost stripped clean and the vultures waited to move in for the scraps. We returned to the camp for breakfast before the second drive of the day at 10:30.

This drive was never going to be as spectacular as the others as most of the wildlife is asleep at this time. However, we returned to the carcass of the wildebeest to watch the vultures finishing it off and then found the three cheetahs, looking totally full, sleeping under a tree. Also saw hippos in the river.

The third drive of the day, after sitting around for a couple of hours at the campsite was at 4pm. We did not see many animals but we were driven to a waterhole where there were three lions sleeping. We watched them for about 10 minutes during which time they woke up and moved around for photos. The drive was slightly curtailed due to suspension problems with our van but it was an excellent end to the day.

We were joined in the camp by four female, English vets so we had someone to talk to at tea. Another good meal and another early night.

Friday, 19th October 2006 Left the camp at 8 (saw a lion while driving out through the park) and spent the next 6½ hours driving to Nakuru. We ate lunch in a restaurant in the town and entered the National Park at 3:20. It was another spectacular drive. We had our first sight of the 1000's of flamingos that live at the lake and also a white rhino. We spent a bit of time admiring the views from the "Baboon Cliff" view point and were then told about some lions further around the lake. We spotted another white rhino en route to the lions.

We saw two young male lions playing by the side of the lake and, slightly further away, two sleepy females. We drove away from the lions as it was raining and we were concerned about getting stuck in the mud, so we returned to the second rhino. Once the rain stopped, we returned to the female lions. We watched them not doing a lot for about 10 minutes before deciding to move on. However, the minibus would not start and we were stuck for about 45 minutes. During this time, the two male lions came to join the females and a few more appeared - in total there were eight of them around us. The light was starting to fade to add to the excitement but we were eventually rescued when another Best Camping minibus turned up. We joined a Spanish couple in their bus and left Mustafa alone with the lions and several other minibuses. We returned to the park entrance (spotting a black rhino on the way) to summon assistance for Mustafa, but while we were there he turned up having got the van started when he was the last one there alone with the lions.

We all proceeded to the Gracelands Hotel, just outside Nakuru, had another good meal and early night.

Saturday, 20th October 2006 We left Nakuru at 8 for the journey back to Nairobi. The minibus had yet another suspension problem which required a 45 minute visit to a mechanic on the way and we eventually got into Nairobi at about 12:30. We collected our rucksacks from the Best Camping office, tipped Mustafa KSH2000 (about £15) and checked into the SixEighty Hotel just over the road. Did not do a lot for the rest of the day. Had lunch, checked e-mail and listened to World Service. Had evening meal in hotel restaurant.

Sunday, 21st October 2006 We were collected from our hotel at 8:45 by Daniel, the taxi driver who had taken us into Best Camping on Tuesday. We had arranged for him to take us to the "Safari Walk" and the Sheldrick elephant orphanage.

We started at the safari walk by the entrance to Nairobi National Park. All the animals there have been orphaned or rescued. It was a bit like being in a zoo to start with but it was good being able to get closer to some of the animals we have already seen. Our guide got into the rhino enclosure and invited me to get in. I climbed the fence and he took photos of me while I stroked the white rhino - an incredible experience. After that, we saw lions, a leopard (only just) and then moved to the cheetahs. There were two cheetahs in their enclosure. We had seen pictures of people stroking them and we were keen to do the same, One of the cheetahs was lying by the entrance and was perfectly happy to let us stroke it and play with it. He purred contentedly while we scratched his neck - so like Harvey it was untrue! It was unbelievable that this was the same thing that we had seen capture and eat a wildebeest a few days ago.

We moved from the safari walk to the elephant orphanage in time for the 11 o'clock feeding. There were more visitors there than we had expected but it was still another incredible experience. We saw nine elephants aged between 6 weeks and 18 months as they were fed and bathed. Again, the elephants came right up to us and were not afraid to be stroked by everyone watching. We watched them for an hour before heading back to Nairobi. Both places had been excellent and it had been a very enjoyable and unforgettable morning.

Spent most of the day either in the room or on the internet although we did venture out of the hotel for a short walk and for lunch (Swansea 2, Millwall 0).
