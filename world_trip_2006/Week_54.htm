World Trip 2006/7 - Week 54

Monday, 18th June 2007 A frustrating day trying without much success to find ourselves another live-aboard. We had a couple that seemed quite hopeful but were either full, not going due to not enough people or just unsuitable for what we want. We had a text message from Graham at about 12:15 but apart from that did not get much help all day. After we had checked out of The Camel Hotel, Roz called the Bedouin Moon in Dahab. They have rooms and seem quite happy to move our booking. They also agreed to come and collect us at 4pm. Had lunch and spent an hour by the pool but spent a lot of the afternoon looking for live-aboards.

We were picked up by a minibus driver at 4:15 and were back at the Bedouin Moon by 5:30. Got a room at the front of the hotel again but as the place seemed fairly deserted, this was never going to be a problem. We received and sent a couple more e-mails and finally had an e-mail from Graham explaining his lack of help today and telling us that he would try and find something for us tomorrow.

We walked into Dahab for tea and had an excellent meal at our usual place on the seafront before getting a taxi home.

Tuesday, 19th June 2007 We finally managed to start diving today. We turned up at the dive centre after breakfast and it was good to see a few familiar faces around - yesterday the whole place had seemed pretty dead. We did two dives with Mark who we had dived with last year, at The Canyon and Rick's Reef. Both very enjoyable and relaxing dives. Had my first experience of a cleaner wrasse in my mouth at The Canyon.

Back at the hotel by about 3 and we returned to trying to sort out our live-aboard. We had a couple of e-mails about trips that we could go on leaving on Saturday. Both are a bit more expensive than the one we were originally booked on but seemed quite good options. We also had an e-mail from Graham and he is trying to get us onto one of these for the same price as it is operated by the same company as the one we were booked onto. Also had a text about my job contract faxed over here for me to sign. Not the most relaxing couple of days of the trip so far!

We did manage to get an hour or so by the pool in the late afternoon - it is possible to sunbathe far later at the moment than it is in September. We stared walking towards the seafront area again in the evening and stopped at an Italian restaurant to eat.

Still nothing definite confirmed re the live-aboard!

Wednesday, 20th June 2007 Another two dives "Down South". Dived with a Dutch couple, Monique and Boris. First dive we had not dived before and had a couple of nice caves. The second was Golden Blocks which was OK but a site that we have dived before and not been particularly impressed with.

Back at the hotel, there was still nothing from Graham. We sent him another e-mail and eventually he did call us at the hotel  It looks as if he has managed to get us on a live-aboard for Saturday at last. It also looks as if the boat we will be going on will be a lot better than the one we were booked onto so things have not turned out too badly in the end. Whether or not the upgrade will have been worth all the hassle we have had to endure over the last few days remains to be seen.

Got a fax through from work with all the paperwork that I have to complete and fax back. Hopefully this is also something that is coming to an end.

We started on our Nitrox course in the evening. We were hoping that there would be free Nitrox on the live-aboard, so it finally seemed a good time to get it done. Roz started on the book and we both watched the video before going for tea. It was already 8ish by this time so we ate at the Bedouin Moon. They still do a good chicken Balti!

Thursday, 21st June 2007 Two good dives at the Blue Hole. First we dived from Bells to the Blue Hole and on the second dive we did the right side. We were in quite a big group today but we had asked to be able to do longer dives and really ended up doing both dives with Adam. One drawback to diving with Mabruk, the Egyptian DM is that he likes to drag the day out for as long as possible. It takes about an hour from when we arrive at a dive site until we get in the water, we have a 2 - 3 hour surface interval and sit around having drinks in the restaurant after the second dive. Did not get back to Reef 2000 until after 4.

We got half way through our Nitrox course which is all pretty simple coming as close as it does to the stuff we had to learn during DM training. Had a few beers in the evening firstly on our balcony, a couple in Adam's Bar on our way into Dahab and a couple more with our meal. Quite pissed by the time we got our taxi home.

Friday, 22nd June 2007 Another days diving Down South with Mabruk and two Dutch couples. Once again we spent far too long killing time in the restaurant. The first dive we went off on our own after 30 minutes due to the others having worse air consumption than us. It was a bit of a confusing site to find our way around but we sorted ourselves out eventually and got out at the right place. The second dive we did with Adam again.

Back at the dive centre, we completed our Nitrox course and both passed the exam without any problems. I finally managed to get all my details e-mailed back to Ericsson something that has dragged on for most of this week. Once all that was finished, we had a few beers again. A similar evening to last night but instead of going for a meal, we got a few more takeaway beers and bought kebabs to eat back on our balcony.

Saturday, 23rd June 2007 We had asked to spend less time away from the hotel today so did two local dives. We came back after the first so had a couple of hours by the pool and then went out again at 1 for the second. The second dive at Islands was particularly enjoyable.

After the second dive we checked out of our room and sat around waiting for our transfer back to Sharm. After a bit of confusion about which jetty we should be taken to, we eventually boarded Typhoon round about 7. It is a much better boat than anything we have been on before for a live-aboard. There is loads of space, we have a decent size air-con cabin and everything about the boat seems very luxurious compared to some we have been on. We were the first guests on the boat so had a couple of hours to sort out our equipment and generally look around. The other guests (14 of them in total) had all arrived by 10 so we had our welcome briefing followed by a late tea. Back in our cabin by 11:15.

Sunday, 24th June 2007 Woke up as the boat left Sharm just before 7. It was only a short journey to the first dive site, Ras Katy where we all had to do a check dive. There were fourteen other guests on the boat, all from England, including a group of eight who all knew each other. We were split into two groups, each group went with one of the two dive guides, Adam and Tracey, also from England. The site was nothing spectacular but it was an easy 40 minute dive to make sure everyone was happy with their equipment, etc.

After the first dive, we had a couple of hours journey down to Abu Nuhas. The itinerary of the live-aboard we are on is entitled "Brothers Wreck Special" meaning that not only do we travel down to The Brothers, we also will be diving a lot of wrecks. We did two at Abu Nuhas, Carnatic and Giannis D. Most of the divers on the boat were happy doing the dives unguided but we started both dives with Tracey before finishing up on our own. Both wrecks involved some penetration and we were both happier entering the ships with someone who knew where they were going!

Both dives were very good with the second being the better of the two due to the amount of the ship that was still intact and the fact that there was more to see in the shallower parts of the dive.

It was gone 7 by the time we finished the last dive. We had our evening meal before the boat began the ten hour journey South to The Brothers. Sat around in the lounge for an hour or so and had a relatively early night.
