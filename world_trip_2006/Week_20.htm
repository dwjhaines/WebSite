World Trip 2006/7 - Week 20

Monday, 23rd October 2006 We checked out of the hotel at 11 and returned to Best Camping to book another safari. We had decided on a 2½ day trip to Amboseli just before we fly home but ended up booking a 4 day trip that includes Tsavo West as well for $480. This fits in better with our plans and means that we spend the last night of our trip in a lodge. We got our photos backed up to CD while in the office but later discovered that about 60 of my pictures had somehow been deleted (from 2nd and third drives on Thursday)

Killed time for the rest of the day waiting for the evening train to Mombassa. We got a taxi to the station at 5:30 in time for the 7:00 train.

The train pulled in at 6:10 and we went straight to our cabin. We had paid for first class tickets which meant that we had a two birth cabin to ourselves. Our carriage had no electricity so we were all given torches but otherwise it was all very comfortable. We departed at 7:20 and we headed straight to the restaurant car for dinner. We shared a table with Margaret and Angel - two ex-pats wives. The food was pretty good although it was all served in quite a rush to ensure we vacated the seats for the second sitting. All in all, a quite enjoyable experience.

When we returned to our compartment, I discovered that someone had stolen my torch and a packet of batteries. This would almost certainly of happened while our luggage was stored at the hotel during today.

Our beds had been made up while we were at tea and we were both in bed by about 10:30.

Tuesday, 24th October 2006 Up at 6 for breakfast after a bumpy night with what felt like frequent emergency stops. The train pulled into Mombassa at 9:30, only 90 minutes late - quite good from what we had been told previously.

As soon as we were off the train, Mombassa seemed a more pleasant, laid back city than Nairobi had been. We walked the mile to our hotel and were given an excellent room. Lonely Planet had said that the hotel was one of the best deals in Kenya and the room alone made staying in Mombassa a second night seem like a good option.

We went for an Indian thali meal for lunch which was also excellent and made up our minds about staying a second night. We walked out to the Scandinavian Express office and booked our ticket on the 8am bus to Dar es Salaam on Thursday. Spent the rest of the afternoon catching up on sleep and reading.

For tea, we walked a couple of blocks to an excellent curry house - one of the best meals we have had in Kenya. Watched Chesterfield v West Ham in our room before going to sleep.

Wednesday, 25th October 2006 In the morning we did the walking tour of Mombassa's old town. Parts of it were quite nice but it was nothing too spectacular. We did not bother going into the Fort as  it was ksh800.

Had another thali for lunch and then got a minibus out to a handicraft cooperative looking for souvenirs. It was quite interesting looking around the area where a lot of the stuff was made but we ended up not buying anything. Got a minibus back to the hotel for siestas.

We ate at the same Indian restaurant as last night - the food has definitely been the highlight of Mombassa - and returned to the hotel for more live (?) football (Coventry v Colchester for some reason)

Thursday, 26th October 2006 Breakfast at 7 followed by a short taxi ride to the Scandinavian Express office for the 8 am bus to Dar es Salaam. The journey was 10 hours and not too bad really. We crossed the border into Tanzania at about 10am (another $50 each for a visa) and we arrived in Dar on time just before 6. We got a taxi to the Safari Inn via a cash-point and had much needed showers before going out to eat. Another city where it is not really safe to go wandering around after dark.

Friday, 27th October 2006 We were woken at 1am when Dar's electricity went of and generators started up all over the place. The noise was deafening but our hotel's generator did not power fan or air-con so the room became hot as well - not ideal for sleeping!

When we eventually got up, we walked to the port to buy our ticket to Zanzibar on the 12:30 ferry ($20 each). We then went to the Coastal Aviation office to see what options we have for the week between finishing our diving and starting our second safari.

We returned to the hotel and phoned up and booked a hotel on Zanzibar using the "Zantel" SIM card that we had bought (calls to the UK about 12p a minute!).

We checked out and got a taxi to the port. The ferry journey was just over three hours. Much of the journey was in heavy rain and we were lucky with the seats that we got - half the seats in economy were soaked. The rain was at its heaviest as we approached Stonetown and the whole deck was soaked. We waited of the ferry for about 10 minutes after we had docked until the rain eased. After getting our passports stamped again, we were met by someone from our hotel.

After checking in, we went for something to eat and then went for a walk around Stonetown which was a far nicer place than anywhere we have stayed before on this part of the trip. After showering, we went to Mercury's for a meal and a few beers. It was the first decent place we have found to drink since arriving in Kenya - we stayed there until our money ran out and returned to the hotel.

Saturday, 28th October 2006 We decided against a "spice tour" and spent most of the day in Stonetown. We had quite a few things to do before we head off to Nungwi tomorrow. We had to take out Tshs1.2 million to pay for our diving. WE found out about diving from Stonetown and more info about Pemba, Mafia and Arusha NP. It looks as if our best option after Nungwi will be to spend a few days back in Stonetown before flying to Tanga. However, we will not make any decisions until we have seen what Nungwi is like. We continued our souvenir shopping with plenty more wooden animals and a few other things.

We were back in the room to listen to the football on World Service (Northampton 1, Swansea 0) and also had a choice of two live premiership games on our tele. After the football we watched the end of England's O.D.I. against W. Indies and went to Mercury's again. We had pizzas and a couple of beers but an earlier night than yesterday.

Sunday, 29th October 2006 We were picked up by a minibus at 8 and driven North to Nungwi where we arrived at about 9:15. We were dropped off at Sensation Divers and met the owner. He arranged for someone to take us round a few hotels before we chose where to stay as his guest house was full. We ended up at Amaan Bungalows in a decent room for $50 a night.

We paid for the room and diving in advance getting rid of about $900 in cash that we had been carrying around, and carried our luggage to our bungalow.

We had lunch on our balcony and then walked down to the beach which was nicer than we had expected - nice sand, clear water, a few bars but not too many people. Definitely one of the best we have ever been to! This together with the fact that our room is good and cheaper than expected means we will probably end up extending our stay in Nungwi.

We returned to the dive centre at 4 to find out what is happening tomorrow and then walked out to an aquarium about a mile South of the village. The aquarium is home to a family of about 14 hawksbill turtles and we paid Tshs3000 each to see them being fed. Neither of us were totally sure about how good this was for the turtles but they all seemed well enough looked after and it was excellent being able to see so many of them close up.

By the time we had walked back there was a really good sunset that we watched from one of the beach bars. We had our evening meal in one of the hotel's beach side restaurants and this probably further increased the chances of us staying longer than planned.
