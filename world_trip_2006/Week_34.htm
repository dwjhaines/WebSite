World Trip 2006/7 - Week 34

Monday, 29th January 2007 Another typical day of not doing a lot. Roz wandered round a few hotels at Long Beach during the afternoon but found that no-where is as good value as Kaw Kwang so we will probably return here when we come back to Lanta next month. We walked into Saladan again in the evening and ate at Lanta Seafood.

Tuesday, 30th January 2007 Two dives at Koh Ha. Both dives pretty good again - the vis is a lot better than at Christmas.

It looks as if our travel plans for the next few weeks may have to be altered slightly. We had enquired about staying at Kaw Kwang from Feb 15th for another 9 nights but discovered that the whole place is full for the second half of February. Not sure what we will do instead as the other options at Lanta are not as good.

Wednesday, 31st January 2007 We decided that as we would not be returning to Kaw Kwang later in the month, we would stay here another four nights before going up to Khao Lak. We eventually established that a room is available and we move from 205 to 204 tomorrow.

Another fairly quiet day. Spent time by the pool and went into Saladan for lunch. While in Saladan, we booked another couple of days diving with Lanta Diver for Friday and Saturday. I ran along the beach in the evening before we started drinking again. We ate at Sunset with a couple of bottles of Sang Som.

Thursday, 1st February 2007 We were up for breakfast at about 8:30 and moved rooms immediately after we had eaten. We spent the morning by the pool, and had lunch at Kaw Kwang. I walked into Saladan in the afternoon only to get thrown out of an internet cafe due to there being police on the island. Not totally sure what was going on but all the internet places seemed to be closing down and locking up in quite a panic. I spoke to one of the police involved who told me it was something to do with copyright but it should all be back to normal tomorrow.

I ran along the beach again in the evening. Neither of us was enthusiastic about going too far to eat so we had tea in Kaw Kwang and had an early night.

Friday, 2nd February 2007 We had booked a trip to King Cruiser today which did not leave until 1:30. The trip included the dive at King Cruiser and a night-dive at Koh Bida. We spent a couple of hours by the pool in the morning and were picked up by Lanta Diver at 1:15. We arrived at King Cruiser at 4:30 only to find that the buoy lines had been removed. Due to the currents it is not safe to dive here without the buoy lines so we had to divert to Shark Point instead. This did not turn out too badly as this was a really good dive. We dived with Tod, a Thai DM as Anjali had hit her head on something and did not feel like diving. He had never dived the site before (neither had just about anyone else on the boat except us) but seemed to find his way round with no problems. Excellent coral and plenty of fish.

Anjali had recovered to lead us on the night dive. The dive was OK- plenty of crabs - but probably the strongest currents we have experienced on a night dive. We did not get back to Saladan until 11pm and got back to Kaw Kwang at 11:15

Saturday, 3rd February 2007 We had not realised that we would be back from King Cruiser so late when we had booked two days diving but were collected again at 6:45 for a trip to Hin Muang and Hin Daeng. Once again, it was too windy for us to reach the dive sites we were aiming for so we did two dives at Koh Ha again.

Both dives were pretty good although we missed out on the whale sharks yet again. There was at least one supposed to be in the area and we spent most of the second dive looking "into the blue" but without any luck. Roz was obviously not concentrating on looking for the whale shark as she managed to spot two turtles instead!

We were back at Kaw Kwang by mid afternoon so caught up on some sleep before we started drinking again. A typical session - beers on the balcony, a couple of bottles of Sang Som with our meal at Sunset and back to the room for the end of the football. Could not really hear World Service due to poor reception but vaguely remember seeing the end of the Chelsea game before falling asleep.

Sunday, 4th February 2007 Our final day at Kaw Kwang. We will have spent 28 nights here by the time we leave but will not be coming back here when we return to Lanta due to it being fully booked.

Spent the morning by the pool and walked into Saladan at lunchtime. We spent an hour or so on the internet before having lunch at Lanta Seafood and walking  back to Kaw Kwang. I wrapped up Roz's birthday presents but did not do too much else for the rest of the day. I could not be bothered to run along the beach but did manage a walk for the sunset. Roz slept instead!

We had tea in the Kaw Kwang restaurant for the final time and were back in the room just after 9.
