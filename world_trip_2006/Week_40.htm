World Trip 2006/7 - Week 40

Monday, 12th March 2007 Could not dive today due to the lack of electricity meaning that tanks could not be filled. The electric went off for seven hours on Friday evening and then went off again at 6pm on Saturday. It returned for four hours on Sunday morning but the compressor is electric and it has not been possible to get yesterdays tanks refilled. We managed to get a few customers in the water with the staff diving with partially used tanks but the Norwegians had to make do with a day of theory.

We did not have much to do so continued our DM theory work and managed to get the physics exam out of the way.

Marjan and Ton returned from Badah Aceh with a petrol compressor and generator during the afternoon so we could start filling tanks again but it was a slow process.

During the afternoon, Roz decided that she had had enough of the whole thing and wanted to give up the DM training and leave Pulau Weh. Their have been a lot of things that have added up to her being pretty miserable with everything. Last week, both her glasses and her computer broke. Since Saturday, things have been pretty chaotic at the dive centre and we seem to be spending more time trying to get together enough BCD's and regs for everyone to dive than we have spent doing anything else. Roz has been in Mette's group for teaching the Norwegians - this means that everything has been done in Norwegian and she does not know what is going on. Gapang is not the ideal place for us and the lack of electricity makes it even worse. Restaurants are closing earlier than usual, if they open at all, there is a water shortage at the dive centre and our room is way too hot without a fan.

She was going to speak to Marjan tonight to give up but eventually decided to give it another 48.hours. (I am enjoying the course and am keen on staying to complete the training!)

Tuesday, 13th March 2007 Not a good day! Roz was getting more and more fed up about not understanding what was going on while Mette was getting her group ready, and when she was told that she would have to give up the computer that she was borrowing, she left the dive centre having decided to quit. She returned to the room while I went out with David's two groups and Maria covered for her with Mette's two. She was very upset about everything and wanted to leave Pulau Weh as soon as possible.

Marjan came up to speak to her about while I was in the water but she was almost 100% certain that she was going to leave. After a lot of discussion through the day, she decided to wait until Monday before making her decision. The diving should get better after the Norwegians have left and life should generally get better when the electric returns. We also swapped groups so that I will be assisting Mette and she will change to be with David.

The diving was fairly boring today as I spent a total of about five hours watching David's two groups go through almost the whole set of confined water skills.

Wednesday, 14th March 2007 Roz was a lot happier today. Being with David and having everything explained in English made life a lot easier and having the use of my computer also helped. I am now relying on borrowing the shop computers and having to listen to a lot of Norwegian but will probably cope!

Diving today was also easier as there were only a few skills before following the groups on their second open water dives. All the students are pretty good so there are no major problems to sort out.

The electricity came back on again for about six hours in the afternoon and early evening, the restaurant next to the dive centre reopened and Lumba Lumba received a new delivery of Diet Coke. Everything is looking much better!

Thursday, 15th March 2007 Another busy day. We both did Open Water Dive #3 with two different groups in the morning - I assisted Mette with her two groups and Roz continued with David. In the afternoon, we went with the Norwegians on the boat to Rubiah Sea Garden where 13 of them completed their Open Water course. Maria and I went with Mette's groups while Roz and Luca went with David's. Roz also went out with David on an Advanced night dive.

Friday, 16th March 2007 The final day of the Norwegian groups stay here. I did an OW #3 dive in the morning with four students who had not managed to finish the course yet for whatever reason. After about 5 minutes of the dive, I had to take one of them back in as she was not feeling well. Meanwhile, Roz managed to get a 70 minute dive at Batee Takong! Of the 19 Norwegians who turned up for the OW course, one did not start, two dropped out after the first day, one could not complete the course because she was ill and 15 passed.

Spent a few hours sitting around the dive centre studying physiology and then went for another fun dive from the beach (and managed to find our way back without having to go to the surface).

Saturday, 17th March 2007 One of the worst days we have had since we arrived here. The dive shop was busy due to it being a bank holiday weekend and there were too many customers for the amount of equipment again. People who had booked dives where being turned away and Roz, Maria and myself were left to try and sort out the problems and deal with angry customers as Ton and Marjan were in Banda Aceh. And the electric went off again!

In the evening we did have a very good meal cooked by one of the customers before returning to the room to listen to World Service. We also had a couple of glasses of Bacardi, the first alcohol we had consumed in 18 days!

Sunday, 18th March 2007 Due to the number of problems we had yesterday with people wanting to dive, we had decided to do three boat trips today at 8:30, 11:30 and 3:00. The first two dives were OK but Roz got the short straw with both of them and was fairly certain that she would be leaving early. She had a short dive at the first site due to one of her group being low on air. In the second she was in strong currents with an inexperienced group who had quite a few problems. The third dive was by far the best of the day but unfortunately she had had enough by then and did not go.

At this point, Roz was 100% sure that she would be leaving early. However, during the evening, everyone kept emphasising how unusual the last 10 days had been and how it was going to be a lot better in the coming days.

Tonight was Maria's last night here so she completed her snorkel test at the dive centre
