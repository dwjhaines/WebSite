World Trip 2006/7 - Week 9

Monday, 7th August 2006 Spent most of the day travelling from Kotor to Tirana in Albania. The first direct bus to the town nearest the border was at 10:50 but we  decided to get to the bus station early, keep on getting local buses in the direction that we wanted to go and see how far we got. This worked quite smoothly at first. We got the 7:00 bus to Bar and almost immediately got on another bus, the 9:10 to Ulcinj where we arrived at about 10:00. We needed to get a bus from there across the border but after a lot of asking around, found that there was nothing going in that direction until 1pm. We spent an hour wandering around trying to find other options but eventually went back to the bus station and got a taxi to take us for €30. The transport system in Albania apparently grinds to a halt after 2pm and we did not want to risk arriving after this time and being unable to reach Tirana.

Lonely Planet described the journey from Ulcinj to Shkodra as being 17km and 40 minutes. It turned out to be a lot longer than this both in terms of distance and time. It took us about 25 minutes to reach the border and then about 90 minutes to get through it, the main delay being the Albanian border guards who took over an hour to process about 20 - 25 cars. We eventually got our passports stamped and were driven, fairly quickly to Shkodra, 13km the other side of the border. Shkodra did not look the sort of place that you would choose to spend the night, or any time at all really, and we were pleased to be dropped at the minibus station for minibuses (or furgons) to Tirana. We did not have to wait long for the minibus to fill up and we were on our way by 1:30.

The journey to Tirana was shorter than expected and we arrived near the centre at about 3. The bus conductor found one of the passengers who was walking into the centre and he escorted us the 1km or so into the main square. Tirana did not look a wonderful city at first sight and after the journey that we had just had, we were not keen on having to spend too much time looking for a room so we relieved to get a room in the fist place we tried (recommended by LP). Another nice room with air-con and free internet access for €40 a night including breakfast.

Sight-seeing did not take too long as there does not seem to be anything too spectacular here to see. The main square is quite interesting with clock tower and mosque, but still very communist looking and a lot of the city still looks in need of some renovation. I got my hair cut for just over £1 and we bought our souvenir bunker ashtray but we were back in the room after just over an hour.

We made the most out of the free wireless internet that we could access from our room and then went out to sample our first traditional Albanian meal. We could not understand the menu but the waiter bought us a series of traditional dishes. We did not really know what most of it was but it was all reasonably tasty and made a change from pizza.

Tuesday, 8th August 2006 The place we were staying in was American owned and did an excellent breakfast - Mexican omelette and our first cup of tea in ages. We checked out of the room and the manager of the hotel got a taxi for us to take us to the bus station for minibuses to Berati. This led to some confusion as the taxi driver took us to a car park behind a petrol station with one or two buses but no signs of customers or ticket offices etc. After a bit of a disagreement, the taxi driver took us to the minibus stop where we immediately got into a furgon. The furgons are quite comfortable so it was a reasonably enjoyable three hour drive to Berati. On arrival there, we went to the hotel described by Lonely Planet as "the best budget B&B in Albania and got our cheapest room of trip so far (€25 including breakfast).

We went straight out to see the sights of Berati which seemed a much nicer place than Tirana. We wandered down to the centre of the town checked bus times for tomorrow and had lunch. We then headed up to the citadel above the city. This was all quite interesting in itself and gave excellent views looking back over the town. Berati was also reasonably interesting with plenty of different styles of buildings as well as the communist concrete blocks. There was nothing there that was too spectacular but it was a very good place to stopover on our way South.

We had our evening meal in the restaurant on the ground floor of our hotel. Excellent meal and I had my fist taste of Albanian raki.

Wednesday, 9th August 2006 We had another really good breakfast, slightly more traditional than yesterdays, and walked back down to the bus station for the 8am bus to Saranda. This turned out to a very enjoyable journey of just over six hours. The bus was probably the oldest one in the bus station but was reasonably comfortable. Being older also meant it did not have air-con so we were sat by an open window. There was plenty to look at out of the window for most of the journey. Initially we went through a few small towns with plenty going on. Then the scenery became very spectacular with large mountain ranges on either side of the road. Towards the end of the journey we had to climb up to a high pass through one of the ranges to get through to the coast. Along the way there were plenty of the concrete bunkers of which there are about 700,000 dotted around the country.

We arrived at the seaside resort of Saranda just after 2 and walked down to the hotel we had booked on the sea front. Once again it was an excellent room - the accommodation has been one of the highlights of Albania. The beach here is nothing special (stones again) but the place is nice enough and is ideal for a couple of nights before we head to Corfu. We had lunch and wandered along to the port to find out details of ferry times for Friday and did not do a lot else for the rest of the day.

Had a couple of beers on our balcony, which just about overlooks the sea, in the evening and ate out in the restaurant of the hotel next door.

Thursday, 10th August 2006 I was woken up at about 2 by another spectacular thunder storm. However this one did not clear quickly like some of the others we have seen on this trip and it was wet windy and surprisingly cold when we got up at about 8. It showed no sign of clearing by the time we had breakfast so we decided that it may be a good idea to leave for Corfu today instead of tomorrow. We spent half an hour at an internet cafe and got the number of the hotel we had booked in Corfu so we could let them know we were arriving a day early and checked out of our hotel by 11:15. The weather appeared to brighten up briefly but it was raining heavily again as we departed on the 1pm boat so it looked as if we had made the right decision.

The journey across to Corfu Town only took about 40 minutes but with the clocks going forward an hour, it was gone three by the time we had cleared Greek customs. We had a mile or so walk to get to the local bus station (moved since we were last here) and we got the 4:00 bus to Pelekas. We had another short walk on arrival at Pelekas to get to our room. Pelekas is a really nice small village high up on a hill with great views of the sea and a few really nice looking restaurants. It is a long way back up from the beach but there is a free bus shuttle service so this should not be a problem. I really liked the place although Roz was not so impressed!

We eventually got a room with a balcony overlooking the sea - not as good value as our Albanian rooms have been but an excellent location nonetheless. We spent an hour or so in the sun on the balcony and then went out to buy beer and wine for a few drinks before eating. We ate in Alexandros's restaurant, part of the hotel we are in. The food was excellent, far better than I remember Greek food and the souvlaki were nothing like the ones we used to eat 20 years ago! Also got through a litre of wine and half a litre of retsina during the meal followed by another drink on the balcony so it was quite late by the time we got to bed.

The temperature here is excellent and for the first time in ages it was cool enough to wear long trousers in the evening. Far cooler than it has been in either Croatia or Germany.

Friday, 11th August 2006 Had a relatively late breakfast and at 11ish we walked out of Pelekas towards Vatos to visit some of the places that we knew 20 years ago. Vatos village is only about 3 or 4 km from Pelakas, mainly downhill so it was a fairly easy walk. Surprisingly Pam Pam's was still there. It did not look any different to when we were here last time. We walked down to Camping Vatos which apart from the addition of a swimming pool, was also remarkably similar to how it was 20 years ago. It was strange seeing the place where we always used to camp as well as the bar and restaurant in exactly the same places. Even the shower blocks were in the same place and had not been modernised! A few other places had either changed beyond recognition, closed down or disappeared but after a bit of effort, we managed to locate Spyro's Taverna again. This had been modernised slightly but was basically still the same.

As we were in the area we decided we would go to Myrtiotissa beach. We walked as far as the point where you could see the beach for the first time and saw how much smaller the beach was than it had once been. It was also totally packed with a steady flow of people heading down to it. We decided not to bother and walked back up the hill to the road and on to Glyfadda. By the time we got there, we had been walking for over three and a half hours and were totally knackered. Glyfadda also was far more crowded than I remember it but it had the advantage of the free shuttle bus back to Pelekas at 5:15 so we did not have to walk back up the hill.

When we got back to the room, Roz checked her mobile for messages and discovered that her mum had had an accident and was in hospital with a broken arm. This was to have quite an effect on our plans for the next week!

We had a similar evening to yesterday apart from eating in a different restaurant.

Saturday, 12th August 2006 We got the 10:30 bus into Corfu Town. We had planned to find out options for travelling to Athens but due to Roz's mum's accident, Roz had decided that she should fly home or a couple of days. The flight prices were cheaper than expected (about €330 return) and we discovered that it was possible to include the England v Greece game in the trip so I decided that I may as well go back as well. We spent a while in an internet cafe checking the options and then went back to the travel agent and booked tickets. We are flying to Birmingham (on different flights) on Monday afternoon and flying back to Athens from Heathrow on Thursday. We returned to the internet cafe and ordered our England tickets and also booked a hire car for the trip.

It was gone 2 by the time we had our flights sorted out and tickets printed. We headed off for a walk around the old town and visited McDonalds for lunch. We failed to find "the milk bar" but the old town was all quite nice for walking around. By 4 we went for our first beer of the day and then got the 5 o'clock bus back to Pelekas.

We could just about pick up World Service from our balcony so had a couple of hours listening to the football again (Oldham 1 Swansea 0, Sunderland also lost). We had a few drinks on our balcony before going down to eat in Alexandros' restaurant again. Had a few more to drink than on previous nights and got to bed at about 1am.

Sunday, 13th August 2006 Had a late breakfast again and walked down to Pelekas beach at mid-day. Spent 4 hours on the beach getting burnt and then got the 4:45 shuttle bus back to Pelekas village. While having drinks on our balcony, we watched another spectacular storm pass by a couple of miles out to sea. By the time we went to eat it was quite cold again. Had a final evening meal in Alexandros' restaurant.
