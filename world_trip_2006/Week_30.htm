World Trip 2006/7 - Week 30

New Year's Day 2007 Not too bad hangovers despite the late night and not remembering getting back last night. We got up for breakfast before going back to bed for a couple of hours. We spent an hour or so sorting out some of our stuff ready for tomorrow and took down our Christmas lights before going for lunch at 2ish. Spent an hour or so by the pool in the afternoon.

Regan and Annette came round to eat at Kaw Kwang in the evening. They head off to New Zealand tomorrow and we will meet up with them again in Bangkok in three weeks time. Were back in the room watching football by 9. Managed to stay awake for the early game but fell asleep during the 1st half of the second match at 10.

Tuesday, 2nd January 2007 Had a couple of hours by the pool in the morning and at midday we checked out of our room. We had been in the same room for 15 nights, the longest we have stayed anywhere on the trip so far. The time has gone very quickly here and it was quite sad to be leaving again despite the fact that it is only three weeks until we return.

We left a load of our belongings, including all the diving gear and everything else that is not required for the next three weeks with reception before checking out. We also left a lot of our unwanted things, such as Christmas lights, for the cleaner, so were carrying considerably less when we left than when we arrived. We were taken by pick-up truck to the ferry pier for the 1:00 ferry to Phuket via Phi Phi. The first ferry turned out to be an over-crowded speed boat which was not particularly comfortable but did get us to Phi Phi in about 40 minutes. We got straight from that on to a ferry that left Phi Phi at 2:30 arriving at the pier in Phuket at 4:20. There was not too many transport options from the pier so we walked the 3km into Phuket Town. It was not too hot, we had a more reasonable amount of luggage for such walks and my broken toe is a lot better than it was a few days ago so the walk was not too bad. We checked into a guest house for B350 for the night.

By this time it was getting on for 6 o'clock. We had showers, and went straight out to eat not far from our hotel. I phoned Dad on the way back from eating but we were still back in the hotel by about 8 and asleep by 10.

Wednesday, 3rd January 2007 After breakfast, we went for another walk around the town. We posted a parcel back to the UK containing things we no longer need out here such as Christmas decorations, an assortment of spare cables and a cuddly snowman! We checked out of the hotel at 10:45 and walked to the bus station and got the 11:30 bus to the airport.

It took about an hour to get to the airport. Despite our check-in only taking a couple of minutes, the few people in front of us in the queue seemed to take forever to sort themselves out. When we eventually had our boarding passes, we went through to the departure lounge and to Burger King for lunch. We did not have long to wait for our departure and we took off 20 minutes late at about 2:15. We arrived in Bangkok at about 3:25. At Bangkok airport we tried, without success, to find the Royal Jordanian office and then got a taxi to our hotel.

We had booked a room at the Samran Place Hotel, just round the corner from the Asia Hotel which is where we normally stay when we are in Bangkok. The taxi driver had not heard of the hotel but with a combination of phone calls to the hotel and us giving directions we got there eventually. We had looked at rooms here when we were in Bangkok in November. The rooms are just as good as at the Asia and it is about half the price.

After checking in, we spent an hour or so using the internet, had showers and headed out for tea. We bought a couple of things from the MBK and then went to our usual restaurant in Siam Square.

Thursday, 4th January 2007 We checked out of our hotel at 8 and headed off with our luggage to the Royal Jordanian office on Silom Road. This involved a rush hour journey on the sky train with our rucksacks as we did not want to have to return to the hotel. We got to the office just after it opened and were told that we could not move our return flights to England to the date we wanted - 31st May - as the flight was full. After a bit of indecision, we changed the date to 24th May which means that we will be back in England for the play-off final in the unlikely event that either of our teams make it to Wembley. We will have the option of giving up that flight and buying a one way ticket from KL or Bali if we want to fly back on the 31st but we will worry about that nearer the time!

We got a taxi to the airport for our midday flight to Yangon, Myanmar. We had a couple of hours to kill at the new airport before boarding the plane on time. The flight was uneventful and we landed in Myanmar just before 1. Getting through immigration and customs was far smoother than expected and we were in a taxi on our way to The Traders Hotel by 1:15.

The hotel was excellent and was one of those places that we felt out of place in as soon as we walked into reception. It is owned by the Shangri-La group and had everything you would expect from that chain with a very grand looking reception area that we looked far too under-dressed for. When we checked in, we were given an envelope containing the airline tickets that we had booked via the internet from Koh Chang.

After checking in and leaving our luggage in the room, we headed out to change money and to begin our sight-seeing. There are no ATM's in Myanmar and travellers cheques and credit cards are next to useless. We have all our money in $US cash and have to change these on the black market to get any sort of decent rate. We walked to the area suggested by Lonely Planet and were approached by a few people. We were approached by someone offering a better rate - in retrospect, a too much better rate - and walked off to a shop to exchange $200. The first problem came with the sheer number of notes that we were dealing with. One US$ equals about 1250 Kyat. The highest note available is K1000 but the most common note appears to be K500. It took us about 10 minutes to count and check the amount of Kyat that we were being offered (260,000) while being watched by several locals. At this point everything seemed to be in order and we handed over our $200. The bloke doing the exchange then tried to switch one of our $50 notes for a $1 note. We assume that he was going to claim that we had only given him $151 and try and get $50 more from us. However, I spotted what he had done and pointed out where he had tried to hide the $50. He then backed out of the whole deal, claimed he only wanted $100 notes and we gave him his money back while reclaiming ours. We knew exactly how many dollars we had to start with so knew that we had not lost anything but it was easy to see how this sort of scam could of worked.

We returned to where we had started from and went with another local that approached us. We followed him to a shop which seemed a far safer environment for the transaction and exchanged $100 for K122,500.

After we had sorted this out, we begun our walking tour of the city. We were saving the main sight for tomorrow but wandered round some of the places to visit near our hotel. The most spectacular was our first pagoda of our visit, Sule Paya. We wandered round there for 15 minutes or so and then walked round a few of the old colonial buildings in the area. It was all quite interesting and appeared to be a bit of a cross between Bangkok and some of the Indian cities we visited 16 years ago.

We returned to the hotel for showers and went out for an early tea. We went to an Indian restaurant near our hotel and had thali meals for K775 each, less than 30p. Excellent food and the cheapest meal of the trip so far. It had been a long day and we were back in the room by 8 and asleep by 10.

Friday, 5th January 2007 We were up at 6 so we could begin our sightseeing early. We had an excellent breakfast in the hotel and got a taxi out to Shwedagon Paya, the biggest and most famous pagoda in Yangon and the most sacred Buddhist site in Myanmar. We arrived there at 7 and climbed the covered steps to the main part of the pagoda. We stayed at the top for about 1½ hours. It was an incredible site, one of the most spectacular places I have ever seen. It was more impressive than the Grand Palace in Bangkok and a more enjoyable experience as there was a fraction of the tourists that you get at Thailand's major sites. We walked quite quickly round once and then took our time going round again looking at everything in more detail. We got given food at the same time as a monk we were talking to - neither of us really knew what was going on - and managed to find our "planetary posts".

Once we left the pagoda, we got a taxi to where we thought the Yangon Airlines office was so we could re-confirm tomorrows flights to Mandalay. When we got there, we found that it was marked in the wrong place on the Lonely Planet map and we had a 20 minute walk back to the office that was probably only a 10 minute walk from Shwedagon Paya! In the office, on the 5th floor of an otherwise deserted former bank, there was some confusion about what we wanted. We asked to reconfirm our tickets and the woman got the impression that we wanted to change the time of our flight. As we had been booked onto the 6:15am departure for some reason, we were quite keen to change the time and got moved to the 10:30 flight instead.

We got another taxi back to the hotel and spent the remainder of the morning by the pool. We went out for lunch at 1ish, changed another $100 at the same shop that we had used yesterday with no problems and then visited the Bogyoke Aung San Market. This was quite a large market with a lot of different shops but was nothing too spectacular. We returned to the hotel and had another hour by the pool while it was still in sunlight.

We went out to a different restaurant to yesterday but had a similar meal. Once again, it was an early night and we were back in the hotel by 8.

Saturday, 6th January 2007 One of the main reasons we had been keen to move our flights was that it meant that we could have breakfast at the hotel before leaving. Once again it was excellent with a huge range of food including curries and the best bread we have had since leaving home. After eating, we checked out of the hotel and got a taxi to the airport. The airport was a bit on the basic side but check-in was far quicker than the other ones this week, mainly due to the lack of any sort of id check. We were in the departure lounge by 9:30 and started boarding at 10. The plane was in the air by 10:25, five minutes before the scheduled departure time. The flight took 90 minutes including a 10 minute stop at Heho.

At midday we arrived at Mandalay airport which is actually about 40 km from Mandalay. We were not in a very good bargaining position with the taxi drivers but managed to reduce the cost slightly by sharing a taxi with a German couple. An hour later we had checked into The Mandalay City Hotel, not quite the same standard as Traders had been but very nice anyway. After lunch at a Chinese restaurant, we went to find out about hiring bikes for tomorrow. We ended up hiring a couple straight away that we could return tomorrow evening. They were not quite up to the standard of bikes we had been hiring in Germany but at K4500 (less than £2) for the two we could not really complain.

We returned to the hotel for an hour by the pool and then headed off to climb Mandalay Hill. It was fortunate that we had got the bikes today as the base of the hill was further away from our hotel than we had thought. It was almost 4km to the steps leading up the hill.

As we cycled round the moat, we were joined by Tonto (or something like that) who said he wanted to join us walking up the hill to practice his English. We were a bit sceptical at first but at the base of the steps we were joined by other members of his English class who all seemed genuine enough. It was a 30 - 40 minute climb to the top from where the views were very good. The students stayed with us all the way up and back down again and seemed genuinely pleased to be able to talk to us.

We cycled back to the hotel in the dark accompanied by two of our new friends. We had showers and then went out to eat at the chapatti stand just round the corner from our hotel. It was an excellent place to eat with about 8 people involved in making and cooking chapattis which were served with curries and dhal. We sat at plastic tables and chairs on the pavement and had the best meal we have had since getting to Myanmar. It was also our cheapest meal costing K1100 (less than 50p) for two.

We were back in the room to watch the FA Cup 3rd round coverage. We watched Tamworth v Norwich and then Birmingham v Newcastle while listening to World Service. A good night for Swansea winning 3 - 0 at Sheffield United. We even had commentary of Leon Britton scoring the third from the penalty spot!

Sunday, 7th January 2007 Another day of sight-seeing. We were up for breakfast just after 6 and were on our bikes by 6:45. We cycled out to Amarapura, about 11km South of Mandalay. The main sight there is U Bein's Bridge, a 1.2km long teak footbridge across a lake. We found the bridge by about 8 o'clock. The bridge itself was not too spectacular but the surrounding scenery was excellent. We locked up our bikes and walked across the bridge having arranged for a boat to come and collect us from the other side. The walk across was very pleasant with fishing boats all across the lake and pagodas glistening in the sun in every direction.

At the other side, we visited Kyauktawgyi Paya before getting our boat back. The boat journey back was better than we expected. We were taken out to the middle of the lake to get a close-up look at a group of fishermen pulling in their nets and also went under the bridge a couple of times - all for K2000. We cycled back through Amarapura looking at another couple of pagodas on the way. Everywhere we went the people were incredibly friendly, waving to us and giving us directions whenever we stopped to look at the map.

We were back at the hotel by midday and spent an hour or so by the pool before going out to lunch. We stopped at the bike rental place and paid to keep our bikes for tomorrow and then went to a Burmese restaurant for our first attempt at the local food. The meal was OK but nothing to make us keen to repeat the experience!

After eating, we cycled round the palace again to visit the a couple of temples near the base of Mandalay Hill. Before going into the first, we got taken for a cup of tea with a woman who seemed keen on being a guide for our trip round the ancient cities in a couple of days time. We declined her offer and visited Kuthodaw Paya followed by Sandamani Paya. Both were very spectacular again despite the confusion about which one we were in initially! We had a brief visit to Atamashi Kyaung, a monastery, before things started closing for the evening and we returned to the hotel.

We returned to the chapatti stand for tea and were joined by an American who is staying near-by. Were back in the room just after 9 for the Man United v Aston Villa game.
