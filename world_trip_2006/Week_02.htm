World Trip 2006/7 - Week 2

Day 8 - 14th June 2006 After an hour or so lying in the sun, we packed up our tent and drove the short distance to Nurnberg where England play their second game tomorrow. We found the hotel easily enough and were relieved to find that the hotel had a car-park and allowed us to check in early. Mark arrived shortly after us and the three of us set of for a days drinking.

It turned out to be an excellent day. We started off in the Bratwust House near the main square before heading out to the fans cricket event in a near by park. We did not make it quite as far as the cricket match but had a couple in a beer garden where we met another of Mark's Palace mates. At 6 ish we went back to the Bratwurst House where Roy joined us. We went up to the restaurant underneath the castle for another couple and then returned to the Bratwurst House for the third time today. By the time we got there, it was totally packed due to the Germans playing at 9pm. We had seats as we had reserved a table but the service was so poor that Mark and I had still not got our drinks 50 minutes after ordering. We left at half time and headed back to the area around the castle which was not as busy. We found an excellent bar where we stayed for the rest of the night. Neither me, Mark or Roz remember leaving the bar but apparently we woke Steve up at 2:30 when we got back to the hotel.

Day 9 - 15th June 2006 The second England game was due to kick off at 6pm. I met up with Steve, Mark and Roy at the hotel reception at mid-day and we headed into the centre of Nurnberg. It was another very hot day and the whole area was already packed with England fans. We met up with some more of Steve's fiends in a cafe and, after Roz had joined us, headed back to the castle restaurant which was not as busy. We spent an enjoyable couple of hours there before getting a bus to to the ground. No problems getting in to the ground again. We were sat with Steve this time but not with Mark as he got his ticket in the second allocation. Not the best of games but the win meant that we had qualified for the second round - our best start since 1982.

We got the train back into Nurnberg after the game and met up for a few more drinks but had a relatively quiet and early night.

Day 10 - 16th June 2006 Checked out of the hotel at 12 and drove up to Limburg, about 50 miles South of Cologne. We eventually found the camp site and erected our tent in about half of the time that our first attempt took. Steve turned up with Kelly and Kim at 6ish. After they had pitched their tent next to ours, we left them at the camp-site and headed into the old town to meet Regan and Annette who have flown out for the weekend. Limburg was an excellent place for a session and we ended up staying out until the pub we were in closed at 2am. Do not remember a lot about the walk back to the camp-site!

Day 11 - 17th June 2006 With the exception of an hour or so wandering around the sights of Limburg, we spent just about the whole day in beer gardens or bars. Before meeting Regan and Annette, we went round the cathedral and wandered around the old town for an hour or so. We briefly saw Steve, Kim and Kelly again before they headed off to a more child-friendly camp site.

We started drinking in a beer garden near the river where we stayed for a couple of hours. We then wandered back into the old town and went round a few bars before ending up in the same place we had been in last night. No one really remembers the latter stages of the night but somehow we managed to stay in the bar until 2am again. A very good day out!

Day 12 - 18th June 2006 We went into Limburg to say goodbye to Regan  and Annette at about 1pm but did not do a lot else all day due to hangovers. We went back into the old town for a pizza in the evening but were in our sleeping bags in the tent by about 9:30. During the night, there was a heavy thunder storm and it looked as if the tent was not going to be able to keep out the rain. Water had started coming in but after the storm had passed and the rain had eased off a bit, no more came in and we could go back to sleep.

Day 13 - 19th June 2006 After drying out the tent in the sun for an hour, we left the camp site to drive up to Cologne. The drive took about an hour but it took almost as long again to find the hotel where we were staying. The street was not marked on our map so Roz had phoned for directions. The directions were slightly inaccurate which meant we were looking on slightly the wrong place and we were not helped by the one way system and more torrential rain making road signs almost impossible to read. We eventually checked in to a room/apartment and went off to park the car about a mile away - the closest free parking.

After we had eaten lunch, showered, etc, we walked into the centre of the old town. The whole area was already filling up with fans for tomorrows game. We climbed the tower of the very impressive cathedral for the views of the city and then caught a train out to Dussleldorf where we had arranged to meet up with Mark, Steve, etc who had been to a game in Dortmund. Spent most of the night in the Zum Uerige brewery apart from when Roz and I went off to try and tick off another couple of beers from my beer list. Got the train back to Cologne at about 23:45.

Day 14 - 20th June 2006 We went back into town at about 12:30 by which time the place was totally packed. There were reportedly 90,000 English and Swedish fans in town as well as all the Germans who were there to watch the final German group game on the big screens that had been erected in the city centre. We managed to get a table at the Frue Brewery at 1:30 and stayed there for an hour or so but then struggled to find anywhere where we could watch the German game. There were 8 of us wandering around by this stage and everywhere was either totally full or did not have a tele. Eventually we found a small bar that we could just about all get into and see the tele. Spent a couple of hours in there drinking Kolsch and watching the Germans win 3 - 0 to go top of their group. Excellent atmosphere during and after the game!

We tried to find another bar to go to before heading out to the stadium but ended up all going for a pizza - not what we had planned but it worked out quite well. We gat a tram out to the stadium at about 19:30 and eventually got into our seats about 15 minutes before kick-off (21:00). Once again, not a great game but a draw with the Swedes meant we won our group and avoided playing the Germans in the next round. We had played our best football of the tournament so far for 45 minutes but had once again fallen apart in the second half.

After the game, the tram took us back to Neumarkt, which was near our hotel, so we did not bother going into the city centre with everyone else.
