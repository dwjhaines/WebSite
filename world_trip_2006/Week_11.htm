World Trip 2006/7 - Week 11

Monday, 21st August 2006 Did not get up until about mid-day. We had breakfast on the balcony and made it down to the beach by about 2. We met up with everyone else on the beach and were back for a slightly later start to the drinking by 6. A similar evening to last night but in a different restaurant - this one with far quicker service. We returned to the same bar we had been drinking in last night for a couple of hours and were in there until at least 3am. Do not remember coming home.

Tuesday, 22nd August 2006 An almost identical day to yesterday. We had reserved a table so were able to eat on the roof-top bit of the restaurant. Dave, Melissa and Ed left early as they get the ferry to Naxos tomorrow. Did not make it to our usual bar as already pissed enough when we left the restaurant.

Wednesday, 23rd August 2006 Dave, Melissa and Ed all departed for Naxos on the 7am ferry. The rest of us do not leave until tomorrow. Only Hoggy and Sarah made it to the beach. We spent most of the day in our room until 3ish when the six of us still here, set off to visit Hora. It was a spectacular 30 minute bus journey to get to Hora. We were there for about 3 hours, enough time to walk down to a monastery that is built into the side of the cliff, walk back up the hill, get something to eat and get the 6:45 bus back to Aegiali, where we are staying. We ate in the same restaurant as we had done on the previous two nights but without so much wine due to having an early start tomorrow. We were in bed by 10.

Thursday, 24th August 2006 Due to a change in ferry schedules, the only option for us to get to Naxos today was the 6am ferry from Katapola, about 45 minutes by bus from where we were staying. Fortunately, there was a bus departing at 4:30am from Aegiali so we had our earliest start of the trip so far. The bus and ferry journey were uneventful although the sun-rise was spectacular. We arrived in Naxos at 9, about 20 minutes late. We were met by the minibus from Pension Sofi - the same place that Dave, Melissa and Ed were staying at.

We had a bit of a wait until the rooms were ready and spent the time looking around the town (although Regan and Annette inevitably ended up with the first room available again and went to sleep). Our room is a studio apartment again and is probably the nicest we have had in Greece. We had lunch on the balcony and then had a couple of hours sleep to make up for the early start this morning.

We met up with Hoggy, Sarah, Dave & Melissa at 4 and went for a walk around the castle area of Naxos town, returned for showers and then all met up on Ed's balcony/terrace for another session - the last with the Dave, Hoggy and partners. The terrace was far better suited for us to sit around drinking on than the ones in Amorgos had been and we stayed there for almost three hours. A few of us were already quite pissed before we went out to eat, myself included and only vaguely remember the latter stages of the meal, although I do remember that it was excellent food again. After a few litres of wine in the restaurant we headed for another taverna where four of us stayed until 12:30 (The others were either leaving early tomorrow or, in Ed's case, had already drank enough).

Friday, 25th August 2006 By the time we woke up, Dave & Melissa had already departed for Athens and Hoggy and Sarah were on there way to another island. We had breakfast on the balcony again then went off to exchange books and check e-mail.

We were joined by Ed at 1 and we got a bus up to Halki, another picturesque village in the centre of the island. The village was reasonably nice but not as spectacular as our book made out and after a few pictures and a walk around a distillery, we did not have too much to do before the 4:30 bus back to Naxos town. Spent the rest of the time in a cafe with coffees and a diet coke.

We were back in Naxos town just after 5. Ed returned to his room, Roz went to use the internet for an hour and I went for a walk around the Roman Arch near the port. The arch was not too interesting itself but there were good views of Naxos from the hill it stands on. The five of us ate in a different restaurant to last night but again the food was excellent. Not too much alcohol consumed and a relatively early night.

Saturday, 26th August 2006 We left the apartment at 11:20 to get the 12:10 ferry to Santorini. We arrived there at 2ish and got the bus straight from the port to Fira, the main town on the island. We had booked an apartment on the side of the caldera and we knew that we would be getting rooms with good views and we were not disappointed. We are high up on the caldera overlooking the volcano in the middle of it. The room is excellent and the view from our balcony is one of the best we have ever had. The place we are staying also has a pool with just as good views.

After an hour or so by the pool and a trip to the supermarket for supplies, we spent a few hours on the balcony listening to World Service (Orient 0, Swansea 1) and watching the sun set that Santorini is famous for.

We had started on the wine while watching the sun set and continued in a taverna in the centre of Fira. Another good meal and very enjoyable session. Got to bed after 2am.

Sunday, 27th August 2006 Regan, Annette and Ed all went on a boat trip to the volcano in the centre of the caldera and the hot springs there. Roz and I could not be bothered as we are happy spending the time on our balcony or at the pool. We did go for a walk down to the old port during the hottest part of the day, the return trip almost killing Roz! On our return, we had lunch and spent another hour or two at the pool. None of the others were particularly enthusiastic about their trip when they returned so it looked as if we made the right decision.

Had another evening of watching the sun setting from our balcony while consuming wine or retsina. We had booked a table in the taverna that we ate in yesterday and stayed in there from about 9 until gone 1.
