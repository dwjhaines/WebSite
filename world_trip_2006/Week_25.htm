World Trip 2006/7 - Week 25

Monday, 27th November 2006 The start of the second half of our World Trip - the beginning of six months in S. E. Asia.

We were up at 7 and collected by taxi from Cathy's at 8. Our bus left The Quadrant a few minutes late, just after 9 and we had an uneventful journey to Heathrow where we arrived on time at 1:20. We checked in immediately for our flight at 4:20 and killed time in the departure lounge until boarding. The flight was a bit late taking off but we arrived in Amman at about10:50 local time and had an hour wait for the onward flight to Bangkok. We left the gate on time but had an hour delay due to technical difficulties once the engines had started and did not depart until about 1am. We had another meal, a glass of wine and managed to get some sleep.

Tuesday, 28th November 2006 We slept reasonably well for about 3 - 4 hours. Both flights had been less than half full meaning that we had extra seats. Royal Jordanian were certainly not the best airline we have ever flown with but the extra space made the journey one of the most comfortable we have had out to Thailand. We touched down at the new Bangkok Airport just before 3pm. It was not too different an experience to arriving at the old airport - queue for immigration, collect luggage and proceed to meter taxi stand - and we were safely in our room in The Asia Hotel within about 90 minutes of landing.

We went out to stock up on drinks from the 7-11, had much needed showers and started on the alcohol at about 6:30. I started on the beer, Roz on the Sang Som. We went to our usual restaurant in Siam Square for our evening meal and got through a bottle of Mekong. We stopped for another bottle on the way back to the hotel and finished the end of the first one we had bought back in the room. I do not remember much after the second bottle.

Wednesday, 29th November 2006 We had to get up early to try and sort out our visas for Myanmar. We got up at 7:30. I was feeling a bit rough but Roz had one of her traditional Mekong hangovers and was not feeling at all well. We got the sky train to the nearest station to the Myanmar embassy which was then just a 5 minute walk away. We joined the queue at 8:15 with no real idea of how long we were going to have to be there, having read of very inefficient service and long unsuccessful waits. We entered the embassy at 8:45 and they finally started processing customers at about 9:15. We were 6th on the list for individual applicants and handed in our forms by 9:45. We paid Baht 810 each for the visas and an additional 225 each to be able to collect them tomorrow instead of Friday. We got the sky train back to the MBK Centre so that we could go to McDonalds for breakfast. Roz was still not well but managed to eat her burger.

Back at the hotel, Roz went straight back to sleep and after an hour or so I was also asleep. By 2, we woke up feeling a lot better than we had done previously. We went to have a look at another hotel as The Asia Hotel is becoming a bit expensive, then returned to the MBK for lunch and an hour or so looking round the shops.

Back at the hotel, we both went off to use the internet for about an hour each, before we went out for tea. Instead of our usual restaurant, we went to an all you can eat bar-b-q place opposite the Asia Hotel. The food was  pretty good and incredible value for only Baht 89 each.

Thursday, 30th November 2006 Up at about 8 and we both headed straight out to the main train station to book our overnight train tickets to Chiang Mai for 10 days time. We got the tickets without any problems but it was a good thing that we went when we did as all trains a couple of days before were booked and there were only a few berths left on one train for the day we wanted to travel. We had breakfast in the station food centre and returned to the hotel.

At about 10:45 we checked out of the hotel. I then went to collect our passports from the Myanmar Embassy while Roz checked to see if we had manages to book a hotel for tonight. The passport collection went as well as could be hoped for with only a 20 minute queue and I was back at the hotel by midday. Roz was not so lucky with the hotel reservation - the internet company could not sort out the reservation at such short notice - so we decided to start heading for Koh Chang immediately. We got the Sky Train out to the Eastern bus station and bought tickets for the 1:15 bus to Trat. We had lunch while waiting for the bus and departed just about on time. The bus was air-conditioned and reasonably comfortable and we arrived at Trat just before 6:30.

We got dropped outside the centre of Trat due to the opening of a new bus station since our book was written, and had to get a songthaew into town. We got a room in the second hotel we looked at for Baht150, probably the cheapest room we have had so far on this trip. After showering, we went out to the night market to eat - it was good food and again, very cheap.. I got my hair cut on the way back to the hotel and we were back in the room by 9.

Friday, 1st December 2006 We were up at 7:30 and went out to get breakfast before checking out of the hotel. We got another songthaew out to the pier at Laem Ngop about 20 minutes away and caught the 9:00 ferry across to Koh Chang. There were not too many options for transport when we arrived in Koh Chang about 50 minutes later and we eventually had to share a taxi with another couple for Baht100 each to get to White Sand Beach. We were dropped at the Northern end of the beach and started looking for a room for the 8 or 9 nights we are planning to stay. It was too hot for walking around with rucksacks so after finding a room we both liked, Roz spent 30 minutes or so looking around for an alternative while I stayed with the luggage. She did not find anything that she preferred so we checked into the Lagoon Resort. It cost slightly more than we were hoping to pay but was a really nice big room with a good balcony and views of the beach.

We went for a walk around the beach area which all seems quite nice. We had been warned that it was more developed than some beaches on the island but it was nothing like Samui or Phuket and has probably about the right amount of shops and restaurants for us. We ate lunch in one of the beachside restaurants and spent the remainder of the afternoon lying on the beach in front of our hotel.

We went for another walk to the other end of the beach as the sun was starting to set and then I left Roz on the beach and went for a run for the first time in over 6 months. This was the start of my attempt to get fit again and lose some weight. I ran the full length of the beach and back in about 15 minutes and did not feel too bad at the end of it. When we returned to the room, we discovered that Roz had managed to lose the key on the beach. We returned to the beach to look for it but there was not much chance due to the fading light. I got a replacement from reception but it looks as if we have lost our Baht 500 key deposit.

We ate at our hotel restaurant which was right on the beach and served excellent food. After another walk on the beach we returned to the room.

Saturday, 2nd December 2006 Spent most of the day lying on the beach. On our way back from lunch, we booked ourselves onto a days diving trip for tomorrow. We discovered that we can get free wi-fi in the hotel reception (but not in the room) so made use of that to try and sort out accommodation in Chaing Mai and Bangkok (without much success). I ran along the beach for about 26 minutes before giving up. It rained in the evening ruling out dining on the beach so ate while watching Arsenal v Spurs in a slightly less traditionally Thai restaurant.

Sunday, 3rd December 2006 We were picked up from the hotel at 8 by Wing Divers and driven to the dive centre at Bang Bao. We were given our equipment and walked down to the pier to get on the boat. We had chosen Wing Divers as they have a proper dive boat instead of the speed boats that most companies here seem to use. The boat was fairly full but there were only a few fun divers so it was not too crowded. It was only a short journey out to the first dive site and we were in the water by 10:30. We dived on our own with a dive master called Ray,

We had not seen great reports about diving from Koh Chang so did not really know what to expect. The first dive was nothing special, particularly the first half of it although we did get to see a couple of blue spotted stingrays. The second dive was far more spectacular, the highlight being a huge school of juvenile barracuda that seemed to cover half of the dive site. It was probably the biggest single school of fish we have ever seen and we spent ages just watching them all swim around us. We also saw a school of about 20 - 30 squid and the dive site had a couple of narrow swim-throughs. A very enjoyable dive!

We were back at the hotel by about 4:30. We had another unsuccessful attempt to sort out hotels for later in the trip and started drinking just after 6. We had a couple of beers on our balcony and then got through a couple of bottles of Sang Som in a restaurant on the beach. We did not drink that much but neither of us remember too much about getting back to the hotel.
