World Trip 2006/7 - Week 46

Monday, 23rd April 2007 Up at 6:15 for breakfast before walking with our luggage to the Komtar bus station. Our bus was supposed to leave there at 7:30 but it did not leave until 8 meaning that we were 30 minutes late leaving the main Penang bus station at 8:30. The journey was not too bad and we were dropped in the centre of KL at about 1:15. We were about 1km away from the BB Plaza, a shopping centre that we hoped may have somewhere selling camera housings. Roz waited in a restaurant near where the bus station while I walked to have a look.

I visited about 8 - 10 shops. None of them had the exact housing I wanted but I was able to get an Ixus800 and housing for roughly the price we wanted to pay. I had decided that I wanted a camera for the trip to Sipidan so purchased the camera and walked back to where Roz was waiting. We got a taxi out to the Sentral Bus Terminal from where we got the airport bus to the "Low Cost Carrier Terminal" at KLIA. This took just over an hour and at about 4:45 we checked in for the Air Asia flight to Tawau. We had a late lunch at McDonalds and went to wait for the 5:50 flight.

At about the expected departure time they announced that our flight was going to be delayed. It turned out that an incoming flight was unable to land at KL due to the bad weather (it had been raining very heavily) and had been diverted to Penang. We had to wait for this plane to fly back from Penang before we could depart. We eventually took off just after 8:30 - almost three hours late - meaning that we did not land in Tawau until about 11:20. We collected our luggage fairly quickly and were relieved to see that a driver from Borneo Divers was still at the airport to meet us. We were driven to the centre of Tawau where we checked into the hotel that we had booked by phone. Checked in a few minutes before midnight.

Tuesday, 24th April 2007 Checked out of the hotel just before 7:00, almost exactly seven hours after checking in. We were picked up by Borneo Divers again and driven back to the airport where we picked up three Japanese divers, also heading to Mabul. We then had another hours drive to get to Semporna where we were dropped at the Borneo Divers office.

It was a 45 minute boat trip to the resort where we arrived at about 10:30. We were dropped on the beach in front of the resort and were taken up to the main reception area. We filled in all the paperwork and were given a briefing on dive times, meal times, dos and don'ts, etc, etc before being shown to our room. Another very nice place to stay and close enough to the main building to be able to use the free wi-fi!

We had lunch at midday and went for our first dive at 1. We were diving with the three Japanese that we travelled here with and Jojo our DM. The first dive was supposed to be a check dive although not too much actually got checked. It was an easy site, five minutes away from the resort by boat, with good vis and no currents and lots of nudibranchs.

The second dive was at 3:20 and was even closer. The dive site - Paradise II - is the one that we are allowed to go off and dive on our own each evening now that we have been checked! It was an incredible dive. We saw three different types of frogfish including a couple of giant frogfish and once again, loads of nudibranchs. Roz managed to get photos of 12 different varieties today! Also saw the first turtle of our stay here.

Both dives were very enjoyable. I enjoyed the diving at Pulau Weh but it was nice to be able to dive with no current, good vis and not have to worry about guiding, mapping or anything else again. The dives are a bit short here - 45 minutes seems to be normal - but apart from that it looks as if it is going to be a good few days diving.

Wednesday, 25th April 2007 First dive of the day at 8:30 and our group from yesterday was joined  by John and Leslie from Australia. The dive was all a bit messy to start with. John and Lesley had problems with their weights and one of the Japanese broke his mask strap. We were told to go down to 5m and wait but only two of the Japanese ever appeared together with Jojo. After a few minutes one of the Japanese had problems with his BCD and disappeared with Jojo leaving three of us to continue the dive on our own. Another very good dive and it did mean that we could go at our own pace and stop to look at things.

The second dive, our first at Sipidan, was one of the best we have ever done. We had been told that we were not guaranteed to see turtles but saw three as soon as we were in the water. Roz counted 13 or 14 in total as well as 3 or 4 white tip reef sharks. We had the classic shot of a turtle being followed by a bat fish which all made for some excellent photos. Both the sharks and the turtles came very close to us and the vis was the best we have seen here so far.

The third, also at Sipidan was not as good. We still saw turtles and sharks and it was another excellent dive but could not match the previous one. We returned to the resort where we did our fourth dive of the day with an American called Walter. A similar dive to the one here last night with lots of nudibranchs, pipe fish and a sea moth. All in all an excellent days diving!

After our evening meal, I sent an e-mail to my previous manager at Ericsson asking about the possibility of getting my old job back in July. Within an hour or so, had a reasonably encouraging reply that could be very good news.

We sat on our balcony and had a couple of Bacardi's before going to bed.

Thursday, 26th April 2007 Up at 7 to open my birthday presents - new camera and underwater housing as well as a bottle of wine for celebrating tonight. We departed for Sipidan at 9 for another two dives there. Both dives were excellent again. Roz counted 32 turtles on the two dives. We lost count of the number of sharks that we saw and both dives in general were very spectacular. We are already starting to think about Sipidan as a destination for a two week holiday in the near future. It was also very good to be diving with a camera again!

Did two more dives in the afternoon including our daily sunset dive at Paradise II. The diving at Mabul is not as spectacular as at Sipidan but there is still a lot to see and they were once again very good dives.

After the last dive we started celebrating my birthday as well as our 10th wedding anniversary. We finished off our duty free Bacardi before going to the poolside for a bar-b-q meal with "entertainment". We had a few beers with the meal and then returned to the balcony to drink the bottle of wine. We had another beer before all the bar closed and were quite pissed by the time we went to bed. A very turtley terrific birthday!

Friday, 27th April 2007 Another very good days diving despite feeling slightly rough when we woke up. We only did one dive in the morning, a local dive at Mabul. We did two more dives at Sipidan in the afternoon. Both were excellent again although Roz was not happy about only seeing one turtle on the first of the two dives. Twelve on the second dive seemed to make up for this however! We had another good sunset dive at Paradise II to complete the day. Had an early night due to having arranged to go to Sipidan at 5:30 tomorrow morning.

Saturday, 28th April 2007 Up at 5 for a 5:30 departure. We were at Sipidan and in the water by 6. The dive was very nice again. Plenty of sharks and barracuda, five turtles and plenty of other fish. We also saw a couple of bump head parrot fish. They are one of the main reasons for getting here early although they were not too spectacular. We had our one hour surface interval on the island before the second dive. The second dive was as good as any we have done here. Plenty more sharks and Roz counted 22 turtles bringing the total to 100 so far. The end of the dive in the shallow part of the reef was excellent with turtles everywhere.

We had a few hours to kill back on Mabul before the third dive. We have been looking into options for our month in Indonesia. We had made a few enquiries into live-aboards in the Kimodo area but have not been able to get anything booked. We have now knocked this idea on the head and are instead planning on spending 10 days or so at Bunaken off Sulawesi. We sent a few e-mails to a couple of the dive operators there and eventually decided on a package with "Two Fishes". We will spend a couple of weeks in Bali before flying out there for about 10 days from 16th May.

The third dive at 1pm was round the base of oil rig just off the beach from our resort. This, and the sunset dive were both very good again. By the time we did the forth dive we were both starting to feel a bit rough. We both had sore throats and were starting to get colds. The weather here has not been wonderful and it has been quite cold after diving. This and the fact that we have been doing four dives a day seems to have finally taken its toll on our health. I was feeling worse in the evening but we stayed up initially to watch the Everton v Man Utd. game and then to listen to world service (Carlisle 1, Swansea 2).

Sunday, 29th April 2007 Had another relatively early start as we were doing a dive at 7:45 before our departure later this morning. It was another good dive - frog fish, leaf scorpion fish, nudibranchs and a final turtle - although I don't think either of us felt like getting out of bed so early. The diving here really has been better than anything we have experienced before. The Sipidan dives have been unbelievable and the muck diving around Mabul has been far more enjoyable than I would have expected.

After the dive we packed up our stuff and checked out of our room by 10:00. We had an hour or so sitting around waiting for the boat back to Semporna which left at about 11:30. We got the free transfer back to Tawau where we checked into a nice hotel for the night. We had a quick walk around the town to get Roz more medication for her skin infection which seems to have returned and then spent the rest of the afternoon in the room trying to recover from the last few days. I was still suffering from my cold/sore throat and we were both totally worn out from not getting enough sleep the last couple of days.

We went out for Indian food in the evening but were back in the room by 8:30. Spent an hour or so waiting for texts to confirm that Sunderland had been promoted to the Premiership while editing some of the 500 or so photos we had taken between us in Sipidan. When the confirmation came from both Mark and Cathy, there was not too much celebrating. We were probably both asleep within 15 minutes of the final whistle!
