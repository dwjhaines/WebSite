World Trip 2006/7 - Week 3

Day 15 - 21st June 2006 After last nights result, we knew the path that England would have for their remaining games in Germany so we spent some time in an internet cafe while Roz cancelled hotels in the cities we will not be visiting.

We checked out of our hotel and headed South along towards Stuttgart. We travelled down the bank of the Rhine, stopping for a two hour walk up to The Loreley from St Goarshausen, before continuing to Rüdesheim where we planned to camp for the night.

Once at our campsite, we drank the wheat beer that we had bought out to Germany with us and headed into the town to watch Holland v Argentina.

Day 16 - 22nd June 2006 Spent about 5 hours or so walking up to the Niederwald Denkmal, a large monument on top of a hill and then along the top and down into Assmannshausen, the next small town along the Rhine.

Day 17 - 23rd June 2006 We had a two hour drive to Stuttgart where we arrived at around 1pm. Roz had signed up to work for the The Football Supporters Federation (Free Lions) and they had provided us a room in a hotel right next to the stadium. We met up with a couple of people from FSF, picked up our tickets for the Ecuador game, and then went for a look around Stuttgart. Roz met up with more people who she would be working with while I wandered round some of the sights and we met up again in the Paulaner beer hall for a drink before returning to the hotel.

Kevin Miles from the FSF took everyone (about 15 people) out for a meal in the evening. I went along as well and somehow managed to get volunteered into helping out as well for the next few days.

 Day 18 - 24th June 2006 We met up with the rest of the FSF lot at breakfast and were briefed by Kevin about what we would be doing - mainly distributing Free Lions and answering peoples questions. We were all given our "Fans Embassy" t-shirts and everyone headed off into Stuttgart. I had to move the car away from the stadium and find out about a ticket for Mark so followed them in by about 11:30.

I found Roz by the Fans Embassy and we spent the rest of the day wandering around the city handing out Free Lions fanzines. It was all quite enjoyable. We chose to go around the area containing some of the nicer bars of Stuttgart, further away from the main station. Met loads of people and were generally able to answer questions although we never did find out where the Irish bar was! Once again, the city became completely packed by mid afternoon due to the English fans and the amount of Germans heading in to watch their game on the big screen. By 5:30 were worn out and had had enough of the crowds so Roz and I got a train back to Bad Cannstatt the suburb where we were staying. We handed out more fanzines and then stopped for a drink at a bar by the station to watch the end of the German game.

We were eventually joined by the rest of the FSF lot and also by Steve and Kim who had come to collect their tickets. Kim had flown out for the game only to find that she did not have a ticket due to Steve getting confused about what game her voucher was for. She put her (and Mark's) name on a waiting list for returns but at this point was not too optimistic about seeing the game. Steve was not too popular! We had a couple more drinks with them before returning to a beer garden near the hotel to watch the Argentina v Mexico game.

Day 19 - 25th June 2006 Roz had suggested handing out Free Lions at the camp site near the ground. We volunteered to do this and together with Evan, another volunteer, were given 6 boxes (1500 copies) to distribute. We were quite keen to do this as it meant we would not have to go into Stuttgart and could meet up with Mark, Steve, etc in Bad Cannstatt which was walking distance from the ground. We based ourselves by a petrol station and walked around not only the camp site (large car park!) but the area around the ticket collection point. This worked out remarkably well and we got rid of all fanzines within a couple of hours - far easier than yesterday. We got a train into Stuttgart and returned in a taxi to Bad Cannstatt station with another 2000 copies. We handed these out to people at the station as the trains came in from Stuttgart and spent most of  the day directing people to the ticket collection point. During the day, we were joined in Bad Cannstatt by Steve, Kim, Mark and Dave who all now had tickets due to the Mark and Kim getting tickets through the waiting list. We had got rid of all 2000 by 2:30 so were able to join the others in a bar for a few pre-match drinks.

We made our way to the ground at 4ish and were in our seats with about 20 minutes to spare before the 17:00 kick-off. Another poor game and bad performance by England but at the end of it we were in the quarter finals. Hard to get too excited about it as it was such a poor game but at least we are still in the competition and have got to get better soon!

Spent the rest of the evening drinking in the bar back at Bad Cannstatt station where we were joined by Janet, who we had met in Nuremburg and her friend, another Palace fan. Vaguely remember going for a kebab at about 11:30.

Day 20 - 26th June 2006 We were supposed to work until 1pm today but not a lot was happening in Stuttgart so we were allowed to leave at about 11:30. Working for the FSF has been a pretty good experience. Although it has been hard work in the heat, it has been very enjoyable and something that we would definitely consider doing again in the future. It has also been quite good financially as we have had our accommodation paid for three nights, the meal on our first night, and €30 expenses per day each for four days despite only really working for two days.

By the time we were back at the hotel, we were both totally knackered. We had planned to drive up to about halfway to Gelsenkirchen and camp for a couple of nights before driving the rest of the way. However, we both agreed that we did not want to drive very far and that we would also like a hotel room for a couple of nights. We settled on Würzburg and hoped that we would be able to find an Ibis where we could stay cheaply. We looked at a few hotels before finding the Ibis and it was really good to have an air-conditioned room with an en-suite bathroom for the first time in a while. We watched the first of today's games in the hotel before going out and watching the second in a beer garden in Würzburg.

Day 21 - 27th June 2006 We decided that we needed a day of not doing very much. We spent a couple of hours making use of the hotel's free internet access before heading out on a walking tour of the old town. We had planned to walk up to the big castle on the hill overlooking the town but it started raining as we set out so started on a tour of the old town instead. This proved to be quite lucky as it rained quite heavily after we had been out for about 10 minutes. We spent about an hour sheltering and having lunch before the rain cleared and we could continue the tour. The sights were pretty spectacular, particularly The Residence.

We watched the first football match of the day in our room before going out for a couple of beers and a pizza.
