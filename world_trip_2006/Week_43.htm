World Trip 2006/7 - Week 43

Monday, 2nd April 2007 We did our third mapping trip to Batee Tokong in the morning. Poor vis and strong currents made mapping difficult again but I think we now have enough information to complete our map. For the second dive, Roz and I took three customers to Rubiah Sea Garden.

Roz appears to be suffering from a ringworm type skin infection. After consultations with both Mette and Peter she has started taking antibiotics that are leftover from Tsunami Aid!

Tuesday, 3rd April 2007 We managed to get a couple more things ticked off towards our DM course today. I spent an hour or so in the morning being the victim for Peter who was completing his rescue course with Mette. I was recovered from the bottom a few times and then towed to the shore while being resuscitated, etc. Roz had not been feeling well when we got up due to a combination of the infection and the antibiotics but she made it into the water for us to complete the rescue assessment that we needed.

After lunch I was again the victim while Peter looked for a missing, unconscious diver and we then had the enjoyment of our stress test. This involved me and Roz having to swap fins, BCD's and masks underwater while buddy breathing. David "helped" by switching off our air and undoing tank straps. This all went reasonably well and once completed means that we only have a few skills left to demonstrate and our map to complete to successfully finish the course.

After the stress test, we spent a more relaxing 50 minutes diving with David around the beach.

Wednesday, 4th April 2007 My day on shop duty so I stayed on my own while everyone else went off to Arus Paleh. Roz went with David and two customers, Wilco and Alesha, who were all doing their deep check. This involved going down to 45m and ensuring that there were no problems and is always done before a trip to the deep wreck. Wilco and Alesha along with Cedric and Diane are all booked to go to the wreck tomorrow

While everyone was away, I made quite a bit of progress on the map that I am trying to draw with Photoshop. Should have it finished after one more mapping dive.

In the afternoon, we went back into the water with David and completed our skill circuit. Roz had three more skills remaining than me but we both completed everything without too many problems. With these done, our course is almost complete - we just have to finish our mapping and get ourselves certified fit to dive by the local doctor. After our skills, we spent an hour or so diving at the beach with David and also went back out again for a night dive.

Thursday, 5th April 2007 Cedric and Diane had both pulled out of the trip to the deep wreck by departure time meaning that there were now two spaces. Roz was able to go as she had been on the deep check. Because I had not done the deep dive yesterday, I was not able to go. This was annoying as I was keen to go to the wreck and if I had known that Cedric and Diane were not going, I would have made more of an effort to have done the deep check. I spent most of the day in the shop again and started reading a book for the first time since we arrived here.

I did go out for a dive from the beach again in the afternoon with Luca and Flo. This was quite good again as we saw a couple of sea moths and the ornate ghost pipe fish again but it did not sound as good as the deep wreck.

Eleven of us went to eat at Mama Jungle's again in the evening but she doubled the price of the meal for some reason so we will probably not be going back there again!

Friday, 6th April 2007 We both did the two boat dives today. The first was at Batee Tokong where we just about finished our mapping exercise. It was mainly a fun dive with a few rocks marked out on the plateau. I should be able to finish the map with what we have which will complete our course.

The second dive did not go quite as well. Roz led the dive at Arus Paleh with three customers as well as me and Luca. It went OK until we reached the channel. At the channel, the current was as strong as anything we have experienced here. The customers all went too high and started going all over the place. As we tried to catch up with them, I got my SPG caught in some coral and could not get free. The current made it just about impossible to turn round to see what was caught and I remained stuck until one of the customers managed to free me. Roz also had problems with a customer whose SPG used psi and who could not correctly convert into bar. This caused communication problems and confusion between all concerned. However, we all made it safely back to the boat and everyone except Roz seemed to enjoy themselves.

Saturday, 7th April 2007 We could not go on the first boat trip of the day as the boat was full and we had never been to the site before so were unable to guide. Spent most of the morning reading at the dive centre. We went on the second trip - to Batee Tokong again. This went OK. I was leading Cedric and Diane with one other customer (the one who had rescued me yesterday) and Roz. The dive was not great due to poor vis and strong currents. When we returned to the dive centre, we found out that we were not able to go on the third dive to the Sabang Wreck due to David thinking that too many customers there would spoil the dive for the customers. This did not seem to make much sense and Roz in particular was not very happy.

At this point, Roz decided that she just wanted to leave. We had spent all day at the dive centre for the sake of one pretty average dive. We are here for the diving but spending much more time here for days like today is pretty much a waste of the time we have remaining in SE Asia. I agreed that it would be a good idea to leave a week or so earlier than we had planned.

We spent the remainder of the afternoon on our balcony and returned to the dive centre after 6. We got David to sign off our map and told him that we were keen to leave early. We had a bit of an argument about a few things, including the dive to Arus Paleh yesterday, and we were told that we are going to have to have at least one more attempt at supervised guiding before being signed off as DM's. Roz was happy to take a referral but after further conversation with David and Mette we agreed that we would do the supervised guiding in the next couple of days, complete the course and depart on Wednesday.

By the time all this was sorted out, we were ready for our first beers in 5½ weeks. We drank the last four cans at the dive centre and returned to the room to listen to the football on World Service (Bristol City 0 Swansea 0).

Sunday, 8th April 2007 We got to go on all three boat trips today. I did my supervised guiding at the canyon with David plus Mario and Sven. This all went OK but I am still not sure if this means I have finished and can be signed off yet. Roz led the second dive at Rubiah Sea Garden, also with Mario and Sven. This was not supervised but was her first attempt at guiding since Arus Paleh. Although very nervous about guiding again, this also went OK so she should now be OK for supervised guiding tomorrow. The third was basically a fun dive at Batee Tokong which we did in different groups.
