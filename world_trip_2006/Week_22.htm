World Trip 2006/7 - Week 22

Monday, 6th November 2006 Another trip out to Mnemba for a repeat of the dives we did last Tuesday. Dived with 8 people from an overland trip - again with varying levels of experience. We saw turtles on both dives including the biggest we have ever seen on the second. Both dives were shorter than they could of been due to the size of the group and the lack of experience of some of the other divers - a definite negative to diving with Sensation Divers.

Tuesday, 6th November 2006 Our last two dives in Zanzibar. After having a word with Peter, we managed to get into a smaller group (Bret, a DM, us and two others) allowing us to dive for longer. The first dive was nothing special but the second was one of the best we have done here (Shayne's Reef again). We saw an incredible amount of fish and a final turtle.

We were back in time to have lunch on our balcony and then spent an hour or so on the beach.

At 4:45 we went back to Sensation Divers to collect our passports, etc from their safe. This did not turn out to be as easy as expected as no-one knew where the key was or how to get in touch with Peter. We ended up going for a beer in Cholo's Bar while Brett tracked down Peter to get the key. We got another round in when Brett eventually appeared with the money belt and ended up staying until about 8:30. We were joined by Peter who got a couple more rounds in and we were both already quite pissed when we left the bar.

After getting changed, we went back to the same restaurant that we have eaten in for the last eight nights for a final tuna curry. We had a few more drinks there and one more in a bar on the way back. Neither of us remember anything about getting back to the room!

Wednesday, 7th November 2006 We checked out of our hotel at 9:30 with slight hangovers. We got straight into a minibus that took two hours to get us to Stonetown. We had half an hour on the internet, had lunch and then got a taxi out to the airport for the 2:30 flight to Tanga.

The flights were excellent. We were in a 13-seater plane which only had 4 - 5 other passengers for the ZNZ → Pemba leg and just one for the flight from Pemba → Tanga. The views were superb especially for Roz who was sat next to the pilot  for both flights.

We arrived in Tanga at 4ish and, after failing to agree the price of a taxi, got a free lift into town with a Pilipino security worker. We checked into a hotel and went to try and sort out bus tickets for tomorrow. We failed to do this due to there being no ideal buses to Arusha and too much hassle from the touts at the station. We considered the possibility of going back to Nairobi via Mombassa instead (using Scandinavian Express) but eventually decided to try again for Arusha tomorrow (with the option of going to Mombassa at 1:30 if that fails).

Tanga is relatively safe compared to some cities in this part of the world but still was not too pleasant for wandering around after dark. We went out for a meal but were back in the room by 8.

Thursday, 8th November 2006 We were awake early and waited for the rain to stop before checking out of the hotel and walking to the bus station. There was just as much chaos there as yesterday but by 7:45 we were sat on an Arusha bus which departed on time at 8.The bus had 5 seats in each row - something we had hoped to avoid - but was not full until Moshi so we had more than one seat each for most of the way. We arrived in Arusha just after 3. There was more hassle from the touts at the bus station but we eventually managed to lose them and checked into the Flamingo Hotel.

Someone at reception said he could take me to somewhere that repaired cameras. This turned out to be a waste of time - the main purpose of which was to try and sell me a safari. However, we were later directed to a shop that did repairs. It was closing as we got there but we were given the name of somewhere that will be able to look at the camera tomorrow. We decided that we would give this a go and then head on to Namanga on the Kenyan border. Regardless of whether or not it can be fixed, we will not go back to Nairobi but will get picked up by Best Camping at Namanga.

We returned to the hotel for showers and then went out for an early tea - yet another city where being safely back in the room before 8 seemed a good idea. The electricity went off at about 8 so it was a very early night.

Friday, 9th November 2006 After breakfast, we walked to Naaz's Photo Studio in an attempt to get my camera fixed. "Mr Norman" appeared at about 8:30 and we left the camera with him for his technician to look at. We checked out of the hotel at 10 and returned to the shop to get our photos put onto CD and then went and spent an hour or so on the internet. We returned to the shop for 12 and were given the news that the main board of the camera will need replacing and it would have to go back to Canon. We got the camera back (no charge) and returned to the internet cafe to look at possible replacements as repairing it could be too costly and not very convenient. If it has to go back to Canon it is unlikely that we would be able to do this before we go to Thailand. Replacing it did look the best option but this will have to happen in England and certainly not before the safari on Saturday.

We collected our luggage and walked to the bus station where there was the usual scramble of touts trying to overcharge us. We eventually got onto a minibus that charged us the same as the locals - Tshs3500 - to Namanga.

At Namanga, we could not get into the only decent hotel and ended up in a pretty grotty room - the worst of the trip so far. The camera made an incredible return to life before we went out to eat but had died again by the time we got back.

Saturday, 10th November 2006 We had a relatively late breakfast before checking out of the hotel at about 10:30. We walked up to the petrol station where Best Camping were supposed to be picking us up between 12 and 1. There was a reasonably good place to sit and wait without being hassled by touts and salesmen.

No-one had turned up to collect us by 1. We phoned Best Camping at 1:45 to find that the bus had not left Nairobi until 12:00 and was unlikely to reach us before 2:30. It eventually turned up at 3 meaning that we had spent 4 hours at the petrol station and almost 24 hours in Namanga. We were not too pleased with this and after several phone calls, Roz managed to speak to Sheraz's wife who promised us a $25 refund each.

Once this was resolved, we could concentrate on the safari! We had a better minibus than on the fist safari but we are sharing it with a German couple. It took about 90 minutes to reach the entrance to Amboseli N.P. and we began the first game drive as soon as we were through the gate.

There is not the same concentration of animals as in Maasi Mara but this is compensated for by excellent scenery and views of Kilimanjaro which was clearly visible for most of the drive. Still plenty of animals to see including a lion, elephants, zebra. We could not get as close to the animals as in the Maasi Mara due to the rules about staying on the roads being more strictly enforced but it was all very spectacular nonetheless.

We arrived at the campsite at about 6:30. It was more basic than the previous one we had been to but was a much nicer setting, with "Kili" visible behind the tents. We got football results via a combination of World Service and text messages, had fish & chips for tea and headed off to our tent at about 9:30.

Sunday, 11th November 2006 Our first game drive of the day was at 6:30. Once again, Kilimanjaro was clearly visible for the whole drive. Plenty of elephants all over the park in larger herds than we have seen elsewhere. We spent a lot of time watching a lion guarding a buffalo that he and another lion had killed. The lions rest after a kill before eating but one of the lions had to guard the dead buffalo from the packs of hyenas and vultures. The scene was incredible with the dead buffalo, the lion sat next to it, hyena waiting to move in, all with herds of elephants and Kilimanjaro in the background.

On our second drive, after breakfast, we walked up to an observation point for good views of the park and got close up views of three elephants. We returned to the dead buffalo and spent a while watching the lion, still resting by its side. After a while, the lion moved off to move to the near-by swamp only to come rushing back to chase off the hyenas as they started on the buffalo.

The third drive did not start brilliantly and after an hour or so "safari fatigue" seemed to be setting in. No-one could be bothered to go and look at buffalo and there did not seem to be too much else around. Things did pick up however. We got the classic picture of elephants in front of Kilimanjaro and must of seen well over 100 elephants in total. We returned to see the two lions still guarding their buffalo and got great views of the sunset on the way back.
