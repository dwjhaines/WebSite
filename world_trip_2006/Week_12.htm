World Trip 2006/7 - Week 12

Monday, 28th August 2006 No-one really did anything for most of the day except lie by the pool, eat on the balconies and play with the family of cat plus three kittens that spend a lot of time with us.

At 4:30, Roz Ed and I went to Murphy's bar to watch Sunderland get their first points of the season live on Sky. The bar was very expensive compared to where we have been drinking but had a big TV showing the game and was air-conditioned so we could not really complain. We returned to our balcony for the best sunset since we have been here, before heading off to Stani's Taverna for the third night in a row. We got through a few litres of wine but it was a relatively quiet night as we all leave Santorini tomorrow.

Tuesday, 29th August 2006 Another day where we did not really do a lot except sit on the balcony or by the pool. Regan and Annette were the first to leave as they were flying home via Athens (and Munich) at 12:50. Ed, Roz and I all checked out and left our rooms at 12:00. Ed went for a walk, Roz and I spent the afternoon by the pool. We all left Scirocco's just before 4pm and walked to the bus station. We got the 4:30 bus to the new port where we said goodbye to Ed and got on the 5:30 boat back to Paros. We were able to stand outside at the back of the boat for our final spectacular views of Santorini as we left.

We have both really enjoyed Santorini and will probably return. The views from where we have been staying will be hard to beat and have made it well worth paying the €110 a night - more than double what we have been paying everywhere else. We have not done too much while here, but we have not got bored of just sitting on the balcony watching the boats in the day time when there has always been a large cruise liner moored beneath us and plenty of other activity. Sitting watching the sunsets in the evening has been one of the most enjoyable parts of our visit to Greece.

We arrived in Paros just before 8 and were collected by Mark again. We were taken to the same apartments as we stayed in 10 days ago but this time were not in one of the budget rooms. Today is the first day of "mid-season" and for €40 we got a much nicer studio apartment with the biggest balcony I think we have ever had. Not quite the same views as we have had the last few nights but very nice all the same. We had a couple of beers on the balcony and then went out for a pizza. Neither of us felt like carrying on drinking so we were back in the room by 11:30.

Wednesday, 30th August 2006 Spent a couple of hours by the pool before lunch. We walked into Parikia and got takeaway kebabs for lunch. On the way back we found a supermarket selling gift-packs consisting of three bottles of Paulaner wheat beer and a glass for less than €5. This seemed a bargain after a couple of weeks of Greek beer so we bought one to consume on our terrace.

By the time we got back to our room, we had been provided with sun-loungers so we spent the rest of the afternoon lying on the terrace without having to walk all the way down to the pool. We consumed the wheat beer on the terrace before going out to eat. We ate in the place that we had drank the cheap wine in last time we were in Paros and got through another couple of litres with our meal. Quite pissed by the time we got back to our apartment.

Thursday, 31st August 2006 We checked out of the apartment at 11:30 and had a 10 minute walk to the port. At 12:15, we boarded "High Speed 2" for the three hour trip back to Piraeus, the end of our Greek island hopping. We had a free business upgrade on this journey but it was not the most enjoyable journey we have had. The sea was rough meaning that we were an hour late arriving, the roof leaked and my seat got soaked and the cabin seemed to contain loads of screaming children. However, by 4:30, we were back in Piraeus and we got the metro into the centre of Athens. It was a 10 minute walk to the Novotel that we had reserved for two nights.

After checking in, we went out to the train station to find out about our departure from Athens in two days time. After queuing for about 30 minutes, we asked about couchettes for the 22:55 train to Thessalonica on Saturday night, wanting to find out if any were available and what other options we had. We were unsure about exactly what we wanted to do on Saturday but the ticket seller just about made up our minds for us by issuing tickets for two couchettes!

We returned to the hotel for showers and then walked back to the Monastiraki area where we stayed two weeks ago. We returned to the restaurant where we got our takeaways from last time, this time sitting down to eat. Excellent kebabs again!

Friday, 1st September 2006 We were up reasonably early to begin our sight-seeing. We went first to the Parliament building to watch the changing of the guard. This proved to be highly amusing and very much like something out of Monty Python. We spent most of the next couple of hours following the Lonely Planet walking tour. We walked through the National Garden to the Temple of Olympian Zeus. We bought a €2 ticket to get in here, meaning that we were now committed to not paying to get into the acropolis as an all-in-one ticket would have worked out cheaper. It was reasonably interesting to wander around before we continued on to Hadrian's Arch. From here, we entered the area immediately below the acropolis and walked around the Northern edge of it. This was all quite pleasant and gave some good views of Athens. After a bit of debate, we left the walking tour and walked up Filopappos Hill, just to the SW of the acropolis. This proved to be a good move as the hill gave us excellent views of the Parthenon as well as the rest of Athens and Piraeus.

After taking many photos, we walked down again, continued the walking tour as far as Monastiraki (more kebabs) and returned to the hotel by 2. We spent a couple of hours by the pool and headed out for tea at 8ish.

We ate in the same kebab place again (after walking through one of Athens not so pleasant  areas). The food really is excellent there - four souvlaki, grilled tomato and onion, all served on pita bread for €7 - enough for both of us to be totally full. After eating we walked around the Plaka area beneath the acropolis. This had changed quite a lot since I was there 20 years ago and was surprisingly enjoyable. We had a drink in one really nice bar and then started walking back to the hotel. Up to this point it had been a really good evening. However, on the way back, Roz stopped to phone her mum. For the second time in three weeks we received bad news that was to cause us to change our plans. Aunty Marjorie had died yesterday morning and the funeral is to be next Thursday. Roz would need to go home earlier than planned to drive her mum up to Newcastle. This also meant that she would miss England's game in Macedonia on Wednesday.

Saturday, 2nd September 2006 After last night's news, we were both awake early and started to look for options for Roz to fly home for the funeral. We had internet access in the room so could look at flights assuming that Roz would continue to Skopje as planned and try and fly home on Wednesday morning. At 9 we went out and visited a couple of travel agents and also the Olympic Airlines office as we have flights booked back to London from Thessaloniki on Friday morning. When we returned to the hotel, we checked the englandfans web site and found that Roz would not be able to register with them to prove she had been to the game until Wednesday, making it fairly pointless for her to go to Skopje at all.

Olympic were fairly generous with allowing her to change her existing ticket which gave Roz the option of getting a flight from Athens back to LHR on Wednesday morning for about €35. This was by far the cheapest option and the one we eventually chose. This meant that we would cancel our planned trip to Lake Ohrid and head out to another island for three days instead. We phoned the hotel in Andros that both Hoggy and Dave had stayed in during the last couple of weeks and managed to get a room for three nights. Once we found that the room was available, our minds were made up and the decision confirmed.

By the time we had sorted out what we were going to do, it was time to check-out of our room. We left our bags at the hotel and went to sort out our respective tickets. Roz went to Olympic again and changed her ticket so she could fly back to London from Athens on Wednesday. By the time she got there the price had gone up to €75 but it was still the cheapest option. I went to the railway station and cancelled Roz's ticket to Thessaloniki (full refund except €1) and changed my couchette reservation to Tuesday night (no charge) meaning that I would arrive in Skopje at about 11am on the day of the England game. We met back in the hotel and booked Roz's hire car using the internet access we still had.

Once everything was sorted out, we collected our bags, walked to the bus station and got a bus out to Rafina, from where the ferries to Andros depart. We had a two hour wait for the 5:30 ferry and after making a couple of phone calls, sat in a taverna by the port for a couple of much needed beers. Towards the end of the two hour ferry journey, we sat on the deck listening to first half commentary of England's game against Andorra. After we got off the ferry, we got straight onto the bus for Batsi, where we are staying and we were at our hotel just after 8.

We had another room with a balcony with an excellent view, overlooking the beach which is just across the road from the hotel. We had a couple of bottles of retsina on the balcony before going out and then got drunk on white wine in the restaurant where we ate. We had another glass on the balcony when we got back and got to bed at about 2. An enjoyable end to the day!

Sunday, 3rd September 2006 A more relaxing day than yesterday! We were quite late waking up and both had slight hangovers. We had cheese pies on the balcony for breakfast and spent an hour or two by the pool in before going for a walk around Batsi. This is probably the nicest place we have stayed in Greece with the exception of Santorini. As well as being a nice village with an excellent beach, the temperature is a lot lower than it has been (long trousers required in the evening for the first time in ages) and there are fewer people around - the high season is definitely over.

We had gyros for lunch and spent the remainder of the afternoon on the beach opposite our hotel. We finished off the litre and a half bottle of wine that we bought last night before going out to eat and managed to get through another two and a half litres in the restaurant. Don't remember too much about getting back to the room!
