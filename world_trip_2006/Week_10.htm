World Trip 2006/7 - Week 10

Monday, 14th August 2006 We got the 10:30 bus into Corfu Town and then walked the 1.5km to the airport where we arrived at about 11:30. We were not expecting too much from Corfu Airport but were surprised at what appeared to be total chaos when we arrived. The airport is not big enough to cope with the amount of people using it so there were long queues of people standing outside in the sun. Roz and I joined our respective queues and waited to check in. It turned out to be reasonably well organised and I was checked in for my flight by  12:15 with 45 minutes until departure. I left Roz in her queue and headed off to the departure gates.

My flight left on time and I was at Birmingham by 14:30. I sorted out the hire car details and waited in the car-park until Roz  arrived about two hours later. It had all worked out reasonably successfully and we were at the hospital for visiting before 6pm.

Tuesday, 15th August 2006 Spent the morning at Roz's mum's house sorting out washing and using the internet to cancel, change or make new hotel bookings for the next few weeks. Most of the rest of the day was taken up with visiting the hospital.

Wednesday, 16th August 2006 We went to a pub with John for lunch and then had our final visit to the hospital. We said our goodbyes and drove directly to Manchester for the Greece friendly. We found an ideal parking space close to the ground, picked up our tickets and just had time to meet Steve, Kim and Kellie for a pint before kick-off. We got totally soaked walking to the ground due to heavy shower and no wet weather gear but made it into our seats in time for the national anthems.

The game was very enjoyable and certainly more entertaining than some of the games in Germany had been. We were sat in the ideal place to get back to the our parking place and were in the car within 5 minutes of final whistle. We then had to drive down to Wheatley where we had arranged to stay with Regan. We arrived there at about 12:45, totally knackered. Regan had left the door open for us and gone to bed so we went straight in and joined Harvey in the spare room.

Thursday, 17th August 2006 We left Wheatley at 7:20 and drove to Heathrow in just over an hour. We returned he car to Hertz hoping they would not notice the new scratch on the side of the car from the hospital car-park although I will be surprised if they do not charge me for it. We were early arriving at terminal 2 and were first in the Olympic check-in queue. We checked in at 9:30 and had no problems at all with the increased security checks and hand luggage restrictions. In fact we seemed to get through quicker than usual. We had a couple of pints in the Wetherspoons before departure and made our way to the gate at midday. The flight was due to leave at 12:30 and pulled out not long after. However, we then joined the longest queue of planes we have ever experienced at Heathrow and were running an hour late by the time we took off.

The flight was pretty good and gave us excellent views of both Venice and Corfu. While flying past Venice, it was possible to clearly see both the Grand Canal and St Mark's square. All of Corfu was visible and we could make out Corfu Town and the airport we left from a few days ago. We had a few glasses of wine during the flight and by the time we landed in Athens, we were, once again, both totally knackered due to a combination of alcohol and a lack of sleep over the last few days. We booked our ferry tickets for tomorrow at the airport and got the metro into the centre of Athens. We checked into our hotel in the Monastiraki area which was handy for both the airport metro and trains to Piraeus. After showers we got takeaway souvlaki, a speciality of this part of Athens, and had an early night.

Friday, 18th August 2006 Another early start as we had to get the 8:10 ferry from Piraeus to Paros. We got the metro down to the port and boarded the ferry which left on time and arrived in Paros just before 1:30. We were met at the port in Paros by someone from the apartments where we were staying and by 2:30 were lying by the pool. We are staying in one of the "budget" rooms which means it is below ground level and only has one small window close to the ceiling. However, it has a fridge, is reasonably cool, it's close to the centre of Parikia and is excellent value at €31 a night. We had a couple of hours in the sun and then went for a walk around Parikia, the main town on Paros.

We had been unsure of what to do between leaving everyone in Santorini in 11 days time and getting into Macedonia for the England game eight days later. We were quite keen on visiting the monasteries in Northern Greece but due to our trip back to England, we missed out on a couple of days in Athens and still want to see the sights there. Getting to the monasteries seems easy enough but getting from there to where we want to go in Macedonia looks like hard work and may not be possible in a day. Given that we both liked our accommodation and liked Parikia, we decided that we will probably return to Paros on our way back to Athens and give the monasteries a miss.

On our walk around Parikia we bought a few bottles of beer which we drank by the pool before going out for our evening meal. Roz had found out that her mum was out of hospital and we both felt like having a few drinks. We ate in a pizza place overlooking the sea and then found somewhere doing house white wine for €5.50 a litre. By the time we left there, we had drank 3.5 litres of wine and neither of us remember walking home.

Saturday, 19th August 2006 Woke up quite late and had breakfast in the restaurant where we are staying. After a couple of hours by the pool, we walked into Parikia for lunch. We booked our ferry tickets for when we leave Paros for a second time in 10 days (we had previously booked an apartment for two nights in the place we are staying at the moment) and then wandered round the Church of 100 doors, the main sight here. Had another couple of hours by the pool and then listened to the football on World Service  (Swansea 2 Doncaster 0 - Sunderland lost again!)

We got bored waiting to be served when we went out for tea so ended up getting our second take-away kebab of the day for our evening meal. We were back in our room by 9:30 and had an early night due to having to get up at 5:20 tomorrow.

Sunday, 20th August 2006 Woke up just before the alarm and walked down to the port. We had kebabs for breakfast, making it three consecutive meals. The boat was due at 6:20 and was only about 10 minutes late. Once aboard we found, Regan (complete with shaved head), Annette, Hoggy, Sarah and Ed and we were joined an hour or so later by Dave and Melissa who got on at Naxos. We arrived on Amorgos just before 11 and six of us were picked up by the Poseidon mini-bus. Dave, Melissa and Ed were booked into a different hotel (although their booking failed and they ended up all being in the same room!)

Once our room was sorted out, we had lunch on the balcony and then spent a couple of hours on the beach which is only a 10 minute walk from where we are staying. We started drinking at 5. Although our balconies were not too big, they were all in a line which meant we could have one guest each and the nine of us could all sit together. We stayed on the balconies until 8ish and then went out to eat. We got fed up of not being served in he first place we tried but finally got our drinks in the next restaurant just after 9. Despite the slow service, we managed to get through quite a lot of wine and retsina before moving on to another taverna at about 1am. There were just six of us left by then but we managed to get through another couple of litres before staggering home at about 2.
