World Trip 2006/7 - Week 55

Monday, 25th June 2007 An excellent days diving at Big Brother Island, with slightly less emphasis on wrecks! The first two dives of the day were supposed to be wreck dives at The Namidia and Aida wrecks. At The Namidia, it was just about impossible to stay on the wreck after being dropped near by due to strong currents. We had a quick look at it but left almost immediately and did the rest of the dive along a wall running the length of the island. The second wreck started at about 30m so again, we went down for a look but then did a drift dive along the wall. The third dive of the day was a drift dive along the other side of the island. All three dives were very enjoyable.

We started all three of the dives today with a guide but went our own way fairly soon after the start. We are by no means the most experienced divers on the boat but seem to be the only ones interested in doing hour long dives. Most people seem to be spending about half as much time as us in the water for some reason. So far the live-aboard has been pretty good. We do not seem to have as much in common with the other divers as on some trips but it is still quite an entertaining crowd. The diving has been very good so far and the food has been excellent.

After eating, spent an hour or so putting photos through Photoshop.

Tuesday, 26th June 2007 Woken at 5:30 for the first dive of the day. The plan was for two relatively early dives at Little Brother island before heading further North again. The two dives were very good again. The first was a drift dive around one side of the island. The second was supposed to be a drift round the other side but we spent most of the dive swimming into a slight current. Another reason for the early start was to increase the chances of seeing sharks and we did see a couple including quite a large oceanic white tip. The highlight of the second dive was a Napoleon wrasse that came right up to us and swam along with us for a minute or two.

We left Brothers at midday and spent the first couple of hours watching Finding Nemo in the lounge before catching up on some sleep. The journey took just under five hours and we were back in the water by 5:15. For this dive we had a choice of two small wrecks to visit. We chose the one to the right of the boat while everyone else went the other way. Not the most spectacular of wrecks but we were accompanied by three bat fish wile we swam around it. We also did a night dive in the same spot but stayed closer to the boat and did not reach either of the wrecks. Due to the long days at the moment, did not get back from the night dive until 9:15. Just enough time for snacks before bed.

Wednesday, 27th June 2007 A bit of a lie in today - the first dive was just before 7. The first dive was one of the best so far, the wreck of the Salem Express. This was a passenger ship that sunk killing at least 500 people. It was still in good condition, was at an ideal depth and we were the first divers there again. It was quite an eerie experience swimming around the wreck, looking through portholes into the cabins and entering the restaurant area still with tables and chairs. There are still items of luggage lying around on the sea bed near the wreck.

The second wreck was not so spectacular but a reasonable enough dive. On the third, we only stayed on the wreck for 15 minutes before swimming back towards the boat along the reef. Finally we had a night dive which was better than average although I was not feeling 100% when we went in the water. I had not been able to warm up all day and seemed to be constantly cold whether in or out of the water. Got back from the night dive, had plenty of hot drinks and bed by 11.

Thursday, 28th June 2007 Not a good start to the day. We were diving the Rosalie Muller Wreck. Once again Roz and I were the first ones on the wreck and it could have been a very good dive. However, the top of the wreck is at about 30m so even with Nitrox, we only had about 18 minutes bottom time. Due to poor visibility, you could not see anything from higher up so we were back on the boat after 28 minutes. As well as the fact that the dive was not wonderful, my camera flooded on this dive. I tried taking a picture when we were on the wreck and found that the housing was full of sea water. I rinsed it and dried it out when we were back on the boat but the battery is totally ruined and I have no idea whether or not it will ever work again.

After the disappointment of the first dive, the second was a lot better. The wreck was a lot shallower and had a lot more fish on it. The wreck itself was not that spectacular but the coral and fish made it all very good to look at. Again, we were not on the wreck long before drifting along the reef back towards the boat.. This was all very enjoyable and made even better by the turtle that joined us for a 10 minute safety stop.

The third and fourth dives were both on the Thistlegorm. Both were excellent again. The first time we came to the Thistlegorm, there were 15 - 20 other dive boats on the site and the wreck was crowded with divers. This time there was just one other boat and we went in as they were coming out meaning we had the wreck to ourselves. In the afternoon we went inside the wreck, past all the trucks and motor-bikes, etc. We went back down to the wreck for our night dive and this was one of the best night dives we have ever done. It was quite light due to the lights from the two boats moored above the wreck and the full moon. There was loads to see but the highlight was an encounter with our second turtle of the day. It swan from behind us, hitting Roz on the shoulder and almost bumping into me as it went past before swimming down to the lower decks. Excellent end to a very good days diving.

Friday, 29th June 2007 The last day of our live-aboard and two more excellent dives to end the trip. The first dive of the day was at 6:45 on the Thistlegorm again. Roz and I were the first in the water as usual and did not see another diver for the first 10 minutes of our dive. We did the deeper stern end of the wreck using Nitrox but still managed to get a one hour dive in. The second dive was at Shark and Yolanda Reefs. This was a superb dive with an excellent reef, another very close encounter with a Napoleon wrasse and a few minutes with another turtle at the end of the dive. A perfect way to finish what has been a very good trip.

From the last dive site, it was only about 45 minutes back to Sharm and we were in the port just after midday. We had three hours on the boat before being picked up so we could dry all our gear, say goodbye to the crew, etc. The bus picked us up just after 3 and we then spent about an hour going through security to get out of the port. The live-aboard price included one night in a hotel in Sharm and we arrived at the hotel at about 4:30.

We spent an hour or so at the pool with most of the other divers from Typhoon before going down for a walk around the shops. We met up with everyone again in the evening and had a really good night out. We ate in the hotel as the meal was included and then headed down to the Camel Bar for a couple of hours. We all moved on to The Tavern at about 10 and ended up staying there for about 5 hours getting totally wrecked. We were drinking with the group from Cheltenham plus a few others and were still in the pub when it closed at about 3, although this is a bit of a vague memory.

Saturday, 30th June 2007 Quite badly hung-over and knackered all day. We were picked up from Sharm at midday after we had said our goodbyes to everyone. We were back at the Bedouin Moon by 1:15 where we got a room at the front again. The bad news was that the air-con was not working. When we arrived, we thought it was just a bit weak but after it had been on a while, we realised it was not working at all. We spent an hour or so by the pool as the room was too hot to sleep in but were assured by the receptionist (and the owner) that an engineer was on his way to resolve the problem. When we returned from tea we were told that the engineer had not been yet so had a fairly sleepless night in a very hot room. We later discovered that the engineer had been and had not had the part required to fix it!

Sunday, 1st July 2007 We were both awake at about 5 as we had the door and curtains open and the sun was rising and already getting hot. Roz initially decided that she was too tired to dive but changed her mind and did come along as well in the end. At the dive centre spoke to Flo who assured us that we would be able to move rooms when we returned from diving. This would mean losing our sea view but would mean we had air-con again!

We did two dives at the Canyon with Mabruk again, together with Rebecca and Jane. Jane was doing a check dive on the first dive which was not ideal but in the end it did not have too much effect on our dive. The first dive was to the right of the Canyon along a wall we had not dived before. The second was at the Canyon itself. On both dives, we were left on our own after about 30 minutes as the others went back in due to being low on air.

It was a relatively early finish to the days diving for Mabruk and we were back at the hotel by 3. We then had another argument with the hotel staff because our room was not ready. We had to speak to Flo again and he resolved things fairly quickly. We ended up in another sea view room with assurances that we would not have to move again. Roz had a couple of hours sleep while I walked into Dahab and got my hair cut.

We had a few beers in the evening - more than we intended to. We had a couple on the balcony and then walked to Adam's Bar for a few more. Quite pissed by the time we left there so got takeaways and a taxi back to the hotel.
