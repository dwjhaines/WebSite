World Trip 2006/7 - Week 29

Christmas Day 2006 We were up by 7 and opened a couple of presents each (new camera!) before heading off for another days diving. We were picked up late, probably due to Lanta Diver's Christmas party last night, but were still on the boat and on our way by 8:30. We had chosen to dive with Regan and Annette and after a few problems getting down initially had two dives of around 36 minutes each. The dives - both at Koh Ha - were not wonderful as the visibility was far worse than we had seen on previous days but it worked out reasonably well that they got sorted out before starting their advanced course on Wednesday.

We were back at Kaw Kwang by 4:30 and after showers and a couple of phone calls home, opened the rest of our presents. We also started drinking and were joined on our balcony by Regan and Annette just after 6. We had a couple of plates of spring rolls and fish cakes while having a few more beers and headed off to our usual restaurant on the beach sometime after 8. We had decided on Thai food instead of a more traditional meal and had another very good meal. We stayed in the restaurant until it closed and moved to the bar next door. By this time we were already pretty pissed due to the amount of Sang Som that we had consumed. We stayed there until about 3ish before staggering home. Don't really remember too much about the latter stages of the evening but it had been a very good way of spending Christmas!

Boxing Day 2006 Had the worst hangover since getting to Thailand. We overslept and missed breakfast, which probably did not help, and did not get up until lunchtime. I had also managed to inflict more damage on the foot that I had injured last week. The original injury has not healed and staggering around with no shoes on seems to have made it quite a bit worse. I finally made it to the pool just after 3 but did not really recover until after my shower at 5ish. We were joined by Regan and Annette for tea at Kaw Kwang and were back in our room by 8:30. We watched Chelsea v Reading on our tele and then slept while receiving text messages from Steve and Cathy about Sunderland and Swansea (who both won!). Finally got to sleep properly at midnight.

Wednesday, 27th December 2006 Two dives at Koh Bida. We were not on the same boat as Regan and Annette as they were doing the first day of their Advanced Diving course. The dive sites were pretty spectacular again but the visibility was still not good. After the second dive, Anjali, who is a physiotherapist, decided that my toe might be dislocated and she tried to pull it back into place. This was quite painful and did not achieve a lot. An Indian/Malaysian doctor who happened to be on the boat was happy to have a look at it and confirmed that the toe was broken. He advised going to a clinic of some sort to get it properly taped up. We got back to Saladan, bought a roll of surgical tape for 40 baht and did it ourselves. How effective this will be, I do not know but it should be OK to keep diving for a few more days. If it is still bad when we leave Lanta I can sort it out in Phuket.

We walked to the German restaurant on the beach for our evening meal and were back in our room not long after 9.

Thursday, 28th December 2006 We finally made it to Hin Muang and Hin Daeng today. It was about a 3½ hour journey to the sites and we were on the same boat as Regan and Annette as they continued their course. The dives were both excellent and it was worth the wait. There was still poor visibility in places and strong currents caused by thermo-clines but these were by far the best dives we have done since getting to Thailand. Highlights included a large school of batfish and a school of some of the biggest barracuda we have ever seen.

Ate at Kaw Kwang. Foot slightly better!

Friday, 29th December 2006 We returned to Koh Bida for another two dives. These were better dives than two days ago despite the visibility not being much better. We dived from Lanta Diver's smaller boat, Macha Nou. We were on our own with Anjali again after her day off yesterday and were on the same boat as Regan and Annette who were completing their Advanced course. There was plenty to see on both dives including an octopus, our first leopard shark of the trip and another turtle. The others had one or two problems with their dives but by the end of the day had completed their course. It was a relatively early day compared to yesterday and we were back at Kaw Kwang by about 4:30.

Another day off diving tomorrow meaning that we could all get drunk again tonight. We left Kaw Kwang just before 6 and stopped for a beer with the Belgium couple we had met a couple of days ago. We left their resort about an hour later and walked down to Summer House. We had a couple more beers on Regan's balcony and then returned to Sunset restaurant for the usual selection of curries and Sang Som. We moved to the bar when the restaurant closed and walked back from there sometime around 1am. I tried not to drink as much as normal to avoid doing more damage to my foot and a repeat of the hangover of Boxing Day. Drinking at the same speed as Regan seemed to achieve this although a few sub-rounds were required to avoid complete soberness! I just about remember walking back although Roz remembers nothing from when we moved to the bar.

Saturday, 30th December 2006 A bit less Sang Som last night seemed to make a big difference to the hangover. We made it to breakfast and I was able to make it to the pool by about 11. We walked into Saladan for lunch at Lanta Seafood and called into Lanta Diver mainly to find out about their schedule for the end of January. I got my haircut while Roz looked at the internet and we walked back to Kaw Kwang for another hour by the pool.

We were joined at Kaw Kwang by Regan and Annette at about 6:30. We ate in the restaurant and then spent an hour or so playing Perudo on our balcony. After they had left, we watched Spurs v Liverpool in our room while listening to World Service. (Huddersfield 3, Swansea 2)

Sunday, 31st December 2006 Our last days diving for this visit to Lanta. We dived Koh Bida Nok and Hin Bida from Macha Nou again but this time had Regan and Annette in our group. Apart from a few problems with Regan's ear, the dives were without any major incidents. The vis was still poor but they were still both enjoyable dives with the leopard sharks on the second being the highlight. We only had a short surface interval to ensure that we could get back to Saladan early and we were back at the dive centre by 3. We bought Anjali a beer and had our first of the day before getting our lift back to Kaw Kwang.

We had another beer on our balcony before walking down to meet the others at Summer House for 6:30. We had a few more beers on their balcony before going out to eat at Sunset Restaurant. The restaurant was packed when we arrived and we only just got a table on the beach. This meant that we did not end up eating until fairly late and were already quite pissed by the time the food came. It was a pretty good atmosphere on the beach with plenty of fireworks and big balloons being released. We were still in the restaurant at midnight which I just about remember but I can not recall much after we moved to the bar next door. Apparently, me and Roz left sometime after 2, leaving Annette to try and wake up Regan.
