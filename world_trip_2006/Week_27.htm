World Trip 2006/7 - Week 27

Monday, 11th December 2006 I was awake at about 6:30 due to the amount of noise caused by the train being shunted around a station. Once awake, I got up as I could not see anything from the top bunk. Roz woke a couple of hours later and we had our beds converted back to seats so we could sit down for the remainder of the journey. We got into Chiang Mai slightly late at about 1:15, earlier than normal according to the French family that we had been talking to on the train.

The hotel we had booked in Chiang Mai was just across the road from the station. Our room was not ready when we arrived so we made use of the free internet access until it was sorted out. After showers we used the internet again as we had tried to book a room at the same hotel for next Saturday. This booking proved unsuccessful so we booked a different hotel. We had expected to go into Chiang Mai by lunchtime but it was already past that time so we decided that we should stay a second night here instead of leaving tomorrow. To get the hotel we are already in at the cheaper rate, we had to contact the booking agency via an internet chat room while they were in contact with the hotel by phone and the hotel staff were coming over to discuss the booking with us. It was all far more complicated than it needed to be but it meant we saved about 40% on the room rate. We also have to move to an executive room tomorrow.

It was getting on for 3:30 by this time. We walked into the centre of Chiang Mai where we finally had lunch. We had a short walk around stopping at a couple of wats (which were closing by this time). We also found a very good second hand book shop where I got rid of the books I had been carrying in exchange for two that I had been looking for. We had a quick look at the night market before heading back to the hotel.

We were both pretty knackered by this time and did not feel like walking back into town to go to the Riverside Restaurant as planned. We ate in the hotel restaurant instead and were in bed by 10.

Tuesday, 12th December 2006 We spent a couple of hours at the pool in the morning and eventually moved to our executive room just after 1pm. After sorting out the room, we walked back into Chiang Mai and headed to McDonalds for lunch. On the way we managed to buy a couple of pairs of the shorts that we have both been wearing for years. My last pair had just about become un-wearable and we had not seen them on sale for a while so this was quite a find. After eating, we spent a few hours wandering around more wats. A few of them we had been to already when we were here six years ago but they were still spectacular to look around.

We made it out to The Riverside Restaurant in the evening which was excellent again. After we had eaten, we returned to the night market and got two more pairs of shorts as well as a bag to leave some of our belongings in when we leave Chiang Mai for a few days tomorrow. Got a tuk-tuk back to the hotel by about 10.

Wednesday, 13th December 2006 We left the hotel at about 9:45 having left our diving gear and one other bag in the hotel's luggage room. We got a tuk-tuk to the bus station and bought tickets for the 10:30 bus to Pai. The bus was fairly old, non air-conditioned, totally full and not overly comfortable, but despite all this, the four hour journey was not too bad and quite scenic. We did not leave Chiang Mai until about 10:50 and we stopped for lunch en-route so eventually got to Pai just after 3.

We had phoned ahead and reserved a room in a place just outside the centre of Pai. It was about a 1km walk from the bus station and we checked in to another good room for Baht700 a night. We had an hour or so in the sun before it set and then showered and walked back into the centre. It seemed quite a nice place and could definitely be described as a "backpacker centre". We ate at the restaurant that Lonely Planet reckons served the best pizzas in Thailand. I am not sure how accurate that description is but the pizzas were excellent.

Thursday, 14th December 2006 We were quite late getting up and ate breakfast at the hotel. After breakfast, we walked up to a wat on a hill in a village just outside Pai. The walk only took about 20 minutes each way so was not too tiring, but the views from the wat were pretty good. We walked back into Pai as we had decided that we would go for a massage. The place recommended in the book was busy but we made appointments for 1pm which gave us about an hour to kill back at the room.

The massage lasted for an hour and cost us B150 each. It was all very relaxing and we both really enjoyed it, probably more than we had expected to. The hour went very quickly and we could easily of gone for the two hour option without getting bored. We had lunch in Pai and headed back to the hotel for another couple of hours in the sun.

We returned to the same pizza restaurant as yesterday for tea, had a few bottles of Leo with our meal (the same two pizzas as yesterday) and were back in the room by 9.

Friday, 15th December 2006 Checked out of our room just after 7:30 and walked down to the bus station for the 8:30 bus to Mae Hong Son. We were at the bus station before 8 but were already too late to get a seat as they had all been taken, mainly by locals who obviously knew what they were doing. We ended up sat at the front of the bus on the engine compartment. This turned out to be more comfortable than the normal seats, particularly after the breakfast stop at Soppong, where a lot of people got off the bus. The journey took just over 3½ hours and was once again, very scenic. Sitting at the front gave us excellent views and it was quite an enjoyable journey.

At Mae Hong Son we walked to a hotel near the lake in the centre of town and checked into a room for B600. We wandered around the lake which was pleasant enough and checked e-mail before having lunch. By the time we had eaten, it was gone 3 and we walked up the hill to the main sight in Mae Hong Son, "Wat Phra That Doi Kong Mu". It took about 30 - 40 minutes to walk up the hill via the road. At the top, the wat was pretty good and the views of the town itself and the surrounding countryside were excellent. We walked back down into town via a set of steps, definitely the shorter route!

We had not been sure where we would eat tonight as there was nowhere that got a particularly good write-up in Lonely Planet. However, when we came out of the hotel, a night market had been set up along the edge of the lake. We chose our meals from different stalls and ate sitting at the lakeside. It was excellent food in a really nice setting. We wandered round the other stalls before taking banana pancakes back to eat on our balcony.

Saturday, 16th December 2006 We got up early so that we could climb up to Wat Phra That Doi Kong Mu again to get the views of the mist over the town. We walked up the steps and were at the top before 7:45. The whole place was still  in cloud and the views were not that spectacular. However, we stayed at the top for about an hour while the mist started to clear and the views were once again very good.

We walked back down the steps again and returned to our hotel for showers followed by breakfast in the hotel restaurant, overlooking the lake.

We checked out of the hotel at 10:30 and walked to the airport which was only about 500m away. We checked in without any problems and took off on time at 11:55. Half an hour later, we landed in Chiang Mai, collected our luggage and got a taxi to the hotel. The hotel was all a bit strange, being split into two different parts, on different levels, with a conference hall just off reception and an attached shopping centre. The whole place was very similar in age and standards to The Asia Hotel in Bangkok. The room was fine and the shopping centre had a food hall for lunch. We got a taxi to the Bossotel and back to collect our left luggage and were back in our room by about 3.

We did not do a lot else for the rest of the afternoon. We had a look around the shopping centre which was not as good as MBK but had enough to look around for an hour or so. We went out just before 6. We walked through the old town, a lot of which had been turned into a street market and stopped for a couple of wheat beers before heading out to The Riverside. We had a bottle of Sang Som with our meal and bought another bottle to drink in the room while listening to world Service (Swansea 5, Carlisle 0).

Sunday, 17th December 2006 We checked out of the hotel at 9:30 and got a taxi to the airport for the 11:15 flight to Phuket. After checking in, we changed the return part of the ticket so that we can just fly to Bangkok on Jan 3rd and not Chiang Mai. This was a free "reward" flight so we had not known if this would be possible or not.

When we got through to the gate, there were a couple of desks selling transfers to Phi Phi that would get us to Phi Phi today. We had planned to stay in Phuket but this seemed a good option as we would have to go to Phi Phi to get to Lanta tomorrow anyway. This ticket cost B1000 each and included the transfer from Phuket airport and the through boat ticket to Lanta. We landed slightly late in Phuket and by the time we had found the transfer vehicle, we only had about 40 minutes to get to the ferry pier. We arrived at the pier at about a minute after the departure time and just got on the boat before it left. The journey took about 90 minutes and we arrived in Phi Phi just after 4.

We checked into a bungalow near the ferry pier. We took the first bungalow we looked at for B1200 a night. I could not be bothered to look around too much as I was feeling hung-over and we had too much luggage to carry, but the room we got was pretty good value compared to others we looked at later on. The room was not air-conditioned but the hotel had a pool which we were both in within about 10 minutes of checking in!

Once the sun had gone down, we went out for a sandwich (we had not really had any lunch) and a look around. Most of the island seems to have returned to the way it was pre-tsunami. We looked at a few places to stay as we are thinking about coming back here for a few days at the end of January but a lot of places have gone upmarket since being rebuilt and did not seem particularly good value. We ate at our hotel restaurant which is on the sea front - probably a more enjoyable meal than the ones we had eaten at the Riverside!
