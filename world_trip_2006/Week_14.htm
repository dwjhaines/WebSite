World Trip 2006/7 - Week 14

Monday, 11th September 2006 I was feeling a lot better than yesterday with my cold almost totally cleared up. After we had checked out of the room, we spent most of the rest of the day lying by the pool apart from another trip to McDonalds for lunch. Just before 5, we left the hotel, walked up to the main road and got a taxi out to the bus station. Despite what I had been told yesterday, we were able to buy tickets in advance and paid LE100 (about £9.30) each for the journey to Luxor. The bus turned up before 6 and we departed at about 6:10.

Lonely Planet reckoned that the journey took 12 - 15 hours but this did not seem likely given the times that it stated for the individual legs of the journey. The journey was not wonderful but probably not the worst we have ever had. Up until midnight, the bus was too hot and we did not have a lot of space. However, there was enough going on every couple of hours to mean that I was never totally bored despite not being able to sleep much. Roz managed to sleep quite well from midnight but I don't think I slept at all until after the drinks stop at 3am. We were all woken at 5 at Hurghada when a group of seven Western tourists got on and got half the bus to change seats so that they could be seated together. At 8 we finally managed to get a double seat each which meant that we slept well for the last hour of the trip. The bus finally pulled into the new bus station at 9:15 - just over 15 hours after it had left Sharm.

Tuesday, 12th September 2006 Once off the bus we got a minibus into Luxor and had a twenty minute walk to the hotel we had booked from our room in Sharm. The hotel is only a few minutes walk from the hotels we had stayed at during our first visit to Luxor. Thankfully, we were given a room straight away so were able to catch up on a couple of hours sleep. At 1, we went out for lunch. We ate near the hotel but the kushari places that we were looking for seemed to have closed or disappeared. After eating, we walked to the train station and booked our 1st class tickets to Aswan for Thursday (only about £3) We walked back to the hotel via the Nile. The whole time we were out, we had to put up with people offering us taxis, boat trips, souvenirs, etc, etc - all part of the fun of being in Luxor.

We spent an hour or so by the pool (sleeping in my case) and then went out to begin our sight-seeing. We got to the Luxor temple just before sun-set. We had visited 11 years ago but were still impressed. We wandered around the site in daylight and waited for the sun to set before walking around again while it was illuminated. I had not been that keen on doing this but it was worth the wait as it was more spectacular seen this way (we did not bother waiting 11 years ago!) After taking about 80 photos between us, we left the temple and went straight out to eat. I think we would of both fallen asleep if we had returned to the hotel for showers before going out.

We were back in the hotel by 9:20 and had an early night.

Wednesday, 13th September 2006 After breakfast, we used the hotel's internet access to book our room in Aswan for tomorrow night. We also contacted Dive Tours to see if they could book us an additional night in Sharm for next Tuesday. (Also found out that Swansea had won 2 - 0 at Brentford to move up to third place in League 1)

We continued our sight-seeing with a trip out to Karnak. We got out to Karnak by minibus from the railway station. The ruins were once again, very impressive, particularly the great hypostyle hall. We had just managed to beat the tour groups to the site so had an hour or so wandering round before it really started to fill up. By the time we had finished looking around and had got a minibus back to Luxor again, it was time for lunch and today we were able to find somewhere serving kushari. We wandered back to the hotel stopping to take pictures of some of the more interesting shops along the way. By 1 pm we had returned to the room for a siesta.

We made it to the pool by about 4:30 and stayed by the pool (which is on the roof of the hotel) until the sun had set. We had showers and returned to the same restaurant that we had ate in last night despite the fact that my meal yesterday had contained a piece of glass from a light bulb!

Thursday, 14th September 2006 We had breakfast, checked out of the hotel and walked to the train station by 9:10. There was a lot of confusion at the station and we were told that our train, due to leave at 9:25, was running three hours late. The 7:15 train had not departed yet either but we were told that we could not use our tickets on that train and we could not buy new tickets either as the train was full. At about 9:30, a train bound for Aswan came into the station, We assumed that it was the 7:15 and although we had been told that we could not get on it, we decided that we may as well get on anyway - the worse thing that could happen is that we would be made to pay again or get kicked off. It turned out that the train was not the 7:15 but another train that foreigners are not supposed to use. There are only three trains a day that we are allowed to buy tickets for, all of which have armed guards and are supposedly safer.

After the train had left the station, we were told by the ticket collector that we were on the wrong train. We pleaded ignorance and he eventually just said "no problem" a couple of times and left us where we were. We had been prepared to bribe him to let us stay but it was not necessary. We eventually arrived in Aswan just before 1pm.

We had decided that we would leave Aswan by overnight train on Sunday as neither of us were keen on another 15 hour bus journey back to Sharm. We reserved seats at the station as soon as we arrived, visited the tourist information and then had a 10 - 15 minute walk to our hotel. More confusion at the hotel as they have not yet received their voucher despite the fact that we have paid for a room but we eventually got given a room with an excellent view of the Nile.

We went out for lunch (kushari again) wandering through the souk in both directions. There is not quite as much hassle here as in Luxor but still plenty of people trying to sell you things. We spent a couple of hours by the pool and then returned to the room from where we watched the sun setting over the Nile again - this time with plenty of feluccas.

We walked back to the station area for our evening meal. On the way, we found out details of tours to Abu Simbel and used the internet to book a room in the Cairo Hilton for Monday night. We ate excellent "Egyptian pizzas", walked back to the hotel and booked our tour to Abu Simbel for Saturday.

Friday, 15th September 2006 We were up early to begin our sight-seeing and, after breakfast, headed for the West Bank. We got the ferry across the Nile for LE1 each despite being told that "the ferry does not start until 9 o'clock", "the ferry is for locals only", "you must use felucca", etc, etc.

Once on the West Bank, we walked up to the Tombs of the Nobles. We had a personal guide which we did not really want but he turned out to be pretty good showing us round everything we wanted to see. The tombs were all quite interesting, maybe not as spectacular as the Valley of the Kings, but we were the only tourists there which made it more enjoyable. After an hour wandering around there, the next sight we wanted to visit was the Monastery of St Simeon. We were prepared to get there by a couple of ferry trips but I was also quite keen on a camel ride as I had never been on one before. We managed to negotiate a couple of camels for LE20 each and had a reasonably enjoyable 40 minute ride round to the monastery.

The monastery itself was pretty good. Once again, we were the only tourists there - I'm not sure where everyone is going during the day! We were shown around by a member of the tourist police which was not necessary this time as we could easily of wandered around on our own. From the monastery, we had planned to walk down to another ferry point to get the ferry to Elaphantine Island. After we had walked down to where we thought the ferry should go from, there was no ferry in sight and it looked this time as if everyone was correct when they said that there was no ferry. Instead of paying for a felucca or another camel, we walked back through the sand to our original ferry point. This was quite hard work and was not a route travelled by too many tourists but it did keep us away from the touts for 40 minutes or so. We got the ferry back without any arguments and went straight for lunch at the same kushari place as yesterday.

We were back in the room by 1:00 and were planning on a couple of hours sleep before going to the pool. I was woken at 2 by reception who have still not got our payment voucher and want us to pay for the room despite us already paying lastminute.com. I then spent about an hour and twenty minutes going round internet cafes trying to get a printout of the e-mail confirming that we had paid. They were still not convinced when I showed them the voucher but did find the name of another agency that they could call. Ten minutes after giving them the voucher they had sorted it out but it wasted a lot of the afternoon.

We had a  couple of hours by the pool, early showers and another pizza before heading off to see the Sound and Light show at the Isis temple complex on the island of Philae. We paid a taxi LE30 to take us there and back and another LE40 for the boat to get us to and from the island. The Sound and Light show was pretty good although it did drag on at times. The temples were very spectacular and would be worth seeing in day light if they were not so much hassle to get to. Got the taxi back to our hotel and were in our room before 10.

Saturday, 16th September 2006 We got up at 3 am for our day trip to Abu Simbel. We were picked up from our hotel at 3:30 and after collecting from a few more hotels, headed off to join up with  the "convoy". The convoy is the only way that tourist coaches can travel to Abu Simbel. It involves all the buses and minibuses congregating in the South of Aswan and then travelling together under armed police escort. This is apparently for our own safety but seems a totally ridiculous idea. It also means that just about everyone who wants to visit Abu Simbel arrives at the temples at exactly the same time.

We all got to the meeting point at 4:15 and left 15 minutes later. There were about 15 - 20 coaches in the convoy as well as an assortment of smaller buses and minibuses. The 260km journey took less than three hours and we were dropped off at 7:15. We then had 2.5 hours at the temples before the return convoy.

The two temples were both very spectacular particularly the main one. However, once we had taken a few photos of them both, there was not too much else to do and the amount of time there was ample. The insides of the temples were full of tourists and not much more interesting than the tombs we saw yesterday. We spent most of our time there sat outside looking at the statues at the front of the temples. However, we both thought that the temples were worth seeing and were glad we had gone.

The convoy left again on time at 10 and we were dropped back in Aswan just before 1. We had lunch and then returned for three hours sleep before listening to the football on World Service (Gillingham 3, Swansea 1) in our room while watching the sun set again.

Before eating we had a walk through the less touristy part of the souk, heading Southwards from our hotel. This was much better than the other part that we had previously walked through with a lot less people trying to sell us things. We ate in a restaurant overlooking the river and were back in the room by 9:15. Both totally knackered following our early start despite catching up on sleep this afternoon.

Sunday, 17th September 2006 We were up at 7:15 for our last sight-seeing trip in Aswan. We had breakfast and then got a taxi out to the "unfinished obelisk". This was a 45m obelisk that had been abandoned when a flaw was discovered in the rock from which it was being cut. It was quite an impressive sight - probably better that I was expecting it to be. We stayed at the quarry for 20 minutes or so before walking back to the hotel. We walked back through the Fatimid Cemetery which was not quite as interesting.

We stayed in our room until check-out time and spent the rest of the afternoon lying by the pool. We did not go out for lunch as Roz had an upset stomach and did not want to risk eating anything other than biscuits before our train journey.

 We had tickets for the 6pm train to Cairo. We left the pool at 5, got a taxi to the station and got a take-away pizza for the journey (Roz's stomach getting better!). The train left about 15 minutes late. The first class seats were infinitely more comfortable than the bus seats had been on our previous overnight journey and we were both able to get a reasonable nights sleep.
