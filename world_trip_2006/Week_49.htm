World Trip 2006/7 - Week 49

Monday, 14th May 2007 Returned to our room after breakfast and did not get up until midday. We still had a few things to buy before we go to Sulawesi tomorrow so spent a couple of hours walking around shops. Included in this was a trip to DiveMasters again to buy a replacement dive computer for Roz. Hers has been resetting itself regularly since Pulau Weh and as we had the chance to buy one cheaply ($275) decided now was the time to change it.

We had a couple of hours by the pool again before going out to complete our shopping and to check e-mail, etc. In the evening we went out for a meal with a couple who we had dived with yesterday, Chris and Ray. We had met Ray in the bar we watched the football in last night and they had phoned our room this morning to see if we wanted to join them tonight. It was a more upmarket restaurant than we have been eating in but the food was superb and well worth the extra money.

Tuesday, 15th May 2007 We were up at 6:30 and were collected by a taxi after breakfast at 7:30. We had an uneventful journey to Manado - two Garuda flights, the first to Makassar on Sulawesi and the second taking us the rest of the way to Manado. We arrived in Manado just after 1 and were met at the airport by Rifke with whom Roz had been exchanging e-mails while we were at Sipidan. We were taken by taxi to the port and then had a 40 minute boat journey to Bunaken. On arrival, we filled out all the paperwork for the diving and spent the rest of the afternoon on the balcony of our bungalow. All meals are included in the price of the accommodation so we ate in the restaurant here with the five other guests and had an early night.

Wednesday, 16th May 2007 Did three dives today. The boat went out after breakfast at 8 and we did two dives. We then returned to Two Fishes and had lunch before going out for the third dive of the day at 3. The diving was not as spectacular as we had been expecting it to be but we did see quite a lot over the three dives - two sharks, two turtles, a new species of sea-horse, plenty of nudibranchs including ones we had not seen before and I saw an eagle ray on the second dive.

The diving is well organised here. It is all done in small groups and we have a guide to ourselves. They allow long dives as long as your air lasts so we dived for well over three hours today. Also, there are not too many people staying here but they still took out two boats today so there is plenty of space in between dives.

There is not a lot to do in between dives here but in a as with a live-aboard, doing three dives seems to totally fill the day. We were back in our bungalow by about 5:30 and by the time we had showered and looked at pictures, it was time to go and eat again. Meals are at a communal table with all other customers and so far have been reasonably good. Were back in the room again by 9ish.

Thursday, 17th May 2007 Similar diving to yesterday. Three wall dives with plenty to see - ghost pipe fish, Napoleon wrasse, leaf scorpion fish, shrimps, nudibranchs, etc. the third dive had very strong down currents which were not much fun.

Friday, 18th May 2007 First two dives similar to the previous days. Both very good dives again, especially the second where we spent about 10 minutes watching a large green turtle move from its ledge to go up for air and then go back to sleep again. The third dive was to Mandarin at sunset to see the mandarin fish. This fish only comes out at sunset so we spent almost an hour kneeling on broken coral trying to photograph them as they appeared. A bit different form the other dives and quite amusing as we both desperately tried to take pictures of fish that would not stay still.

The dive ended in darkness and we were back at the resort by about 7. We had a couple of drinks after eating as a DMT who had been here a while is leaving tomorrow, but were still in bed by 11.

Saturday, 19th May 2007 Two more dives in the morning followed by a night dive at 6pm. Excellent Napoleon wrasse on the first dive - probably the closest we have ever got to one. The night dive was also very enjoyable. Spectacular crabs, shrimps, etc and a white tip reef shark in a small cave right in front of us.

At 10pm we interrupted the staff watching Titanic on the staff TV and got them to turn over for the FA Cup final - a pretty unexciting game that unfortunately went to extra time. Got to bed just before 1.

Sunday, 20th May 2007 A day trip to a couple of islands North of Bunaken with three dives and lunch on the boat. It was an excellent days diving. We started by going down to 43m looking for hammer heads. We did not find any but did see another white tip swimming around. During the day, we saw eagle rays, a turtle, a bump head parrot fish, sea-snakes, loads of nudibranchs and the whole trip made a change from the wall diving around Bunaken.

We were back at the resort by 5. The evening meal was a whole pig that looked better than it tasted.
