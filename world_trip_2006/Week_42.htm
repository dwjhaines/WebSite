World Trip 2006/7 - Week 42

Monday, 26th March 2007 We had our first attempt at mapping Batee Tokong this morning. We were a bit unfortunate with the visibility which was particularly poor. This made it a lot more difficult than it should have been but we did at least make a start and know what we have to do. We missed the afternoon dive as we were assisting with the 10 Indonesians who are all doing their open water.

Spent most of the evening working on our Emergency Assistance Plan. This mainly involved copying and pasting bits from all the other plans that previous students have completed but there did not seem too many other ways of doing it.

Started taking antibiotics (doxy) on Mette's advice in an attempt to heal the infected cuts on my legs. Some of these, I have had now for nearly four weeks and they are showing no signs of healing.

Tuesday, 27th March 2007 Roz's day to be on "shop duty" so she did not dive. I went on the morning boat trip to The Canyon where I did my first supervised guiding. I led David and Peter and had to do the boat briefing, dive briefing as well as lead the dive. All went reasonably successfully. Myself and Luca assisted with the Indonesians in the afternoon while Roz was "busy" in the shop. Another painfully boring hour that did not do my ear much good.

Roz had to make an addition to our Emergency Assistance Plan while I was with the Indonesians but we did get it signed off by David in the end. Arranged to do our stamina test tomorrow. By the evening, Roz was fairly sure she was going to fail this and not complete the DM course here.

Wednesday, 28th March 2007 Spent the morning completing our stamina test. We started off by marking out a 100m course across the bay. Me Roz and Peter stretched out a 100m tape measure and secured it by weight belts and surface marker buoys. The three of us had to complete four tests - 100m tired diver tow, 15 minute float, 400m swim and 800m snorkel. All of the tasks were timed with 1 - 5 points being awarded depending on the speed. We all pushed Ampon, the smallest member of the Indonesian staff here, for the tired diver tow and all scored 4 points. The 15 minute float was an easy 5 points each leaving the two most difficult tests remaining. We all got at least 2 points on the swimming meaning that we could take our time over the snorkelling and just collect a point for completing the distance. All passed first time without any problems despite Roz's lack of optimism!

Today was my day on shop duty so I did not do a lot for the rest of the day. Roz assisted David with his Indonesians in between looking after the kitten that has appeared at Lumba Lumba yesterday evening.

Thursday, 29th March 2007 I did the morning boat dive to Pantee Peunateung while Roz assisted David with his remaining OW#3 dive with the Indonesians. The visibility at Pantee Peunateung was worse than on any dive since we have been here - it really was like a night dive in places.

All the Indonesians went out on the boat for their final dive in the afternoon meaning that Roz and I could dive from the beach on our own. This was probably the best beach dive we have done here - we found our way round without any problems and there was loads to see, including a ghost pipe fish. We also did a night dive with Peter and Lara a customer who started diving with us this morning. Did a similar route to the one this afternoon and saw the ghost pipe fish again as well as plenty of other stuff that made the dive reasonably interesting.

Friday, 30th March 2007 Two dives today. We both did the morning trip to Batee Tokong. Once again the vis was not really good enough for mapping but it was a nice dive and we did get a few more notes down. I think most of our map is going to be copied from an existing one with a few additions and modifications! The second dive was back to the tug boat wreck in Sabang harbour with Lara, Peter and Eric. It was an enjoyable dive again and the stop at the hot springs on the return journey was a lot better than last time due to the water being considerably warmer.

Saturday, 31st March 2007 We were at the dive centre by 8 for the 9am dive but found out at about 8:40 that it was postponed until midday due to the death and immediate funeral of one of the village elders. Their were only four customers for the dive, two who did not mind waiting and two who were happy to do beach dives instead. Lara came with me and Roz. The vis was dreadful and it was not as enjoyable a dive as the recent beach dives.

The second and third dives were much better. I guided three divers at the Canyon. One had not dived for five years so we did not go to the main site but had a very enjoyable dive along the shore line. Saw a white tip reef shark swimming around which seemed to keep everyone happy. The third dive was supposed to be at Arus Paleh but we changed it to Rubiah Sea Garden due to the experience of the divers we were taking. Once again, it was a relaxing dive, led by Roz, which everyone seemed to enjoy. Ended the dive spending a few minutes with a hawksbill turtle.

Returned to the bungalow by 9 to listen to the football on World Service (Millwall 2, Swansea 1) while finishing off our litre bottle of Bacardi.

Sunday, 1st April 2007 Another good days diving although the day did not start well. At the dive centre, we found out that Luca's mother had just died. This was just a day after Marjan flew back to Holland because her father has also died recently. David and Mette are both ill at the moment and there was a bit of concern about whether or not we would have enough guides who knew the site.

We eventually headed off to Pantee Peunateung at 8:45. My first dive was led by Arun with just one customer and me. Roz dived with David's group but he had to end his dive after 20 due to is illness. The second, I led with four customers and Peter. This did not go too well as we went down to 28m and there were strong currents. Two of the group struggled to get back up to a shallower depth as we were swimming into the current and used up most of their air. Ended the dive early after 40 minutes.

We were not on the boat for the 3pm dive so me and Roz dived off the beach again. It was a very enjoyable dive and included 15 minutes of watching a turtle destroying coral. Also saw a juvenile bat fish, the stone fish and plenty of blue spotted rays.

Ate fish at Mama Jungle's again although this time we got fed up with waiting for Cedric, Diane and Peter so ate on our own.
