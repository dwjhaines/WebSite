World Trip 2006/7 - Week 6

Monday, 17th July 2006 After a few days of visiting friends and relatives, we left Nottingham to begin the second leg of our World trip. We had an uneventful drive down to Gatwick stopping for a pub lunch in Reigate just before we got there. After a bit of confusion, the car was collected from outside South terminal and we went off to try flying EasyJet for the first time. There was more confusion when we arrived in the terminal due to the luggage belt being broken, but this was soon fixed and check-in was a lot quicker than expected. We killed a couple of hours in the departure lounge before another uneventful journey, arriving in Venice at about 21:30, only 40 minutes later than expected. All in all, not a bad flight for £26 each.

We got the public bus into Venice for €2 each and had about a 20 minute walk to our hotel. Checked in, had showers and went straight to sleep.

Tuesday, 18th July 2006 After breakfast, we had a quick trip to bus and train stations to get information for leaving Venice in two days time. We then began our sight-seeing. We started with a journey along the Grand Canal on a Vaporetto, a water bus type thing which took us from the bus station area all the way to Piazza S. Marco. It was a very enjoyable 40 minute journey, taking in all the sights along the canal. St Marks Square itself was too crowded with tourists to be described as enjoyable and we did not bother joining the queues to get into the cathedral or the tower. We went from there to the Bridge of Sighs and onto the Rialto Bridge which were also very crowded. However, away from the main attractions, it was all quite pleasant wandering around the streets and canals with far fewer people.

After a pizza slice for lunch, we headed back to the area where our hotel is, Cannaregio, found an excellent wine shop and somehow ended up buying 1.5 litres of draught white wine for €3. We also found a few nice looking places for eating and drinking later on.

We started on the wine at about 6ish, drinking 1 litre of it before leaving the hotel. We then went to some of the "Osterias" we had seen earlier. These were similar to tapas bars in Spain and all served half litres of reasonable white wine for €4 - 5. After visiting 5 of these, we made it back to the hotel by about 11:30.

Wednesday, 19th July 2006 Roz was quite hung-over today so while she slept, I went to the station to buy train tickets for tomorrow and also went for a walk around the area we had been drinking in last night. She had recovered by midday and we spent a few more hours wandering around the sights. We walked a different route to St Mark's Square and then crossed the Grand Canal on the only bridge we had yet to use. We went to the church on the opposite side of the canal to St Mark's and then made our way back to the hotel stopping in the same place as yesterday for a late lunch. In the evening we found a restaurant in the streets behind our hotel and had a pizza and an early night. 1st alcohol free day since 18th June!

Thursday, 20th July 2006 Spent the day travelling to Rovinj on the Croatian coast. We got the 9:10 train from Venice station which got us into Trieste at about midday. The next bus for Rovinj was not until 3pm. We did not want to pay the daily rate to leave our rucksacks in left luggage so had lunch (bought from a supermarket) in the park opposite the bus station and then waited in the bus station for the last hour. The bus took us through Slovenia and into Croatia, arriving in Rovinj at 17:15. It was a fairly enjoyable way of spending the day as both train and bus were no-where near full.

We found the room that Roz had booked in advance, only a short walk from the bus station. The room was ideal - air-con, a fridge and a decent size balcony that just about overlooked the sea (The room also came with free wireless broadband through someone's un-secure connection which was a bit of a bonus!). We spent an hour sunbathing on the balcony before heading off for a look around Rovinj. We were not too impressed initially but once we had found our way to the old town, it seemed a pretty nice little place. We went back to the room for a beer on our balcony and then went out for a pizza. The plan of cutting down on food by ordering one pizza between us failed to work but we had a very enjoyable evening anyway getting through one and a half litres of wine with the meal.

Friday, 21st July 2006 We had breakfast on our balcony and then went for a wander through the streets of the old town which was all quite pleasant without being too spectacular. We returned to the balcony at lunchtime to finish last nights pizza.

Our plans for the next 7 days are a bit vague but we managed to contact Croatia Divers on Korcula and they arranged for us to do two days diving with them and sorted out our accommodation from next Thursday. We were also not sure how long we would stay in Rovinj. We had planned to leave on Sunday but decided that Monday may be a better day to be travelling. We decided to check how good the beach was before finalising our plans and at 2ish, we headed off to the beach. It was about a 25 minute walk to get to the beach but once there it was very nice. Still no sand, as with a lot of Croatia's beaches but  it was comfortable to lie on and easy to get in and out of the water compared to some places. Spent a couple of hours sunbathing before returning to our room. Our room was not available for Sunday night but we managed to book another room at the same place instead.

Spent the evening with another "Jumbo" pizza and a couple of litres of wine.

Saturday, 22nd July 2006 A similar day to yesterday - walked around old town, leftover pizza on the balcony for lunch, a couple of hours trying to get information for the next month's travel (making the most of the free internet access) and a couple of hours on the same beach as yesterday. Had a couple of litres of wine on the balcony with a takeaway pizza in the evening.

Decided today that a few alcohol free, healthy eating days were required as I was feeling decidedly unhealthy following Germany and the drinking that has taken place in the last couple of weeks. I also still have the sore throat that I have had on and off since before the world cup. Will start tomorrow!

Sunday, 23rd July 2006 We changed rooms to another just over the corridor by 11 (almost identical room but no balcony) and shortly after, we went out and hired bikes for a few hours. We did not cycle very far but went a few km around the coast, stopping for picnic lunch along the way and spending a couple of hours at another beach before cycling home.

We went back to the same pizza place as we went to on our first two nights here and had another jumbo pizza before an alcohol-free early night.
