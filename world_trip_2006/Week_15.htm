World Trip 2006/7 - Week 15

Monday, 18th September 2006 We were not expecting the train to get into Cairo until 9ish but we arrived just after 7. We got a taxi to the bus station to buy our tickets for tomorrow morning and another taxi to the Ramses Hilton where we had booked a room for the night. We were able to check in straight away and got a room on the 16th floor with a great view of the city. Despite sleeping reasonably well on the train, we were both still tired and spent most of the rest of the morning asleep. There was nothing we particularly wanted to see in Cairo and we wanted to make the most of the relative luxury of our hotel.

We went out for lunch at mid-day and spent the rest of the afternoon by the pool. We both felt like a change from Egyptian food in the evening so headed out looking for Pizza Hut. However, the area where the restaurant was supposed to be was packed with riot police due to some sort of demonstration that was taking place. There did not seem to be any trouble there but we decided to get away from the area anyway and ended up going for an Italian meal in the Nile Hilton, a couple of hundred metres from our hotel.

Tuesday, 19th September 2006 Another early start. We checked out of the hotel at 6:45 and got a taxi to the bus station for the 7:30 bus back to Sharm. The bus left on time and immediately went back to the area outside our hotel to pick up more passengers! It took about an hour to get out of Cairo and then another five and a half hours to complete the journey to Sharm. We got a taxi from the bus station to Na'ama Bay and walked to the Camel Hotel. This time they had our reservation sorted out and we got our room straight away.

Did not do much for the remainder of the afternoon apart from a trip to McDonalds for lunch and some time checking e-mail etc. we decided that it was time for a few beers as we are yet to have any alcohol since arriving in Egypt. We bought bottles of Stella from a bottle shop and drank most of them sat on our balcony. We ate at the Indian restaurant at our hotel. This cost a bit more than the rest of the food we have been eating in Egypt but was well worth the money as the food was superb. We bought a few more beers from another shop and finished up the evening with a couple more on our balcony.

Wednesday, 20th September 2006 Did not do a lot today. We had free internet access in the room so spent quite a bit of time sending e-mail, catching up on football news, etc. Other than that, we lay by the pool and read for most of the day. We got takeaways for lunch and returned to the restaurant where we had eaten on our last visit to Sharm in the evening. We finished off the beer that we had left over from yesterday on our balcony and were in bed by 11.

Thursday, 21st September 2006 We were picked up at midday by a minibus to transfer us to the Bedouin Moon Hotel in Dahab. This was included in the package that we had booked with Dive Tours back home. We were dropped at the hotel just after 1pm. We checked into a room overlooking the sea, a few doors down from the room that we left 52 weeks ago.

After lunch, we checked into the dive centre and sorted out all our equipment for tomorrow. We were allocated our lockers and put all our own stuff away together with the equipment that we were renting. Spent the rest of the afternoon by the pool. We walked into the main part of Dahab in the evening and found that the restaurant that we always ate at last year had not changed much and the food was still very good. In fact not much had changed at all in Dahab apart from the fact that there did not seem to be as many people around as last year and the taxis to get back to our hotel have gone up from LE5 to LE10.

Friday, 22nd September 2006 Our first day of diving with Reef 2000. We were up at what we thought was 7:30 only to find out that the clocks had gone back an hour and it was in fact 6:45 when we got to the restaurant for breakfast. We were expecting the clocks to move in a weeks time but it had been moved forward due to the start of Ramadan.

At about 9, we set off for the dive sites about 15 - 20 minutes South of Dahab. There were five of us diving with Mark, the dive leader.

Dive 211: Golden Blocks → Moray Gardens
First dive of a twenty dive package with Reef 2000 in Dahab. We were driven to dive sites 15 minutes South of Dahab. A nice, easy dive to start with - nothing particularly spectacular but very enjoyable anyway. Roz had problems with her weights and had to be given 2 extra kg towards the end of the dive.

Dive 212: Three Pools → Moray Gardens
Another enjoyable dive, this time starting South of Moray Gardens and swimming back. The dive started by swimming through three pools with shallow entries/exits to each one before getting out to open water.

We were sat in a restaurant while we were not diving, in a very spectacular setting with the sea on one side of us and mountains on the other. We liked the other people in the group and the trip was an ideal start to our diving here.

We were not back at the hotel until just after 4pm and then spent an hour or so in the dive centre, putting away our kit, writing log books etc. We stopped for a couple of beers on our way into Dahab and then went for an all you can eat meal  at a restaurant that had been recommended to us earlier. Spent a lot of the evening discussing whether or not it would be a good idea to do our Rescue Diver course while we are here instead of doing it in Lanta in December.

Saturday, 23rd September 2006 Another two dives, this time with a trip back to the hotel for lunch in between. The first was 5 minutes North of the hotel, the second just to the South of Dahab.

Dive 213: Rick's Reef → Canyon
A repeat of the first dive we did last year. Dived with Mark again and Tim, a very experienced diver from England. Another good dive with plenty of small fish and colourful coral

Dive 214: Islands
One of my favourite dive sites from last year in Dahab. We entered the dive site through a narrow crack in the reef followed by a small swim-through. Lots of very spectacular hard coral formations. Saw some bigger fish on this dive including a large puffer fish and a Napoleon wrasse.

We finished earlier today so were back in the room for the start of the football but had next to no reception on the World Service frequencies until the second half (Swansea 1, Huddersfield 2). We had showers and walked into Dahab after the football had finished. We went to check e-mail (I have a summons from Chesterfield police but no world cup refund) and also to compare the prices of doing the Rescue Diver course in Thailand instead of here. There was not a huge difference and after a couple of beers, we decided that we would go for it while we were here. Had another beer with our meal at El Fanar and got a taxi back to the hotel.

Sunday, 24th September 2006 After breakfast, we notified the dive centre that we wanted to proceed with the Rescue Diver course. We discovered that we could do EFR without missing a days diving which was good news. Did two dives with JP, two other customers and a trainee DM.

Dive 215: Canyon
We dived with JP and three others, including a trainee DM and Rob who we dived with two days ago. Very spectacular dive here again although not as good as last year due to the "fish bowl" exit from the canyon being considered unsafe because some of it had collapsed.

Dive 216: Bells → The Blue Hole
Spectacular entry through "Bells", a 26m chimney, open to the sea most of the way down except the last 2m. Drifted down past the Blue Hole and then swam back before doing safety stop while swimming across it. A very colourful dive with loads of small fish again

We were back at the hotel by 3 and, after sorting out equipment and showering, we made a start on the EFR course. This is a basic first aid course which is a prerequisite of doing the Rescue Diver course. The first part of this was a 90 minute video that we watched before retiring to our room to complete the knowledge reviews. When these were complete, we started on the Rescue Diver book. We only had one copy to save money which meant Roz reading the first chapter, completing the knowledge reviews and then passing the book on to me. The first chapter was 60 pages so this took up most of the rest of the evening. We ate at our hotel restaurant, mainly to save time but the food was really good - chicken balti, rice and popadoms.
