World Trip 2006/7 - Week 8

Monday, 31st July 2006 We had a late breakfast and then spent a couple of hours walking into Korčula Town which was another really nice old town. We spent an hour or so wandering round the town and then caught the bus back to Lumbarda. Spent the remainder of the afternoon at the beach again.

We had a similar evening to yesterday with a couple of bottles of wine on our terrace and a meal (black risotto) in a restaurant overlooking the sea.

Tuesday, 1st August 2006 A similar day to yesterday. We walked round another part of Lumbarda in the morning, beach in the afternoon, wine and pizza in the evening.

Wednesday, 2nd August 2006 Due to slightly more wine than usual last night, we did not do a lot in the morning. We used the internet in Lumbarda to find out that we have got some a room sorted out for Corfu but did not do a lot else apart from read on our terrace. We had not intended to drink anything in the evening but were given another bottle of wine (red this time) by the family we are staying with (as well as more meat, a plate of pancakes and two lemons), so drank that before going out for pizza again. Had another litre with our meal but a relatively early night.

Thursday, 3rd August 2006 We had bought tickets for the 6:45 bus to Dubrovnik and were driven to the bus station by Robert, the owners son. The journey took just over three hours and we were met in Dubrovnik by the owner of the next place we are staying in. We left our luggage in the room and went straight back to the bus station and booked tickets for the Kotor bus in two days time.

From the bus station it was about an hours walk to get to the start of the old town. We have seen a few "old towns" since arriving in Croatia, but Dubrovnik is the most famous and certainly, the most impressive. We spent about an hour walking around the sights within the walls and then the highlight which was a 2km walk around the walls. This walk took another hour and included all the towers and ramparts which provided excellent views of the city and surrounding coast. We walked back through the town, stopping for lunch on the way and then walked back to our room near the port.

While walking about, we had tried to find a way of visiting Mostar (3 hours away by bus in Bosnia and Hercegovina), tomorrow on a day trip. We could only find out bus times there but not back again which was not ideal as we could not be sure of getting back! The other option was an organised tour that would of cost us 360 kn each (about £34) which seemed expensive and we are not that keen on escorted tours anyway. After a lot of indecision, we found that we could hire a car for 410 kn which seemed the ideal solution. We paid the deposit and arranged to collect it at 7:30 tomorrow morning.

Did not do a lot for the remainder of the day due to being totally knackered. Spent a couple of hours sleeping or reading and went out for a meal near the port.

Friday, 4th August 2006 We collected the hire car (Fiat Punto) from just in front of the port and were on our way North from Dubrovnik by about 7:45. We stopped at Ston as it was not far off route and it had looked a reasonably interesting place when we had come through on the bus yesterday. It also had a supermarket so we could buy breakfast! We continued up the coast before turning inland towards Mostar where we arrived at about 11.

We had been unsure whether or not to bother going to Mostar but it was really worth the effort. There were only a few sights that we were keen on seeing, mainly the new old bridge, but it was a really interesting place to wander around. The amount of still damaged buildings bought home how badly and how recently the town had been damaged during the war. The way that the town has been restored and generally has recovered from it all was very impressive.

As well as spending a fair bit of time at the bridge, watching the divers, we visited The Turkish House - not very impressive in itself but Roz was able to chase the tortoises around - and also wandered around the area where all the copper beaters used to work, now mainly souvenir shops.

On our way back from Mostar, we stopped at Počitelj about 25km to the South. This was a much smaller town that suffered a lot of damage during the war. A lot of the town is still derelict but parts have been restored and the mosque is still intact. We climbed up to the old fort and were able to get to the top of the tower. This was not a particularly popular tourist attraction so was free to enter and totally empty by the time we were at the top. Both Mostar and Počitelj had been excellent places to visit.

We drove back to Dubrovnik, stopping for 30 minutes at a beach near Ston and also headed round the road above Dubrovnik for more great views of the old town. We returned the car at about 7 having spent a total of 550 kn, cheaper than the tour and far more enjoyable!

We ate locally again and had another early night.

Saturday, 5th August 2006 We left our room at 9:30 and walked down to the bus station, stopping to buy breakfast on the way. The bus to Kotor was totally packed and did not pull out of the bus station until about 10:45 - 15 minutes late. It then parked just around the corner from the bus station and waited there another 30 minutes for people who were joining the bus from Zagreb. It took about half an hour to reach the Montenegro border where we had another wait of about 50 minutes, but did get our passports stamped for the first time on the trip. It was obvious at this point that we were going to be late but things got worse once in Montenegro as the traffic along the coast was horrendous and we were travelling through busy seaside resorts. We got caught up in a very lengthy queue to get on a ferry despite the fact that we were not going on the ferry and once past this obstruction, we had the driver driving at suicidal speeds to try and make up some of the lost time. Eventually got to Kotor at about 2:45 only just over an hour late.

We walked from the bus station to the old town and managed to get an apartment just outside the walls. We got the apartment through an agency and were quite lucky. Not only is this a busy time of year, but we had managed to coincide our arrival with the last day of the Kotor carnival. The apartment had a kitchen and air conditioning and was excellent value for €40. We had only planned to spend one night in Kotor but after a quick look around the town we decided that we both wanted to stay for an extra day. This meant that we could have a few drinks tonight, we could walk up to the fortress that is above the town tomorrow, and we would be travelling into Albania on a Monday instead of a Sunday.

After a couple of hours listening to the first day of the football season on World Service (Swansea 1 Cheltenham 2) we drank a bottle of wine (€1.22 a litre) in our kitchen before walking back into the old town. It turned out to be an excellent night. Kotor is possibly my favourite of all the old towns we have visited in the last couple of weeks and has a reputation for being a place that you go to for partying. The carnival added to an excellent atmosphere with most of the people from the carnival wandering around the streets in their costumes. We spent most of the evening in a few bars/restaurants and also bought a couple of bottles to drink while walking around the squares. Everywhere was packed with people drinking and dancing and the festivities still seemed to be going strong when we stopped drinking sometime around 2am.

Sunday, 6th August 2006 We were woken at about 8am by what sounded like a pneumatic drill in some part of the building that our apartment is in. We managed to get back to sleep in between the drilling and eventually got up for breakfast at about 10:30 with slight hangovers.

Just before midday, we began the walk up to the fort. The steps (1500 of them) started just inside the gate nearest to where we are staying. Kotor is on the side of the deepest fjord in Southern Europe and the fort is built immediately above the old town. The walk took a total of about 2 hours and gave us excellent views of the old town and the rest of the fjord.

Did not do too much for the rest of the day but did walk out to the bus station to check up on bus times for tomorrow and Roz spent time on the internet looking into accommodation in Greece. Pizza for tea again.
