World Trip 2006/7 - Week 31

Monday, 8th January 2007 Had a later start than yesterday and did not get up until about 9. After breakfast, we cycled to the Diethelm Travel office and booked our ferry tickets to Bagan on Thursday. We also booked the Traders Hotel in Yangon for our last two nights in Myanmar. We returned to the hotel and went to the pool until 1ish.

We went out on our bikes again at about 1:30. Outside our hotel, we found a taxi driver and arranged a trip round a couple of the ancient cities in the area for tomorrow. We then went to book tickets for a traditional marionette puppet show tonight. We bought bananas for lunch as neither of us is that impressed with the food here so decided we may as well go for a cheaper healthier option. We ate the bananas sat by the moat of the royal palace and fort, the first place we were to visit on our afternoons sight-seeing.

The royal palace was OK but nowhere near as spectacular as what we saw yesterday. We went from there to the two sights at the foot of Mandalay Hill that we had missed yesterday. Shwenandaw Kyaung is a traditional Burmese wooden monastery which was good as it was a bit different to some of the pagodas we have visited. It was also very touristy with young monks asking for money for photographs. Kyauktawgyi Paya complete with 8m, 900 tonne Buddha was also worth visiting and completed our tour of this part of Mandalay. We cycled back to the centre of Mandalay, returned our bikes to Mr Htoo and were back in the hotel by 5:15.

We went for an early tea (at the chapatti stand again) and walked to the Mandalay Marionette Theatre for the 8:30 performance. It was a bit of a strange show, ranging from totally crap in parts to mildly amusing in others. Overall it was just about entertaining but we would not of wanted it to have gone on for more than an hour!

Tuesday, 9th January 2007 We were picked up from our hotel at 7:15 for our tour of two of the ancient cities near to Mandalay. We had chosen the budget taxi option which meant that the taxi was a sort of miniature songthaew and looked not much bigger than a toy car. It was however comfortable enough for the two of us particularly as the cities were only about 30km away.

We arrived at Sagaing about an hour after leaving the hotel and were dropped at the base of Sagaing Hill. It took about 30 minutes to walk to the top passing stupas, monasteries and nunneries along the way. The views from the top were excellent with plenty of temples dotted around the surrounding countryside. It was quite misty as we climbed but getting there early meant that there were no other tourists around. We spent about 30 minutes looking around the pagoda at the top before walking down and went to Tilawkaguru, a mural filled cave temple near the base of the hill. It took a few minutes for our driver to find a monk with a key to let us into the cave but we were then given candles to wander around the inside with.

We drove from Sagaing to Inwa stopping at Hsinmyashin Paya (or Pagoda of Many Elephants) for about 10 minutes on the way. Inwa is on an island formed by rivers and a canal so we needed a short ferry ride to get to the start of our tour there. Once on the island, we hired a horse and cart for a couple of hours as this is the only way of getting around. We visited the three main sites here, two monasteries and a 27m high watchtower. Both monasteries were pretty good. The first was made entirely from teak and was still in use as a school. The second was probably more spectacular from the outside - made from stone and with a paya next to it. The walk up the tower gave good views of the whole area and of Sagaing Hill where we had been previously. The two hour trip round Inwa cost K3000, again excellent value. We got the boat back across to the mainland and were returned to our hotel in Mandalay by 1:45. It had been another very enjoyable days sight-seeing. We paid $15 for the taxi for the day and arranged for the same driver to come and pick us up to take us to the ferry pier in two days time.

Back at the hotel, we had more bananas for lunch and went to the pool until 3:30 when it becomes almost totally shaded. We did not do a lot for the rest of the day until going out to the chapatti stand for tea at 7ish. We ate with the same American bloke we met two nights ago as well as an Indian who is staying in the same hotel as him. Were back in the room just before 9.

Wednesday, 10th January 2007 Had a quiet day of not doing much today. We have seen a lot in the last few days. There are only so many temples that we can visit before we get fed up of them and thought it was a good idea to try and avoid this before getting to Bagan! We had a late breakfast and spent the rest of the morning by the pool. We had decided that we would hire bikes for a few hours in the afternoon and visit the one sight that we were interested in that we had not visited yet. However, when we got to Mr Htoo's the place was all locked up. We were not overly disappointed and went for lunch instead.

The walking tour in the book did not sound wonderful but we spent an hour or so wondering round the street markets near our hotel which were all quite interesting. We returned to the hotel by 5 and with nothing else that we really wanted to do, watched Happy Feet on the laptop.

We returned to the chapatti stand for the fifth and final time for tea. It has been an excellent place to eat. Not only has the food been good and very cheap, but watching the chapattis being made and cooked every evening has been entertaining as well. Definitely one of my favourite eating places of the trip so far!

Thursday, 11th January 2007 Another early start as we had to catch the 7am ferry to Bagan. We were up at 5:30 for breakfast and were picked up by our taxi driver from two days ago at 6. We were on the boat by about 6:20 and chose a couple of seats out on the deck at the back of the boat for the 10½ hour journey down the Ayeyarwady River.

The journey was quite enjoyable and the time went pretty quickly although we had probably both had enough by the time we arrived in Bagan at exactly 5:30. Arriving in Bagan was quite spectacular due to the number of temples visible from the river. Once off the boat, we paid our $10 entrance fee for the Bagan area and got a taxi to a guesthouse recommended in our book. We paid for two nights accommodation using Kyat as the pile of money that we changed in Yangon does not seem to be going down too quickly.

We had done the boat journey on a couple of bananas and a small packet of biscuits so went out early for a pizza. The bill seemed very expensive after five nights at the chapatti stand but was still just over £4 for two pizzas and our first Myanmar Beer since getting here. The food was very good again.

Friday, 12th January 2007 We were up at 6:45 for our first day of touring Bagan's temples. We had breakfast at 7 and then departed on bikes that we had hired from the guest house. We spent a total of about 6 - 7 hours cycling round the temples, mainly the ones in the Old Bagan area and on the North Plain. There are literally thousands of temples in the area. Roz had highlighted the ones that we wanted to visit and we managed to find most of these as well as a few more. A lot of the temples were very spectacular but the views of the whole area from some places were equally impressive. We ticked off 12 from the book and also found others that were not mentioned that were just as good.

We were back in Nyaung U, the area we are staying in for lunch and back in the room by about 2:30. I had not been feeling 100% all day and by this time I was totally worn out and just wanted to sleep. I stayed in bed until about 4:30 while Roz read on our balcony. We went out again to see the sunset at the Shwezigon Paya in Nyaung U. This was not the ideal place to see the sunset but was one of the best pagodas we have seen here. We spent an hour or so there as it got dark and the place became lit up.

We ate out at Aroma 2, one of Lonely Planets top 5 restaurants in Myanmar. The place served excellent Indian food on banana leaves and it probably was the best meal we have had since getting to Myanmar.

Saturday, 13th January 2007 We had decided to spend three days and four nights in Bagan - one day longer than planned. this meant that we could do a half day trip to Mt Popa. The staff at our hotel had found another couple who wanted to do this trip today so we joined them in a share-taxi and headed off at 8 o'clock (I had fully recovered from whatever I was suffering from yesterday by this morning). It was an hours drive to the base of the mountain and then a half hour walk up steps to the temples and monasteries at the top. The views from the top were pretty good but it was probably not the highlight of our visit to Bagan. We were back in Bagan by midday giving us the afternoon to continue visiting temples.

We left our hotel again at about 2:30 and continued working our way through the temples. We got away from the main roads and visited some of the harder to reach places ending up at Pyathada Paya to watch the sun set. This was a much better spot than yesterday as we could climb onto a terrace that gave excellent views of the area with the sun setting behind the silhouettes of several other temples. The temple was relatively crowded compared to yesterdays but it was still easy enough to find a spot away from the crowds. As soon as the sun went down, we started heading back as we needed to get back to the main road before complete darkness. The views of the sky changing colour behind the temples as we cycled back were also excellent.

We ate out at Aroma 2 again and also had a couple of bottles of beer with our meal. We bought a couple more beers to take back to the room and drank them while watching Man United v Villa and listening to World Service (Bradford 2, Swansea 2)

Sunday, 14th January 2007 We were still enthusiastic about seeing more temples despite the amount we have seen in the last few days. We left the hotel before 8 and cycled to a few that we had missed in the Old Bagan/North Plain area. By 10, Roz was finding it hard work cycling on some of the sandy paths but as most of the others that we wanted to see were closer to the roads, this did not seem too big a problem. We stopped at a few smaller temples and then cycled through New Bagan and out to Dhammayazika Paya. This was the last of the main temples that we had wanted to visit and was definitely one of the best. Not only was the temple itself excellent, but it was possible to climb up to a terrace for the best views that we have had since getting here. With the sun behind us, it was possible to view the whole Bagan area and see nearly all the temples we had been to in the last few days.

By the time we left here, Roz was feeling ill and we had to stop after about 20 minutes as she was feeling faint. When she eventually got back on her bike, it was a very slow journey back to our hotel, not helped by a problem with her bike that we were not aware of at the time. Back at the hotel, she went straight to sleep. After I had eaten, it was my turn to read on the balcony while she appeared to be suffering from whatever I had two days ago.

Roz had recovered sufficiently by 5'clock to cycle out to another temple for sunset. We went out to Shwesandaw Paya, one that we had visited two days ago. It is one of the most famous sunset temples in the area and was therefore crowded with loads of buses and taxis filling the car park. We managed to find a decent space to sit and once again had superb views of the setting sun. We were about the last people on the temple as everyone else seemed to disappear as soon the the sun went and we cycled back to Nyaung U in darkness again.

We ate out at Aroma 2 for the third night in a row and were back in the room by about 9.
