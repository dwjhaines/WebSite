dwjhaines - 2012 week 4

Monday, 18th June - Slept until 10:30 and met up for breakfast which was included in the price of the room. We checked out at midday and spent an hour or so wandering round the one or two sights of Poltava. It was another nice enough place again without having anything too spectacular to see.

We were back at the hotel by two, collected the car and started driving towards Donetsk. We had booked a hotel for two nights in Yenakievo, about 50km N.E. of Donetsk. The drive took about six hours. This time, we experienced pot-holed roads that were every bit as bad as we had been led to believe! We had decided to let the sat-nav choose the best route and it chose a direct route between two main roads that involved two hours of very rough roads. It was not too bad for driving on but there were a lot of holes that would do serious damage to the car if you hit them at any speed. We will probably choose a different route back! I got stopped for speeding for the second time in two days (84km/h in a 60km/h zone) and went through exactly the same thing as yesterday. After telling me that I would get a ticket, they could not be bothered with the paperwork and told us to go. Lonely Planet had suggested that if you stand around talking to them in English and they do not get their bribe, they will get bored and send you away and this does seem to be the case.

Our sat-nav did not have any details of Yenakievo so we had to find the hotel ourselves. This involved getting directions in Russian a couple of times but people were very helpful and we got there eventually at about 8:30. First impressions of Yenakievo were not good. It is a very industrial town and looked about as appealing as Port Talbot. The hotel was equally uninviting and grim looking, and Steve was quite keen to leave as soon as possible after being shown to our rooms. However, once again, it is clean enough and will do for a couple of nights. We will not be spending that long in it as we will be in Donetsk all day tomorrow and will leave first thing on Wednesday.

We had a quick look around the town before returning to the cafe attached to the hotel. There were three other England fans in there (they have been in Yenakievo for a week!) and we watched the Spain v Croatia game in there with them. The food was surprisingly good and after a few beers, Yenakievo seemed a much better place!

Tuesday, 19th June - England v Ukraine - We met up with Steve at 10 and bought breakfast from a supermarket. We ate it in the grounds of the church with lovely views of the steelworks. We had decided not to take the car into Donetsk as it would mean one of us not drinking so we got the hotel to call us a taxi. It was a 50km journey for about £16.

We were dropped in the centre of town and walked out to Pushkin Ave. where most of the bars are and spent a few hours back in the place we spent a lot of time when we were here last week. We met up with Dave and Nick again as well as a couple more of Steve's mates and spent the rest of the afternoon wandering round a few different bars, trying not to drink too quickly due to the late kick-off again. Despite all the reports of potential for crowd trouble, the atmosphere everywhere was pretty good. There were no-where near as many Russians around as there had been for the first game and the Ukrainians seemed much friendlier.

The three of us left for the ground about an hour before kick-off and got in without any problems. Another enjoyable game saw us comfortably win our group and make it into the quarter finals. We were desperate for England to win the group as coming second would mean a quarter final in Donetsk and a possible semi in Donetsk as well. We have got to quite like Donetsk but the lack of decent accommodation is a real problem. Winning the group means our quarter final is back in Kiev. This probably means about 10 hours less driving and means that we have good accommodation booked for the rest of our time in Ukraine - none of us were particularly keen on having to stay at Hotel Mir again! It also means Mark will fly back out - he would not of come if the quarter final had been in Donetsk.

After the game, we got a taxi back to Yenakievo (£28 this time) and were back at the hotel just after 1am.

Wednesday, 20th June - We had breakfast in the hotel cafe before checking out at about 11 and starting to drive towards Dnipropetrovsk. We had chosen to stay here for a couple of nights as it is in between Donetsk and Kiev and would leave us quite well placed whichever of the quarter finals we had ended up in (or if we had been knocked out!). The journey was not too eventful. We had one stop for petrol and one stop by the traffic police. This time it was not for speeding but a standard documents check. Once they realised that we were here for the football they wished us good luck and told us to go.

We arrived in Dnipro at about 3:30 and then spent about 45 minutes trying to get to the apartment. We got to the right address but the person on the end of the contact phone number spoke no English and it took a while to find someone to let us in. Once in, the apartment was again excellent. It is more modern than the one in Kiev, has superb views of the river and is a world away from last night's accommodation. We did not do a lot for the rest of the afternoon but started looking into options for Warsaw in case we make it to the semi-final. Went out in the evening for a few beers and something to eat and were back in the apartment by about 10:00. This was the first day of the tournament with no football to watch.

Thursday, 21st June - A day with nothing to do, which was quite nice after the  last week or so. We spent a lot of the morning trying to get our electric sorted out. Everything went off when the bathroom lights were switched on. This was down to a faulty light-bulb but it took six Ukrainians (including at least two electricians, the owners of the flat, and a student translator) about three hours to get the bulb removed and the electric switched back on. How many Ukrainians does it take to change a light bulb? At least six apparently.

We went out for a couple of hours to see the sights of Dnipro. Two hours was more than enough! It is another pleasant place and it is OK to walk around but there is nothing of any great interest to go and see. Spent most of the day making the most of the apartment. It had a washing machine so we could get all our washing done and we continued to look at options for Warsaw and also for later in our trip. Went out to a local bar in the evening and had a few beers while watching the first quarter final.

Friday, 22nd June - Left the apartment at 8:30 for the drive back to Kiev. An uneventful journey, we had no stops for speeding or document checks. We had no idea which route would be the best but the journey took just under 7 hours so probably could not of done it a lot quicker if we had gone another way. The sat-nav seemed very confused at the start of the trip due to new roads that it did not recognise but once in Kiev, it got us back to the apartment very easily. We had booked a different apartment near to the last one that we had here. It is not as nice and is smaller but it is fine for a couple of days and is US$100 a night cheaper. Mark does not arrive until 1:30 on Sunday morning so we do not need as much space anyway.

Did not do a lot else for the rest of the day. Roz was not feeling that well by the time we got to Kiev and Steve and I were both knackered. We have had a lot of days with either too much driving or too much beer and have not been getting enough sleep We went out to a restaurant just round the corner to eat and returned to the apartment to watch the Germany v Greece game. My second alcohol free day of the tournament.

Saturday, 23rd June - All woke feeling better after a good nights sleep. We left the apartment at about 11 and went off to collect our tickets for tomorrow's game. We got the metro to the main square and walked out to the Dynamo Kiev Stadium collecting our Free Lions on the way. There were no queues at the collection point but it was predictably farcical. They photocopied our membership cards which is going to cause long delays tomorrow when lots of people turn up at the same time, and when they did give us our tickets, none of us were sat together. We did complain to the UEFA staff but they were not interested. We also spoke to the two FA officials who were there and they were not happy with UEFA. We were given the option of returning our tickets as they were about to close the collection point and try and reprint the tickets but we decided to stick with what we had. We assume that as with previous games, we will be able to sort it out when we get in the ground. Apparently the FA have only sold 2,000 tickets for this game.

When we left the collection point, we spent 10 - 15 minutes wandering round the Kiev Stadium which was surprisingly small. After taking our photos, we walked out to the Chernobyl museum, about a mile away. There was hardly any information in English for most of the exhibits but it was interesting enough to wander round for an hour or so. This was the last sight we had any interest in seeing in Kiev so we walked from the back to the centre, stopping for lunch on the way. Roz and Steve went back into the fan park so Roz could pick up another programme and we then went back to Kupidon for a drink before walking back to the apartment. By the time we got back to the apartment, we had time for quick showers before heading back to Kupidon for tea and to watch Spain v France. Not too many fans around from either country and  the bar was a lot emptier than it had been during the group stages. We got the metro back to the apartment after the game and sat around waiting for Mark to arrive. Despite an emergency stop in Brussels due to another passenger having a heart attack, he arrived on time and was at the apartment just after 2am.

Sunday, 24th June - England v Italy - We checked out of the apartment just after 11. Steve, Roz and I were booked into a hotel near the airport for tonight as we all fly home tomorrow. Mark is flying at 5:30 am so will go straight to the airport after the game. Roz and I took all the luggage out to the airport hotel while Mark and Steve went to collect Mark's ticket. We got out to the hotel without any problems and checked into two rooms before driving back to the airport. The plan was to return the hire car to the airport and then get the bus back into Kiev. However, there was no-one to meet us at the airport and, after a phone call to the office, we were told to take the car back into Kiev. This did not work out too badly for us, as it was a cheaper and quicker way of getting back to the centre of  Kiev. We met the manager of the hire company at the metro station near where we had been staying (the office was in the part of Kiev closed off for the football) and by 1:40 were on the metro.

We got to Kupidon at 2:00 where we met up with Mark and Steve again. We stayed there for a few hours and were joined by Fred and Southend Steve who we know from previous tournaments. At 6ish, we walked from there back to the same place we had been before the Sweden game. Whereas before the Sweden game, we had struggled to get a table, this time we were the only fans in there. There did not seem to be many fans around from either England or Italy as we we went towards the ground and the atmosphere generally before the game was very quiet. After eating, we stopped at one more bar on the way to the ground and were in our seats by 9:30 for another 9:45 kick-off.

The game itself was fairly predictable I suppose. A 0 - 0 draw followed by England going out on penalties. We had low expectations at the start of the tournament and did not think we would be that bothered about how England did. Two weeks ago, the prospect of an early exit and a quick return to Germany seemed quite appealing. Despite this, going out in the quarter finals on penalties felt just as painful as it has done in the past. The tournament is certainly not the best we have been to but it was a lot better than we had expected and getting to a semi-final in Warsaw against the Germans would of been excellent. The fact that we were ahead and then  messed it up so quickly seemed to make it even worse.

We all met up after the game in the same bar we had been in after the Sweden game but no-one was that enthusiastic about drinking. We had a couple of beers and got a taxi out to the airport at about 3:15 (the game had not finished until well after midnight). We dropped Mark at the airport and got the taxi to take us to our hotel about 3 miles away. We said goodbye to Steve who was due to leave the hotel again at 7:30 and finally got to bed just after 4.
