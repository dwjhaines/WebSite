Monday, 3rd Oct We went to the beach for about 3 hours.

Tuesday, 4th Oct We spent the morning back at the Olympic swimming pool. In the afternoon we went shopping. Dave, Roz and Gary bought t-shirts, I got a new pair of sun-glasses to replace the ones that Gary trod on this morning (which were only boght in Quito on Monday!) and Dave & Gary also bought hammocks for their expedition around the Darién Gap (Roz and I are flying from Cartagena.

Wednesday, 5th Oct We got a taxi out to the bus station and caught the 9:30 bus to Cartagena. The journey took just over four hours. At Cartagena, we got another taxi into the town and checked into a hotel.

After showers, we went out for a walk mainly to look for a supermarket. We bought four and a half bottles of rum and a load of bread rolls, ham, cheese, etc.

In the evening we sat in the hotel garden eating and drinking. Despite buying all the rum, no-one was really that enthusiastic about the session and it eventually died out at about 2 with no-one too pissed.

Thursday, 6th Oct We got up at 10ish and finished off last nights food for breakfast.

Roz and I went off to look at beaches and other hotels as we plan to stay here for about a week while the others try aand get rounnd the Darién Gap. Boca Grande had a reasonable beach, some very expensive hotels and a couple that were within our price range. We returned to the hotel where we spent the afternoon lazing around and reading.

In the evening, after another meal of sandwiches, Roz and I went for a walk around the old town which was very plesant and scenic.

Friday, 7th Oct After breakfast, Roz, Gary and I spent about an hour changing travelers cheques. Roz and I then checked out of the hotel and moved to another at Boca Grande. Our new hotel cost about twice as much as the last one but it is a much nicer and cooler room, has a private bathroom and is just across the road from the beach. The resort of Boca Grande is just like a lot of Spanish resorts, two roads lined with restaurants, bars, hotels and souvenir shops. Hardly the authentic Colombia but very enjoyable for a few days.

We spent most of the afternoon walking around the shops and travel agents. We managed to find a decent second hand book shop for the first time in South America and also booked our flights to Panama for Monday costing us about $140 each. The rest of the time was spent lying in the room reading as it was too cloudy to go to the beach.

In the evening, Dave and Gary came down to Boca Grande and we went out for a meal at a nearby restaurant. We then said our goodbyes as we will not be seeing them for at least a week and perhaps longer depending on how quickly they get round the Darién Gap.

Saturday, 8th Oct The day was mostly cloudy but we still managed to get quite burnt by spending 4 - 5 hours on the beach.

In the evening we got drunk on the left-overs from Wednesday night's session. We started drinking in the room, had a half bottle of rum in a restaurant and finished off by taking another bottle down to the beach. There seemed to be more people on the beach than there had been during the day. I don't remember leaving the beach or going to bed.

Sunday, 9th Oct I woke up with a slight hangover which didn't really go away all day. We managed to go to the beach for a couple of hours in the afternoon but didn't do a lot else all day. In the evening we went for a pizza.
