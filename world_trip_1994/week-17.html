Monday, 14th Nov At 9am, we walked to the post office to send the letter and cards that we couldn't post in Honduras because of a postal strike. We changed money and had breakfast on the way back. Breakfast consisted of a couple of pupusas each (tortillas filled with eggs,, beans, or cheese) costing about 2 colones each. We walked back to the hotel, packed our bags and then walked to the bus stop for buses to La Libertad. The jorney took about an hour and we arrived at La Libertad at about midday. After looking at a few hotels, we eventually found quite good accomodation for about $3 each.

We were hoping to spend a couple of weeks in La Libertad to make up for Tela. Here the weather is exellent. However, the "black sandy beach" described in the book does not seem to exist. What beach there is, is stoney and totally unsuitable for sun-bathing. No-one felt like doing a lot so we decided to wait until tomorrow and then go and look at the places a few km along the coast before deciding how long to stay here.

We had more pupusas for lunch and then Gary and I went for haircuts which seemed to take up most of the afternoon.
