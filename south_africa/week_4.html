dwjhaines - south africa - week 4

Day 22 - Friday, 2nd July A much better day than yesterday. There was a lot of rain during the night but it had stopped by the time we left to go to the beach. By the time we left for the first dive, the sun was starting to shine and the cloud finally began to clear. The conditions had improved sufficiently for us to be able to get out to Nine Mile Reef which was a better dive site than yesterday's. The journeys to and from the dive site were eventful with dolphins and a manta being spotted on the way out. On the return, a whale shark was spotted and we were able to get off the boat and snorkel with it for about five minutes. It was a small whale shark, similar in size to the one we have seen in Thailand but an incredible experience nonetheless. The second dive was also good with plenty to see.

We were back in our room to watch the Holland v Brazil game but did not do much for the rest of the day. We had a few drinks before eating in the lodge restaurant again. We returned to the room for the Uruguay v Ghana game but we could hardly stay awake during the first 90 minutes and both fell asleep before extra time had started.

Day 23 - Saturday, 3rd July Another good couple of dives to end our visit to Sodwana Bay. We also got to snorkel with dolphins on the way out to the first dive site. The diving has been different here to Aliwal Shoal and it has been well worth coming up for a couple of days.

By the time we had completed the second dive and collected our luggage from the lodge, it was gone 3:30. The drive back to Scottburgh was easy enough and we were back at the police station just before 8 to collect the keys to the house. Unfortunately, the keys were not there as there had been some confusion and the cleaner had picked them up and taken them home. This took about 30 minutes to resolve so we did not get in until 8:30. We were both pretty knackered but made the effort to go out to the French restaurant again. Another very good meal and we made it home in time for the last 15 minutes of the Spain v Paraguay game.

Day 24 - Sunday, 4th July Back to Oceanworx for two more dives. The first was a a wreck we have not dived before. It was a relatively challenging dive. The wreck is in about 30m to 20m, the light was not good and the currents were fairly strong. It was still a good dive and something I would happily dive again. The second dive was more like the usual dives in this area - three turtles, at least three raggies and plenty of other fish. We had finished by midday as they start early at weekends so had the rest of the day lazing around the house. We caught up with washing, logbook writing etc but did not do a lot else. It was good to have some time not doing anything after the last couple of days.

We went out for a curry in the evening and were back in the house finishing the remaining wine when Mark, Dave and Fred returned at 10 (Steve was by now on his way home to England). We spent about an hour discussing our respective trips and plans for the next week before heading to bed just after 11.

Day 25 - Monday, 5th July Another day of diving - this time with Mark. He had dived in the aquarium on their trip to Cape Town but these were his first dives in the sea. The first dive was not wonderful - we missed the site, the vis was poor and the current relatively strong. The second was a lot better with good views of raggies and Mark managed to find a turtle!

Back "home" just after 1 and another fairly lazy afternoon. Plenty of discussions about the plans for the next couple of days. Mark has tried unsuccessfully to change his flight home to tomorrow, Fred tried unsuccessfully to buy tickets for the semi final in Durban on Wednesday. Roz and I will definitely dive for another two days and may try and go to the semi. The discovery of the fact that Croc World has meerkats and tortoises to look at means that this has been added to our itinerary for tomorrow.

We ate at The Tin Roof for the first time in a while, and returned to the house to watch Dave's shark cage DVD's!

Day 26 - Tuesday, 6th July Roz and I were driven to the dive centre by Fred as Mark was not diving and Fred wanted the car to go into Durban. The two dives today were excellent despite poor vis and strong currents. We somehow managed to dive the same site twice - Northern Pinnacles. The first dive was  very good with four or five turtles, more raggies and an eagle ray that swam right beneath me - too close to get in a photo. The second was similar with three more turtles and about the same number of raggies. At the end of the second dive, as we were getting low on bottom time, we drifted away from the reef towards the area where a shark dive had been taking place. Dick (our guide for the last time) spent ten minutes or so trying to attract sharks to us. Not much happened for a while but by the end of the dive, we had six or seven oceanic black tips swimming around us - a definite highlight of the diving here.

After we had cleaned our gear, we rang Mark to come and collect us. We drove directly from Umkomaas to Croc World which is on the way back to Scottburgh. The crocodiles were more impressive than I thought they would be and there were also a lot more of them than I had expected. There were loads of them in several pens covering quite a large area. We seemed very close to them despite being separated by fences. They were far bigger than the ones we had seen in the game reserve and all looked quite unreal. The highlight of the park, however was the meerkats. We spent more time looking at them than the crocodiles and taking pictures and generally commenting on their cuteness. Next to the meerkats was a tortoise enclosure which kept Roz amused for another twenty minutes or so! By the time we had walked round everything and spent some time buying meerkat t-shirts, it was getting on for about 3:30. We returned to the house for a late lunch and another afternoon of not doing a lot.

Mark drove us to the "Sports Club" to eat and watch the first semi-final and we were back home and in bed not long after it finished.

Day 27 - Wednesday, 7th July Our last day of diving before flying home. The dives were OK but no-where near as good as yesterday. The weather was poor - windy and no sun - so the conditions were about the worse we have had here. This was a bit unfortunate for Mark, who did not dive yesterday but and was ill for most of the trip today. It was a two tank trip but he only did the first dive so spent about four hours on the boat for less than 40 minutes underwater. Despite the dives not being wonderful, the second dive ended with a large turtle swimming up from where it had been woken so a good way to end our diving.

We were back at the house by just after 2:00 and dithered about a bit deciding what to do for the rest of the day. Fred and Dave were definitely going in to Durban to try and get tickets for the semi-final - the rest of us were undecided. We had planned to go in and see if we could get cheap tickets close to kick-off but by the time we were back from diving I could not really be bothered. Everyone else was keen however, so after a quick shower, we all headed into Durban. We took both cars as it was always likely that three of us would be coming back to watch the game in Scottburgh.

It took about an hou>r to get into Durban and park the car. We walked to the beach area and had a late lunch at an excellent Indian restaurant. We then spent an hour or so walking towards the ground, taking photos and asking about ticket prices. The ground itself did look very impressive and the atmosphere was pretty good. However, the vuvuzelas were still annoying and the whole thing was a bit of a depressing reminder of what it could of been like if we had not played so badly. There were plenty of tickets around, mostly Cat 1 tickets going for R2000 (about £180). Myself, Roz and Mark were not interested in tickets at this price but by the time we had reached the ground Fred and Dave managed to get two Cat 2 tickets for R3500. Just after 6, the three of us without tickets gave up and started walking back to the car. We would probably have got cheaper tickets if we had waited longer but we did not want to end up in Durban at kick-off time without tickets so were all just as happy to go back and watch it The Tin Roof.

We made it back to Scottburgh by 8:15 and were in the pub by kick-off time at 8:30. It turned into quite a good night. We stayed in the pub until the game finished and returned to the house to finish the remaining wine. Fred and Dave were back before 12 and I vaguely remember getting to bed at around 2am.

Day 28 - Thursday, 8th July We were up reasonably early considering what time we went to bed. We had to leave Scottburgh today to drive back to Johannesburg for flights home. We left the house at 9:30. It had been a superb place to stay and had been a very good choice. It was quite sad to be finally leaving.

We drove via Umkomaas and called into Ocenworx to pay the remainder of our diving bill with the cash we had saved by not going to the football (in fact, most of our diving has been paid for with money that we have saved by not going to football!). We were on our way from the dive centre by 10. Again the journey was uneventful. We stopped at the same service station we had visited on our way down, three and a half weeks ago for something to eat and got to the airport by 5. We filled the cars with petrol and returned them to Budget. We then seemed to spend a couple of hours standing in queues waiting to get things sorted out. There were a few problems in the car hire office, we had to queue to check in and there were three different places to visit to claim the VAT refund on the things we had bought in South Africa. Even buying duty free seemed to be an incredibly long and drawn out process. We said our goodbyes to the others - Mark and Dave were going home via Zurich, Fred flying back to the States - and went to the gate for our flight to Madrid. This eventually left about 45 minutes late at 9:10 by which time we were both totally knackered. The drive had not been too bad but sorting everything out at the airport seemed to take it out of us and we could not even be bothered to go and get anything to eat or drink.

We had a few bottles of wine on the plane as usual and got to sleep by about 1am.

Day 29 - Friday, 9th July We landed in Madrid just after 7 and had got the metro into the centre of the city by 9. Luckily, the hotel we had booked was next to the metro station exit (my dive bag is not that easy to carry) and, better still, they had a room available immediately. We had much needed showers and headed off for a walk.

We stopped for breakfast and walked to Plaza Del Sol which is only about five minutes away. We did not really have any sights that we wanted to see but spent most of our time looking for a bar that we had drank in when we were here for the England friendly a few years ago. When we finally located it, we returned to the hotel and spent an hour or so by the pool. We had lunch at a tapas bar round the corner from the hotel before a three hour siesta to catch up on much needed sleep.

We had a very good evening out in Madrid. It started with the discovery that we had been upgraded to a "Club Emporador" room that entitled us to unlimited free beer! We had access to a lounge which had a self service beer tap and free nibbles. We stayed in there for a few drinks before starting a pub crawl around the bars of Madrid. We managed to visit about eight different bars and got back to the hotel sometime after 2:00. A very enjoyable last day of the holiday.
