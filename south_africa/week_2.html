dwjhaines - south africa - week 2

Day 8 - Friday, 18th June

We were up for breakfast at 8 and had ordered a taxi to take us to Table Mountain at 8:15. We were incredibly lucky with the weather for our stay in Cape Town. The forecast we had seen showed clear, sunny weather for today, tomorrow and Sunday with cloud and rain on the days either side. As today was clear, we wanted  to get up Table Mountain as soon as possible so we could experience it without the thick cloud that covers it for most of the winter. We were dropped at the lower cable car station just after 8:30 and after queuing for about 10 minutes, bought our tickets and caught the cable car up to the top. The ride up to the top in a cable car complete with rotating floor was very spectacular - the views of Cape Town from the top were even better. We stayed at the top for and hour or so taking countless pictures of the city below (particularly of the stadium) and the views South towards the Cape of Good Hope. It was a bit windy up there but we really could not of asked for a much better day to be there as we had completely unobstructed 360 views. The whole thing was very high up in the list of best sights we have ever been to.

We got a taxi back to the guest house by midday and headed off to the Cape Town Aquarium shortly afterwards. Roz and I had booked a dive in the predator tank with sharks rays and a turtle. I had not been over keen on this idea but it was another very enjoyable experience. We were given a short dive brief by Iain, the Scottish dive guide and were then led through the aquarium to the area at the top of the tank. We geared up with two other customers before climbing into the top of the tank. We all dropped down to the floor of the tank and spent 35 minutes watching the sharks and rays swimming above us. I had a very close encounter with the resident turtle and another with a large feather-tail ray. We were also able to get very good close up photos of the raggies swimming overhead. While we were doing this we had Mark, Steve and Dave watching from the viewing area outside the tank.

The others had left the aquarium by the time we had finished. We went back to the guest house to get rid of our diving stuff and then returned to the waterfront for a late lunch and a few beers. We ate at a South African restaurant with superb views of Table Mountain and then headed to the Paulaner Haus for a few more wheat beers.

We started heading to the ground at 7ish and, despite the organisation not being too clever, we got into the ground by 8 in time for the 8:30 kick-off. The stadium was very impressive and up to this point, it had been an excellent day. However, the football was awful, probably the worst performance I have seen from England in a competitive game. It was hard to believe just how bad we were capable of playing and leaving the ground, it felt as if we had just been knocked out. We are not out of the tournament yet but it is going to take a huge improvement if we are going to get much further. At least we may not have to go back to Rustenburg.

We had a couple more beers in the bar near the guest house after the game but we were not really in the mood for drinking and we were back in bed not long after midnight. All in all, a superb day out despite the football!

Day 9 - Saturday, 19th June

The remaining Cape Town attraction that we were keen on was the penguins at Boulders. There were a few options for us to get there but we eventually decided to get a taxi for the whole day. This worked out at R1500 which was less than £30 each between the five of us. Mark was keen on the train option so went on ahead. We arranged to pick him up at Simons Town. The taxi picked the rest of us up just after 10.

The journey down to Boulder was pretty scenic so we had plenty of photo stops en-route. Mark's train journey was not as long as expected as there were engineering works and he had to complete the trip using a replacement bus service! We were all dropped off at Boulder just after 11 and headed off to see the penguins. This was another attraction that was well worth the visit. The viewing area consisted of a couple of boardwalks leading away from the visitor centre to the beach. As soon as we started walking on the boardwalks, we could see penguins. They were all over the place and there were plenty of females with their young. We spent about an hour taking photos and generally commenting about how cute they were. We all ended up buying stuff from the souvenir shop before heading off again. We had timed it very well as bus loads of Brazilians were arriving and filling the place up as we were leaving.

The taxi took us from Boulder down the coast to the Cape of Good Hope Nature Reserve.

This was superb again and the views were a lot more impressive than I had expected. We were dropped in the car park at Cape Point and set off to walk up to the lighthouse. A few minutes into the walk, Mark had his bag snatched by a Baboon. His shout of "there's nothing in it" did not seem to have any effect! I managed to get the bag back by standing on it at which point the baboon gave up but all very entertaining nonetheless. After admiring the views from the lighthouse, we headed along the trail to the Cape of Good Hope. It was a very scenic walk and got us away from the majority of the tourists. Dave's was struggling with his ankle a bit but other than that it was an easy enough walk and it took us about an hour to get along the trail to where the taxi was waiting to pick us up. The drive back to Green Point took us along the West Coast of the Cape which again was very spectacular. We were back at the guest house by 6 and it had been another excellent day. Cape Town has been a brilliant city to visit and the fact that we have had good weather was incredibly lucky.

Roz and I went straight out to the Paulaner Haus for a couple of beers. We were joined by the others and spent the remainder of the evening in a Belgium restaurant. We had a very good meal and several Belgium beers - a very good end to our stay here.

Day 10 - Sunday, 20th June

We were collected by a taxi just before 9 and taken to the airport for the 10:35 flight back to Durban. Since the start of the trip, we have been taking photos of Lee being held by fans of the different countries playing in the world cup. Cape Town has been particularly good for this game as there have been a lot of fans around from different countries. At the airport, we reached the half way point. A picture of him with a Brazilian fan meant that we have pictures of him with fans of half the countries taking part. Sixteen more to go although some could prove a bit more difficult as teams start to get knocked out.

The flight back was quite scenic and I had excellent views of Cape Town as we left. We touched down in Port Elizabeth on the way but were back in Durban by 2ish. We were in Scottburgh an hour or so later. Roz had been talking to Karen at the dive centre about whether or not Dave would be able to do a DSD tomorrow. At about 3:30, she asked us if we wanted to do a night dive as the conditions were very good. Roz was keen, so an hour later, we headed out again. The dive was OK but quite different to other night dives we have done. It was similar to the day dives we have done here - several raggies and a turtle - but there was not many of the things we usually see at night.

We did not go out to eat - the others had bought steak and chips to cook when we got back - we stayed in the house and watched the football.

Day 11 - Monday, 21st June We were all up early and headed of to the dive centre at 6:45. Mark was finishing his open water course, Steve was doing a DSD and Dave was planning to go out and snorkel as he had still not managed to find out if he could dive on the blood pressure tablets he was taking. At the dive centre he was told by a doctor that he could do the pool session of the DSD but should not actually dive in the sea. He was happy with this and joined Steve to get ready for the pool as we headed out on the boat

We had two good dives again - plenty of raggies and we maintained our record of seeing turtles on every dive here. Steve joined us on the second dive during which Mark completed his open water course.

We all returned to Scottburgh and did not do a lot for the rest of the day. The football was on the tele but I don't think any of us managed to stay awake through both of the afternoon games. We ate at the Tin Roof again in the evening and had a few drinks more than usual but were still in bed before 12.

Day 12 - Tuesday, 22nd June Mark, Steve and Dave had booked flights to Port Elizabeth where we play our third group game tomorrow. They departed for the airport at about 7:30. We had decided to drive and left just before 9. The drive was 920km in total. We had arranged to pick up Fred at East London airport about two thirds of the way along. We had a half hour lunch stop and arrived at East London by 5. We were able to watch the second half of the South Africa v France game in the airport Wimpy before Fred arrived at about 6:30. We added Fred's name to the car hire agreement and continued to Port Elizabeth. By 10 we had checked into Park Place Hotel. We could not be bothered to go out anywhere after the day in the car so were in bed by 11.

Day 13 - Wednesday, 23rd June

Mark, Steve and Dave had booked a morning trip to Addo Elephant National Park. Roz, Fred and I are driving there tomorrow so were able to spend the morning at the beach front area of Port Elizabeth. We got a taxi down to the beach and spent an hour or so walking down to the Boardwalk area. This area was a packed with shops and restaurants. It had a bit of a Disney type feel to it all but was full of football fans - mainly English - and had a pretty good atmosphere. We sat around in the sun and had a couple of beers and after getting something to eat headed towards the ground at about 1:30. We got a taxi to the stadium and after another couple of beers in a bar just round the corner from the ground, were in the ground by 3:00, an hour before kick-off. We met up with the others briefly before splitting up again as our seats were all in different parts of the ground.

The game was not wonderful but was a big improvement on the previous performance and given what was at stake, was pretty exciting. The win meant that we had qualified and the news from the USA game as we left the ground meant that we had finished second in the group and would be going to Bloemfontein and not Rustenburg. This was the perfect set of results for all of us (except Fred who has a ticket for the Rustenburg game but not the one in Bloemfontein).

We got a shuttle bus back to the area where our hotel is and all got a lift back to the hotel with the owner who happened to be on the same bus. After a bit of time looking into Bloemfontein accommodation, we went out to a B.Y.O. Thai restaurant where we had an excellent meal with a few beers and a box of wine. The scores from the other games tonight mean that the game in Bloemfontein will be against Germany!

Day 14 - Thursday, 24th June Fred, Roz and I departed PE just after 8 and headed up to Addo Elephant National Park. We got to our guest house in Addo by 9:30 and at 10 we were picked up by one of the guides. We were driven to the park entrance where we transferred to another tour that had come up from PE. The first game drive was in a minibus with 11 others plus guide and driver. The drive was very good although the minibus was not the ideal vehicle for such things. There was plenty to see, including various antelope, warthogs, a dung beetle and a leopard tortoise. We also had very good views of the elephants.

We were driven from Addo to Schotia Game reserve where we had lunch before being taken on our second drive of the day. This drive was better as there were only five of us in an open back land rover. The views were much better and it was more similar to the previous safaris we had done. Once again, there was plenty to see.

We came across four lions almost as soon as we entered the reserve and were able to view them from about 10m away. They were not doing a lot but an incredible experience nonetheless. Plenty more to see including giraffes, a close encounter with a couple of crocodiles and a couple of white rhino. Stunning scenery throughout both the days drives and a highly enjoyable day. We were given our evening meal in a camp type thing in the reserve before being driven back to Addo by the woman who had picked us up in the morning.
