dwjhaines - south africa - week 3

Day 15 - Friday, 25th June We had another early start as we had booked a sunrise drive at Addo. We were at the visitor centre by 6:45 and started the drive at 7. We were in a large vehicle with about 20 other people but it was open sided so again was a lot better than the minibus. The drive was excellent again - lots of elephants and a large herd of of buffalo at a water hole being the highlights. We were back at the guest house by 9:30 for showers and a large breakfast before starting our journey towards Bloemfontein.

We had booked a nights accommodation at Aliwal North which was about half way. After a bit of indecision we stopped off at Mountain Zebra National Park and did another two hour drive, this time in our own car. Not as much to see here but there were plenty of zebras and the scenery was again very spectacular so it was a worthwhile excursion. We left there at 4ish and continued to Aliwal North where we arrived just after 7. We dropped our bags at the guest house and went straight out to eat at a bar/restaurant. We had two huge pizzas between the three of us (one would of been enough!), watched the Spain v Chile game and went back for an early night.

Day 16 - Saturday, 26th June We had plenty of time to get to Bloemfontein so had a slight detour to visit Lesotho on the way. This was not such a worthwhile excursion as the most notable thing about the country seemed to be the way that there road-works were organised. We had a trip to the ruins of a mountain fortress, but none of us could be bothered to actually walk up to it when we found it, and headed back into South Africa without stopping anywhere else. If it was not for the fact that we got additional stamps in our passports, it would of been a complete waste of time.

We got to Bloemfontein by six and were joined by the others about 30 minutes later. Due to the number of England fans here (and the Germans being able to book rooms before us) we had not been able to get a room for two nights here. We had a B + B for tonight in the centre of town and somewhere else about 6 miles away for tomorrow. This was a bit of a shame as tonight's place was about 15 minutes walk from the ground and would of been ideal for tomorrow! It was also convenient for bars and restaurants - all within walking distance. We spent most of the evening in Barba's watching USA get knocked out and then stopped for a couple in Die Kellar before walking home.

Day 17 - Sunday, 27th June It was Mark's birthday so after breakfast, he was presented with the cuddly baboon that we bought back from Cape Town. We checked out of the B + B at about 9:30 and Mark, Roz & I drove the two cars out to tonight's accommodation. We checked into three rooms, left all the bags and waited around for a taxi to take us back into town. We got the taxi to the Waterfront area and met up with the others who had got a table outside one of the cafes. The whole area was packed with football fans and the bars were not able to cope. It was difficult to get a beer from anywhere and was not an ideal place to be so after one drink, we walked back to Barba's. The atmosphere here was much better and they were able to cope with the amount of beer that English fans wanted to drink. We stayed there for an hour or so before walking to the ground. We got in without any problems and had another beer before heading to our seats. Up until this point, the day had been really good. We had been very lucky with the weather again and the whole build up to the game had been very enjoyable.

Once again, the game was the worst part of an otherwise very good day. We looked certain to lose after letting in the second goal but were very unlucky not to level the score. We were at the wrong end to see if the ball had crossed the line but as soon as the goal was not given, everyone around us started receiving texts from home confirming that the ball had crossed the line. Texts from Jens (or Nils) in Germany  apologising as well as one from Cathy, made it 100% certain that the goal should of been given. Whether or not it would of made any difference we will never know but it probably denied us the only chance we were going to get of getting back into the game.

The exit was not as painful as in previous tournaments. The Algeria game had been the low point of this World Cup. I think we all realised then that we were not going to achieve anything out here. The fact that we would all of preferred to come second in our group was an indication that none of us thought that we were going to go much further and we were all happy to get the chance of seeing one decent game against the Germans before we went out. Seeing us get beaten in Rustenburg by Ghana would have been a lot worse.

Barba's was charging to get in after the game so we went directly to Die Kellar where we had a table booked for later on anyway. Despite the disappointment, we had a good night in there with a good meal and plenty of alcohol. We left there at about 11 and got taxis back to the hotel.

Day 18 - Monday, 28th June We were up at 7 as Mark and Steve wanted an early start to get back to Durban for the Holland v Slovakia game. They departed after breakfast while we went back to bed for an hour or so. We had planned to leave at 9:30 but Dave's visit to "The Cheetah Experience" meant that our departure got delayed until 10:40. It was an uneventful journey back with me and Fred sharing the driving. Arrived back in Scottburgh just after 6 with Mark and Steve getting back not long after. This completed a round trip for us of more than 2,600 km since we left last Tuesday. None of us could be bothered to go out and eat so we got a takeaway from KFC and stayed in to watch Brazil v Chile (although inevitably, my interest in the football is not too high at the moment)

Day 19 - Tuesday, 29th June Roz, Mark and I were up early again as we had booked to go diving again. We were at the dive centre, in our wetsuits with our gear on the boat when the decision was made that the sea was too rough to launch the boat. This was disappointing especially for Mark as he thought that this was to be the only day he could dive before going home. It meant a day with not a lot to do which did not turn out too badly. We were able to sort out plans for the next few days, catch up with our e-mails and generally laze around doing nothing - something we have not had a lot of chance to do fro the last week or so. Mark trid to change his and Dave's flights so that they could go home early but could only move them a few days. They will now fly home on the same day as us a week on Thursday.

We had all booked flights in advance to Cape Town in case England got to the quarter final. Roz and I have decided to go to Sodwana Bay for  a few days diving instead and will probably head there tomorrow after diving. The others will still go to Cape Town (Steve, Fred and Dave all have tickets for Germany v Argentina) and will go to Imfolosi National Park for a couple of days tomorrow. We have just about decided to not bother with the sardine run after discussing this option with Karen at Oceanworx.

In the evening we all went to the French restaurant recommended by Dick, our dive guide at Ocenaworx. We had an excellent meal and a few bottles of wine and then went to The Tin Roof to watch Spain v Portugal. Ended up drinking more than planned and was quite pissed by the end of the evening.

Day 20 - Wednesday, 30th June Only Roz and I had planned to dive today but once again, it was an aborted trip. This time we got as far as the beach before the decision was made so another frustrating morning. We went back to the house and saw the others off when they departed for Imfolosi at 10 (This was the last we see of Steve as he flies home on Sunday). We left an hour or so later for the 4.5 hour drive to Sodwana Bay. We arrived at Da Blue Juice by about 4pm and were shown to our cabin. It seemed a nice enough place for a few days. We have a six person cabin to ourselves. It is fairly basic but has kitchen and bathroom and a really nice balcony area. After an hour or so of lounging around in hammocks, we had a few glasses of wine before going across the road for a pizza. We stayed in the restaurant after eating as they were showing the cricket from England and for the second night running, ended up drinking more than we had planned.

Day 21 - Thursday, 1st July We were woken at about 3 by heavy rain which had started coming through our roof in several places. We got a phone call at 7:30 to say the diving was in doubt but were called back after 8 to say that they were going ahead. We could not have breakfast as water in the kitchen area had caused the electricity to go off. We drove down to the beach and found Da Blue Juice. The weather was still terrible. There was no let up in the rain, the skies were totally grey and the wind was starting to pick up. This all meant that we would only be able to do one dive.

We dived on Two Mile Reef. The site was nice enough without being too spectacular - plenty of leaf scorpion fish and a few other reef fish we have not seen further South. Back on the beach we found out that our electric was unlikely to work for up to three days so Michelle booked us a room at the lodge next door. This did not work out too badly as this was a much nicer room and with the rain still coming down, we were to spend a lot of time in it. We had lunch in the lodge restaurant and spent the rest of the afternoon in the room not doing a lot. We have been very lucky with the weather so far on this trip so we were probably due a couple of bad days. We are planning on two more days up here but will head back to Scottburgh if things do not improve tomorrow.

The rain had stopped by early evening but we could not be bothered to go out to eat in case in started again. Ate at the lodge restaurant again and had a better meal than last night.
