dwjhaines - south africa - week 1

Day 0 - Thursday, 10th June Arrived at Heathrow at 4ish to be told that our 17:30 flight to Madrid was delayed by 40 minutes. We had about five hours to kill in Madrid so this was not a big problem. We went through security and found that real ale is available in Terminal 3 for the first time in about 15 years, so sat in the bar and waited. The flight continued to be delayed and eventually took off at about 20:00. Uneventful flight to Madrid. We got to the terminal in Madrid by 23:30 to find out that the onward flight to Johannesburg was also delayed, until 3:30. We were not overjoyed at this but after a few beers in the bar with plenty of other fans (mainly from USA, South and Central America) we had quite an enjoyable wait. The flight left at 3:30 and was also pretty good. We counted fans from 13 different countries and after a few more drinks, we got to sleep at about 6ish.

Day 1 - Friday, 11th June We were woken for breakfast at about 11:30 and eventually landed in Johannesburg at 1:30. Everything at the airport went remarkably smoothly. We had to collect our tickets for the first three games from ticket machines - we had read about people having to queue for up to an hour but the whole thing took about a minute. We activated our MTN SIM cards, took out money and collected our hire car. Within about an hour we were on our way out of the airport. We got to Idube Lodge without any problems, dumped our luggage in the room and went to meet Mark, Steve and Dave who had all arrived either yesterday or earlier today.

We arrived at a bar about 10 minutes from where we were staying just in time for kick-off in the first game of the tournament. There was a pretty good atmosphere in the bar with plenty of South Africans watching the game as well as a group of Australians from Idube and a few fans from other countries. The South Africans all seemed to be enjoying themselves but the vuvuzelas are already becoming irritating! After returning to Idube for showers, we watched the second game of the day in a restaurant before heading home. The area we are staying (Melville) seems safe enough and is OK for wandering around at night in a group of five but not the sort of place you would want to walk round on your own.

Day 2 - Saturday, 12th June We had breakfast in Idube, wandered out to a nearby shopping mall for a few things and started heading towards Rustenburg at about 1. There was a bit of indecision about the route to take but after advice from Trish, the owner of the lodge, we took the direct route instead of the main toll roads. This was probably not the best of choices but did not work out too badly. Certainly not a route you would want to take at night and we all agreed it would be the toll road after the game.

We got to Rustenburg at 3:30ish and after driving through the town, which looked pretty horrendous, we decided to go and park near the ground and spend the rest of the time there drinking the beer that we had bought with us. Unfortunately, FIFA had blocked the main access road to the ground for their officials meaning that the only option for getting to the ground appeared to be a park and ride. We returned to the town centre, ate at the KFC (this really was the best option available) and had a couple of our beers stood outside what appeared to be the only bar in town. It was next to a tombstone shop which seemed quite appropriate. There were a few England fans around but most seemed to have had the sense to avoid the town altogether.

At 5ish we headed to the park and ride which looked incredibly badly organised. It was a huge field about 10 miles from the ground with space for thousands of cars and two single track exits. Parking and getting on a bus was not too bad but we were all concerned about how long it was going to take to get out. Predictions for the time it would take to get out started at about four hours!

The atmosphere at the ground itself was pretty good. We had a couple more of our beers outside and then got into the ground relatively painlessly. Roz and I were in a different part of the ground to the others having paid $80 for our tickets instead of $160. There was no restrictions in the ground about where you could go so spent the hour before kick-off with them before heading to our seats (although we rejoined them for the second half as seats in front of them were empty)

Not too much to say about the game! At final whistle, we legged it around the ground and were incredibly lucky. We headed to the area for the park and ride buses and spotted a gap in a fence where people appeared to be jumping the queue. There was no official organisation at all but somehow the five of us managed to get on a bus that was already completely packed. Back at the car park, there was a similar level of organisation but we managed to find a path through the parked cars and were heading out of one of the exits about 35 minutes after the game had finished. We took the toll road back to Johannesburg and were back in Idube by 1:10, just as the local radio was reporting stories of people still being stuck in the car-park.

Day 3 - Sunday, 13th June We departed in two cars at 10ish heading for Durban. Steve and Dave were in one car as they were going to the Germany v Australia game while the rest of us were going directly to Scottburgh where we are based for the next four weeks. We had a few problems getting out of Johannesburg but once on the N3 to Durban, the 650km journey was relatively straightforward. We had one stop for lunch and arrived in Scottburgh at about 4:30. We collected the keys for our house and got in with no problems.

The house was excellent and probably better than we had hoped for. It was bigger and probably nicer than we had all been expecting. Our bedroom was huge, Mark claimed the other double leaving Dave and Steve to share the room with the bunk beds. The three of us were very happy! We had a quick walk around Scottburgh which also seemed ideal. It all seems a lot safer than Johannesburg, and has everything we could possibly need within easy walking distance. We bought a few provisions, went back for showers and then headed out to The Tin Roof Pub which will probably be our local for the next few weeks. Very cheap beer and they were happy to order takeaway pizzas as they were not serving food. We watched the game there before returning to the house and had an early night (apart from being woken at 12:10 when Steve and Dave returned from the game)

Day 4 - Monday, 14th June Roz, Mark and I were up at 6. We had to wake Dave up to let us out of the electronic gate and the three of us headed off to Umkomass and the Oceanworx dive centre. It was the same place we had dived from seven years ago but with new owners and a new name. We had booked two dives which for Mark were the first two dives of his open water course.

We got our gear together in the dive centre and were taken down to the beach from where the boat was launched. There were ten of us on the boat - Dick the dive guide, seven fun divers and Mark with his instructor. The two dives included a trip back to the beach to get new tanks. Both dives were pretty good. The vis was not wonderful - similar to what we remember from last time we were here - but there was loads to see. There were plenty of "raggies" around on both dives and a total of four turtles. The one at the start of the second dive were particularly high quality one. I also got a brief glimpse of a pod of dolphins which I have not seen on a dive before.

We were back at the dive centre by about 1 and had an hour or so wait while Mark had a session in the pool. Did not do too much for the rest of the day. We walked around Scottburgh again, had an early meal in The Tin Roof and returned to the house for the second half of Italy v Paraguay. Totally knackered so went straight to bed immediately after the game

Day 5 - Tuesday, 15th June A relatively quiet day. The three of us were up at 6 again to go diving but just as we were about to leave, we got a phone call saying that the weather was too bad and the trip was cancelled. This did not come as a big surprise as we had been woken at 4:30 strong winds and the sea did look pretty rough.

After a couple of hours back in bed, the rest of the morning was spent trying to get some sort of plan for the second round. We are all (apart from Steve) desperately hoping that England do not win their group as this would mean another trip to Rustenburg. There are plenty of horror stories around of peoples return to Johannesburg on Saturday and the thought of having to drive there again is not appealing. The chances of us having the same luck when leaving the ground as last time are very slim and it would also involve an additional 1000km or so in the car, expensive accommodation and a return to the worst town any of us have been to to watch a football match. Bloemfontein would be a lot easier all round and all we really achieved was agreement that this would be a better option.

We went out at lunchtime to an excellent Indian cafe and then spent an hour using the internet. While we were doing this, Dave managed to fall over somewhere injuring his ankle in the process. Not sure if it is sprained or fractured (an x-ray may be required) but the doctor who treated him may have spare tickets for tomorrow nights game in Durban. We were keen on this game but may not be able to go if Mark's course finishes late tomorrow. We watched the Portugal v Ivory Coast 4pm game in the house and did not really do much else. Tried a different pub for our evening meal and returned to the house for Brazil v N. Korea

Day 6 - Wednesday, 16th June The three of us were up early again but at 6:30, we got another phone call from the dive centre. This time, they were still going out but the conditions were still too bad for Mark to be able to finish his course so Roz and I headed off on our own. The conditions were worse than on Monday and the boat journeys to and from the sites were pretty rough. There was also a lot of surge and current when we got to the sites but the dives were still pretty good. We dived at Cathedral and Manta Point. Loads of raggies again, particularly on the first dive and another three turtles (four for Roz). Back at the dive centre by about 2.

We had hoped to go to the Spain v Switzerland game in Durban today but did not finish diving until it was too late. Mark was able to go due his diving being cancelled so he and Steve had driven into Durban by the time we got back to the house.

Day 7 - Thursday, 17th June Left Scottburgh at 10 for the hour drive to Durban airport. We were on the 13:10 flight to Cape Town and took off about 40 minutes late. We were met at the airport in Cape Town by a taxi organised by the place we were staying at and arrived at Brenwin Guest House by about 4pm. We dumped our stuff in our room and Roz and I headed straight out to have a look around.

We were staying a 15 minute walk from the V & A Waterfront. As soon as we were down there, Cape Town seemed a superb place. There area was packed with restaurants and souvenir shops, there were loads of football fans from different countries all around the area watching the afternoon games and everywhere we went, we had excellent view of Table Mountain. The whole area had an excellent atmosphere and felt very safe. The contrast between here and Rustenburg could not have been greater.

We met up with the others and headed into the Paulaner Haus which would not of looked out of place in Munich and had a typically German meal with plenty of wheat beer. A very enjoyable evening.
