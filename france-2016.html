dwjhaines - france 2016
Photos

Thursday 9th June We left home at 6:30ish and had a 45 minute drive down to Portsmouth. We stopped at Tesco to stock up on food for tomorrow's journey and then drove to Port Solent Wetherpoons for a curry. We drove to the port and checked in for the ferry at about 9pm.

It was a relief to be checked in and waiting in the queue for the ferry as yesterday's equivelent sailing had been cancelled due to the strikes in France. We boarded the ferry by about 10:30, dropped our stuff in our cabin and went up to the bar for a drink before going to sleep. Plenty of football fans in the bar, mainly Welsh, but no-one was too lively due to the long drives ahead tomorrow.

Friday 10th June We were up by 7ish local time and the ferry docked on time at 8:30. It took almost an hour to get off the ferry and out of the port area but by about 9:30 we were on our way for the start of our 600 mile drive towards Marseille.

The journey was not too bad most of the way. It all went pretty smoothly until Lyon where we were delayed for about an hour which was frustrating but the rest of the journey was fine. We had three stops on the way totalling about an hour and the worries about petrol were totally unfounded.

The only time we had any problems with directions was when we got lost coming off the main road in Marsaielle but our sat-nav got us back to the area where we are staying and we made it to our apartment by about 8:15.

We had exchanged a few texts with the owner of our apartment on the way down (all in French) and there had been a lot of confusion about our arrival, but Mark had arrived before us and had got the key and was waiting when we arrived. It took about 15 minutes to ge the car parked but once we had done that, we dumped our bags in the apartment and went straight out to watch the opening game of the tournament. We got the metro into the centre of Marseille and were sat down in a bar with our first beer about two minutes before kick-off.

We knew there was trouble around the old port area so had got off the metro a stop before there and found an ideal bar with only a few other people in it. We watched the game in there before getting the metro back to the apartment where we were joined by Steve who had flown in to Lyon and been given a lift down with friends. We bought a few beers from a local shop and stayed up drinking on our balcony until about 3am.

Saturday 11th June - Emgland v Russia Had a late start to the day as we were not really awake until 11ish. We had breakfast on the balcony of the apartment and headed into the centre of Marseille at about 12:45. We got the metro into the old port area and spent an hour or so wandering round there. The atmosphere was all fairly good at this point although it was obvious that there was potential for trouble in the bars where a lot of our fans were gathered.

After looking round a few of the sights we decided to get away from the area and headed inland to find a bar to watch the Switzerland v Albania game. We stopped for a beer with a couple of people we knew from Ukraine and then caught a tram back to the bar we had been drinking in last night. We got away from the area where most of the trouble was to occur less than an hour before it all started.

After the first game had finshed we got the metro towards the ground and found another bar to watch Wales v Slovakia. As we were about to leave here, there was more trouble outside as England fans were attacked by Russians and we had a few things thrown through the windows. All quite dramatic but no-one in the pub was hurt and we were able to leave after a few minutes. There was still trouble taking place ahead of us as we walked towards the ground but once through the first police check, everything calmed down and we were able to get into the ground relatively smoothly.

The ground itself was one of the most spectacular we have seen and the atmosphere was also superb. The game was good and would have been perfect apart from the late equaliser. More trouble from the Russians at the final whistle meant that we were keen to get away from the ground as quickly as possible. we had planned to go back to the bar where we had watched the Wales game but the police had (sensibly) closed all the bars in the area and we decided to go straight back to the apartment.

Roz and I walked a mile or so back to the Metro line 1 and got a train back to Sant Juste. We had a 20 minute wait for Mark and Steve to get back due to problems on the metro so waited outside the apartment with a beer and were back in by about 12:15. A very good day despite the trouble!

Sunday 12th June Steve was colleced by his mates at about 10:30 and Mark Roz and myself left the apartment at 11:15 after the owner had been round to collect the key. We said goodbye to Filo (the owners cat that we had been looking after) and started driving North. We had a detour to visit the aquaduct at Pont du Gard which was quite spectacular and then drove about three hours further to Mâcon. We did not really know where we would be staying but this seemed a reasonable town for a night. We arrived just before 6pm, checked into the hotel we had booked while on the way and then headed out to find a bar to watch the Northern Ireland v Poland game.

A lot of places were closed as it is Sunday but we found a couple of decent bars and then a pizza place to watch the Germany v Ukraine match. We got through a couple of litres of wine during the game and had a pretty good night in the end.

Monday 13th June We left the hotel at 9ish and continued our journey North. We had a four hour drive to Reims where we were to meet Regan, Annette, Julie and Anne. We stopped at McDonalds for breakfast and had one petrol stop but it was a fairly uneventful journey. We arrived in Reims at about 2:30 and found our hotel without too many problems. We parked in the underground garage and were in our apartment just after 3:00.

We could not watch the 3:00 game on our tele as it is only on satellite TV for some reason and none of us could be bothered to go out and watch it. Instead, we had lunch and then Roz and I went out for a walk around Reims. We headed out to the Mumm Champagne Cellars and booked all of us onto a tour of the cellars for tomorrow afternoon. We then had a walk around the town centre. The cathedral is supposed to be one of the most spectacular in France but the first thing you notice about it as you approach is the amount of scaffolding on it. It did not look too impressive but we did only have a quick look at it before heading back to the apartment.

We walked back via a street which had plenty of decent looking bars on it. We met up with the others back at the apartment and the seven of us all headed out just before 6. We returned to the street we had walked along earlier and went to Les 3 Brasseurs - The Three Brewers - and settled in for the Sweden v Ireland game. It was an excellent bar with a good selection of its own beers. We moved to another table to eat after the game and stayed there for the 9:00 game as well. We left there after the game having paid about €270 between us. Julie, Anne and Annette went back to the hotel at that point while the rest of us went to another couple of bars. Vaguely remember getting back to the apartment at about 2am. A very good night out!

Tuesday 14th June A bit of a late start today due to hangovers. Mark, Roz and I left the apartment at about 1:15 and went for another walk around the town. We had a closer look at the cathedral which was more impressive close up and particularly good inside. There was not really a lot else we wanted to see so we made our way from there to the Mumm champagne house where we met the others at 3:30 for the start of our tour.

The tour lasted just over an hour and was all quite interesting. Mumm have 25km of cellars and produce 8 - 9 million bottles of champagne a year. We were shown round a section of the cellars 14m underground where the champagne is stored. After wandering round the cellars, we were all given a glass to taste although most of us agreed we would rather have a beer!

We all stopped at a bar for a swift beer on the way back into towm. Julie and Anne went back to the hotel while the rest of us went directly to The Three Brewers for the 6pm game. We stayed in there for another five hours and watched both the games there. Julie and Anne rejoined us for the 9:00 game and it was another good evening out. We were not as pissed as last night and went straight back to the apartment when the second game finished.

Wednesday 15th June The three of us checked out of the apartment at 10:15 after doenloading next seasons premier league fixtures. We drove about 150 km to Haute Picardie where we dropped Mark at the TGV station. He is going to the Russia v Slovakia game with Steve (who came over on this morning's Eurostar) in Lille and this station was just about on the way for us.

We had planned to drive straight to Arras from here but as we were driving through the Somme area, we decided to stop and visit a couple of the 1st World War cemeteries as well as the memorial at Thiepval. The cemeteries were similar to the ones in Belgium and the second one in particular was well worth the visit. The memorial at Thiepval is something we had not heard about before but is the biggest British war memorial in the world and was also well worth stopping at. It was evident that preperations are underway for commenmorating the 100th aniversary of the start of the Battle of The Somme which is only a couple of weeks away.

We were only about 30 minutes away from Awas when we left Thiepval and we found our hotel by about 2:30. There was supposed to be car parking near the hotel but the whole car park was sealed off as the England team are staying at The Mercure just round the corner. We were deciding where we should park when a space became available outside the hotel. This is normally a no parking zone but we have a letter on the dashboard from hotel management and apparently the police will ignore the restrictions for the next two days.

We have a huge room for four people - not ideal but the only thing we could get in the area. We were able to get BBC 1 on our tele so watched the Russia v Slovakia game in the room. We then went out for a walk and managed to co-incide going out with England's arrival at their hotel. We waited around to taking photos (mainly of the bus) for a while before wandering round the rest of the town.

The town centre was very Belgium looking with a couple of big squares. There were a few fans getting quite lively in one of them but all seemed fairly good natured at this point. We found a good beer shop where we bought a few French beers from my book and then returned to the hotel to watch the 6pm game. Mark and Steve arrived just after 7. We had a beer during the second half and then went out to eat.

We walked round the centre again and eventually chose a restaurant on the road between the two squares. We had good meals again while watching the France v Albania game and stopped off at another bar on the way home. Again, there was no sign of any trouble and English and Welsh fans seemed happy drinking together. Got back to the hotel at about 1.

Thursday 16th June - Emgland v Wales We were all up by about 9:30. Roz and I walked across the road to buy train tickets to Lens before we all went out for breakfast. The service in the last place we had been in last night was painfully slow so we ended up in a kebab shop round the corner. Chicken kebab and a bottle of Grimbergen was not the healthiest start to the day but was an enjoyable enough meal.

We had a beer in another couple of bars and were also able to see the team departing from their hotel (more bus photos!). We got the 1:23 train to Lens. This was not as crowded as expected. Although full of both sets of fans, it was a long train with plenty of space.

We were in Lens by about 1:45. The advertised alcohol ban had certainly not ben enforced as the town centre seemed to consist mainly of bars serving beer in plastic glasses. We were not really expecting to get another drink but had time for a pint before walking to the ground. We managed to find Regan, Julie and Anne on the way to the ground which was about a 20 minute walk away. Mark had his wallet stolen on the way to the ground but had taken his out so was still able to get in without problems. We got through the ticket checks and search areas without any problems and were in the ground with about 10 minutes to spare.

The game itself was one of the best England games we have ever seen. A poor first half but an excellent second and winning in injury time could not have been much better for us. It puts England top of the group and they are now in with a very good chance of winning it. Winning the group would be very good as it makes our travel for any later stages a lot easier.

No trouble after the game and the walk back to the station was fairly straight-forward. Again, both sets of fans were mixing with no problems. At the station, things were a bit more chaotic - not because of fans but the total incompetance of the police/station authorities. There were not enough trains and the solution was to not let anyone into the station. There was no queuing system or any form of organisation, just a large group of people trying to get through a single narrow entrance. We eventually all got through and were on a train before 6 and back in Arras by about 6:15.

It was a relatively quiet night back in Arras. We watched the Ukraine v Northerrn Ireland game in a bar round the corner from the hotel. We then returned to the same restaurant as last night despite saying last night we would not go back. The service had been very poor but the food was good, the beer good and it was one of the few restaurants we could find with T.V.s. We had another good meal before going back to the hotel to watch the highlights of our game on BBC1.

Friday 17th June We left the hotel just after 10 and drove North to Vimy to visit the Canadian WWI memorial and cemeteries. We had an unexpected tour of the frontline trenches and spent about 15 minutes in the underground tunnels that they have preserved there. All quite interesting again.

We had an uneventful 500km drive from here to Beaune. We had one petrol/toilet/lunch stop after about an hour but did the rest of the journey in one go arriving in Beaune just after 5:00, Mark got a ohone call from the FA on the way to tell him someone had found his wallet and he should be able to pick it up at the next game. Beaune is nice little town less than 100 miles from Lyon where we travel to on Sunday so made an ideal place to stay for a couple of nights.

We walked into the centre of town ffrom the Ibis we are staying in and watched the two evening games in a couple of bars. The centre looked excellent with plenty of really nice looking wine bars and restaurants. Were back in the hotel just after mid-night.

Saturday 18th June We were up quite late again and went over the road to a shopping centre for breakfast.For the first time in a while we had heallthy things like smoothies and fruit which made a bit of a change. From there, Mark and Steve went into town while we went round the supermarket and bought a few things that we needed.

We dropped the stuff we had bought back at the car and then walked into town. We spent the best part of an hour waling around the old town and looking at a few options for wine tours. It is the nicest place we have been since getting to France with plenty of old buildings and it was very enjoyable just walking around. We met Mark and Steve at a bar with teles outside that was showing the 3pm game.

After a beer here, Roz and I went off to the Patriarche wine cellars. This was the tour that we were keenest on doing and we were not as bothered about watching the football as Mark and Steve. They went on a different tour when the football finished but the Patriarche cellars closed at 5pm.

The cellars were excellent and definitely one of the highlights of the trip so far. We were shown to the entrance to the cellars but were then left to wander round on our own. The cellars themselves were very spectacular with all the usual rows of bottles and barrels. The tour included tasting 13 different wines, three whites and ten reds. When we got to the first tasting room, the whites were out on barrels and we were able to serve ourselves. The wine was very nice and we were able to have as many "tastes" as we wanted. We eventually left this area and moved onto the reds. Again, there was no limit to the amount we could try. The tour cost €17 and was supposed to last 60 - 90 minutes. We were down there for about two hours in the end and certainly got our moneys worth!

We were fairly pissed when we came out of the cellars and made our way back to meet Mark and Steve. We stopped for one more drink on the way at a bar we had passed a few times and had liked the look of. We watched the second half of Iceland v Hungary game in the place we had been to twice last night and stayed there to eat and watch Portugal v Austria. We went straight back to the hotel when this game finished although the walk back is a bit of a vague memory!

Sunday 19th June A slightly earlier start today. Roz and I walked back into the centre at about 9:30 for a last look around, breakfast and some souvenir shopping. We went back to the hotel, met up with the others, loaded up the car and left beaune at about 11. We filled up with petrol and had a drive of an hour or so to Lyon where we will spend the next two nights.

We found the apartment easily enough but had a few problems finding a parking space. Parking is a big problem in most of France. The French seem to be able to get their cars into impossibly small spaces and finding somewhere to park a car the size of a Mondeo is not easy. We eventually did find somewhere a few minutes away but Roz is concerned as she will be the one who has to get it out again on Wednesday after I have gone back to England.

We were met at the apartment by the owners who gave us an hours description of how to use the apartment and where to find everything. We had an hour or so of sorting ourselves out and left the apartment at 4:00 to go into Lyon. We walked down to the river together and then went seperate ways. Mark and Steve were going to the Albania v Romania game in Lyon and had arranged to meet Fred and Steve in the old town. Roz and I headed of to Part-Dieu station as I needed to pick up my ticets for Tuesday's journey home. I had booked them before leaving home but needed to collect them from the station.

It took us an hour to walk to the station stopping to look at sights and taking photos of fans heading to the game. At the station I was initially told that I could have problems picking up my tickets as I have lost the credit card that I used to buy them. However, after a twenty minute wait in the ticket office, I was given the tickets without having to show any id at all. We picked up the tickets for tomorrow from a machine while we were there and then headed back towards the old town.

We had not planned to meet the others but after a few minutes of walking round the old town, Fred spotted us so we joined them all for a beer before they headed off to the football. After they had left, we had a beer in another bar and then went for an excellent Italian meal. The France v Switzerland game started while we were in there. We saw about 25 minutes of the game and then started to walk back towards the area where we were staying. We found a very good pub to watch the second half. It had a good range of beers and was the first place we have been to where the French seemed enthusiastic about the football. The pub was packed and had the sort of atmosphere you would expect if you were in a pub at home. We walked back to the apartment when the game finished and had another drink while waiting for Mark and Steve to return.

Monday 20th June - Slovakia v England We had been getting a bit more optimistic about England's chances after the Wales game but our optimism was knocked on the head when we woke to find that Hodgson was likely to make six changes to the team and was going to play Henderson and Wilshire in midfield tonight.

Roz and I went out to buy breakfast which we ate in the apartment and then headed out for the day just after 11:30. We spent an hour walking back to Part-Dieu station where we were due to catch the 12:54 train to Saitt-Etienne. There was an earlier train waiting on the platform when we got there so we ran to get on it but it was delayed and did not leave until 12:54 anyway. We got into Saint-Etienne at about 1:45.

We walked from the station to the centre of the city. The whole place seemed far more into the tournament than other places we have been to with flags and banners everywhere. The atmosphere was pretty good everywhere with fans from both countries mixing with no problems. The weather had improved today and it was quite warm walking around. We stopped for a much needed beer in a small bar beofre walking out to the main square where Steve had arranged to meet friends.

The atmosphere in the square was also good. It was packed with fans and again, everyone was mixing without problems. These areas are not normally the sort of place that me, Roz and Mark have any interest in staying in but the atmosphere was OK and we stayed for another beer before heading off to get something to eat. We ate at a pizza place on the square and were joined in there by Fred and Steve. After eating, Steve stayed in the square with Jer, etc while the rest of us started walking North in the direction of the ground. We found another bar which was not great but had cheap beer that we could drink while sat at he tram stop just outside. We stayed there a couple of hours before walking the rest of the distance to the ground.

Getting into the ground was not quite as smooth as at the first two games but we were still inside after about 25 minutes and were in our seats about half an hour before kick-off. Up until this point, the day had been excellent but the rumours about the team were correct and the game was disappointing. At least we are through but will have a lot more travelling to do in the next week or so than if we had won the group.

After the game we walked back to the station where things were slighty more organised than in Lens. We got a a train by 12:15 and were back in Lyon just after 1am. After walking away from the station for about 5 minutes, we got a taxi back to the apartment.

We then spent an hour or so making plans for the next week or so. Winning the group would have made things very easy for me as I have a train ticket into Paris on Friday night. I will now get a TGV down to Avignon on Saturday morning where I will meet up with Roz and Mark again (hopeflly with my car!). We will then drive to Nice on Sunday for the game on Monday. Steve is not sure what he will do yet. We finally got to bed at 3ish.

Tuesday 21st June I was up by 8 as I was booked on the 11:00 train to Lille. We went out to get bread for breakfast and after eatiing, I said goodbye to everyone and started my journey home. I started to walk towards the station (after checking that the car was still where I had left it) but got as far as Hotel de Ville before getting on a bus. I bought food for the journey from the shopping centre and was at the station about 45 minutes before departure. The train to Lille which was predictably packed with England fans left a few minutes late at 11:05

It was a fairly subdued train journey to Lille. I was not the only one disappointed by last nights result and totally knackered after not enough sleep and too much beer over the last 10 days or so. We arrived in Lille at 14:30 so had just over an hour to wait for the 15:36 Eurostar back to London.

The train to London was also on time and arrived just after 16:00. I got the tube across to Waterloo and after buying a new railcard, got thre 16:39 back to Basingstoke. I had an hour wait for Louise to get back from London to get a lift home and was back in Kingsclere just before 7:00.
