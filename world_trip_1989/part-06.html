Part 6 - Freo to Cairns

  • Diary Index

Monday, 12th June Got up at 7:30 and packed my rucksack whilst still drunk. Roz, Linda and I went to McDonalds for breakfast.

Tim, Linda, Carolyn and I left Fremantle at about 10. Roz is waiting in Freo for Andy, Dave is staying here until he has enough money to fly home to see Kathryn.

We drove to Cervantes where we saw The Pinnacles - a tourist attraction which was actually quite interesting for a change.

We drove from there to Geraldton where we checked into the youth hostel. I have left my jacket in Freo.

Tuesday, 13th June We left the hostel at 10:10 after a night of heavy rain. We tried to get into Kalburri National Park but the road leading to it was closed due to flooding. At one point, we had to wait half an hour as the highway we were on was inpassable. After the water had cleared we proceeded North, stopping for lunch at Overlander Roadhouse. We then left the main highway and headed for Monkey Mia where you can go into the sea and feed the dolphins. We saw a couple there but decided to return to feed them tomorrow.

We drove back to Denham where we checked into the backpackers. We got a self-contained unit for $40.

Wednesday, 14th June Woke at 7:00 after another night of heavy rain. We left Denham at about 8:30 and drove to Monkey Mia. There were about six dolphins who came to the beach to be fed. It was an incredible experiance. The dolphins come to within about a metre of the beach and eat fish out of your hand. We spent about an hour standing knee deep in the water feeding and stroking the dolphins. To get to Monkey Mia, we had gone over 300km out of our way but it had been well worth it!

We drove direct from Monkey Mia to Caernarvon, a distance of 350km. On arrival in Caernarvon, we were told that the road North of the town was blocked due to flood water and we were likely to be stuck there for at least a couple of days. We tried about four caravan sites before finding one with a vacancy - a four birth van for $37 a night. My bunk is about 4'6" long!

We went for a walk in the evening and discovered a banana plantation with bunches lying about on the floor. We went back for the car and now have a three month supply of bananas. We also drove to the bridge over the River Gasgoyne. The water level was rising to 5m while we watched and the bridge was closed at 10pm.

Thursday, 15th June We woke at 8ish and drove down to the bridge again. The normally dry river was now at 6.3 m and flowing over the top of the bridge. The bridge will be closed for at least 24 hours more.

We drove into the town centre of Caernarvon which wasn't very exciting and then walked to the end of 1 Mile Jetty. We spent most of the afternoon sitting around in the caravan doing nothing.

Friday, 16th June The bridge was reopened and we left Caernarvon at about 10. We encountered a few floods on the way North but nothing too deep to drive through. We crossed the Tropic of Capricorn at about midday. We stopped at Borrowdale for lunch and I saw live kangaroos for the first time since getting to Australia. They were in a cage by a roadhouse.

We had a fairly uneventful journey from there and arrived in Karratha at about 5:30. We checked into a camp site with four blokes, two of which we have kept bumping into since Geraldton. Tim and I slept in my tent, Carolyn and Linda in the car.

Saturday, 17th June We drove to Broome via Port Headland, a distance of over 800km. Port Headland was pretty boring without much in the way of sights. We had problems starting the car at Sandfire but Graham, one of the blokes we were with last night got it going again for us. They are also traveling to Darwin.

We arrived in Broome at 6:30 and booked an on site caravan for $48 per night. After showering and eating, we went out to Broome's only pub for a few drinks. We also visited a funfair.

Sunday, 18th June Spent the morning sight-seeing - Anastasia's Pool, Shell House and Japanese Cemetery, none of which I found particularly exciting. In the afternoon we went to Cable Beach, a 22km stretch of almost deserted sand. The weather was not brilliant but it was warm enough to sun-bathe and swim.

In the evening we went to The Sun Picture Theater, the oldest working picture show in the world. It has a large screen in a garden and we all sat in rows of deck-chairs. We were lucky to be there on a film club (SPIFS) night and were treated to free food and beer throughout the film. A good night out

Monday, 19th June We checked out of the caravan park at 10 and went straight down to Cable Beach where we spent all day sun-bathing. We had a few beers in the evening and went to sleep in the car parked about half a mile from the beach.

Tuesday, 20th June We were woken at 6 by a woman whose drive we were blocking. Spent most of the day on the beach and I now have sun burn. We went down to the pub in the evening and had entertainment provided by fighting aborigines. We decided we would stay another day in Broome and also to sleep in the car again.

Wednesday, 21st June We woke at about 7:30. The weather was both windy and cloudy so we decided not to stay on for the extra day. We left Broome at 9 and drove to Fitzroy Crossing where we saw Geikie Gorge. We spent about two hours walking along the gorge and then drove to Halls Creek where we booked into a camp site for the night.

Thursday, 22nd June Tim, Linda and Carolyn went on a flight over the Bungle Bungles at 8:30 and we left Hall's Creek when they returned at 10:30. Stopped at Turkey Creek for lunch and then drove to Wyndam which was pretty boring (Crocodile Lookout with no crocodiles, etc). We had to stop a couple of times because the car was overheating, mainly I think because we drove non-stop through the hottest part of the day.

We drove from Wyndham to Kununurra where we checked into the youth hostel.

Friday, 23rd June We left the hostel at 10 and drove to Kathrine stopping at Timber Creek and Victoria River en route. We crossed into Northern Territories at 10:30 and arrived in Katherine at 6:30. We drove to the hospital and found Paula who lives in the nurses home. Paula was working nights so the rest of us went back into Katherine and got drunk, first at a pub full of aborigines and then at a night club which remained open until 4:00. We left when it closed and got back to Paula's at about 4:30.

Saturday, 24th June We got up at about midday and went on a brief sight-seeing tour of Katherine. We went swimming at the thermal springs and went to look for crocodiles at the homestead, without success.

In the evening, we phoned for a pizza and then went for a few drinks in Katherine. Everyone was pretty tired after last night so we came home by 1:30.

Sunday, 25th June We spent the day paddling canoes through Katherine Gorge. We hired canoes at 9:30 and spent four hours going up stream through the first three gorges stopping at waterfalls, ponds, etc en route. The scenery was excellent all the way, probably the best I have seen since getting to Australia.

We stopped and had lunch on the side of the river at about 1 and then paddled back again shooting the rapids and having frequent water fights on the way. Paula, Tim and me all managed to capsize our canoes at least once. We all got totally soaked and are covered in bruises from carrying the canoes over rapids. We are also totally worn out but it was an excellent day out.

In the evening, Kay, Paula's flat-mate, cooked us a meal then we watched a couple of videos before going to sleep, all of us totally exhausted.

Monday, 26th June Left Katherine at 11:40. Most of the day was wasted because we left so late and had to drive slowly to stop the car over-heating . We stopped at Edith Falls, part of the Katherine Gorge National Park and eventually arrived in Darwin at about 6:30. After getting turned away from a few places we managed to get beds at Sunset City Hostel. We had a quick look around town and then had an early night.

Tuesday, 27th June We walked around Darwin in the morning. It is a new but not particularly exciting city. At midday we went to Mindil Beach where we spent the rest of the day. (Also went to Doctor's Gully to feed the fish)

In the evening, Me, Linda and Carolyn went for a drink at The Darwin Hotel and then bumped into Ed on our way to meet Tim. We went for a meal at The International Food Market and from there to a bar. The city is a lot more interesting at night.

Wednesday, 28th June We picked Ed up just after 8 and set off for Kakadu National Park, about 250km East of Darwin. We arrived at the park HQ at about 11:30 and after collecting various maps, etc we set off on several of the walking tours around the park. The scenery was quite good without being truly spectacular. We walked to Baroallra Springs, one of the few places in the park you can swim without having to worry about crocodiles. At 6 we drove to Cooinda and booked into the camp site. Spent the evening in the camp site bar playing cards.

Thursday, 29th June At 9am we went on a two hour cruise of Yellow Waters. The cruise was quite good, we saw about four crocodiles, but it did drag on a bit towards the end as it seemed to cater mainly for bird watchers. We drove from Yellow Waters to Ubirr where we saw aboriginal paintings and about the best views of the park we have seen so far. The drive from Ubirr back to Darwin took about five hours as the car was overheating and we couldn't open the bonnet to put in water. We arrived in Darwin at about 8 to find all the hostels were fullup so, after a quick look around the market at Mindil Beach, we drove out of the city to a camp site.

Friday, 30th June We left the camp site at 10 and, after sorting the car out, spent about an hour wandering around the town before going down to Mindil Beach. We stayed on the beach until 3 and then started heading South towards Katherine and Alice Springs. It was a shame to leave Darwin after such a short time as it seemed to be quite a nice city. I think I would have stayed longer had I not told Gordon that I would be in Alice at the start of July.

We arrived in Katherine at 8 and after leaving all our stuff at Paula's went out to the same places as last Friday. Ed, Carolyn, Paula and I eventually arrived back from "Wings" at 5:30.

Saturday, 1st July We left Katherine at midday. Paula came with us as far as Mataranka thermal pools and then she and Carolyn hitched back to Katherine. Carolyn is staying there for a few weeks before rejoining us in Alice. We left Mataranka at about 3 and drove as far as Tennant Creek stopping at Dunmarra for food on the way. We arrived in Tennant Creek at 10 to find the hostel full , so again, had to camp out again.

Sunday, 2nd July We left Tennant Creek at 9 and drove to Alice Springs stopping only to look at The Devils Marbles and briefly for lunch on the way.

We arrived in Alice at 4 and got a room for four at the backpackers. Spent the evening in the bar but I did not drink as I am about $350 in debt after the journey from Perth.

Monday, 3rd July Had a letter from Gordon waiting for me at the post office. He is in Melbourne and should get to Alice later this week. I started looking for a job at 9 and was working by about 9:15. It is temporary work at a fruit and veg wholesalers and involves sortinig tomatoes for $7 per hour. It is not very exciting work or very good money but at least it is some form of income. Tomorrow is a day off but there is work here again on Wednesday. Time and Linda also turned up and got jobs there.

I discovered that I will not be gettign a tax rebate today. This was quite a blow as I have paid nearly $1000 and was expecting all of it back. I stayed in and read for the evening while the others went out. Ed got pissed.

Tuesday, 4th July Saw the sights of Alice Springs i.e. Anzac Hill, the telegraph station and the spring.

I spent most of the day trying to decide what to do between now and September when Jane arrives in Cairns. There does nto seem much point in staying in Alice for two months without having a good job Accomodation is expensive and there is not much to do here. Tim and Ed are both keen on leaving quite soon so I think we will probably go to Cairns in about two weeks.

In the evening we celebrated Independence Day by drinking loads of beer with a group of people who are staying at the hostel and then going to a night club. I think I got to bed by about 2.

Wednesday, 5th July Tim, Linda and I spent five hours packing tomatoes. I had a hangover. Got paid $7 an hour cash in hand.

Thursday, 6th July We had two days off work again so we all went off to Ayers Rock for a couple of days. We took about five hours getting to Yulara, the resort near the rock and arrived there at about 3. We looked around the town and then drove out to sunset point and took photos of the rock as it changed colour.

We pitched our tent on the Yulara campsite and then went on a one hour star gazing tour which was quite interesting. We spent half an hour in the bar before returning to the campsite at 9:30 for an early night.

Friday, 7th July Woke at 7 after a night spent trying to get warm. We were caught by the campsite rangers for not payingn our camping fees so after making our excuses, we had to pay $7 each.

We saw the rock at sun rise and then climbed to the top of it. It was quite hard work getting to the top but we all made it eventually. The views from the top were excellent and made the climb worthwhile. After climbing down again, we went on a walk around the rock, a distance of 9km.

We drove back to Alice and went back to the backpackers where after a mix up with the booking, we got given two double rooms.

We went for a few drinks in the evening as Linda is leaving us tomorrow to go and work on a homestead for a couple of months.

Saturday, 8th July Tim and I went to work at 9 and put in seven and a half hours. It is a ridiculous job - the slower you work, the more money you make. We managed to pinch a load of fruit (pineapples, strawberries, etc) while the boss was away. In the evening, we went to The Alice Springs Show and fireworks display.

Sunday, 9th July Spent all day doing nothing. Alice Springs is pretty boring on a Sunday.

Monday, 10th July We worked for four and a half hours. I decided I wanted to leave Alice on Thursday and work for two days at The Tennent Creek Show. Tim is leaving by bus on Wednesday.

We went to the bar for a drink in the evening and ended up getting drunk in someones room. There is nothing to do here but drink.

Tuesday, 11th July Woke at 9 with a slight hangover. At 1, Me, Tim, Ed and Jackie, a girl from work, went out sight-seeing in the car. We drove out to Simpsons Gap and Standly Chasm, neither of which were over exciting.

Wednesday, 12th July Me and Tim went to work in the morning - Tim left at 12 to get a bus to Cairns. I worked until 3 with Jackie who is from London. Gordon arrived in Alice at lunchtime so we all went down to the pub in the evening. After a few beers, we decided not to go t Cairns but to return to Darwin working at the shows at Tennant Creek and Katherine on the way. I wanted to do this, Jackie was going that way anyway, Gordon wanted to go to Kakadu and Ed didn't care where we went so it was practically unanimous. I don't know if Tim will be happy.

Thursday, 13th July We left Alice at 10 without Gordon who is going to Ayers Rock and then rejoining us at Tennant Creek tomorrow night. We arrived in Tennant Creek at about 4 and I managed to get two days work at a "Barramundi and Chips" stall. Jackie found the job for me. It pays $10 an hour.

We had a bar-b-q in the evening at the caravan site - cheeseburgers and wine.

Friday, 14th July I started work at 7. The job is quite good fun and involves preparing, cooking and serving fish burgers, steak burgers, etc. I alos get to eat whatever I want. At the end of the day I got paid $100 for about 9 hours work.

In the evening, we went to The Tennant Creek Hotel, a typical NT pub and waited for Gordon who arrived at about 9.

Saturday, 15th July I worked from 8 until 2 for $50. When I had finished, I met up with the others and we spent a couple of hours looking around the show. In the evening we bought a case of beer and had another bar-b-q.

Sunday, 16th July Left Tennant Creek at 8. We arrived at Mataranka at 3 and went for a dip in the thermal springs. We had a few drinks by the tents and then went to the bar where at 10, we met Carolyn who is working at the campsite restaurant. We continued drinking after the bar shut and got to bed sometime after midnight.

Monday, 17th July We left Mataranka and headed North 100km to Katherine. Carolyn came with us as far as the Cutta Cutta Caves which were quite good. We killed time in Katherine until about 5 and then went to Paula's again.

Tuesday, 18th July We had planned to hire canoes today and go out overnight to Katherine Gorge but booked too late and can only go tomorrow. We got up quite late and after we had all done our washing, we went to the thermal springs and then Low Level Park where we spent the afternoon sunbathing. In the evening we went to a bar-b-q at one of Paul's friend's house. A good night out with plenty of steaks and beer.

Wednesday, 19th July We spent the day at Katherine Gorge. We hit and killed a kangaroo on the way to the gorge but apaart from that, it was a brilliant day. I hadn't expected the gorge to be as good second time around but I enjoyed it just as much as the first time we went.

Thursday, 20th July We spent the day trying to sort out the brakes on the car. In the afternoon, we lazed around by the hospital swimming pool. I had three hours work in the evening serving behind a toy stall at the show and demonstrating "tumble buggies". I get paid $5 an hour.

When I got back, we all went out to a disco at the R.A.A.F. base at Tindel (Me, Ed, Gordon, Paula, Kay and Evonne). Me, Ed and Gordon all got pissed on the cheap beer. When we got back, Ed spent ten minutes in Paula's room looking for a toilet.

Friday, 21st July I worked from 9:30am to 9pm. The work is pretty boring and involves trying to sell toys, most of which don't work. I didn't work very hard as I don't like ripping off kids and get paid the same however many I sell.

Saturday, 22nd July Worked 9:30 to 8. Got paid $125. We had the brakes on the car fixed for $75

Sunday, 23rd July We left Katherine at 9 and arrived in Darwin at 1. After checking into the youth hostel, we spent the afternoon at Mindel Beach. In the evening, we bought a case of beer and sat at the internatinal food market.

Monday, 24th July I took Jackie out to the show grounds where she is staying for the week and I managed to get a job for the three days of the show again. In the afternoon, we went to Mindel Beach.

Ed, Gordon and I are sharing a double room to save money. It is a bit cramped but only costs $20 a night.

Tuesday, 25th July We spent the morning fixing the car and generally lazing around. In the afternoon, we went to the cinema and saw Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade. We ate out at the food market again. The food there is really good value and the place has a great atmosphere.

Wednesday, 26th July Most of the day was spent lazing around. We went to the beach in the afternoon.

Thursday, 27th - Saturday, 29th July I spent three days working at the Darwin Show. I was working for a real con man who owns about 18 of the stalls. I worked on about 5 or 6 of them including the haunted house where I had to hide in a cupboard and scare the kids. Most of the time I was working on games where it was impossible to win the prizes. I worked about 13 - 14 hours per day and earnt a total of $160 plus free camping and food. The money was pretty bad considering the hours worked and the amount of money I took but at least I now have enough cash to get me to Cairns.

Sunday, 30th July In the morning we had the puncture on the car repaired ($20). Spent the afternoon on the beach. In the evening, we went to the Victoria Hotel and then the food market.

Monday, 31st July We left Darwin at 9:30 and set off towards Cairns. Jackie stayed in Darwin as she is heading to Perth later in the week and her place in the car was taken by Tina, a Danish girl who had advertised for a lift. We stopped in Katherine for lunch and at Mataranka where we saw Carolyn again. We reached Daly Waters by 8ish and camped there for the night. We visited the Daly Waters Pub for a few drinks. It is the oldest pub in The Northern Territories.

Tuesday, 1st August We left Daly Waters at 9 and drove 820km to Camooweal, just over the Queensland border. The journey passed without much incident as there is hardly anything between the two places. The car went a lot better than it did yesterday when it was over-heating quite frequently. Ed and I are doing all the driving.

Wednesday, 2nd August Another pretty boring days driving. This time 720km to a campsite at Hughenden. We are camping out every night as not only is it cheaper it also means we can have barbies. We collect wood before getting to the campsite and then cook steak, onions, etc. Steaks are cheaper than mars bars.

Thursday, 3rd August We could have driven to Cairns today but decided to take it easy and visit places along the way instead. We stopped briefly at Charters Towers and then for a few hours at Townsville. Townsville looked quite nice and I will probably stay for longer when we pass through again. We drove from there to Lucinda where again, we camped for the night. This time we camped for free as reception was closed when we arrived.

We left Lucinda early to avoid paying camping fees. After having breakfast in Ingham, we drove the last 250km to Cairns stopping only briefly in Cardwell on the way. We arrived in Cairns at around 1 and checked into the Backpackers Hostel. After collecting my mail, I spent the afternoon looking for work. There doesn't seem to be much about. We met Tim in the evening. He is flying back to Perth on Sunday.

We went out for a meal and a few drinks at Magnums Pub. I left early as I have a cold. Cairns seems quite a good place with plenty to do. I am going to need work though if I am to stay here.
