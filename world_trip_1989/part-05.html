Part 5 - Fremantle

  • Diary Index

Monday, 13th March I was woken at 3:30 by the park's sprinkler system which seemed designed to soak every bench in the park. I could not be bothered to walk anywhere so I stood up for two hours until it started getting light. I sat by the station until the McDonalds opened and then had my breakfast. At 8, I collected mail form the post office. I had one letter from Mum and one from Roz informing me that they were all at the long term youth hostel at Fremantle. I went round the youth hostels looking for Dave, Ed and Paul who were nowhere to be found but then appeared while I was writing them messages at the post office. Dave had caught a train from Adelaide and had arrived at the same time as Paul and Ed.

We caught a train out to Fremantle and walked to the "Freo 200" hostel. The hostel is similar to a hall of residence. We all have single rooms for $50 a week. We booked in for a month and then went to find Roz and Linda. Linda was in but Roz was working. We all went to the cafe where Roz was for cups of tea and then went on the beer for the rest of the day. We stayed in a bar near the station waiting for Roz and then went back to the hostel to pay for our months rent which me, Dave and Paul had to borrow from Linda. We continued drinking at a bar near the hostel until 11 and then returned totally pissed. We then drank wine in Roz or Linda's room until about 1:30.

Tuesday, 14th March I woke at 5:30 on Linda's floor, not too sure how I got there. I returned to my own room and went back to sleep. Dave, Paul and Ed went into Perth at 9 to look for jobs. I went in with Tim and joined them at 1. We spent the afternoon going round job agencies without much success and returned to the hostel at 5.

Wednesday, 15th March Me and Ed had our first morning's work today. We were down at the C.E.S. job centre when someone came and asked us if we wanted an hour and a halfs casual work. We jumped at the chance and spent two hours weeding a garden and dismantling a corrugated aluminium construction for $20 each. I was looking in the job centre again in the afternoon when I was told I might be better off going round factories in person and looking for work.

Thursday, 16th March Paul and I went to O'Conner at about 9 and started asking round various different places. In the fourth place we went, we were told thye almost certainly have a place for one of us, possibly two. We were told to phone back tomorrow. We took the rest of the day off.

Friday, 17th March I went for an interview at Victoria Park and got a job as a salesman. I'm not quite sure what it entails but I will find out on Monday. Had a haircut.

Saturday and Sunday Went to the beach both days. On Saturday, we spent a couple of hours at South Beach and on Sunday went by train to Cottesloe. On the way to Cottesloe, I went to Swanbourne and found the house that Dad had stayed during the second world war. I took photos of the house and surrounding area. Phoned home.

Monday, 20th March Started my career as a salesman at 8:45. Finished it at 1:20. The job entailed walking round shops and offices selling junk to anyone stupid enough to buy it. I'd had enough after four and a half hours and decided to knock it on the head.

Tuesday, 21st March Back to O'Conner to look for work. I walked around the factories for about two and a half hours before I found a job as a cleaner with a company called Steelmark. The job sounds pretty boring but pays $310 a week.

Wednesday, 22nd March The job is pretty boring but I can choose the hours I work which is an advantage.

Thursday, 23rd March I got very bored with the job and Me, Roz, Linda, Tim and Ed got very drunk in the evening.

Friday, 24th March Good Friday - Not much happened.

Saturday, 25th March We went into Perth for a meal in the evening. We went to a Thai BYO restaurant. All ended up pissed again.

Sunday, 26th March Easter Day - We had a picnic at South Beach. Six of us (Dave excluded) together with Julie from Clyne and her friends spent the day at the beach.

A good day out with plenty to eat and loads of beer. We left the beach at 8ish and called in for eight litres of wine on the way back. We then had a party in my room, most of which I can't remember due to being asleep.

Monday, 27th March Spent most of the day cleaning up food from the walls, floor, bed, etc. Went to Cottesloe with Linda for a few hours in the afternoon.

Tuesday, 28th - Thursday, 30th March Three pretty boring days. Work is getting really depressing.. I had some form of flu on Wednesday but had to work as I need the money. I went to bed as soon as I got back and, with the exception of getting up for food, slept until Thursday morning. I felt better by Thursday and was also cheered up by the news that I am earning $10 an hour. I received my first pay packet - $265 take home for four days work

Friday, 31st March Another boring day at work but it was my last day as a cleaner. Next week, I become and "Assistant Guilly Operator". In the evening, we went to a few pubs in Fremantle with some of the girls from Roz and Linda's floor.

Saturday, 1st April I had to get up at 4:45 after three and a half hours sleep to go into work. I did six and a half hours most of which I was paid $20 an hour for.

We went into Fremantle again in the evening, this time for a pizza. We had to come home early after the meal as Roz and Linda were too pissed to go anywhere else after the meal.

Sunday, 2nd April I went to South Perth in the morning to find friends of my grandmother from during the war. Managed to find their house quite easily. They were both really old and the conversation was a bit difficult but I did get free lunch. Mrs Marland gave me $50 as I left.

I met Roz and Linda in Cottesloe and we returned to hit Fremantle's night spots for the third night running. We had a really good night out ending up in a disco. I had to leave early as I am on shift work from now on and have to be up at 4:45 every morning.

Monday, 3rd April My first day as assistant guilly operator. The job is a bit boring but infinitley better than cleaning. (Phoned Dad)

Tuesday, 4th April My tax rebate came through, £381 - more than I expected. Went for a Mexican with Tim, Roz and girls from Roz's floor.

Wednesday, 5th April Put the tax cheque in the bank ($786). It will take 28 days to clear.

Thursday, 6th April Pay day $383. Went out in the evening as Paul leaves tomorrow for a six week surveying job somewhere near Darwin.

Friday, 7th April My career as a guilly operator came to an end. Back to cleaning next week.

Saturday, 8th April Started celebrating Ed's birthday. We went to a fw pubs and a B.Y.O. Vietnamese restaurant. everyone totaly pissed as usual. (Phoned home)

Sunday, 9th April The celebrations continued. We started in The Beaconsfield at 1:30 and gave Ed his present, a Bros t-shirt. Spent all day in various pubs ending up in Clancey's and eventually Cargos. Everyone pissed again.

Monday, 10th April A quiet day. I didn't want to go back to sweeping floors so I packed in my job. Although the money was good, I was not enjoying the job and I decided it was not worth the effort. I had a hangover for most of the day.

Tuesday, 11th April I managed to get a job working for a firm of financial planners. I have to pretend to be doing market research while really trying to get leads for their salesmen. $4 per hour plus $1 per lead.

Ed announced that he is leaving tomorrow to go away and work for six weeks so we went to The Beaconsfield for a few beers. Received a burthday card and cheque for £100 from Dad.

Wednesday, 12th April A disasterous first day's work. I spent about four hours walking around in which time I only got eight interviews and six leads. Earnt $28

Thursday, 13th April Seven interviews and four leads in two hours. A slightly better rate than yesterday. In the evening, I went round door to door with no success at all so I decided to pack it in. Earnt $16

Friday, 14th April I went in to tell my supervisor that I was going to pack in the job but she increased my money and persuaded me to carry on working part time. I went for another job interview in the afternoon and start work in a margerine tub factory next week. Me, Roz and Linda went to The Beaconsfield in the evening.

Saturday, 15th April We all went to Swan Valley to tour the wineries. We only found one that hd free tasting so we didn't stay long. We ended up drinking in a pub and then going for a meal at the Vietnamese restaurant. I went to a party when we got back but left early as I didn't know anyone.

Sunday, 16th April Spent all day getting drunk.

Monday, 17th April Started work at Austral Group Packaging. Another boring job, this time operating a machine that makes margerine cartons. It is better than working at Steelmark. I have to work shifts - this week 3 - 11.

Tuesday, 18th - Friday, 21st April A pretty boring week. The whole day is wasted when working "afternoons"

Saturday, 22nd April Spent the afternoon in the pub and then went for a pizza in the evening. All got drunk again.

Sunday, 23rd April Spent the afternoon drinking again. First of all we watched The Great Escape while consuming a case of Fosters and we then went to The Newport for happy hour. I ended up going to Clancey's on my own. I think I enjoyed myself.

Monday, 24th April Had a day off work due to tomorrow being the Anzac Day public holiday. Had a hangover.

Tuesday, 25th April Played tennis in the afternoon. Started work at 11pm due to being on night shift all week.

Wednesday, 26th April My birthday. I went to bed after finishing work at 7am. Spent the afternoon lying in the sun. Roz and Linda cooked spaghetti in the evening but I couldn't really celebrate due to starting work at 11. Roz and Linda celebrated for me. We had a look at a Holden Kingswood station wagon that someone was selling for $2,700 today. I was keen on buying it but was out-voted.

Thursday, 27th April Slept most of the day.

Friday, 28th April Finished work at 7 and slept for a few hours. We started my birthday session at 5 in The Beaconsfield before going to The Norfolk and The Fremantle Hotel. We went for a Vietnamese meal and then back to The Fremantle Hotel where I was bought a "Green Nasty". This is the last thing I remember but we went to The P & O where I was apparently thrown out. A good night.

Saturday, 29th April A quiet day followed by a small session. Me and Roz were given a four litre box of wine by the bouncers of The Newport.

Sunday, 30th April Me, Roz, Linda, Paul and Carolyn went on a Wine Cruise. This consisted of a two hour trip up The Swan River, a wine tasting session at the Sandleford Winery, a huge meal and all the wine you could drink for about six hours.

It was a great day out and I don't remember too much about the journey home. We spent about two hours in Clancey's which I remember none of. I phoned home at 11pm.

Monday, 1st - Friday, 5th May A quiet week. I worked day shift, 7 - 3. We went out for a meal on Friday but no-one really got drunk except for Roz.

Saturday, 6th May It looks as if my tax rebate cheque has bounced leaving me $500 in debt to Roz. I decided to spend the last of my cash on a cassette plyer and start paying Roz back next week. We went out for a few drinks in the evening but I did not feel like doing much so I came back early.

Sunday, 7th May We arranged to buy a car today. We test drove a Holden Kingswood station wagon in the morning and decided to buy it. The bloke selling it phoned in the evening and said he would bring it round tomorrow morning. It costs $2,500. I do not know if it is a good buy or not but at least we now have some form of transport.

Monday, 8th May We collected the car and paid the money + $263 transfer fee, stamp duty and one years registration fee which also covers the minimum insurance. I now owe Roz $1141. On afternoons all week, 3 - 11.

Tuesday, 9th - Friday, 12th May Again, not much happened all week due to working bloody silly hours. I spent half of Wednesday while various doctors looked at the cyst on my eyelid. It is to be removed on 1st June meaning two days off work.

Saturday, 13th May Me, Roz, Linda and Tim decided to have a weekend away from Freo so we drove to Hyden to see Wave Rock. After about half an hour of sight-seeing, we started a pub crawl from Hyden to Narrogin stopping for a beer in every town on the way. We arrived in Narrogin at about six. Me and Tim hid while Roz and Linda checked into a motel and we all went out and got drunk in Narrogin's two pubs.

Sunday, 14th May We had breakfast at 10:30 and then drove North to York. We went to a couple of pubs and decided it would be worth coming back at some stage to spend more time there. We had quite a good drive back to Freo stopping in several pubs. Me, Roz and Linda went to The Vung Tau and got drunk again. Overall, a good weekend!
