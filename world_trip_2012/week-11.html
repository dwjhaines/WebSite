dwjhaines - 2012 week 11

Monday, 6th August Did not do a lot all day. We got up and had breakfast at 10ish. I walked into Tekek again at midday, a round trip of about five miles, this time to buy more bottles of Bacardi from the duty free shop at the airport. By the time I had carried four litres back to Bamboo Hill,  I was totally worn out so slept for a couple of hours. We went out at about 4:30 and handed in our forms at the dive centre as we will do our first dives here tomorrow. We met Regan and Annette for tea at 7 and were back in our room by 8:30.

Tuesday, 7th August We had booked to do two boat dives with B & J Dive Centre. The diving around Tioman is not great compared to where we have just been or where we will be going to but it is OK and we will do a few dives while we are here. We were at the dive centre by 8:30 and all walked back to the jetty to board the boat. It was a bigger boat than the ones we have been diving from recently and had about 18 customers and five staff on it. We dived with a Finnish guide - Jukka - in a group of four.

The two dives were nice enough without being too spectacular. We saw a turtle on the first dive and the sites had plenty of fish and coral. The vis was not great but there was enough to see to make the dives worth doing. We were back at the dive centre by 3:00.

Roz and I then dived the house reef in front of the dive shop on our own. Doing this dive unguided and with our own equipment only cost RM40 (Â£8). We failed to find the resident turtle but again, there was plenty of fish and it was all quite enjoyable.

We all got drunk again in the evening. It was a similar session to the one on Saturday - a few drinks on the balcony, a few more with tea, a few beers at B & J's bar and a few more Bacardi's before walking home sometime around midnight.

Wednesday, 8th August Another day of not doing a lot. Went out for breakfast and spent some time on the beach but spent most of the day reading or sleeping in the room.

Had problems finding anywhere to eat in the evening. Ramadan is still taking place and a lot of places do not open until an hour or so after sunset. Tonight, just about everywhere on the beach was closed until 8ish when we ended up in same place as last night

Thursday, 9th August Three dives again today. Like on Tuesday, we did two boat dives and the ABC House Reef. We dived from a smaller boat today which was better as it had fewer people on it. We were led by Christian and had a Malaysian couple with us making a group of six. The first dive did not start well as the Malaysian girl had problems with her ears and they aborted the dive after about 5 minutes. The rest of the dive was better (for us anyway). Quite a nice site with plenty of coral.

Everyone managed to get down for the second dive. Once again, a nice enough site. We saw two hawksbill turtles, one of which we stayed with for at least 10 minutes. As usual with hawksbills, it was not the slightest bit bothered by divers and carried on eating the whole time we were with it.

For the third dive of the day, we repeated the dive we did on Tuesday afternoon but this time with Regan and Annette. It was a similar dive but this time we managed to find the cuttle fish as well as "Gilbert" the resident hawksbill turtle.

Once again, we were limited in where we could eat and ended up in the same place we have been three times already since we have been here. The lack of choice for eating is one of the only drawbacks to Tioman we have found so far but it probably would put us off coming back again. We were back in the room just after 9.

Friday, 10th August Another three dives. The boat trip today was out to Labas Island. The first site, at the island itself was excellent. The vis was still not great but the coral was excellent and there was plenty to see. The second site was supposed to be Tiger Reef but it got changed to Golden Reef due to strong surface currents. As it was, we were the only group that managed to get in the water at Golden Reef as they had another change of plan when everyone saw the problems we had getting in. Once down to about 10m, the currents stopped and it was another very nice site although the dive time got shortened due to the amount of air used getting down. Saw loads of nudis on both dives.

For the third it was just Roz and me again, the others being too tired! This time, we found the turtle right at the end of the dive so spent 10 - 15 minutes watching him eat before we got out. A perfect way to finish our diving here. We washed out all our equipment and left half of it at the dive centre to dry and carried the rest back to the room.

By the time we were back at the room, it was already getting on for 6pm. We had time for showers and log books before going and joining Regan and Annette for another evening on the Bacardi. As usual, we had a few on their balcony before eating at the normal restaurant. We had one beer in the bar before returning to the balcony to continue drinking. Got back to our room about 2ish having somehow managing to hurt my knee on the way back.

Saturday, 11th August Did not do a lot all day again. We went for breakfast at 10ish and went out again to pay our bill at the dive centre at lunchtime. We also bought new booties (ours are falling apart and a lot heavier than the new ones we bought) and collected the rest of our gear. Apart from that, we did not leave the room until the evening. Lying in the room or sitting on the balcony is just as nice as sitting on the beach so there was no reason to move.

It was our last evening in Tioman. We finished the remaining Bacardi on Regan's balcony, ate in our usual restaurant (squid sambal for the seventh night in a row) and had a beer in the B&J bar but it was a relatively quiet and early night compared to yesterday.

Sunday, 12th August We were up just after 7 to begin what is likely to be a 26 hour journey to Malapascua in The Philippines. We left Bamboo Hill at 7:30 and walked to the jetty, stopping for breakfast on the way. The ferry back to Mersing was due at 9:00 but we got to the jetty by 8:00. It is peak season and the Sunday return ferries often fill up as well as being subject to last minute schedule changes. It was essential that we got on the ferry so we were not taking any chances. In the end, the ferry did turn up at almost exactly 9:00 and was not that full leaving ABC beach but it filled up completely by the time it left Tekek.

We were back in Mersing just before midday and walked to the Indian restaurant we had eaten in a couple of times a week ago, for lunch. We walked from there to the bus station. We had tickets for the 3pm bus and had about two hours to wait until we left. The bus journey from there to Johor Bahru was only a bit longer than two hours. Johor Bahru is right on the border with Singapore but it took another two hours to get across the border. First we had to transfer to another bus to take us to the border. We were dropped at Malaysian immigration and had to walk through to get stamped out of the country. We then boarded another bus which took us across the causeway to Singapore immigration. Once again everyone had to get off the bus to get stamped into Singapore before boarding the final bus to take us into the centre of Singapore. Each step of this process was fairly chaotic with long queues and no organisation. Having to carry all our diving equipment did not make it any easier.

We finally made it into the centre of Singapore just after 7 and walked to the MRT station to get the train out to Changi Airport. By the time we reached the airport we had come to the conclusion that we were getting too old for this type of travelling! It had been a tough day and we were still 12 hours from our destination. At the airport, we went to the Air Asia and British Airways customer service desks to try and resolve issues we have with flights later in our trip. Neither visit gave us a lot of hope that the issues would be resolved.

We got the bus out to the Budget Terminal, ate at the McDonalds and checked in for the 00:20 flight to Cebu. We had expected to have a long time to kill at the airport, but in the end only had a couple of hours. By the time we had checked e-mail for the first time in a couple of days and gone through to departures, it was already gone 11:00.
