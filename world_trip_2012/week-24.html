dwjhaines - 2012 week 24

Monday, 5th November We had agreed a 3:30pm check out from the hotel so had an extra few hours making the most of the pool, the Wi-Fi and the air-conditioned room before beginning our over-night journey to Sorong where we will join up with Black Manta again. We left the hotel at about 3:45 and got taken to the airport. We checked in without any problems for the 18:40 flight to Ujung Pandang (Makasar) in Sulawesi. The flight was slightly late taking off but this did not really matter as we were expecting a nine hour wait for the next flight!

Bali has been really enjoyable for the last five weeks. The diving has been excellent, particularly at Jemeluk and Tulamben. The break in between at Ubud was better than expected, and as always, Kuta was an ideal place to spend a few days drinking too much. We have been really lucky with the weather. October is supposed to be the start of the wet season. It has started getting a bit cloudy the last couple of days but still no sign of the rain which was a bonus. The food has also been really good most of the places we have been. We have mostly stuck to Indonesian food for our time here but the Italian food of the last three days has been a highlight!

We landed in Makasar at about 8:30. The airport had been greatly modernised since we were last here, or replaced by a newer airport - we were not really sure. Either way, it meant that we could collect our luggage and stay in the main part of the airport which still had some restaurants and shops open instead of having to wait outside for most of the night as we had half expected. After eating, we found some seats in the departure area and sat down to wait for our onward flight to Sorong at 4:30am.

Tuesday, 6th November The eight hour wait at the airport went reasonably quickly, I think because the airport was all a lot better than we had been expecting. We were able to check in and get rid of our luggage at half past midnight and found another row of seats to wait for our flight. The flight was slightly late again but not by much and we landed in Sorong, West Papua at about 8am.

We were met at the airport by Cedric who was also picking up ten others from the same flight. The twelve of us were all driven to the harbour and then taken by the speed boats to Black Manta. We were on-board by about 8:30. We had the same cabin as on the previous trip and also managed to set up our gear in the same place on the dive deck. All the other fifteen guests on the boat were travelling together and knew each other. Most of them were from Australia but there was also one Kiwi and a couple of Americans. Also unlike most live-aboards we go on, almost everyone else was older than us.

We were unsure whether or not we would be diving on the first day of the trip. Diving on the first day was not on the itinerary but we knew it was possible if everyone was on-board early enough and the boat could get all the required paperwork sorted out.

The boat eventually left Sorong at 1pm and, after a few hours sleep, we were able to do our first dive at 5:30. We were diving with Stanny again, the same guide we had on the previous trip. Also diving with us were Joey and Matthew who were both diving with very professional looking camera equipment.

This was a pretty good dive to start the trip. The dive site would of been very nice in daylight as it seemed to have a lot of very colourful coral and plenty of fish but it was just about a night dive by the time we got in. The highlight of the dive was the two wobbegong sharks that we have never seen before but are quite common in this area.

Everyone on the boat had had a longer, more tiring journey than us to get to Sorong so the first night of the trip was not a late one. I think we were asleep by 9:00

Wednesday, 7th November First dive of the day was at "Manta Sandy" or "Manta Spa". This was a brilliant dive. We all got into the water and went down to a line of broken coral at about 15m. From there, we all watched as huge mantas swam around the cleaning stations just above us.

We did not see as many manta as at Komodo but we were watching them closely for a whole hour instead of just getting glimpses of them as they swan past us. As well as coming very close to us, they were as big as any manta we have seen before.

The second dive, "Cape Kri" was a bit more challenging. It was a nice enough site but although the conditions were OK at the start of the dive, the currents picked up a lot after the first 10 minutes or so. We had been told in the briefing that we we would be diving the site in "perfect conditions" but this was not the case! It meant that we got swept along the site very quickly and then had to swim back into the current to continue the dive. The currents were worse as we came shallower meaning that we had to stay down at 18m and got through air a lot quicker than usual. I went from 120 Bar to 80 Bar in about 4 minutes and we all ended the dive after 46 minutes. There were a lot of fish around as well as a few sharks in the deeper parts and it would of been a very nice dive apart from the currents. Dive number three was similar to the previous one - lots of fish and lots of current. It was a bit better in that we did not have to do much swimming into the current but still used a lot of air just making sure we did not get swept along the dive site too quickly.

The night dive was quite good. It was nice to be diving without having to fight current after the two previous dives! We saw plenty of shrimps and crabs as well as another wobbegong and a brief glimpse of a "walking shark" - something else that we had never seen before. Once again, it was not a late night although we did manage to stay awake about an hour longer than last night.

Thursday, 8th November The first two dives of the day were at the same site - Mike's Point. The first dive was very good and at a part of the site that was sheltered from the current. Again there were lots of fish particularly in the shallows. We also saw our first turtle of this live-aboard as well as a few bump-heads and another wobbegong. After yesterday's dives, a lot of people on the boat were keen to do a second dive here instead of risking getting caught in current again. For the second dive, our group went in at the other side of the island, let the current bring us back round to the sheltered side and ended up in the same place as on the previous dive. This was another good dive with a white-tip reef shark at the start of the dive and large schools of fusiliers at the end.

The third dive at "Sardines" also had a huge amount of fish. There were a couple of sharks, a couple of wobbegongs but once again, it was the quantity of fish all over the site that made this a good dive. There was some current to swim into as we came back across the reef but it was no-where near as strong as yesterday and we were able to complete a one hour dive.

We only did three dives today. Once the third dive was over, the boat began it's two night journey to Manokwari and Cenderawasih Bay. We will have a days diving at Palau Amsterdam tomorrow (three dives again) before continuing overnight, arriving in Manokwari by Saturday morning.

Friday, 9th November We did two dives at Palau Amsterdam, both at the same site - Junkyard. These were not the best dives we have ever done. There was a small wreck to look around and a sandy slope with a few nudi's on it but not too much else.

After the second dive, it was decided to start moving towards Manokwari again and stop for the third dive on the way. This did not prove to be a great decision! The third dive was an exploratory dive i.e. we stopped in the middle of no-where, found a suitable looking spot and jumped in to see if there was anything there...... there wasn't...... not a single fish! We gave up after about 20 minutes of swimming around in terrible visibility over sand and continued on our way to Manokwari.

There was no night dive again tonight so it was a relatively early end to the day. Did not do a lot for the rest of the day apart from reading and editing pictures from previous days diving. Dinner was earlier than usual at 6:30 so most people had gone to bed by 8:30.

Saturday, 10th November We arrived in Manokwari, at the entrance to Cenderawasih Bay at 9ish. Cedric went off in one of the zodiacs to report to the police, the national park office and the harbour authorities. Diving in this area requires a lot of paperwork and there are a lot of officials that require "gifts" before permission is given. It is the first time that Black Manta has done this trip and will also be the last due to the hassle involved. Cedric was still not back by 11:00 but we got a phone call saying that it was OK to dive at "Shinma Maru" the Japanese wreck in the harbour. This was a pretty good dive. The vis was not great but the wreck was in good condition and there was no current. Stanny, Joey and Matthew were all either diving on air or had their computers set to air so left us half way through the dive but we had a full hour on the wreck. We did the same wreck for the second dive after Cedric had returned at 1:00. We went deeper at the stern on this dive and also spent more time inside the hulls. Again, it was an enjoyable dive.

We were supposed to do a third dive at another wreck not too far from the last one. Again, Cedric had to go ashore to get permission, this time from the head of the village nearest the dive site. This time he was unsuccessful. Despite the presence of a national park ranger who has to accompany us while we are in the area, the head of the village, who was drunk, would not agree to let us dive. This meant effectively that we have had two days in a row with only two dives and while the dives have been OK, they have not really been what we have come to this area for. Hopefully things will improve tomorrow!

Due to the early finish to a fairly frustrating day, we decided it was time to have a few drinks for the first time since Kuta. We had bought bottles of rum with us and got through one of them during the evening. The rest of the people on board are quite interesting to talk to. More than half of them are in some way connected to S.P.U.M.S. (South Pacific Underwater Medical Society) which is how they all know each other. Plenty of doctors and nurses on board! Despite the alcohol, it was still an early night and we were back in the cabin by about 9:30.

Sunday, 11th November A much better day today! After the problems of yesterday, Cedric decided to take the boat out to sea and dive around Tridacna Atoll. This meant we were no-where near land so there was no paperwork to sort out or permissions required. The first dive at "Mark's Treasure" was a nice enough wall dive. We had been told before the dive that there were turtles around and Roz managed to see three. The second dive at "Wild Fish Ridge" was excellent and one of the best so far on this trip. There were a few big schools of fish at the start of the dive but the hard coral all over this site was really spectacular. There was loads of overlapping plate coral and huge formations of barrel sponges. It was superb to look at and much more how I'd imagined the diving at Raja Ampat would be.

The third dive (Spag Point) was similar to the second but not quite as good. We did see another turtle however. The fourth dive was another "exploratory" dive. We stopped at the corner of an island and jumped in again without knowing if there would be anything there. This dive was a lot better than the one two days ago. There was enough nudi's and crabs to make it a reasonably enjoyable night dive.
