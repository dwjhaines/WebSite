dwjhaines - 2012 week 19

Monday, 1st October Two local dives today. For the first, we were at the dive centre by 8:30. The dive site was only about 10 minutes away by boat so we were in the water by 9:00. Roz and I were diving on our own with an Indonesian guide (whose name we did not find out) who did not speak a lot of English. The dive was not too spectacular. We were not expecting anything to match the diving at Komodo but there was a lot less to see here than at other places we have dived around Bali. It was still nice enough and the vis was excellent but just not a lot to see. We had a few hours back at the resort between dives during which time we made a few plans about what we will be doing for the next few weeks.

We were back out again at 1:30. Again, the dive was not wonderful but still reasonably enjoyable. We are in another place where the diving does not take up the whole of the day meaning Roz has plenty of time to visit the turtle hatchery! We had a few drinks in the evening. We had a couple of beers on the balcony while writing up log books before eating in the same place as yesterday. One more beer when we got back to the room and were in bed by about 11.

Tuesday, 2nd October Two dives today at Menjangan which were much, much better than yesterday's. After watching three of the turtle hatchlings being released on the beach, we had a one hour trip by boat to get to Menjangan. The boat was half full (five people on a boat that can take 10) so although it was small, there was plenty of room. We were supposed to be diving on our own with Wayan but due to a bit of confusion at the beginning, we were joined by Isabelle as well. We saw five leaf scorpion fish at the start of the dive before descending along a wall covered with soft and hard corals in very good condition. Not a huge amount of fish but a really scenic, relaxing, easy dive with lots of huge sea-fans along the way. Lunch was on the boat before the second dive which was probably better again than the first one had been. The whole dive was along a spectacular wall, again with plenty of spectacular coral formations.

We were back at the resort by 3ish giving us plenty of time before the turtle feeding at 17:00. Each evening, the hatchlings are given handfuls of cut up fish and the hawksbill gets whole fish. You make donations to the project when you go and see the turtles and another if you want to release one of the hatchlings.

We had a similar evening to yesterday but with a bit more to drink as we are not diving tomorrow.

Wednesday, 3rd October A day off diving. We were not too hung-over after last night's alcohol. I spent a lot of the day sat on our balcony editing the photos from the Komodo trip while Roz was at the beach. We got take-aways from a warung over the road for lunch but apart from that, did not leave the resort until the evening.

After turtle feeding time, we walked a couple of miles along the main road to a Balinese temple. The temple was not the most spectacular we have ever seen but there is some sort of festival taking place at the moment and we managed to get into the temple for a fifteen minute ceremony. We did not really know what was going but there was plenty of chanting and preying and it was all quite interesting.

We were back at Reef Seen by 7:30 and ate in our usual restaurant before a relatively early night.

Thursday, 4th October Two more local dives today but, before the first dive, it was time for Roz to release one of the turtle hatchlings. She selected one of the ones that are 2 - 3 months old and carried it down to the beach. She put the turtle about six inches from the water. It did not seem to know what to do at first but it eventually made its way into the water and after a few minutes it was swimming out to sea.

The first dive was a beach dive just in front of the resort. This was an OK dive without anything too exciting. The second dive was a muck dive a bit further along the beach and was a very good dive. We were dropped off by boat near a jetty and made our way back towards the resort. It was a shallow dive so we were in the water for 85 minutes. Loads to see including four ghost pipe fish and another frog fish.

Did not do too much for the rest of the day. We did get a taste of Balinese dancing in the evening. Local schools do a performance for tourists that takes place in the restaurant area of our hotel. I lasted about 10 minutes before returning to my beer on the balcony.

Friday, 5th October We did two more dives at Menjangan today. We had been unsure whether to dive again or not as the diving is quite expensive here but agreed that it was somewhere that we are unlikely to return to so thought we may as well go for it. This turned out to be a good move as the first dive was the best we have done here. There were strong currents but these bought in a lot of schools of bigger fish including huge tuna and giant travallies. There were three other English divers on the boat and at least two of them described it as the worst dive they had ever done because of the currents. We kept our views about the dive to ourselves! The second dive was also good - very similar to the dives we did on Tuesday in roughly the same part of the island.

We had a few drinks again in the evening - a couple of beers on the balcony before going out, a couple more with tea (at the same place for the sixth night in a row) and a few more when we got back. Both quite pissed by the time we got to bed sometime around midnight.

Saturday, 6th October After breakfast, Roz released a second turtle. This one was a bit keener on getting in the sea than the first one and headed off with very little help. The turtle hatchery has definitely been the one of the highlights of Roz's trip so far!

We paid our bill and stocked up drinks from the supermarket by the hotel as we did not know how easy it would be to buy things in Tulamben, our next destination. We said our good-byes to Amanda and Adrian and were collected by a car from Tulamben Wreck Divers just before 11. We had a three hour drive to get to Tulamben and were in our room just behind the dive centre by 2:00. We had booked to stay here for five nights but were thinking about staying longer. Once we saw the room, we were both fairly sure that we did want to stay longer. It is a big room with a large fridge and is a few metres away from the swimming pool. It is just over the road from Bali's most famous dive site, the Liberty wreck and we should be able to dive very cheaply here. As we have all our own equipment, and are happy to dive without a guide, dives should only cost about £5 each. We ate at the TWD restaurant and then spent an hour or so by the pool.

We had planned to go out at 7:30 to try and find somewhere showing the Sunderland v Man City game. However, all the electric went off in Tulamben at 6:20 and was still off when the game kicked off. We had a few drinks while sat on our balcony and went out anyway at about 8:30. The electric returned at about 8:45 by which time we were sat in "The Safety Stop". There was a T.V. in there so we were able to watch the second half. We waited in there after the game to see if they would have the Swansea v Reading game at 10:00. They didn't so we returned to the room and followed the game on the laptop while listening to Soccer Saturday.

Sunday, 7th October Did not do a lot in the morning. We had decided not to dive today as we wanted to have a few drinks with the football last night. We had quite a late breakfast and stayed in the room or by the pool until midday. We were told at breakfast that there would be a cremation ceremony taking place in Tulamben today. In Bali, the cremation is something to be celebrated. Many people are cremated at the same time and over a thousand people turned up to this one. It was a strange ceremony but fascinating to watch - we were there for about three hours in total.

Initially there was a procession with everyone going to the cremation walking through the centre of the village towards the cemetery. Most people were dressed in traditional costumes and were carrying some sort of offering so this in itself was a very colourful sight. When we got to the cemetery, a wooden platform had been set up. Two large bulls made from something like papier mache were carried in and put on the platform. A large funeral tower was then carried into the cemetery containing the bodies. The bodies were taken from the tower via a very precarious looking set of steps while live chickens were thrown into the crowd. The bulls were then cut open and the bodies were placed inside as well as most of the offerings. This all took a couple of hours and seemed very chaotic and disorganised.

We were expecting some sort of service or announcement but once the last of the offerings had been placed around the bulls the whole thing was lit and went up in flames within a couple of minutes. The whole thing had been very bizarre but also very interesting to watch.

We had not expected this to have taken up so much of the day - we did not get back to our room until 4ish. We went out to eat (at The Safety Stop again) quite early as we had missed lunch and had a fairly early night.

We think we have managed to book an additional four nights here meaning that we will stay until Monday 15th. However, someone else is booked into our room from the 11th and the place is pretty full so we are not 100% sure what is happening.
