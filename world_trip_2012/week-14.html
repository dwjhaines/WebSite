dwjhaines - 2012 week 14

Monday, 27th August We were up by 8ish, paid our bill at the dive centre and left the resort at about 9. We got a tricycle into Moalboal and were dropped at the main road to wait for the bus to Bato on the Southern tip of Cebu. We only had about 15 minutes to wait.

The bus was air-conditioned with empty seats so the 80 - 90 minute journey was a lot more comfortable than our previous bus journey. When we got to Bato, it was another 4km by tricycle to Liloan from where the ferries depart to Negros island. We arrived in Liloan at 11:03 so had just missed one ferry and had almost an hour to wait for the next one at midday. It was a 25 minute ferry crossing to Sibulan and another tricycle for the 6km journey into Dumaguete.

We only wanted to stop in Dumaguete to get money from a cash-point, something that went very smoothly. We then got our fourth tricycle of the day to Dauin, 15km away, the place that we were actually heading for. We were dropped at Mike's Beach Resort just after 1pm. All in all, the journey had not been as bad as expected. It took just over four hours and cost a total of P720 - just over £11 - for both of us.

We got sandwiches from the restaurant and spent most of the rest of the afternoon catching up on sleep after the last two relatively late nights. We also had our evening meal in the resort restaurant. The resort is very nice again and we have another nice room. However, it is a bit isolated meaning that we will probably eat most of our meals here but this is not the end of the world as the food seems pretty good so far!

Tuesday, 28th August Three dives today. The resort is fairly empty at the moment and the only other guests that are divers were having a day off so we were the only customers on the boat. We were guided by Ace, a local dive guide. Most of the sites here are proper muck dives - looking around black sand for odd fish. During the day, we saw several frog fish including one that was swimming - something we have never seen before. We also saw two more flamboyant cuttle fish, a sea-horse and three ghost pipe fish. The bits of reef that we did see had been largely destroyed by last years typhoon but parts of it were OK and there were certainly plenty of fish. Not too bad a day's diving!

All the sites are close to the resort so, as with Moalboal, there was not much wasted time. We came back to the resort between each dive and were finished for the day before 4pm despite doing three dives of more than 60 minutes. Again, all our meals were at the resort. We did go out for a walk to the centre of Dauin after the third dive but, apart from a market selling animal foodstuff, there was not too much to see. Had another quiet evening and were back in the room by 8:30. Spent a couple of hours editing and sorting out photos from Moalboal.

Wednesday, 29th August Today we had a three tank trip to Apo Island. It is about 30 minutes away by boat is is supposed to be some of the best diving in the area. We left Dauin at 9. On the boat was a Korean couple (plus one of their mothers) and a Chinese couple (plus one of their mothers). We were diving on our own with Gabby. The first dive at Chapel was pretty good. A wall dive to start with and then about 30 minutes in the shallows over a very scenic hard coral garden. Saw our first turtle of the day and quite a few large sea-snakes.

The second dive at Cogan was also good. Plenty of fish (and a hawksbill turtle) at the start of the dive, then some time watching a big school of jacks. Not quite the same numbers as at Barracuda Point but still good to look at for 5 - 10 minutes. The last 20 minutes were swimming around near the boat (our guide today seemed quite keen to get out of the water and leave us to ourselves as soon as possible for some reason). Saw another huge sea-snake and also the smallest Pegasus sea-moth we have ever seen. The third dive was probably the best of the day with some of the most pristine spectacular hard coral we have ever seen. Had three quality turtle sightings.

We were back by about 4:30 and once again did not do a lot for the rest of the day. There was a bar-b-q fish buffet at the resort restaurant in the evening which was very good but it was another early night.

Thursday, 30th August Three more local dives today and once again, we were the only customers on the boat (the Chinese are not staying here and the Koreans only dive every other day). The dives were very good again and were mainly muck dives. We had another new guide today - Marlon. As with two days ago, there was plenty to see. We saw two more ornate ghost pipe fish on the first dive as well as a sea-horse. The third dive was probably the best. We were looking for octopus - there are supposed to be seven different types at this site. We saw three more flamboyant cuttle fish all in a group and finally found a mimic octopus as we were heading back to the boat at the end of the dive. Roz and I spotted it, not the guide. Only the second one of these we have ever seen.

A very early end to the diving today as we were back at the resort by 3:15. Had a similar afternoon/evening to the previous ones here.

Friday, 31st August Another three tank trip to Apo Island for our last day of diving on this part of the World trip. We were on the boat with the Korean couple again (plus one of their mothers) who just did the first two dives. We were diving with Marlon and the Koreans with Ace, although it was unclear who was with who on the two dives that they did!

First dive was excellent. We went in at Rock Point East and swam round the point, ending up at Rock Point West. Found a very photogenic frog fish at about 33m. As we went round the point, at about 10m, we came to an area that seemed to have turtles everywhere. There were five that could be seen at the same time and we also saw another two. They were all sea turtles and were quite laid back about divers so we were able to get close for photos. Second dive was starting from the point that the first ended. The dive was over the same spectacular hard coral that we have seen here before and we spent time with two more turtles (one sea turtle and one hawksbill). The third dive was also very good. It started as a drift dive in quite strong current. Loads of fish around including quite a few giant trevally and big schools of red-tooth trigger fish and pyramid butterfly fish. Once we came out of the current, we were back to the coral and found our final two turtles. Again, it was one sea turtle and one hawksbill. A good dive to end on!

Back at the resort by 4:30, we washed all our gear and hung it up to dry. Another quiet evening although we did have a few beers - our first alcohol since Sunday. We had a couple on our balcony while doing logbooks and a then few more with tea.

Saturday, 1st September We could not dive today as we fly to Hong Kong tomorrow lunchtime. We did not have anything planned for the morning - Roz went to the pool while I finished editing the photos from the last few days of diving. By about 10:30, it had clouded over and 15 minutes later it started raining. The rain continued for most of the day. We had planned to go into Dumaguete at 2:00. By 2:30, there was no sign of it easing but we had nothing else to do, so we went for it anyway.

We walked up to the main road and got a jeepney into the centre of Dumaguete. After lunch at "Jollibee's" (a Filipino version of McDonalds) we had a walk around the town. It may of been due to the weather but no-where looked particularly appealing - the town certainly did not seem as nice as Lonely Planet had implied. We had planned to have a few beers but could not find anywhere that we wanted to sit. I did manage to get my haircut. Haircut plus eye-brow, nose and ear trim plus face and neck massage for just under 70p - one of the best haircuts I have ever had!

We walked out to the main road back to Dauin and did find a reasonable bar for a couple of drinks. We also bought some rum for later tonight before catching a bus back to Dauin. Got back to Mike's just after 6 by which time it had finally stopped raining. We had a few drinks on our balcony and a few more with our meal. We were back on the balcony by 10 to drink the rum while following the Swansea v Sunderland game on the laptop. Only vaguely remember the end of the game which finished 2 - 2.

Sunday, 2nd September Checked out of Mike's Resort just after 10 and had booked a taxi to the airport, about 20 minutes away. We had not been that impressed when we had arrived here but really enjoyed our stay in the end. The diving has been excellent and although we have had to eat in the same place just about every meal, the food has been very good and it is another place that we would happily come back to.

We had a taxi booked to Dumaguete airport for 10:30 and were there by 11. We checked in for our Cebu Pacific flights to Hong Kong via Manila without any problems. The flights were both on time and fairly uneventful. We had a couple of hours in Manila airport where we had lunch and got into Hong Kong at 6:40.

After collecting luggage and getting money out, we got the bus to Hong Kong Island. The bus went all the way from the airport to North Point and the end of its route was about 50m from the Ibis hotel that we had booked some time ago. The hotel room was a bit small but did have very good views of Hong Kong harbour.

We had showers and went to eat in a restaurant near the hotel. There is not much English on the menus of the local Chinese restaurants but we managed to get noodle soup and fish ball type meals which were not too bad. We bought a few beers from a 7/11 and returned to the room to drink them.
