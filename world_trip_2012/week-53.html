dwjhaines - 2012 week 53

Monday, 27th May We did three dives today back at Balicasag Island. There was only one other customer on the boat - Akitoshi, an OW diver from Japan as well as Edgar, our guide. The first dive was at "Diver's Heaven" a spectacular wall. We saw a turtle within about 30 seconds of going down and also got sent down to 31m to see a giant frog fish.

The second dive was at Royal Garden again, the site we dived first yesterday. We went in the other direction today after seeing the jacks and it was a better dive. There were loads of turtles at the end and Roz counted 14 in total. For the third dive, it was just the two of us so Roz chose to dive the same site again. No jacks this time and not as many turtles but there seemed to be more fish and it was another good dive.

We were back at the hotel by around 4:00. We finished the pizza from last night on our balcony and I went out to get my hair cut. We had a few drinks in the evening. Alcohol is still very cheap here so we sat on our balcony with a bottle of rum before going out to eat. We ate at Graziella's again although we had pasta instead of pizza tonight, and then returned to the balcony to finish the rum while following the play-off final on the lap-top.

Tuesday, 28th May Did not do too much all day today. We got up late and went out for breakfast. While out, we bought tickets for the 7:05 ferry back to Cebu for tomorrow morning and went to the dive centre to pay our bill. The weather was poor today so most of the rest of the day was spent in the room reading or editing photos. We went out to eat relatively early  - pizzas at Graziella's again - and were back in the room by 8:00.

Wednesday, 29th May We were awake just after 5:00 as we had a taxi booked for 5:45. The taxi was there early and by 5:40 we were on our way into Tagbilaran. We were dropped at the ferry terminal and we checked in for the 7:05 ferry back to Cebu. The ferry left a few minutes late and got us back to Cebu City just after 9:00. We got a taxi from the port to the Southern bus station.

Roz sat at the bus station for almost an hour while I went out to sort out money and pick up some other things that we needed. The ATM's in Alona all charged to take out money so I wandered round Cebu until I found one that was free. Using two transactions with each of our cards I was able to take out the £600 worth of pesos that we will need for the rest of our stay in The Philippines. I also bought more Doxycycline as my boil has not totally cleared up yet and I finished the previous course of antibiotics today. I think it is fine but taking Doxy for another week will not do any harm and I want to ensure the infection does not come back.

We eventually got onto a bus heading for Moalboal at about 10:30 and it pulled out of the bus station about 10 minutes later. We were on a non air-conditioned bus but it was a lot more comfortable than the one we were on when we did this journey last August. There were not as many people on it and we were able to have three seats between the two of us. It did not rain on this journey which meant no-one closed the windows which also made it a much better journey. We arrived at Moalboal at about 1:15 and got a motorbike tricycle thing out to the beach. By 1:30 we had checked into the same room we had been in last time at Quo Vadis Resort. This was one of our favourite places during the first part of the trip so we booked another nine nights here.

We had lunch in the resort restaurant and then spent an hour or so by the pool. In the evening, we ate out at "The Last Filling Station", the restaurant we had eaten in most evenings when we were here last time. We had pizzas again. We will try and be more adventurous tomorrow!

Thursday, 30th May Three dives today. The morning dive was at Moalboal Bay. It was a wall dive (most of the diving here is on walls). A nice enough dive although the vis was not as good as when we were here last year. We saw a couple of turtles, and two pygmy sea-horses. There were a lot more people diving than last time we were here meaning we were in a group of about 8 people (led by Dhodho) which was not ideal but still a good dive.

The second dive at 2 in the afternoon was over at Pescador Island, about 10 minutes away by boat. Again, the vis was not as good as last year but the big school of sardines has returned which made a spectacular start to the dive. For the third dive we were on the house reef just in front of the resort. We were joined by three other divers and after a lot of messing around, did not get in the water until about 5:25. This meant that really we were doing a night dive as it was completely dark after about 20 minutes. Not the best dive of the day! Still a good days diving with at least one turtle on each dive.

By the time we had washed our gear and had showers, it was getting on for 7:30. We had a couple of drinks while we did our log books and then returned to The Last Filling Station. We decided against being adventurous and had pizza again before going back to the balcony and drinking half a bottle of rum. Got to bed just after 11:00.

Friday, 31st May A similar day to yesterday. Three dives all along the walls of the island near the resort i.e. no trip to Pescador today. The morning and afternoon boat dives were at Lo-ok and Talisay. Five turtles on the first dive and one more on the second. There were less divers on the boat today and there were just three others with us and Dhodho. This was a big improvement on yesterday. For the third dive, Roz and I dived the house reef on our own without a guide. We were in the water almost an hour earlier than yesterday and it was a far more enjoyable dive because of this.

We also had a similar evening to yesterday. Beers on the balcony, pizzas at The Last Filling Station and then we finished the other half of the bottle of rum.

Saturday, 1st June Another similar day with three more dives. Our first dive was back at Pescador Island. We dived "The Cathedral" this time and it was probably the best dive we have had since getting here. There were only two other fun divers on the boat today, Dirk and Anke, and the visibility at Pescador was a lot better than a couple of days ago. The cathedral is a big cave type thing from about 15m going down to just below 30m. There are stunning views looking out from inside the cave. On the wall after leaving the cave, we saw an electric clam, the frog fish and another turtle.

The second dive in the afternoon was back on one of the local walls. A very good dive again with five turtles and a robust ghost pipe fish. For the third dive, we were on our own on the house reef again. We went in the opposite direction to yesterday as the current had changed and we did see a couple of turtles this time.

We did manage to make it past The Last Filling Station in the evening and ate at Chili Bar. I had an excellent half pound cheese-burger and chips with a few San Miguels. As usual, we returned to our balcony for a few run and cokes before bed.

Sunday, 2nd June Another excellent dive at Pescador to start the day. The boat was quite busy again this morning but it was only Roz and I that were fun diving (although Dhodho had his son, Bogs, diving with him this morning). Most of the other customers on the boat were on courses or snorkelling. We spent the first 40 minutes of the dive watching the sardines. They had been quite spectacular a couple of days ago but today the vis was a lot better and there seemed to be ten times as many of them. This was a superb experience as they kept swarming around us. The remainder of the dive was also pretty good with plenty to see.

By the time it cam to the afternoon dive, we were the only two customers on the boat. We dived at Tongo. It was not as good as this mornings dive but we still saw a pygmy sea-horse and our only turtle of the day. We only did two dives today so were finished by about 3:30. Did not do a lot for the rest of the day but did manage to go out for a meal that did not consist of pizza or burgers.
