dwjhaines - 2012 week 26

Monday, 19th November We were up by 7 and walked to the airport to check in for the 9:40 flight to Penang. We had roti canai for breakfast at the food centre and before going through and waiting at the gate for our flight. It was only a 35 minute flight to Penang but we were slightly delayed in taking off and we landed just before 11:00. After collecting our luggage, we got a taxi into Georgetown and to the Bay View Hotel where we had booked a room for a couple of nights. We had a room upgrade for some reason so have a very nice room on the 14th floor.

Georgetown is one of only a few places that we will have stayed in each of the five times we have have gone travelling for a year or so. We went out for a look around after checking in. We walked to the Kontar shopping centre stopping for lunch on the way back. I got my hair cut after eating but once again, we did not do a lot for the rest of the afternoon. We had planned to have a few drinks this evening but neither of us felt like drinking so we ate out in Little India and had another early night.

Tuesday, 20th November A day off travelling today. One of the highlights of staying in Penang is the food. We are staying near Little India so it was the usual Roti Canai for breakfast. Did not do a lot in the morning - Roz spent a couple of hours by the pool while I finished sorting out the photos from the Raja Ampat trip. We went out again for lunch at the same Indian restaurant that we had breakfast in.

We then spent a couple of hours wandering around some of the sights of Georgetown. We had a couple of things to sort out on the way. We booked a minibus to Had Yai for tomorrow, posted postcards, went to second hand bookshops, etc.

The sights are nothing too spectacular but the whole town is reasonably pleasant to wander around. Little India and the Chinese area just about merge into each other and there are plenty of colonial buildings to see as you walk around them both. We walked out to the fort (which once again we could not be bothered to go into) and past the City Hall and Town Hall before returning to the hotel.

Spent the evening making the most of the cheap phone calls (3p a minute with our Malaysian SIM) before going back out to Little India again. For the second night running we ate at Sri Ananda Bahwan, the restaurant recommended to us by Angali in 2007. Once again, the food was excellent. We had a beer with the meal but it was another early night.

Wednesday, 20th November Did not do a lot all morning. The weather was pretty miserable so we went out for breakfast but spent the rest of the morning in the room. We checked out at 11:45 and had a five minute wait before a car turned up to collect us. The car took us to The Swiss Hotel where we had to wait about 15 minutes for our minibus to Had Yai, just the other side of the Thai border.

On the minibus we were told by an Australian that we would only be allowed to enter Thailand for 15 days as we were entering via a land border. He did not seem to know what he was talking about a lot of the time but as soon as he said this, it seemed likely to be true. The minibus spent almost an hour driving around Penang looking for his remaining passengers and we finally left the island just before 1pm.

We had a brief stop before the border where a few of us had an argument with the driver who wanted us to pay about 20p to get our Thai entry cards typed out. Not a huge amount of money but just a rip-off nevertheless. The Aussie bloke kept telling us it was a legitimate fee when it clearly wasn't!

At the border, the Thai immigration officials confirmed that we would only be allowed into the country for 15 days. This is not the end of the world but means a load of hassle. We will almost certainly have to exit Thailand and return to Malaysia at some point in December. We had no idea about this rule - when you enter Thailand by air you are automatically given 30 days. There was nothing we could do about it so we re-boarded the minibus and continued to Had Yai. (We told the Aussie we had got 30 days to shut him up)

We got to Had Yai at about 3:30 Thai time and were dropped by the bus station. It was raining heavily when we arrived so we got a taxi to the train station which is in the area where we want to stay. We checked into the Kings Guest House about 5 mins walk from the station and immediately started looking at options for our "visa run" to Malaysia. It looks as if we have a choice between flying to KL for a day or just going to the border and back in a minibus from Lanta, a round trip of about 13 hours. Neither option is particularly appealing!

We went out and booked our minibus tickets to Koh Lanta for tomorrow and went out again to eat at 7ish. We managed to find the bar/restaurant we ate in last time we were in Had Yai but gave the jugs of Chang a miss this time.

Thursday, 21st November Usually if we travel anywhere in Thailand, we try and use the government buses. These are cheaper than privately owned trips and you always know exactly what you are getting. Unfortunately on some routes, particularly if you are heading out to islands, you are just about forced to use mini-buses. Sometimes these are fine but you have to buy tickets from travel agencies and they consistently lie to you about what you are buying and journeys always seem to take a lot longer than they should. Today's journey was typical.

We checked out of the hotel just after 8:00 and took our luggage to where we had bought the ticket for today's journey to Lanta. We went and got breakfast and were back at the office to get on the minibus just before 9:00. We were driven to the bus station where we got on another minibus which drove around Had Yai for 20 minutes before driving to another travel agent opposite our hotel. At 9:30 we finally departed. We had been told that we would be taken to Trang where we would get another mini-bus to Lanta. At about midday, we went past Trang without stopping. We asked the driver where he was going and he said Krabi. We had no desire to go to Krabi as it is out of our way and there did not seem to be any guarantee there would be any transport to get us to Lanta when we got there. The driver turned round briefly and made a couple of phone calls but then just told us he was going to Krabi. The other Western tourists on the bus did not have a clue where they were going and he could of taken them anywhere so they were not much help.

We decided to stay on the bus and get out at the turning for Lanta. At this point, we were still about an hour from Krabi but only 37km from Lanta. We were stood at the side of the road hoping for a bus while trying to hitch a lift. After about 10 minutes someone stopped and gave us a lift to the first ferry crossing (there are two short car ferry crossings to get to Lanta). We were not sure how far out of his way he went as he could not speak a word of English but it was really good of him to stop for us and it meant that we were only about 8km from where we wanted to go. We walked onto the ferry for the 10 minute crossing to Lanta Noi. As we walked off the ferry, one of the first cars off stopped and offered us a lift. Again, no English was spoken but we got taken to the next ferry and on to Saladan, the main town of Lanta Yai. We were in Saladan by 3 which is a lot earlier than we would of been if we had stayed on the bus.

We bought a few things from a supermarket and then got a motorbike taxi to Kaw Kwang where we are staying for the next week. We were given an upgraded room which is about twice the size of the one we had booked. We were planning to go somewhere else for a week when we leave here but as soon as we arrived, we started thinking about extending our stay here. After an hour by the pool I went for a run along the beach. We are at the end of a bay about 1.5 miles and running along the beach is reasonably enjoyable if a bit hot.

We were in need of a few drinks after the last couple of days. We had a couple on our balcony before eating at Hans' Restaurant, about a 10 minute walk along the beach. We had a bottle of Sang Som with our meal and then another in a new bar that had opened on the beach about half way back. I only vaguely remember leaving there.

Friday, 22nd November Did not do a lot all morning but walked into Saladan at 1:00 for lunch. We stopped to find prices at a couple of dive shops before eating at Lanta Seafood. During lunch, we just about worked out what we will do for the next couple of weeks. We will try and extend our stay at Kaw Kwang until December 5th. We will leave early on the 5th and travel down towards the Malaysian border (probably by mini-bus again). Once we have left and re-entered Thailand, we will start heading North immediately as we want to be on Phi Phi by the 6th. The 5th will not be a good day but doing it this way means that only one day is wasted and it is a lot cheaper than flying anywhere.

We also decided that we would do three days of diving while we are here. We wanted to dive with Lanta Diver who we have dived with before particularly when we found out that Anjali is guiding for them again. However, another company are offering a three dive trip to Hin Daeng/Hin Muang whereas Lanta Diver only do two on their trip. It is a long way to go for two dives so we chose this option instead. We booked up three days diving with Kon-Tiki, starting tomorrow before walking back to Kaw Kwang. I was not feeling well by the time we got back. Not really sure what it was but felt cold and shivery and no energy to do anything. I went to bed at about 4 and only got up to eat in the Kaw Kwang restaurant.

Saturday, 23rd November Woke up feeling fine which was good news as we were collected to go diving at 6:35. It was a pretty good days diving. We were driven to a pier just outside Saladan and were on the boat by 7. The boat was quite nice with plenty of space. It was inevitably dominated by Swedes but most things in Lanta are! Breakfast was served on the boat as we started on the three hour forty minute journey to Hin Muang/Hin Daeng.

Hin Muang and Hin Daeng are two rocks near each other South of Lanta. They can be two of the best dive sites in Thailand but we have dived them when the conditions are rough and they can be very poor dives. Today the conditions were as good as we have ever seen them with no current at all. Both dives were very easy with plenty of fish.

We had been a bit concerned about our guide, Lisa, who was about 25 years younger than us and a lot less experienced. She tried to get us to end the first dive after 30 minutes as one of our group was low on air but managed to send him up with someone else so we could stay longer. It is much too far to go for 30 minute dives! After that everything was fine and we were all kept shallower so we could stay down for almost the full hour.

The third dive was at Ko Ha on the way back to Lanta. We dived at Ko Ha 6 which we have not dived before. This was a very nice dive again although it was starting to get a bit dark towards the end of it. We came up to heavy rain which lasted for about half of the journey back.

We were back in Lanta by about 6:40 and back at Kaw Kwang by 7:00. We had quick showers before heading out to watch Sunderland play West Brom at 7:45. The bar we used to watch football in no longer has the channel but they told us to go to D. R. Lanta resort where we could watch it in their bar. We ate at Hans again after the football and stopped for another bottle of Sang Som at Nong's bar on the way back to Kaw Kwang.

Sunday, 24th November We walked into Saladan for lunch at Lanta Seafood again abut did not do a lot else all day apart from sitting by the pool. I ran to the end of the beach and back again in the evening before we started drinking again.

We had a couple of drinks on the balcony before walking to Hans restaurant again. After eating we walked back to D. R. Lanta just in time for the start of the Swansea v Liverpool game. We went back to Nong's bar again before getting back to Kaw Kwang sometime after midnight.
