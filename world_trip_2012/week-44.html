dwjhaines - 2012 week 44

Monday, 25th March Did not get out of bed until about 10:30 after too many nights of not enough sleep in the last week. We had breakfast in the apartment during which things started going wrong with the electric. The trip switch started tripping every few minutes and sometimes could not be reset. Someone came round with heaters and blankets for us but after a lot of trying, he could not sort out the problem. Apparently the only electrician that can come is somewhere else today so no-one could fix the fault. It got left so that half the apartment does have electric but there is no light in the lounge, no hot water in the kitchen, no heater in the bathroom and the air-conditioning units, which may or may not be able to give us hot air, did not work at all.

We had a few hours of not doing much. As well as the cold, it was raining heavily and walking into Kotor did not appeal much. At about 2:30, we tried without success to call a taxi to come and get us. By 3:00 the rain had eased off a bit and we could make the 25 - 30 minute walk into town. We went into the old town and stopped for a drink in the first bar we came to.

By the time we came out of there, the sun had come out for the first time today so we could spend a bit of time walking around, seeing the sights and taking photos. We went to another bar before finding a restaurant to eat in. Once we had eaten, we returned to our first bar for a couple more drinks. It was a really nice bar - definitely the best we have found in Kotor -  and we would of stayed longer had the music not been too loud. We are all getting old!

We left the bar at 10:00ish by which time it was raining heavily again. We got a taxi back to the apartment where we drank a litre bottle of wine before going to bed.

While in the last bar, we found out that John Hill had died in hospital. We knew that he had fallen and broken his leg last week and although he had seemed to be recovering, he died at 4pm this afternoon.

Tuesday, 26th March We had another late breakfast and set off for Podgorica just after 11:00. We stopped in a couple of places on the way. First stop was Budvar. The Montenegro Lonely Planet described the Budvar old town as a mini Dubrovnik. This was a bit of an exaggeration as although it was nice in places, a lot of it was run down and in need of cleaning up a bit. There was certainly nothing anywhere near as spectacular as the Dubrovnik equivalent and I did not think it compared to Kotor's old town.

We drove from there 5km South to get views of Sveti Stefan, a small island linked by a causeway to the mainland that is pictured on the cover of our guide book. We did get very good views of it as we drove around but it did look a lot more appealing on the book, with clear skies and bright blue sea. In the grey cloud and murky green water, the hotel on the island looked as if it could be a prison. We stopped for lunch at a restaurant on the main road overlooking the island before continuing the rest of the way to Podgorica.

We arrived in Podgorica just after 4:00 and easily found the hotel where we had to collect our tickets. Unfortunately, the car park that we used on our previous trip here was now a building site and we spent the best part of an hour driving around trying to find somewhere else to park. Most places either had metered parking (max 3 hour stay) or where not the sort of places we wanted to leave the car. We eventually found a space and after conversations with a few locals, agreed that we could leave the car there and did not have to pay anything.

We had a few drinks before the game although it was only a few. It was Mark's turn to drive and there is a zero tolerance to drink driving over here i.e. you can not have any alcohol at all. We got takeaways on the way to the ground and were inside by about 8:30.

The game was pretty good overall but disappointing about the way we threw the game away by being crap in the second half. Overall, about what we were expecting really after hearing the team.

As with our last visit here, we were kept in the ground for about 40 minutes for no apparent reason after the game. When we were eventually let out, we had a 10 minute walk back to the car and a just under two hour drive back to Kotor. We were back in the apartment by about 1:30.

Wednesday, 27th March Weather was much better today so once we had all had breakfast, we headed off to walk to the castle above the old town. Roz and I had been up here before but Mark and Steve hadn't and we were keen to go up again anyway. We parked the car just outside the old town, walked through to the starting point and started climbing at about 11:30. It took about 45 minutes to get the top and we then spent half an hour or so taking photos, etc. We walked down again headed back to the apartment stopping at a supermarket for more food and drink on the way back.

We had a late lunch and a few drinks in the apartment before walking back into the old town. We had a few more drinks in a bar and then a couple of litres of wine with our meal in a restaurant before getting a taxi home.

Thursday, 28th March A fairly uneventful journey home again. We left the apartment at midday and walked down to where we had left the car yesterday (the road had been closed and we had left the car at the bottom of the hill). It took us just under two hours to drive back to Dubrovnik airport. We returned the hire car without any additional problems and checked in for the 15:45 BA flight back to Gatwick. The flight left about 75 minutes late and we arrived in Gatwick at about 7pm. |We said goodbye to Mark and the three of us got the bus back to the car-park. We drove back to Kingsclere, dropping Steve in Woking on the way and got back to the house just after 9:00
