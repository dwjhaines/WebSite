dwjhaines - 2012 week 35

Monday, 21st January Did not do a lot all morning. Roz and I got a taxi into Tulum in the afternoon. We went to the bus station to check bus times for Thursday and found a bank to take out the US dollars to pay for our hotel (they charge 5% if you pay in pesos for some reason).

After stopping for lunch, we walked out to the ruins that the town is famous for. The ruins are not the most impressive ones we will see in Mexico but the location of them, on a cliff overlooking the sea made them quite spectacular. We spent about an hour walking around the ruins and another hour walking back to the hotel.

We ate out at the same restaurant as yesterday but had a more sober evening as we are all diving tomorrow. We were back at the hotel by 8:30.

Tuesday, 22nd January Two dives today with Maya Divers, a dive operator based in a resort about 10km from where we are staying. Regan had booked it all and arranged for us to be picked up at 8:30. By 8:50, no-one had turned up. The receptionist from our hotel called the dive centre who had no knowledge of having to pick us up. They sent a taxi to get us and we eventually arrived at the dive centre at about 9:30. More good news once we got to the dive centre. A large group diving the cenotes had taken too many weights with them meaning there was not enough for us. We had to wait around while they went to borrow some from another operator. There were not enough weight belts around when they did get the weights so most of us had to dive with weights in our BCD pockets. It's fair to say that at this point we were very unimpressed with Maya Divers!!

Things did get better after this. We were taken out by boat to the first site. We dived in a group of seven, the four of us plus three others and were led by Jamie. He did a pretty good job considering that the group had very different levels of experience. He took the beginner up when he was low on air, let another pair go up on their own and made sure we had two decent length dives (68 and 90 minutes). The dive sites were nothing too spectacular compared to other areas of Mexico but they were both enjoyable enough dives. 

We were back at the hotel by 4ish and then had a similar session to the one on Sunday night.

Wednesday, 23rd January It was cold and raining when we woke up. We did not do much all morning. At 1:45, Swansea played Chelsea in the League Cup semi-final second leg. I had hoped to watch this in a bar over the road from us but it was closed when I went to have a look at about 1:30. Instead, we bought a few beers from the supermarket and watched the game on the laptop in the bedroom. We managed to find a very good site showing the game although we did have to have the commentary in Danish. The result means an unplanned trip back to London for a couple of days at the end of February!

We carried on drinking on our balcony after the game - it was still cold and wet so there was not a lot else to do - and were eventually joined by Regan and Annette. We all went back to our usual restaurant at 7ish. Had a very good meal again and the usual selection of cocktails. We returned to the balcony for a few more drinks before getting to bed sometime around 11.

Thursday, 24th January Did not do a lot all morning again. The weather was better today so it was possible to lie on the beach. At midday, Roz and I said our goodbyes to Regan and Annette and checked out of the hotel. Regan and Annette are staying another couple of nights.

We got a taxi into the ADO bus station in the centre of Tulum and bought tickets for the 13:20 bus back to Playa Del Carmen. We had lunch while we waited in the bus station and were back in Playa just after 2pm. We walked from the bus station to the ferry terminal and bought tickets for the 15:00 ferry to Cozumel. We arrived in Cozumel shortly before 4pm.

From the ferry terminal, we walked a few blocks South to the Aldora Dive Centre who we have booked both our accommodation and diving with. They seemed a bit more organised than the last operator we were with! We filled in the paperwork and left all our dive gear in mesh bags that they provided. We had arrived a day earlier than expected but they managed to book us on to a dive trip for tomorrow. We were collected from the dive centre by Brent and driven to the Aldora Villas where we will stay for the next 7 or 8 nights.

We walked to a nearby supermarket to buy things for tomorrow's breakfast and lunch before returning to the room for showers. We walked into the centre of town, about a mile South of where we are staying, in the evening to eat (more tacos). After two successive nights of drinking, we were both worn out again so it was straight back to the hotel after eating and a relatively early night.

Friday, 25th January Today was our first days diving with Aldora. After breakfast, we were driven by Brent to a pier in town and were on the boat by about 9. We were diving with Edgar (guide) and one other diver. It took about 30 - 40 minutes to get to the first dive site Columbia Deep) and we were in the water by 10:18.

The dive was excellent, a huge improvement on the other dives we have done so far in Mexico. The visibility was superb, the scenery spectacular with lots of swim-throughs and there was loads to see. There were five or six turtles, eagle rays and a nurse shark. We had just over an hour surface interval, spent mainly on the beach before the second dive at Santa Rosa Wall. This was not quite as good as the first but was still a very good dive with another turtle, another eagle ray and another nurse shark.

Aldora had advertised that they give long dive times. We were given bigger tanks with 240 bar of air in so were able to stay down for 68 and 80 minutes. The only thing limiting our dives was our NDL's so we will probably use nitrox from now on.

We were back at the villas by 4 and after lunch went out to dive the house reef. Diving here is free while we are staying here. The dive was not wonderful and we certainly would not bother doing it again if we had to pay. There was a few things to see however and it was just about worth doing.

We were both worn out again after the third dive. Roz had got up at 3am this morning to watch the tennis and was just about asleep as soon as we had washed our gear and had showers. I did walk into town briefly in the evening but it was another early night.

Saturday, 25th January Two more dives today (using Nitrox this time). The dive boat picked us up from the Villas just before 8. We were in a group led by Liangosta and were with four other divers. Both dives were very good again. At the start of the first, we watched 8 nurse sharks circling for about 10 minutes. We also had another eagle ray swimming past and more turtles. The scenery was quite similar to the first dive yesterday with plenty of spectacular pinnacles and swim-throughs.

The second dive was a long drift dive at 15 - 19m along the top of a wall. We still saw turtles, an eagle ray and a sleeping nurse shark as well as a lot more fish than on any other dive since we have been here. Also saw our first Cozumel Catfish of this trip. We were back at the villas earlier today.

After lunch, Roz spent an hour or so in the sun while I borrowed one of the free bikes and cycled out to the supermarket near where we stayed the last time we were in Cozumel. In the evening we had a couple of drinks before walking into town and eating at a restaurant recommended by Brent.

Sunday, 26th January A day off diving today. Roz was up at 3 again to watch the tennis. I was awake for some of it but managed to sleep through most of it.

We had a slightly later breakfast than usual and then spent an hour or so making bookings for our trip back to London for the League Cup Final. We managed to get our flights all booked. It will mean leaving Mexico five days earlier than planned and flying to Fort Lauderdale. From there, we have return flights to London. We will continue our trip as planned once we fly back to America.

Once this was all sorted out, we had an hour or two in the sun. The weather finally seems to be improving and it is getting hotter again. We both cycled out to Mega supermarket to buy stuff for lunch which we ate outside our room again. Did not do a lot for the rest of the day. Had a similar evening to yesterday and walked in to eat at the same restaurant.
