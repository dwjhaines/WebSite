dwjhaines - 2012 week 6

Monday, 2nd July Left the hotel just before 9. The journey to Bruges was slightly longer than I had expected but we were still there by just after midday.  We were staying at an Etap Hotel next to the station. The room was tiny but very cheap considering how close it was to the centre of Bruges.

We went out at 2ish after getting lunch from the station. We did not have a lot planned for our visit to Bruges apart from visiting the bars. The two we were keenest on were De Garr which we had been to before and 't Brugs Beertje which was closed the last time we were here. We walked to  't Brugs Beertje which was closed again but did look as if it would open at 4pm. We then went to De Garr to find that it was closed until July 14th. This seems quite a common thing for bars in Belgium for some reason - they randomly close for a couple of weeks during what should be their busiest time of the year. There were a few other bars listed in our guide book and we headed for De Kuppe which was good enough for a couple of hours. We went back to  't Brugs Beertje at about 4:30 and this was a superb pub. Our guide book rated it as one of the top 12 pubs in the world and it is definitely one of the best we have been to in Belgium. The pub itself was really nice and the beer menu had about 400 different beers on it. We stayed in there for at least 5 hours and do not remember leaving!

Tuesday, 3rd July We had breakfast at the station and went out on our bikes at about 11:00. We cycled into the centre of Bruges and locked up our bikes before wandering round the main squares and visiting a couple of shops. We then cycled out through Damme to Sluis, a small town just over the Dutch border. It was an easy ride of about 13 miles on a totally flat cycle path along the side of a canal. After looking around  the town, we had our first beer if the day in the bar recommended in our beer guide. It took just over an hour to cycle back and we were back at our hotel just after 3. This was our final bike ride of the trip, so I packed Roz's bike into the back of the car and left mine unlocked outside the station. It is in very poor condition, needs two new tyres and is not worth carrying home but it was still a sad moment when I left it!

After showers, we went back into Bruges.  Both the best bars in Bruges were closed today as 't Brugs Beertje was also shut for the day. We headed out to 't Poatersgat, another bar recommended by both our guide books. This was another very nice place with about 100 beers on the menu. We could not be bothered to move from here so spent the whole evening sampling the beers before leaving at about 11 in a slightly more sober state than we were in last night. My bike had gone by the time we got back to the station!

Wednesday, 4th July The final day of the first leg of this trip. We left the hotel at 10ish and drove to Dunkirque. We had a couple of stops to buy food and beer on the way and were at the port by about 12:45 in time for the 14:00 ferry. The ferry journey was uneventful as was the three hour drive back to Kingsclere. Arrived in a wet Kingsclere not long after 6pm.
