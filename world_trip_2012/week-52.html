dwjhaines - 2012 week 52

Monday, 20th May We checked out at 11:00and were transferred to Jemeluk Beach, about 25 minutes away. We have stayed in Jemeluk a couple of times before but had chosen different accommodation this time. The last time we were here, our room was not air-conditioned and we were far too hot. We are now about 100m down the road at "Ganesh Amed". We got put into a different room to the one we had booked and have one of the best balconies we have ever had. We are overlooking the sea to the front and also have views of Agung to the side.

We did not do a lot for the rest of the day. I felt quite rough and tired from last night and have also started taking Amoxicillin for the sting or bite which looks infected on my leg. I don't know what caused it but I noticed it on Wednesday and it has been getting gradually more red, painful and hot ever since.

We are not sure if we have the correct antibiotics for such things but they are the only ones we have and it is four more days before I get the chance to see a doctor or pharmacist. On a more positive note, the stomach bug that I have had for the last week seems to have finally cleared up.

Spent most of the afternoon sleeping in the room or on the balcony. We walked along to Eco Dive in the evening to book diving for tomorrow. We have dived with them a couple of times before and they are very cheap for unguided dives. We ate back in the hotel restaurant and I was asleep again by 9:00.

Tuesday, 21st May Woke up feeling a lot better than yesterday. The bite is slightly less painful so hopefully the antibiotics are working. We had breakfast, collected all our dive gear and carried it down to Eco Dive. Everything there seemed a bit disorganised but by about 9:00 we were on the boat heading out to Bunutan, the first site of the day. When we dived here last, there were strong currents and loads of activity down at about 30m. Today, there was a lot less current and not as much action. Still an enjoyable dive with plenty of colourful coral in the shallows.

We spent an hour back at the dive centre before going out on the same boat to Pyramids which is the site a few hundred metres from where we are staying. This was also a good dive despite the currents being different to what we have had before here. Previously, this has been a drift dive ending exactly in front of our accommodation but this time, we were swimming into slight current and only covered about half the distance. We did see three or four pygmy sea-horses and quite a few nudibranchs but did not get to the area where we regularly saw a turtle last year. Both dives started with huge patches of garden eels.

We were back at the hotel by about 1:30 and had lunch in the restaurant. Spent the rest of the day by the pool or on the balcony. We went out to eat at about 7:30 and managed to stay awake slightly longer than last night.

Wednesday, 22nd May A similar day to yesterday .We had talked about some of the other options for diving from Jemeluk. Jeff and Dawn had recommended The Japanese Wreck which is a beach dive but is a 15 - 20 minute car journey away. There is also a very good island about 15 - 20 minutes by boat away from there.

However, we have never dived these sites before so we would of needed a guide. Adding on the cost of the transport as well as the cost of a guide, meant the dives would of cost a lot more than the local dives so we did yesterday. We decided to do a repeat of these two dives instead.

There was still no great current at Bunutan but there did seem to be more fish around than yesterday. At pyramids, we were hoping to have another go at photographing the pygmy sea-horses but we got dropped in a slightly different spot and then got distracted by a hawksbill turtle so failed to find the sea fan that the sea-horses live on. (The dives today were 77 and 83 minutes which took my average time time to over 60 minutes for the first time)

Another lazy afternoon not doing a lot. We had a few hours by the pool and I finally caught up with the editing of all the underwater pictures. We ate just about next door to where we are staying and had another early night.

My bite has felt slightly better again today which does imply that the antibiotics may be having some effect. However, it seems to be oozing a lot of nasty looking pus which is not such good news. We have a couple of days without diving now so it should dry out a bit hopefully.

Thursday, 23rd May We had decided on a day off diving today so did not have a lot to do. At 10:30, we wandered along to Eco Dive to pay our bill and collect the gear we had left behind yesterday.

As we were about to leave the dive centre, John the owner, and one of the instructors, were both quite concerned about the lump on my leg which is still oozing pus and looks a bit of a mess. They both seemed to think that it needed immediate medical attention and that I should not wait until tomorrow as I had planned. About 15 minutes later, I was on the back of a moped being driven to Culik. Culik is about five miles away and is the nearest village that has a doctor.

When we arrived at the clinic and found the doctor, he diagnosed an Acute Furuncle which is a type of hair follicle infection..... otherwise known as a boil. He described it as a small problem, not a big problem. He cleaned it all up and gave me something that looks like black vaseline to put on it four times a day. He also gave me two sets of tablets to take, although these seemed to be randomly selected from a box containing a wide range of pills, and told me it should all be better in about three days. I am not totally sure that I share his optimism but we will have to wait and see. He charged me Rp470,000 for the consultation, cream and tablets which is about £30 so not too bad. My moped driver waited for me and took me back to Jemeluk. He charged Rp15,000 which is about £1 and seemed better value.

Another afternoon of not doing much and a similar evening to the last couple. We ate at the place we ate in a couple of nights ago and were back in the room by 8:30.

Friday, 24th May We checked out of Ganesh Amed at midday. We had booked a transfer to the airport, the start of a 21 hour journey to Alona Beach in the Philippines. It took a couple of hours to get to just outside Kuta and then another hour to go the last five miles to the airport. We arrived at the airport just after 3 and checked in for the 17:40 Air Asia flight to Singapore. The flight left slightly late at 6pm and landed in Changi  two and a half hours later. We had almost four hours at the airport but this went fairly quickly.

We had to clear immigration and enter Singapore despite the fact that we were only transiting. We collected our luggage and got the sky-train from Terminal 1 to Terminal 2 and then checked in for the Cebu Pacific flight to Cebu at 00:20 - the same flight we had used when we flew to The Philippines last year. Once we had checked in, we ate at McDonalds before exiting Singapore again and going to wait near the gate. I went into a couple of pharmacists to ask about the drugs I had been given for my leg (which still seems to be getting better) and they both seemed to think there was no problem or need for anything else. We had fast internet for the first time since Surabaya so could upload photos and diary while waiting for the flight which left just about on time.

Saturday, 25th May The flight to Cebu was quite empty so we managed to get three seats each at the back of the plane and did manage to get an hour or two's sleep. We landed slightly ahead of schedule at 3:45 and were through the airport without any problems just after 4:00. We even managed to get money out of the ATM. We had arranged a transfer with the dive centre and were met by someone when we came through arrivals. He told us that the driver had not turned up but took us by taxi to the port about 30 minutes drive away. He left us at the port and we went and checked in for the 6am ferry to Tagbilaran on Bohol. The dive centre had booked the tickets for us and sent us e-tickets. This all worked fairly smoothly and by 5am we had checked in and were sat in the waiting area. The boat left on time at 6:00. It was full but reasonably comfortable.

We arrived at Tagbilaran and were met by someone else from Philippine Fun Divers and he drove us to our hotel at Alona Beach. There is a hotel attached to the dive centre and we are in the Annex about two minutes walk behind it. It does not really compare to the last two places we have stayed in terms of location but it is a nice enough room and we were able to check into it as soon as we arrived which was a relief.. We went out and got something to eat for breakfast and also called in at the dive centre to book diving for tomorrow. We went back to the room for showers and then had a few hours catching up on sleep.

We went for a walk around the shops in the evening and returned to the dive centre to fill out the forms for tomorrow. In the evening, we ate at Graziella's, an Italian restaurant and had superb pizzas. We were not sure if they were really very good or just seemed good after a month in Indonesia where the food is not great. Either way, with a few beers, it was a very enjoyable meal.

Sunday, 26th May We walked down to the dive centre just before 8:00, left our gear and went for breakfast. We went back half an hour later and waited until 9 before getting on the boat. There were only two other divers, a couple from Singapore/Japan and as soon as they arrived just after 9:00, we departed for Balicasag Island. The island is about 45 minutes away and has the best diving in the area.

We did two dives at Balicasag with a one hour surface interval. The first dive at "Royal Garden" was quite good. We spent the first 15 - 20 minutes swimming with a big school of jacks. We were swimming into current along the top of a wall and I'm not sure if they followed us or if they were going that way anyway but it was a good start to the dive. We had been told that we would see plenty of turtles and saw two on this dive.

The second site at "Sanctuary" was a much better dive. This was a far more spectacular wall with really nice hard coral at the top, a giant frog fish, leaf scorpion fish and lots of turtles for Roz to chase at the end. We were back at our hotel just after 2:00 and after showering, finished off the remains of last nights pizzas. Did not do a lot else for the rest of the afternoon.

My boil is a lot better today as the doctor predicted. It had started looking better yesterday but my foot and ankle had really swollen up which was slightly worrying. Today, the swelling had gone down and the boil also has gone right down. There is still the wound where the pus is coming out but it all looks loads better than it did and looks as if it will clear up completely in a couple of days.

We returned to Graziellas to eat and had a very similar evening to yesterday.
