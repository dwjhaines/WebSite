dwjhaines - 2012 week 3

Monday, 11th June - France v England Left the hotel to look for breakfast at 9 but nothing was open. Went back to the restaurant next to the hostel at 10 and spent an hour getting an omelette each. As with a lot of places in Donetsk, the service is friendly enough but they just can not cope with the number of people here. We got the bus into the centre of town at about 11:30 and spent a bit of time walking around before finding a bar to sit in. We had decided to avoid the main gatherings of England fans and found a place recommended in Lonely Planet - a bar/restaurant whose name roughly translated as "3 Fat Guys". At midday, it was uncomfortably hot sitting outside in the shade so we had to sit inside. This was probably the nicest bar we had found here and also had the cheapest beer and good food. It was never full of football fans but there were plenty from both countries and was a very enjoyable afternoon.

We started making our way to the ground at about 5:30 (K.O. 7:00).  It was about a mile walk and although we did see a couple of minor scuffles between some Russian and England fans, all the problems predicted in the media never seemed likely to occur. At the ground we had to split up as, due to the UEFA ticketing system, my ticket involved going in through a different gate to everyone else. I thought I would be in a different part of the ground but are seats were very close and as there were a lot of empty seats we were able to sit together anyway.

The match was OK without being anything to get too excited about. Both teams seemed happy with the 1 - 1 draw in the end and it was a pretty good result for England. The atmosphere was not great. There were a lot less England fans than usual at tournament games, probably less than 500 French fans and the rest of the crowd seemed to be made up mainly of Russians. We got out of the ground with no problems and walked back to the restaurant next to the hostel where we endured even worse service than this morning but were just about able to get something to eat while watching Ukraine v Sweden. Went straight back to the hostel when this game finished as Mark and Steve have an early start tomorrow.

Tuesday, 12th June Mark and Steve left at 6ish as they had a flight booked to Kiev. We were travelling overnight by train and will join them tomorrow morning.

We checked out of the hostel just before 11. We did not want to eat in the same place as yesterday so we decided to go out to the train station, collect our tickets, leave our luggage there and then get something to eat. This did not go quite according to plan. We got to the station and found the queue for the tickets. I had already pre-booked the tickets using the Ukrainian Railways website and had a 15 digit Cyrillic alphabet booking reference. In theory, all I had to do was present this at the counter and collect the tickets. The queue moved slowly as each person was dealt with unbelievably inefficiently. I have no idea what some people were doing but some transactions were taking about 20 minutes. Most of the time nothing was being done but once the woman had started one transaction, she could not serve anyone else until it was finished even when it involved the person being served going off to get some other piece of paperwork. When I eventually got to the front of the queue after 75 minutes, it took 40 seconds for them to issue the tickets. It then took us another half an hour to leave our rucksacks in left luggage by which time it was 1:30 and there were no options for eating anywhere near the station. We got the bus back into town and eventually had our breakfast at about 2:30 at the same restaurant we had spent most of yesterday in.

After eating a meal we had another walk around Donetsk and went back to the stadium to take photos. It was still too hot for doing much walking about so by about 4, we bought a few things from a supermarket and got the bus back out to the train station. We had a beer there before collecting our rucksacks, buying a few more drinks and getting on the 19:10 overnight train to Kiev.

The train was very nice. We had booked two sleepers in a compartment of four. Probably the best overnight train we have been on but when we got on, it was like getting into a sauna. The train was sat in the sun with no windows open and the air-con not yet turned on. We waited on the platform until just before it departed and it did cool down once the air-con was turned on but it did stay pretty hot. There were loads of other England fans on the train although the two in our compartment were from Wales. They were England supporters and probably disliked Cardiff more than I did!  It was a pretty good atmosphere on the train and, despite the heat, we got to sleep by about midnight.

Wednesday, 13th JuneWoke at about 6:30 by which time the air-con had been switched off again. The train was due to get into Kiev at 7:28 and I think was only about 10 - 15 minutes late. We got the metro to where the apartment was located and once outside, texted Steve and Mark to come an let us in. It was another very good apartment - air-con, televisions, wi-fi, loads of space - and seems complete luxury compared to the accommodation of the last few days. It is also only a 20 minute walk from the stadium where we play our next match on Friday.

After having much-needed showers, we had breakfast and then set off to start our sight-seeing. We got the Metro back into the centre and headed for the main square which is also the main sight in Kiev. Unfortunately, this had been completely taken over by the UEFA fan park and was filled with big screens and loads of stalls promoting Coke, McDonalds, Carlsberg, etc. After managing to get round the park, we walked away from the main square and went to  a couple of churches. They were both quite spectacular - plenty of gold domes - and we spent an hour or so wandering around them and climbed the bell tower of the first for good views of the second.

After a couple of hours, interest in sight-seeing was starting to fade and we headed back down to the main square for lunch. We briefly visited the fan park to buy t-shirts and then went  for lunch. We went to the place where the next englandfans "event" will be held as we we thought it would be a good idea to visit before that started! We had a couple of drinks there with lunch and had planned to return to the apartment. However, we went to have a look at another couple of bars to see if they would be good for watching the football later, and ended up staying out all day. The second bar we came to was excellent and after sitting outside for one, we went inside to get a good table for the Denmark v Portugal game. We ate in there during the game before getting the metro home. We bought a few bottles of beer on the way and watched Germany v Holland back in the apartment.

Thursday, 14th JuneOnce we had got up and had breakfast, it was a fairly similar day to yesterday. We went out at about mid-day and got the metro one stop further to the river. There was not a lot of any interest at the river but we wanted to get the funicular railway back up to the centre. This was not as good as we thought it would be as it was totally packed and very hot without any good views. At the top we were back at the second of the two churches we had been to yesterday.

We walked to St Andrews Church and the spent an hour or so wandering down the street that has a lot of souvenir  stalls. It was all quite pleasant but there was not too much that we haven't seen in every other Eastern European capital city that we have been to.

We made our way back to the centre and walked out to collect our Free Lions from the fans embassy which had been given a very poor location this time. We had lunch in a restaurant near the main square and then went back to the pub that we liked yesterday (Bar Kupidon, a bar with loads of photos of a bear taken in different locations). We had  a beer outside again and then went inside for the Italy v Croatia game. Steve went to try and meet friends in the fan park when the game finished while Mark, me and Roz got the metro back. We had a beer by the metro station while watching the first half of Ireland v Spain and returned to the apartment for the second half.

Friday, 15th June - Sweden v England We were up early this morning to begin our sightseeing as we wanted to visit "Kyevo-Pecherska Lavra", a site with loads of churches and caves containing mummified monks. We wanted to get there early for two reasons - firstly to avoid the crowds and secondly, Lonely Planet said it was free to get in if you arrived before 9am! It took us a while to find the entrance to the cave which was reasonably interesting and then spent an hour or so wandering round some of the churches. We got the metro back to the apartment by midday for snacks and afternoon naps. The game tonight was not due to kick-off until 9:45 so we did not want to go out too early!

We left the apartment again at 3 and went to a bar in the local market. We had a few in there and then started to walk towards the ground. We wanted to find somewhere to watch the Ukraine v France game but as we got closer, everywhere seemed to be full or booked out for private parties for the Swedes. It looked as if we were going to have to watch it in an English bar paying London prices for crap beer but managed to get the last table n a local place next door. This was a much better option as it sold the same beer as Kupidon at much cheaper prices. We would of watched the whole game there but, due to the rain delay, we left at the start of the second half, just after 9 and made our way to the ground (kick-off having been moved to 10pm). There was a much better atmosphere at the ground than at the first game despite us being totally outnumbered by the Swedes. It was probably one of the best England games we have ever been to and the win leaves us nicely on course for the quarter-final in Donetsk!

We met up with Dartford Dave and his mate for a couple of beers after the game and got back to the apartment sometime after 2am.

Saturday, 16th June We checked out of the apartment at midday and got a taxi to the car-hire office in the centre of Kiev. We had thought we would have to go out to the airport but when phoning for directions, they told us we could pick up the car from near to where we were staying. After sorting out the paperwork we were given our car and headed off towards Chernihiv, about 100 miles North of Kiev. We got off to a bad start when the sat-nav tried to take us through the centre of the town which is completely blocked off by the fan park. The sat-nav is just about essential here as all the street names and road signs use the Cyrillic alphabet and, outside of Kiev, not many people speak English. Once we were out of the centre, there were no other major problems and  we reached our hotel in Chernihiv just after 3pm.

We had chosen Chernihiv as there are a few things to see and it has reasonably priced accommodation compared to the host cities. We wandered round the town and out to the park where there are several more gold-domed churches. We were all getting to the stage where we had had just about had enough of these by now but it was a nice enough town to wander around for an hour or so. We went out about 8ish to a restaurant/bar recommended in Lonely Planet and very good meals thanks to a local who translated the whole menu for us. Also tried a few of the local beers while watching the football. The football was far more exciting than expected with both Russia and Poland getting knocked out. A very enjoyable evening.

Sunday, 17th June We left the hotel by 6am as Mark was flying home today and needed to be dropped at the airport by 8. Kiev airport is on the East of the city so was en-route to Poltava, our next destination - an overnight stop on the way back to Donetsk. Steve had done all the driving yesterday so I did the first part of today's trip. About 50km South of Chernihiv, I was stopped for speeding. It is not clear what the speed limit is a lot of the time but I got stopped doing 97km/h we think the limit may have been 80. After showing all my paperwork to one policeman, I was taken to an office on the other side of the road where two others studied my licence. They managed to tell me that I was speeding and that I would be fined but after a few minutes they seemed to get bored and decided I could go. It looked as if they could not be bothered to fill in the paperwork and as I had not offered a bribe, they gave up. The whole thing only took about ten minutes and we got Mark to the airport with plenty of time to spare.

We said our goodbyes to Mark at the airport and the three of us continued to Poltava. The roads here are no-where near as bad as we had been led to believe and after a relatively uneventful journey we arrived in Poltava around midday. We checked in to a hotel that we had booked and spent a couple of hours catching up on sleep. Again we had chosen Poltava because it is not a host city so is reasonably priced.  However, it is only about 85 miles from Kharkiv where Holland play Portugal tonight. We had not planned to go to this but a few weeks ago, Steve was able to get cheap tickets so we decided we may as well go along - particularly as there is not a lot else to do between England games.

We drove out of Poltova just after 3:00 and were in Kharkiv at 5ish. We found somewhere to park about a mile from the ground and walked to the ground to collect our tickets. We then  got the metro into the centre. The main square in Kharkiv is one of the biggest in the world and should be worth seeing. However, as with Kiev, the whole square had been taken over by the fan-park. We walked through the fan-park but it was totally packed and particularly unpleasant. The atmosphere was quite good but because of the crowds, we were all keen to get out as soon as we could. We walked down to another square, stopping for pizzas on the way and then got a metro back to the ground. We were in our seats about 45 minutes before kick-off and although in theory, the seats had a "restricted view", we could just about see the whole pitch.

It was the first time Roz and I had been to a tournament game that did not involve England and we did quite enjoy it. The game itself was not bad, particularly the first half. It is a shame that Portugal got through but apart from that it was a good evening out. We were back at the car 20 minutes after the final whistle and on our way before midnight. Got back to the hotel just before 2am - earlier than expected but still a very long day!
