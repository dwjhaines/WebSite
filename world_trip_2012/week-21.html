dwjhaines - 2012 week 21

Monday, 15th October We were picked up from the villa at 11 and driven to Padang Bai. We were dropped off at the Water Worx dive centre just before 1pm. We left all our luggage there and went to look for somewhere to stay as we had not booked anywhere. The first place we looked at was the hotel we stayed at when we were here on holiday in 2008. They had air-con rooms for Rp280,000 so we checked in for four nights. After we collected our luggage, we went out for lunch and then spent a couple of hours sleeping off the effects of last nights rum.

We ate at Puri Rai, a restaurant that we have eaten at a few times when we stayed here before and had a relatively early night.

Tuesday, 16th October Two local dives with Water Worx today. We were put in a group with three other divers and were led by Wayan. The first dive was not great. We dived at Jepun and for some reason the water temperature was down to 18ºc in some places. It did warm up a bit in the second half of the dive but this was closer to English diving temperatures than those we have got used to in Indonesia. We did see two sea-horses on the dive but not too much else. We were not that impressed with our guide. People started coming up early due to being cold or low on air and he did not seem to know what was going on...... or if he did, he certainly did not let us know. We stayed down on our own to end the dive but by that time we had covered most of the site anyway.

The second dive was a lot better. David (the owner of Water Worx) came as well so the group could be split up, the water was back to 28ºc and there was a lot more to see. We saw a total of four octopus and a couple of leaf scorpion fish as well as a lot more fish than there had been on the first dive.

Spent the afternoon back at the hotel, either by the pool or in the room and ate out in the evening at a restaurant that we first visited about 17 years ago.

Wednesday, 17th October We were on a trip today to two dive sites about 30 minutes away by boat - Gili Mimpang and Gili Tepekong. Again we were in a group of five (not the same group as yesterday), and again we were led by Wayan. Also on the boat was Irene, a Swiss instructor. The first dive was at Gili Mimpang. We dived here four years ago and it was one of the best dives we have ever done. We had dived on our own with a guide, we had seen no other divers and had spent about 10 minutes with two mola mola. We were looking for mola mola again this time but did not see any. The currents here were much stronger than before and after moving away from the mola mola area, we tried to make our way round to the other side of the island. This involved pulling ourselves along rocks while fighting a mixture of up and down currents. We think Irene had been sent on the trip to take charge of things like this as Wayan would not of been able to cope! We all got round eventually but the others in the group were either low on air or unhappy with the conditions. They all went up with Wayan leaving us to complete the dive with Irene in the calmer shallows. Despite the currents, the cold water and the lack of mola mola, it was an enjoyable dive if not quite up to the standard of the one four years ago.

Having seen the problems on the first dive, Irene decided the conditions were not suitable for us all to be diving at Gili Tepekong so we headed back and dived near to where we did the second dive yesterday. This dive was also quite enjoyable and we finally saw a turtle after thirty-five consecutive dives without one! Roz also managed to spot a Pegasus Sea-moth.

We ate lunch at the dive centre before going out for a walk around Padang Bai. We successfully took money out of a cash-point, I got my hair cut and we started asking around about transport options for the next couple of days. We had always planned to go across to Gili Air for a few days. This is an island about 90 minutes away by the fast, expensive boat or about 7 hours away by the cheap ferry option. It is the place we learnt to dive twelve years ago. However, a few people have told us that the diving there is poor and we are starting to wonder if we want the hassle/cost of getting there and back.

An alternative option is to stay in Padang Bai for an extra couple of days and then go to Amed which is on the way back to Tulamben. We have stayed there before and liked it about six years ago. We asked around for a few prices for different boats but did not really come to any decision. Back at the room we looked into accommodation options in both Gili Air and Amed but still could not decide.

After log books on our balcony we went out and ate at Puri Rai again. We were back in the room by 9ish and asleep by 10 although I did get up at midnight to listen to the second half of Poland v England.

Thursday, 18th October Two more local dives today. We were with Wayan and one other diver today. Today's sites were easier and Wayan was able to cope without too many problems. The dives were OK without being great. The first dive was a nice enough reef and we saw another turtle (Roz saw two). The second dive was a muck dive but there was not too much to see. Both dives were very cold again but today we had borrowed full length wet-suits which made it a bit more bearable.

Back in the room, we came up with another option as an alternative to Gili Air. We had talked about visiting Ubud for a few days if we got bored of diving. We are not bored of the diving but as we did a lot more than planned in Tulamben, and we are going back to Tulamben, it seems like a reasonable place to go to have a few days out of the water. We both agreed that this was the best thing to do so sent a few e-mails to hotels. We paid our bill at the hotel we are staying in now and booked a car to take us there tomorrow at 11:00.

We went out and ate chicken sate at Puri Rai for the second night in a row and were back in the room by 8:30.

Friday, 19th October We had breakfast on our balcony just after 8:00 and then walked down to the dive centre to collect our gear. We had to go back down at 10:00 to pay our bill for the last three days and were picked up from our hotel just before 11:00. It took just over an hour to get to Ubud and by 12:15, we had checked into our hotel.

Ubud is the "Cultural Heart of Bali". It is packed with art galleries, spas, temples and places for watching traditional dancing. It is also a nice place to spend a few days relaxing and not doing a lot. It is a very busy town that has hundreds of tourists visiting every day but in the middle of all the shops and galleries, etc, there are quiet lanes with really nice hotels. We had booked one of these for three nights and have a room with a balcony overlooking rice fields.

We left our stuff in the room and went out to walk round the main streets of the town. We first visited Ubud in 1989 but there is nothing at all that we recognise from then! We walked down the street we are staying on and back up Monkey Forest Road, the main street, stopping for lunch on the way. We had a quick look around the palace that is supposed to be the number one sight here before going back to the hotel for a few hours by the pool and on the balcony.

We had a couple of drinks on the balcony before going out to eat. We found a nice place to eat near where we are staying but the atmosphere of the restaurant was slightly spoiled by the sounds of a pig being slaughtered across the road as we were waiting for our meals. We were both glad we had not ordered pork! We bought a few more drinks from the nearby supermarket (the first we have seen in a month) and returned to drink them on our balcony.

Saturday, 20th October Of the things to do here, the only thing that we were that interested in was walking round the local rice fields. The scenery here can be spectacular and our guide book has a couple of 3 - 4 hour walks starting from the centre. We headed out at about 9:40. The walk was all quite pleasant although it was probably too hot to be doing that sort of walk!

The book said that at one place guides will help you down the steep side of a hill to get to a river and sometimes farmers will charge you to cross their land which all sounded fair enough. When we got to this point, the guide wanted Rp150,000 (the book had said Rp5000 was about the right amount to pay). We told him that we did not need a guide and managed to make our own way down the path - it was not that difficult!

At the bottom, there was a woman who wanted Rp100,000 for us to use the path along the river. There was no way we were going to pay this so gave up and climbed back up the hill instead. We completed the walk by sticking to the roads and got back into Ubud by 1ish. We had lunch before getting back to the room and after much needed showers, we spent the rest of the afternoon by the pool again.

In the evening, we managed to avoid the Balinese dancing and found a restaurant showing Spurs v Chelsea instead. We had a couple of beers while watching the game and then returned to our balcony where we got through a couple of bottles of rum while following the rest of the football on the laptop (Swansea 2, Wigan 1). Got to bed sometime around 1:00.

Sunday, 21st October Did not leave the hotel until we went out for lunch at 2ish. We ate at the same place as yesterday and wandered round a couple more sights that were just about on the way back to the hotel.

We went out again at 4:30 and walked to the monkey forest. This is an area with a few more temples in a forest with plenty of monkeys. It was just about empty going at this time as most of the day-trippers had left and it was a reasonably nice place to wander around. The temples were quite small but the jungle type setting made it all quite interesting. The monkeys here were quite cute compared to at some places we have been although one did jump on to Roz's shoulder in an attempt to get her camera!

We were late going out to eat as we wanted to find somewhere that would be showing the Sunderland v Newcastle game at 8:30. We were not keen on the place we had been in last night as the food was not great but managed to find somewhere better just round the corner from it. We stayed there until the end of the game and were back in our room by 10:45.
