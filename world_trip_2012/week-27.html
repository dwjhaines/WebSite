dwjhaines - 2012 week 27

Monday, 26th November The start of a few days without a lot to do. Spent most of the day reading or lying by the pool. The weather was not great - we had quite a lot of rain and cloud during the day. Ate lunch at Kaw Kwang and walked into Saladan to eat at Lanta Seafood in the evening. We managed to get confirmation today that we can stay in our room for an extra 6 nights so will be here until December 5th.

Tuesday, 27th November Another cloudy day with not too much sun The rainy season should of finished by now but it seems to be lingering. Plenty of rain again in the afternoon. Could not be bothered to walk into Saladan for lunch so ate at the resort again.

We had a couple of drinks on the balcony while waiting for the rain to stop in the evening. It was dry by about 8 so we were able to walk along the beach to Hans. We had a bottle of Sang Som with our meal and another at Nong's on the way back. Ended up a lot more drunk than we had planned to!

Wednesday, 28th November Spent most of the morning in bed recovering from last night. It was sunnier today so we could spend a few hours by the pool again in the afternoon. Did not really do a lot else. It rained again in the evening. We waited for it to stop before walking into Saladan to eat again.

In the last few days, we have just about finalised our plans for the last two and a half months of our trip before we return home to look for work. We have managed to get a few things booked as well.

Thursday, 29th November Three more dives today. We were picked up at about 7:50 and were driven to the boat again. We had booked a three dive trip  Ko Ha, a group of five or six islands just under two hours away (Ha means five but there is a sixth island in the group). The boat was fuller today. Ko Ha is easier diving so plenty of students, DSD's and snorkelers were on the boat.

We were in a better group today with an instructor who knew the sites (Anton) and two other experienced divers. All three dives were pretty good. Ko Ha is not the most spectacular of sites but there was enough to see, including lots of little yellow box fish and it was another enjoyable day's diving.

We were back at Kaw Kwang by 5:30. We had a couple of drinks on the balcony while writing up log books. Could not be bothered to go out any where to eat so ate in the Kaw Kwang restaurant and had an early night.

Friday, 30th November Another quiet day. We spent the morning by the pool and then spent an hour or so writing postcards to be sent instead of Christmas cards. We walked into Saladan for lunch at Lanta Seafood again. We posted the postcards, took out money, bought a Thai SIM card and paid for next week's visa run. We have paid Baht1100 (about £22) each to go on a minibus all the way to the Malaysian border and back next Tuesday a journey of about 11 hours if we are lucky. We really needed to go on the Wednesday but the trip only goes every other day at the moment. The organised trip will be a lot easier than trying to do it ourselves on the Wednesday but means that we will overstay by one day when we leave Thailand to fly home. Apparently, Thai immigration are not bothered if the overstay is only one day so we should not have any problems! We stocked up on drinks (and festive Pringles) and walked back to Kaw Kwang again.

I ran along the beach in the evening but by the time I had come out of the shower, it was raining heavily again. Still no sign of the wet season ending! We had planned to walk to Hans to eat but again decided to eat at Kaw Kwang due to the weather. Food is very good at Kaw Kwang so not the end of the world!

Saturday, 1st December Our third and last days diving with Kon-Tiki. A similar day to last Saturday i.e. a 6:40 pick-up and three dives at Hin Muang, Hin Daeng and Ko Ha. All three dives were very good. The conditions at Hin Muang/Hin Daeng were excellent again. There was a bit more current than last week but better visibility making the dives slightly better. We dived at Ko Ha #1 on the way back. We have dived this site many times but not for a few yeas. Neither of us could remember the chimney that was probably the highlight of the dive so not sure if we have dived the same part of the island before. Overall, Kon-Tiki have been pretty good to dive with although the shortened dive times mean that we probably would not choose to dive with them again.

We were back about half an hour later than last week so were not back in the room until about 7:30. We had a couple of beers on the balcony before walking to Hans to eat. We went to D. R. Lanta hoping to watch Arsenal v Swansea but they were already showing a different game which one person wanted to watch.

Someone there told us of another bar that may show it further down the beach. We walked down to "Indian Bar" which did have the game and was probably a better place to watch the game anyway. After watching Swansea beat Arsenal, we stopped at D.R. Lanta for the first half of Reading v Man U although things are a bit vague in my memory by then. Don't really remember walking back to Kaw Kwang.

Sunday, 2nd December Slept for most of the morning and made it to the pool by about 2:00. We had a few drinks again in the evening. The Norwich v Sunderland was the late kick-off. As usual, we had a couple of drinks on the balcony before heading to Hans to eat. We got to D. R. Lanta by 10:45 and watched the game in there. We were back in the room by 2am, slightly more sober than last night but not by a lot.
