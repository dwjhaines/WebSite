dwjhaines - 2012 week 9

Monday, 23rd July Up for breakfast by 7, we checked out of the hotel at 7:45 and walked across the road to Scuba Junkie. We loaded our gear onto the boat and set off for Mabul at about 8:30. We were in Mabul by 9:15 and were met at the pier by Carys, one of the managers who we have known for some time from diving in Koh Lanta. We left our luggage at the dive centre and returned to the boat for the first dive of the day. We dived with Debi and one other diver - Rick, a dive instructor from Bournemouth. All three dives today were "muck dives" in the area quite close to the pier. The fist dive was at Kapali house reef, about 5 minutes away by boat. The second at Artificial reef just round the corner. The third was at Awas, right in front of the pier. All three dives were very good. We had done the same three sites as our first dives here last year and there was still loads to see. All the usual stuff was here - frog fish, ghost pipe fish, nudibranchs, etc. We saw a seahorse on the third dive where we also saw our first three turtles of the day.

We returned to Mabul between each of the dives. In between the first and second dives we checked into our room - it was the one that we stayed in last year and had requested again. It is the closest room to the beach and has a slightly better balcony than the others. Lunch was between dives two and three.

We were back at the resort by 4:30 so had time to unpack before the evening meal at 7:00. Food seems to have improved since last year - both meals today have been pretty good. Sat chatting to Rick and his girlfriend for an hour or so after eating and were back in the room by 9:30.

Tuesday, 24th July Our first day diving at Sipadan. Sipadan is one of the best diving locations in the world. To preserve the island, only 120 permits are issued to dive there each day. Scuba Junkie only get 7 of those permits despite the fact that they are now the biggest dive operator in the area. To get one of the permits, you have to do several days diving at other sites and book well in advance. Because we are doing a total of 14 days diving with Scuba Junkie. we should be going four times during our stay on Mabul.

A Sipadan day starts early. There is a small breakfast put out in the dining room at 6:00 so that we can all be on the jetty by 6:15. There were two guides on the boat (Simon and Khai) as well as the seven customers. We were in a group with Rick, who we had dived with yesterday, and his Thai girlfriend, Ann. Our group was led by the Malaysian guide - Khai.

As expected, it was an excellent days diving. The first dive featured a lot of sharks but surprisingly, no turtles. Loads more sharks on the second dive and three turtles. The third at Barracuda Point was quite eventful. It was the strongest current we have ever experienced at Sipadan. We went straight down but Ann struggled with the current and could not go down so the others drifted away from us. We were 3 or 4 minutes into the dive when we realised that they were no-where to be seen so surfaced to find them. We all got picked up by the boat and were returned to the entry point and all managed to get down this time. Loads more sharks and three more turtles. The final dive was at Drop-off. On this dive Roz counted 32 turtles - a new record for a single dive. Particularly towards the end, there were turtles everywhere. Whichever direction you looked, another one seemed to appear from somewhere. A very good dive to end the day with!!

We had planned to have another week off alcohol and have not had anything to drink since Sandakan. However, tonight we both felt like having a couple of drinks so had a beer in the bar and then a few Bacardi and cokes on our balcony.

Wednesday, 25th July Another day of local diving around Mabul. We dived with Khai again and a Canadian couple, Brett and Heather. Another good days diving with three dives at Black Ray Channel, Sea Ventures and Eel Garden. Plenty to see on all the dives with more frog fish (one on each dive) a robust ghost pipe fish at Sea Ventures and on the third dive, two sea moths, two ribbon eels and two more turtles.

Totally worn out after the three dives today. We ate with the couple we dived with earlier and were back in the room by 9:00.

Thursday, 26th July Second trip to Sipadan. A very similar day to the one we had on Tuesday. This time, the guides were Dave and Sofia. We dived with Dave and Brett and Heather again.

Once again, it was an excellent days diving. Three of the four dives were at the same sites as on Tuesday. We started at South Point again. The visibility was better than it had been on Tuesday and probably better than at any time we dived here last year. We saw over twenty sharks on the first dive and Roz spotted five turtles although I only saw one of them. The second dive was at White Tip Avenue, a site we had never dived before. This was also very good with plenty more sharks. Barracuda Point was superb again. The huge schools of jacks and barracuda were more spectacular in the increased visibility and once again, there seemed to be sharks everywhere.

The best dive of the day was the fourth again. We went in at Drop-off but this time, we could make it round to Barracuda Point. Roz counted over twenty turtles (some of which caused me to go into deco!), there were more sharks, we saw the jacks and barracuda again and the dive ended by spending 5 - 10 minutes watching a school of bump-head parrot fish. We have seen one or two of these before and have been out looking for them on a previous trip to Sipadan, but we have never seen them in such large numbers. There were about 25 - 30 of them hanging around in about 5 metres. This was an ideal way of spending a slightly extended safety stop and was a perfect end to what has been another very good day.

Once again, we did not do a lot for the rest of the day. We went up to the bar for a beer before tea and spent an hour or so chatting to the people we have been diving with today. Everyone was worn out from the four dives so we were still back in the room by 8:30.

Friday, 27th July We had expected to be diving Mabul again today but found out last night that there we were on a two tank trip to Simamil, another island about 40 minutes away.

We dived with Brett and Heather again and were led this time by Sofia. The first dive at Second Beach was not great. We did not really see a lot during the dive and the coral was not particularly spectacular. Some of the other groups saw eagle rays but we didn't. The second dive was much better - a 72 minute "muck dive" in the shallows. Loads to see here although probably not as much as when we did the dive with a Malaysian guide last year.

We had lunch on the beach before returning to Mabul for the third dive of the day. A whale shark had been spotted at the back of the island earlier in the day so we headed round to lobster wall near to where it had been seen. Inevitably, we did not see it but did see another three turtles and it was a nice enough dive again.

Had another early night without doing a lot.

Saturday, 28th July Another brilliant days diving. It was our third trip to Sipadan. Dave and Khai were the guides. Like two days ago, Dave was our guide, and we were diving with Brett and Heather for the fourth day running.

The first dive of the day was at Barracuda Point . This was Roz's 1000th dive and was almost as good as the previous dives we have had here. The vis was better again than it was two days ago. Only 4 turtles but Roz counted 40 sharks during the dive. The second dive at South Point was excellent again. There seemed to be sharks everywhere. Roz counted 27 but it seemed as if there were at least as many as on the first dive. Everyone says you do not go to Sipadan to look at the reef but the hard coral at the end of this dive was very spectacular - one of the advantages to limiting the number of divers here. The third would have been a pretty good dive anywhere else but the vis had deteriorated quite a lot and we "only" saw 5 turtles and 7 sharks. Although still good, it was a bit of an anti-climax after the previous two.

The final dive of the day, and my 1000th, was back at Barracuda Point. This was another brilliant dive and a very good way to bring up the 1000. The first 25 minutes of the dive were spent at 5 - 8 metres with the huge school of Jacks and the school of barracuda. The vis was back to being really good again, the sun was shining, and hanging in the middle of the jacks in particular was a genuinely awesome experience. After drifting away from the barracuda, we continued along the reef to the channel where once again there were sharks and turtles everywhere (16 sharks and 14 turtles). A perfect dive to end the day.

There was a quiz night in the Scuba Junkie bar after the evening meal. We had a team of six with Brett and Heather plus another couple who had been to Sipadan with us. We had intended to have a few drinks tonight anyway and had  a few beers while in the bar. We managed to win the quiz so got given more beer and did not leave the bar until about 11:30. We had a couple more drinks on our balcony while watching the rain and got to bed sometime around 1am.

Sunday, 29th July Three local dives today. We dived in the same group as we have been in for the last few days i.e. with Brett and Heather and Dave guiding. The first two dives were back at Kapali at two sites we have already been to. We went first to Black Ray Channel where we saw 10 turtles. We had not been expecting this as we had not seen any the first time we came here. The second dive was Kapali House Reef which was pretty good again. We had lunch back on Mabul before diving Awas in front of the resort again. This was also a good dive especially the huge sea turtles at the end. Back on the jetty, we said goodbye to Brett and Heather who left for Semporna. They have been very good to dive with for the last five days but we will have to find new people to dive with from tomorrow.

Once again, we were very tired from the diving and lack of sleep last night. I was back in the room by 8 after eating and it was a much earlier night than yesterday. We were both asleep not long after 9.
