dwjhaines - 2012 week 13

Monday, 20th August Our third visit to Monad Shoal to see the thresher sharks. We had been unsure whether or not to do this for a third time but decided we are unlikely to ever see them again so got up at 4:15 for a final time. Although not as good as our first sight of them, it was better than the second visit here. We saw two sharks, one of them in particular was very close to us and stayed around for a few minutes. Definitely worth getting out of bed for! (As the boat left from in front of the dive shop we were in the water earlier than on the other two trips. The dive started at 5:42 - our earliest ever dive)

We were back at the dive centre by 7:30. After breakfast, we checked out of our room at Tepanee and carried our luggage to Blue Corals where we had booked a room for two nights. Our room was not ready so we left our luggage in reception and returned to the dive centre for our final two dives here. The morning dive was to Bugtong, a submerged pinnacle and the afternoon dive was to Deep Rock - probably our favourite of the local sites. Both dives very enjoyable as have been most of them here.

After the last dive, we washed our gear and walked back to Blue Corals where our room (complete with sea facing balcony) was now ready. About five minutes after checking in, the rain was coming into the balcony at about 45 degrees soaking everything and making our sea view not very pleasant!

The rain stopped after about an hour and we were able to go for a walk around the island. We bought our souvenir thresher shark fridge magnets and also found a shop selling bottles of coconut flavoured rum. We stopped at Oscars for a beer and then had a few drinks on our balcony. We ate at La Isla Bonita and finished the rum on our balcony by about 11:30.

Tuesday, 21st August We had breakfast at 8 in Blue Corals and then headed back to the dive centre. We paid our bill both for the extra diving we had done as well as the add-ons for day trips, etc. Also paid our food and drink bill from Oscars for the last eight days. We spent an hour or so sat outside the shop making use of their wi-fi before collecting the rest of our gear and returning to the hotel.

We had lunch at Oscars again and I then spent half of the afternoon trying to fix my plug adaptor which broke when trying to use a Filipino socket. Gave up after about two hours and created a home-made adaptor held together with sticking plasters. Not particularly safe but seems to work!

Today was our last day in Malapascua. We had a few beers again in the evening starting with one on our balcony. We went for one at Oscar's and then had a few more with our final visit to La Isla Bonita. Once again, we had excellent meals. The food has been one of the highlights of Malapascua, particularly after the week in Tioman.

I sent another e-mail to British Airways while at Oscar's and by the time we had finished eating, they had moved us on to direct flights from Hong Kong to London and given us a choice of dates. I have no idea why it took so long but they got there in the end.

Wednesday, 22nd August We were up just after 7, finished packing and took our luggage to breakfast. We checked out as soon as we finished eating and walked to the dive centre as they had arranged our transfer back to Cebu. We got the boat from outside the dive centre just after 8:30 and were met by a taxi when we got to Maya 30 minutes later. The taxi journey was quite scenic and was an enjoyable drive of about three and a half hours. We got the driver to stop at a cash point in Cebu City before dropping us off at the Southern bus terminal.

As soon as we got out of the taxi, we were put onto a local bus heading for Moalboal. We took the last two seats so the bus pulled out immediately. The bus journey was just under three hours. It was not too bad at times but was very crowded for about an hour in the middle of the journey and was very hot when it rained as everyone shut the windows. We ended up in Moalboal earlier than expected at about 3:30, and got a motor tricycle out to Panagsama Beach where we had booked five nights at the Quo Vadis Dive Resort. We checked in and were shown to a room about 10 - 15m from the dive centre. The beach here got completely washed away by a typhoon quite a long time ago but the room we have is only a few metres from the sea and is in an excellent location.

We went for a walk around the Panagsama area - plenty of dive shops, resorts, bars and restaurants. It seems a very nice place and I think we have made the right decision coming here instead of Bohol as originally planned. After watching the sunset from our balcony and having showers, we went out for a pizza at one of the nearby restaurants. Very good meals again. We had another drink on our balcony when we got back (bottles of rum coast 80 peso - about £1.25) but were still in bed fairly early as we will dive again tomorrow.

Thursday, 23rd August First days diving with Visaya Divers, the dive centre based in our resort. We were at the dive centre by 8:30 to fill out the paperwork and show our cards, etc. We walked out to the boat and had a 20 minute journey to Pescador Island, one of the best dive sites in The Philippines. We dived in a group of four, led by Dhodho. There was only one other diver on the boat so we all had plenty of space.

Before we went down, it was obvious that the vis was better than anything we have experienced for a while. Once we went down, we must of been able to see for 40 - 50 metres. There were lots of schools of fish, particularly at the start of the dive - certainly a lot more than at Malapascua. Our second dive of the day was another spectacular wall dive at Tongo, about 5 minutes away from the dive centre. On this dive we saw two turtles, the first we have seen since our arrival in The Philippines.

The third dive of the day at 5pm was the house reef. This involved walking over to the dive centre, putting on our gear and walking straight into the sea. Within a few minutes, we were at the top of the wall looking at another turtle. We saw five in total on this dive which was probably the best of the day. We dived with Roly and one other diver and were underwater for 86 minutes.

So far we have been really impressed with the diving here. Not only is the diving itself pretty good but because everything is close by, the whole day is not taken up by two hours of diving as was the case with Tioman and to some extent Malapascua. We were back at the resort for two and a half hours between the first and second dives (meaning I had time to go into Moalboal and buy a new plug adaptor for the laptop). The house reef is far better than we were expecting it to be and we can be diving there within about 10 minutes of leaving our room. Also, the dive guides seem happy to just about let you do what you want, so you can swim off and look at things or spend as long as you want taking photos without them being too concerned. They also allow very long dives which is ideal.

By the time we were back from the third dive, it was gone 7. We had a couple of drinks on the balcony while writing log books and went out for another pizza.

Friday, 24th August Another very enjoyable days diving. We did not go to Pescador today but did two boat dives about 10 minutes up the coast and the house reef again. The dives along the coast are all quite similar. They are all spectacular wall dives with great vis and plenty to see. The first five at Lo-ok was led by Dhodho again with us and about six other divers of varying levels of experience. Although the group was big, once again it meant that we could just about dive on our own. We managed to see seven turtles on this dive which is the second most that we have ever seen on a single dive away from the Sipadan area. There were none on the next dive at Tuble but it was still a nice enough site. For the third dive, we were allowed to dive the house reef on our own. This was another good dive. I spotted the first turtle before we had descended as I was looking for the drop-off. We quickly went down and stayed with it for a few minutes before it headed off into the shallows. We saw one more during the dive and again, loads of pipe fish.

Had a similar evening to yesterday except that we went slightly further away to eat and had curries instead of pizza.

Saturday, 25th August Three more dives. The first was the same dive at Pescador island as we did two days ago. I thought this dive was better than the first time we were here - there was more sun and everything seemed a lot more colourful. The second dive was at Talisay, the site next to House Reef. Another wall spectacular wall dive and we saw another three turtles. The final dive of the day was on the house reef again. Tonight we were led by Dhodho and went North for the first time. Another 80 minute dive and we saw ten turtles - a new record apart from dives at Sipidan! Also saw a "hairy octopus" for the first time.

As usual, we had a couple of drinks on our balcony while doing our log books. Swansea v West Ham kicked off at 7:45 local time and we were able to get commentary through the laptop. We listened to the first 15 minutes and then went out to eat. If we had been just about anywhere else in S. E. Asia, we would be able to watch this game but The Philippines is not big on English football...... or any non-American sport. We were back on the balcony to listen to the second half (Swansea 3 - West Ham 0). Sunderland's game got called off due to the rain so there was not too much interest in the rest of the afternoons football. We had a few more drinks and went to bed after the final scores had gone through.

Sunday, 26th August Just two dives today as we want to dry our gear before we leave tomorrow. We had planned to have a day off diving but it has been so good here that we did not want to miss out on another couple of dives. Also, by doing a tenth dive this morning, we get one free this afternoon! The first dive of the day was at Pescador again. We dived a different part of the island this time dropping in at "The Cathedral", a huge crevice in the rock dropping down to 35m. It was a very spectacular part of the dive with a large swim-through at the bottom. The rest of the dive was also very good.

We finished our diving here with a 90 minute dive along the house reef. We were diving with Dhodho again and one other couple. We were dropped off by boat a few 100m along from where we are staying. The other couple were low on air after about 40 mins and got back on the boat leaving us to swim back to the dive centre. We saw four or five turtles in the same place we had seen a lot of them last night.

The diving here has been excellent - probably better than we expected it to be. Lots of turtles and very long dive times - we have averaged just over 73 minutes per dive. This is another place we want to come back to!

We had finished washing our gear by about 4:00 and did not do a lot else for the rest of the afternoon. We had a similar evening to yesterday (without the football), i.e. a few beers on the balcony before going out for a pizza. We ate with Kari, the Finn who we dived with on our first evening dive here and his girlfriend, and then returned to our balcony to finish a bottle of rum.
