dwjhaines - 2012 week 47

Monday, 15th April We did two dives today with Master Divers, a dive centre owned by a couple we had met while diving in Pulau Weh six years ago. We did not need to be at the dive centre until 11:45 so had the morning to ourselves. Once again, the weather was poor so we did not do a lot except laze around in the room or on the balcony.

At the dive centre, we sorted out our gear and were taken by long-tail boat to the Master Divers dive boat. We were diving with Mayumi, a Japanese instructor who hardly spoke English and Sarah, an English instructor. There were only two other divers on the boat, both students. The dives at Green Rock and White Rock were both OK.

We had quite low expectations of the diving here but they were both enjoyable enough dives. The visibility was not great but there was plenty of fish on both dives and just about enough to make the dives interesting. Both dives were just over an hour.

We were back at the dive centre by 4 and after a bit of a chat with Ayesha, we walked back to the room. We went out to eat in the evening at Bam-Bam, somewhere we had not eaten before but that was recommended by Ayesha. Here, we had probably the best food we have had since arriving in Thailand. We ate quite early as we had missed lunch and were back in the room again by 8:00.

Tuesday, 16th April A similar day to yesterday with two afternoon dives. We went to Red Rock and White Rock today. We dived with Mayumi again today. We also had Dan and one other diver along with us for both dives (not really sure what they were doing) and Ayesha joined us on the first. Both dives were quite good again and we saw a turtle on the second. This was not quite the same sort of turtle experience that we were having in Bonaire! As soon as I banged my tank when I spotted the turtle, another big group of divers appeared and we moved on after a couple of minutes. We also saw one of the biggest sea-snakes we have ever seen and a juvenile harlequin sweet-lips.

We were back in the room slightly later (we had longer dives today), ate out at Bam-Bam Restaurant again and were back in the room by 8:30.

Wednesday, 17th April Our final two dives here today. We had an earlier start today as we were on the morning boat and had to be at the dive centre by 7:00. When we woke at 6ish, there was another storm outside. Dark clouds, thunder and lightning and more heavy rain made the thought of going diving particularly unappealing. The rain had eased a bit by the time we walked to the dive centre but we did not see the sun all day again.

Once again, we were diving with Mayumi but with no other divers in our group today. The first dive of the day was at the HTMS Sattakut wreck. This was a WWII ship in pretty good condition - it had been sunk deliberately for divers a couple of years ago. The conditions on the surface were a bit rough but once underwater, vis was fine and the wreck was quite enjoyable. We only stayed on the wreck about 15 minutes before moving to the nearby, and shallower site, Hin Pee Wee. As with the other sites here, this was all OK without anything too spectacular.

The second dive was at Potteries and was probably the worst of the sites we had been to. Max depth of 10m and not a lot to see.

The early start meant that we were back at Ananda by midday. We had lunch on the balcony and spent the afternoon reading or sitting on the balcony again. We went for a walk around a couple of dive shops at 4ish as I need a new mask. After just over 12 years, mine has started leaking regularly and needs replacing. We looked at a couple of different options and returned to the room to look at reviews on the internet before making a decision. We went to eat at Bam-Bam again in the evening and had another early night.

Thursday, 18th April Had a day of not doing much today. We had only booked three days diving here and although it was enjoyable, three days was probably about right. We had breakfast at Mae Haad Seafood. The weather had improved by this morning and for the first time since we got to Thailand we had blue skies and sunshine.

We spent an hour or so on the beach just in front of our hotel in the morning. At lunchtime, we went out and purchased my new mask (which will be a birthday present) and bought tickets for tomorrow morning's ferry back to Samui. We bought baguettes for lunch and ate them on the balcony.

Spent most of the afternoon reading in the room. We had another walk around Mae Haad at 5ish before watching the sunset on the beach. We went out to Bam-Bam Restaurant for a final time in the evening. The food there has been excellent the last few days and very cheap compared to some places we have been in Thailand.

Friday, 19th April We had breakfast at Mae Haad Seafood again before walking back to Ananda, packing and checking out. We left just after 9:00 and walked round the corner to where the Seatran Discovery ferries leave from. We got straight onto the ferry which left on time at 9:30. It was an uneventful two hour journey back to Bank Rak on Samui.

We did not have any accommodation booked for tonight. There did not seem any point in getting a taxi back to where we stayed last week so walked down the road to Samui Mermaid Resort. We got an air-con room here for Baht1000. It is only round the corner from the airport and they provide free transfers so it seemed a better deal all round.

We had lunch in the restaurant just over the road from where we are staying and then spent a couple of hours by the pool, mainly in the shade due to burning yesterday. In the evening we walked along to the 15m high "Big Buddha" statue which is less than a mile from where we are staying. We have been here before years ago but it was good to see it again as the sun set behind it. We walked back to the resort, had showers and went out to eat over the road in the same place we had eaten lunch.

Saturday, 20th April We had breakfast in the restaurant over the road - the third meal in a row here. Spent the morning sitting around in the room or on the balcony before checking out at midday. We were not due to fly until 5:30 so spent a few hours by the pool before we were transferred to the airport at 3:30. We checked in for the Phuket flight and had an hour or so sat at the gate before departure. We were called to board at about 5:00 and the half full flight took off slightly early at 5:25.

We were expecting to land at Phuket at 6:30 but we were on the ground by 6:00 and were outside the airport with our luggage by 6:15. At 6:30 we met up with Marco, the boat leader who we knew from previous live-aboards with DiveRace. We had to wait about 15 minutes for three other customers (they were not expecting us until 6:45 so had gone to the bar) before we all headed North in the mini-bus to the pier at Khao Lak. We got on the boat just after 8:00.

West Coast had replaced the boat since the last time we dived with them. The new boat, Pawara is a lot bigger than the old one. There were 19 divers in total plus six guides, including Brian who we also knew from previous trips. We were given a boat briefing by Marco and were introduced to the rest of the crew before eating at 9:00. We stayed up and talked to some of the other guests while following the football and went to bed when that finished at 11:00.

Sunday, 21st April We were up at 7:00 for "breakfast 1" before getting the long dive briefing from Marco. We were put into a group led by Tristan, with Tom and Lauren, an American couple. They have a lot less experience than us (they are open water divers with about 30 dives) but they are the only other couple on the boat diving on Nitrox. The day dives today were all in The Simlians at Anita's Reef, West of Eden and Elephant Head.

All three dives were excellent. There seems to be a lot more fish around than on our last few trips to the Similans and the visibility was superb - certainly a lot better than in Koh Tao and a lot better than it has been here the last couple of weeks apparently. During the day we saw a white-tip reef shark, a ghost pipe fish, sea moths, a ribbon eel and large numbers of reef fish.

We did a night dive (the only one of this trip!!!) at Turtle Rock which was also OK. On this dive we saw a cuttle fish and a flying gurnard which is a lot more than we have seen on some night dives here.
