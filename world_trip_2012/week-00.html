dwjhaines - 2012 week 0

Friday, 25th May The start of the trip was three days earlier than planned due to England's friendly in Oslo. We left Kingsclere at 1:20 and headed for Heathrow, stopping for lunch in a pub on the way. We got to Heathrow, parked and got through security without any problems and were sat in the bar by about 3:30. There were supposed to be five of us going to Oslo but Paddy Dave had already had to pull out and Steve and Kim almost missed the flight as they got stuck in traffic - they eventually made it with a few minutes to spare. Had an uneventful flight and then a 30 minute taxi ride to the apartment in the centre of Oslo.

It was quite late by the time we arrived but there were bars and restaurants round the corner from where we were staying so we went out for a few drinks. All seemed nice enough but with beer costing around £10 a pint, it was only a few! Ended up sat in a brewery, sipping our beer until about 2am.

Saturday, 26th May Woke up to find that Roz had lost her iTouch. This was not a good start to the trip, but after phoning the very efficient lost property office at Oslo airport we were told that it had been found on the plane and we could pick it up tomorrow. Once that panic was over, we had breakfast in the apartment and by about midday, headed off to start our sight-seeing of Oslo.

We went first to the hotel where the FA were staying so we could check in and return Dave's ticket. We collected our Free Lions from the station and went for a walk around the harbour area and fort where we saw the changing of the guard. All quite nice without being too spectacular - something that could be said to describe Oslo in general! We walked from the harbour back to the centre of town taking in a few of the other buildings on the way.  We thought about stopping for a drink but were put off by the prices so we all headed back to the apartment stopping at a supermarket to buy a six-pack of Tuborg on the way. Spent the rest of the afternoon sat around in the apartment drinking the lager.

We headed out again at about 5:30 and managed to find a bar with slightly more reasonable prices - about £5 for half a litre of lager! After a couple in there, we had something to eat in a tapas restaurant and caught the metro out to the football ground. The game was also not too spectacular but at least England did win (1 - 0). Got on the metro as soon as the game had finished and were able to spend another couple of hours at the brewery before returning to the apartment at 2 again. Although none of us were that impressed with Oslo, it had been a good day out despite the ridiculously high prices for just about everything.

Sunday, 27th May Had another late breakfast before checking out of the apartment just after midday. Roz and I had another walk round Oslo, this time walking up to the Royal Palace, while Steve and Kim went looking for presents/souvenirs. We were back at the apartment by 1:30 so had an hour lying in the park making the most of the weather.

We had booked the same taxi driver that had bought us here to take us back to the airport. He turned up early and we were on our way by 2:45ish. At the airport we collected Roz's iTouch  and waited for our flight which left on time at 5:10. We were back at Heathrow by 6:30, collected the car and drove to Maidstone for the start of the next leg of the trip. We checked into a Travelodge and got a takeaway pizza before a relatively early night.
