dwjhaines - 2012 week 10

Monday, 30th July Another three local dives. We were supposed to be going back to Siamil today but the boat we were supposed to be on had engine problems delaying departure and the sea was rough following storms the last two nights. I not sure which of these contributed the most to the trip being cancelled but it meant another day of diving around Mabul/Kapali.

Our diving team from the last few days has completely broken up - Brett and Heather have left and Dave was guiding at Sipadan today. We were guided instead by Ella (an instructor from Stockport) and were also with Katherine who we have been on the boat with a few times in the last week. The first dive was Kapali House Reef again. This is a site we have dived a few times and is always quite enjoyable although there does not seem to be as much to see as last year.

The second site was Cleaning Station, a site we have not dived before just a bit further along from House Reef. Towards the start of the dive, there was an area where once again, there seemed to be turtles everywhere. Like yesterday, they were mainly big sea turtles and were not in the slightest bit bothered about having divers very close to them.

The third dive after lunch (with Kimberley replacing Katherine in our group) was back at Mabul and another site we have not dived before - Nudi Nook. As the name suggests, this site had loads of nudibranchs - something that we have not seen so many of on this trip.

We had been considering trying to do a fourth dive on some of the days we were diving locally. However, doing at least three or four dives a day for the last eight days has been quite tiring and we decided against doing an extra dive this evening. Getting back by 4:30 and having an hour or so on the beach has been quite enjoyable and we have plenty more diving to come in the next few months. Although not quite as tired as yesterday, we had another relatively early night. We did go to the bar for a beer before tea and stayed in the restaurant for an hour or so after eating but were still back in the room by 9ish.

Tuesday, 31st July Our fourth and final trip to Sipadan. The guides on the boat were Apek and Khai. There were only three other divers on the boat - Philipe and Sybille from Switzerland and Begum  from Turkey. The other couple on the boat were snorkelling so the seven of us dived in one group. The last three nights, there have been severe storms and heavy rain in the area. They have only lasted a couple of hours each night but they have had a dramatic negative effect on the visibility. As soon as we jumped in for the first dive, it was obvious that the diving was not going to be as spectacular as on our first three trips here.

It was however, still a very good days diving. We dived Barracuda Point, South Point, Coral Garden and Barracuda again. The dives at Barracuda were the best ones again particularly when we found the school of chevron barracuda on the last dive. Roz counted more turtles than on any other day we have been here - 46 including 3 that were spotted form the surface.

Before tea, we went for a couple of beers at the Borneo Divers bar on the beach. After eating, we went upstairs to the Scuba Junkie bar and joined everyone else from today's trip for log book signing etc. After an hour or so in there, we returned to our balcony to finish the duty free Bacardi.

Wednesday, 1st August Another day of local dives. We were back diving with Debi again and had Peter with us for the first two dives. We are limited at the moment in which sites we can dive. Although the weather is good, it is still windy and one side of the island can not be dived due to the big waves. We went back to Kapali for the first dive and chose to dive at Cleaning Station again. There was plenty of turtle activity in the same place as last time and we spent about 15 minutes here before moving on. The second dive was back at the Sea Venture Oil Rig and like last week, this was done in strong current. Not the best of sites in these conditions! The third (on our own with Debi by this time) was a 70 minute dive at Awas again. The seahorse had moved on and there was not too much to see in the sandy part of the dive but there were a couple more turtles at the end and it was still reasonably enjoyable.

Got back to our room to find a 1 metre long monitor lizard in our bathroom. Five or six members of staff appeared at our room to stand around thinking about how to remove it before Debi walked in and chased it out!

Again, we were worn out from the diving so had an early night.

Thursday, 2nd August Our final day of diving from Mabul. It was a late start this morning (9:45 on the jetty) so we had time to pack and check out of our room before the first dive of the day. Choice of dive sites was still limited by the weather so it was similar diving to yesterday. Today, we were led by Ryan and were joined by Montserrat and John, a loud and annoying American. We were relieved that his first day here coincided with our last as we would not want to have spent too much time on the boat with him!

First dive was Kapali House Reef which we were not that pleased about but it turned into quite a good dive. We did not see too much of any interest but did see three turtles which we were not expecting here. Second dive was at Nudi Nook which we enjoyed again and saw another four turtles. The third dive of the day (and our last here) started at Froggies and covered four dive sites due to the strong currents. We went all the way past Paradise I & II and were eventually picked up on Panglima. Enjoyable enough dive despite the current and the worst vis so far. We saw another five turtles bring the total to 251 in the 14 days we have done with Scuba Junkie.

We were back at the jetty by 4:30 and left Mabul before 5. It was sad to be leaving despite the fact that we are continuing to travel. It is still the best place we have dived and has been a superb place to stay again. Yesterday, Carys (one of the managers) had said we should stay and work as dive masters for Scuba Junkie. We were not sure how serious she was but they are short of staff at the moment. It may be something to think about next year. Either way, I think we will be back here before the end of our trip.

We were back in Semporna before 6 and hung up all our dive gear to dry in the gear room. I managed to get my regs fixed by Tino, the owner of Scuba Junkie. They had started leaking after two days of diving from Mabul and I had to use shop regs for the rest of the time there. They could not be repaired on Mabul but Tino managed to sort them out in about 15 minutes which was good news.

We walked to the SeaFest Hotel where we had booked a room for the night. We had a few beers in the room before going out to eat in our usual Indian restaurant. We had hoped to meet up with Peter again but he was not in the bar so we returned to the room after eating and had a few more drinks before getting to bed at about 1:30.

Friday, 3rd August Got up at about 9:00 and had breakfast at the hotel. We had managed to get a late checkout from the hotel so kept the room until 2pm. We had stayed here as there is a pool and we do not get our transfer to the airport until 4pm. After we had checked out at 2:00, we left our bags at the dive shop and went to get money out. My card is blocked by Santander again so had to have another 10 minute phone call to them to try and sort it out.

We returned to the dive shop, paid our bill, collected all our gear and re-packed our luggage. It was gone 3:00 by this time. We had roti canai for lunch before getting picked up from the shop at 4:00 along with three others from Mabul who are on the same flight as us.

It took just over an hour to the airport in Tawau and after checking in, we had about an hour to wait before boarding the Air Asia flight to Kuala Lumpa. The flight took off on time at 7:00pm and landed 15 minutes early at 9:30. We bought bus tickets for a 7:30 bus tomorrow morning while waiting for our luggage and then started walking out towards the Tune Hotel about 5 minutes walk away. We stopped at the food court to get something to eat on the way and were checked into our room just after 10:30. The Tune Hotel is a budget hotel chain -  there is only just enough room for our luggage to fit on the floor beside the bed. It is not the sort of place you would want to stay for any length of time but as we are only in KL for less than 10 hours, it is ideal.

Saturday, 4th August Got up before 7 and checked out of the hotel. We walked back to LCCT where I managed to take out money and we also bought breakfast to eat on the bus. We were travelling to Mersing so that we can get a ferry to Palau Tioman (Tioman Island) tomorrow morning. There are no direct buses from the airport so we had the choice of getting a bus into the centre of KL or going to Malacca and changing there. We chose the second option. Although slightly longer, the first option would of involved getting across KL which would not of been that easy with all our luggage. In Malacca, everything goes in and out of the same bus station.

Our bus left on time at 7:30 and got into Malacca at 9:30. We had no problems getting tickets for the 12:45 bus to Mersing so had three hours to kill at the bus station. We spent nearly all the time in McDonalds as it was air-conditioned, had free wi-fi and was just round the corner from the bus ticket office. The bus left slightly late but made up the time and got into Mersing at 5:15.

It was only a few minutes walk from the bus station to The Emperor Hotel, a fairly basic Chinese hotel in the centre of Mersing. Once again, it is fine for a night and at RM55 is pretty good value. We left our luggage in the room and went for a walk around the town. Not too much to see and do again. We bought ferry tickets for tomorrow and returned to the bus station to buy tickets for the bus back to Singapore in eight days time. I also got my hair cut. We returned to the room for showers before going out to eat at an Indian restaurant. We were back in the hotel by 9, luckily just in time to watch the tennis from the olympics.

Sunday, 5th August We were up by 7 and went out for roti canai for breakfast. We left the hotel just after 8 and walked down to the jetty for the boat to Tioman. The original time for the ferry had been 8:30. We knew that the time had changed to 9:30 but wanted to get there early just in case. As it was, we needn't have bothered as the boat was late getting in and did not leave until about 10:15. The journey over to Tioman was uneventful and we were dropped off at ABC Beach at about 12:30. We walked to Bamboo Hill Chalets dropping off our dive gear at B&J Dive Centre on the way.

We had booked one week at Bamboo Hill. The room is basic but we had chosen it as it is on the side of a hill at the end of the beach and has superb views.  Tioman seems a pretty nice place. It is quieter and less developed than we were expecting but there are plenty of shops and restaurants and it is a duty free island so the alcohol is cheap!

We had lunch in a restaurant by the beach and then I walked to and from Tekek, the main village on the island while Roz lay on the beach. I bumped into Regan and Annette in Tekek as they had just arrived from Singapore (Tekek is not a large place and the airport is in the centre of the village).

We met up with Regan and Annette at 6 and started on their duty free Bacardi while sat on their balcony. The restaurants here let you take your own alcohol in as long as you are buying soft drinks so we carried on drinking during tea. We stopped for a couple of beers in the B&J bar before returning to the balcony for a few more drinks. I vaguely remember walking home but have no idea at what time!
