dwjhaines - 2012 week 36

Monday, 28th January Another excellent days diving. We were picked up by the dive boat from in front of the villas again at 7:30. We were diving with Liang again and five other divers. The first two dives were similar to the ones on Saturday but without seeing as many rays sharks or turtles. We still saw a turtle on both dives, and on the second, an eagle ray and had a few minutes with a nurse shark swimming around us.

During the surface interval between these two dives, we were talking to one of the other customers who told us that they sometimes take the boat up to the Northern sites in the afternoon to an area where you can see eagle rays. We decided that we should do this at some point and within a few minutes seemed to have been booked onto the boat for this afternoon. We were back at the villas by about 1:30 and, after a quick lunch, were on our way again less than an hour later.

The third dive was the best of the day. We dropped in on top of a wall at about 24m in quite strong current. Quite early on in the dive we saw a large school of eagle rays on the sand quite a way away from us. Once these left, we clung onto rocks at 24m waiting for them to swim along the wall.

The biggest group came along just as I was down to 4 minutes of deco time. We watched them swimming past before starting to ascend and immediately saw another group swimming behind them. These probably looked even more spectacular as we were viewing them from above. In total, we must of seen 40 - 50 of them making the dive well worth doing.

Again, we had a couple of drinks while doing our log books in the evening and walked into town to eat at the same restaurant for the third day running.

Tuesday, 29th January Two more dives today. Not quite as good as some of the other dives we have done here but still pretty good. We were diving today with Ernesto and three other divers. The first dive was at Punta Sur, Sur. The first half of this dive was excellent with plenty more eagle rays swimming around the pinnacles and once again, very spectacular scenery. The second half was mainly over sand and got a bit boring after a while. The second dive was at San Francisco Wall. This dive was not as spectacular as some we have done here but we did see two turtles, the first of which was one of the smallest we have ever seen underwater.

We had hoped to be back early today as Sunderland v Swansea was being shown live on Mexican TV at 1:45pm. It looked as if this would not be a problem at one stage but just before we got into the water for the second dive, one of the other divers had a problem with his computer and I got a leak in my high pressure hose. It took about 15 minutes to get both of these sorted out and we then did a 96 minute dive - the second longest we have ever done! We got back to the room by about 2:15 so missed the first 30 minutes of the game. The game was a fairly unexciting 0 - 0 draw and was over by 3:45.

I cycled into San Miguel straight after the game and bought a new high pressure hose (US$31). I took it back to the villas and spent about 45 minutes replacing the old one before discovering that there was a small part missing from the connector on the one I had bought. I had to cycle back into town again to buy the additional part (which probably should of been included with the new hose) and by the time I had completed the repair, it was gone 6:00.

We had a couple of drinks while doing our log books and then walked into town and went for a pizza for a change.

Wednesday, 30th January Our final two dives in Cozumel today. A couple of people on the boat were keen to go to the Northern Cozumel sites which we had not been to before. One of them Todd, was the one who had recommended the eagle ray dive the other day so we said we did not mind. Both dives were OK again but in our opinion, no-where near as good as the Southern sites. The first site, Barracuda Reef is known as being the most challenging of the Cozumel sites. It was quite a nice wall but had very strong currents.

The big steel tanks they use here enable longer dives but make buoyancy control a lot harder. Trying to battle against down-currents while being forced away from the reef at 28m was not the most enjoyable of experiences! It was the closest I have been to panicking in a long time. Everyone on the dive got through their air quicker than usual and it was a relatively short dive of only 58 minutes.

The second dive at San Juan Reef was better - still strong currents but they were a lot more manageable and it was a slightly more relaxed dive. We saw turtles on both dives and they were still good dives, just not what we would of chosen for our last dives here.

We were back earlier today as the Northern sites are closer and the first dive was under an hour. We had last nights left-overs for lunch and did not do much for the rest of the day. We had a couple of drinks before walking into town to eat and a couple more when we got back but were still reasonably sober when we went to bed at about 11.

Thursday, 31st January We did not have too much to do today. Unfortunately, it was probably the worst days weather we have had since arriving in Mexico so lying in the sun was not an option. We got a few things sorted out in the morning for later in the trip and finally managed to cancel our original flight out of Mexico (we have booked a new one because of the football and have had a few problems cancelling the original). We also looked into options for our one week in Florida and found a dive centre that looks reasonable for a few days. At about midday there was a break in the rain so we cycled into town.

We had to pay our bill at the dive shop and spent a while wandering round trying to get cash out. We thought we would have to pay in US$ which caused us a few problems (the only proper US$ ATM was not working). The shop eventually gave us a decent rate for using pesos so we were able to get pesos out and pay in cash. We went round a few of the souvenir shops and then cycled out to Mega supermarket to buy stuff for lunch. When we came out of there, it was pouring with rain again. We waited until it had eased a bit but still got completely soaked cycling back to the villas. Finally had lunch at about 3pm.

After lunch we booked our accommodation in Key Largo for the last three nights of our stay in Florida in March. We could make free phone calls to the states from our room so it was a good time to get this sorted out.

We had a similar evening to yesterday. We had a few drinks before walking into town and eating in Parrilla Mission for the last time. We walked back to the villas, had a couple more drinks and were in bed by about 11.

Friday, 1st February We were up by 7:30. We had breakfast outside our room, finished packing all our gear and were given a lift to the ferry terminal by Brent at about 8:40. We caught the 9:00 ferry back to Playa del Carmen. The crossing was a lot rougher than it was when we arrived in Cozumel due to the bad weather. There was lots of people being sea-sick and Roz came very close to joining them. We arrived in Playa at 9:45 in torrential rain so were soaked again by the time we got to the bus station. We just missed getting the 10:00 bus but got tickets for the one at 10:30. We were at Cancun airport by 11:30.

We checked in for our flight to Havana without any problems having bought our Cuban visas from Cubana Airlines. The 50 minute flight was on time and we landed in Havana just after 4pm local time (one hour ahead of Mexico. Everything at the airport went reasonably smoothly. We got through immigration, collected our luggage after a bit of a wait and managed to get money out of an ATM before getting a taxi to our hotel. We were at the hotel by 6ish and were relieved to find they did have our reservation after the problems we had with the agency.

The hotel is a big colonial style building just on the outskirts of the old town with a very spectacular entrance and reception area. We are on the sixth floor which Al Capone once hired out apparently! After having showers we went out to a few local bars. We did not want to go to far as we do not know our way round yet but there were a couple of places within 100m or so that are recommended in Lonely Planet. Rum is very cheap here (a bottle costs about £2) so we had plenty of mojitos and Cuba Libres and neither of us remember getting back to the hotel.

Saturday, 2nd February We had breakfast in the hotel and then returned to the room to watch West Ham v Swansea. We have no internet access here but were able to watch the game live on a Mexican TV channel.

Once this had finished, we went out and spent a few hours sight-seeing. This mainly involved walking round the old town looking at the buildings and squares as well as getting plenty of photos of the old American cars that are everywhere (and are one of the highlights of wandering round the town).We saw the Capitol Building which is very close to where we are staying and then walked round the four main squares.

After an hour or so back in the hotel, we went out for the evening. We walked back to the old town and went for a beer in a beer hall type place on Plaza Vieja. This was an excellent place to drink but had terrible service. We left after a couple in there and went to one other bar that Lonely Planet had recommended. This was quite expensive by local standards so we walked back to two of the bars that we were in last night. We discovered that rum and coke cost half as much as Cuba libres so had a few of these before getting back to the hotel at about 11:00.

Sunday, 3rd February A similar day to yesterday. We continued with our sight-seeing when we got up (at about midday). We did the walking tour from our guide book and then walked along the Malecón, the walkway along the sea-front, as far as the National Hotel - one of Havana's most famous sights. The hotel was impressive but a lot of the tour was not as interesting as walking around the old town yesterday.

We had a few hours back in our hotel and walked back into the old town in the evening. We returned to the beer hall and spent an hour or so sat there looking at the Plaza Vieja (the service was slightly better tonight!). We had a meal at a pizza restaurant and walked back to the hotel to finish a bottle of run that we had bought when we arrived. We got to bed by about 10:30 in a slightly more sober state than on the previous two nights.
