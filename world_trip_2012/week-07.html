dwjhaines - 2012 week 7

Monday, 9th July After a few days at home, we left for Heathrow at about 4:30 for the start of the second leg of our trip. We got to Heathrow without any problems and were checked in for our flight to Singapore by about 6:30. Because of British Airways' recent takeover of BMI, our BMI silver cards have been transferred to BA meaning that for one year, we have BA silver status. This is something that we would never be able to get normally and is already proving to be a big bonus. We were upgraded to "economy plus" for this flight and also had two hours in the BA lounge before the flight. We are not usually big fans of airport lounges but this one was very good and a big improvement on having to sit in the main part of terminal 3. After a few drinks in the lounge, we boarded the plane which left on time at 9:15.

We would not usually pay the extra for economy plus but the extra leg-room and bigger seats meant we had a very enjoyable flight. We had quite a few more drinks before falling asleep and managed to sleep well for about 5 or 6 hours.

Tuesday, 10th July Arrived at Changi airport just after 5pm. We were through the airport quite quickly and after taking out money, we got the MRT train to City Hall. It was a 5 minute walk from here to the Carlton Hotel where we had booked a room for two nights. After showers, we went out to eat. We walked to the area we had eaten in the last time we were in Singapore. The food centre that we had been to before had been replaced by a new shopping mall but we found another one slightly further away that served Indian food and beer which is what we were looking for. After our first roti canai of the trip and a few beers we walked back to the hotel. In our room, we started to work out how much we had drunk over the last six weeks and came to the conclusion that we needed a week or so without any alcohol. We reached this conclusion while consuming most of one of the litre bottles of Bacardi we had bought from duty free!

Wednesday, 11th July We did not do a lot today. We got up at about 11 and went out for breakfast before returning to the hotel. There was nothing that we really wanted to see or do in Singapore so spent most of the rest of the day lazing around the hotel and using the internet to make some bookings for later in the trip. We did go out for a walk around the area near the hotel. It is quite well located, just across the road from the Raffles Hotel and near the Padang. We walked over to Little India in the evening  for tea.

Thursday, 12th July We went out for breakfast at 8 and checked out of the hotel just after 9. We got the MRT back out to the airport and checked in for the first of our two Air Asia flights - the 11:40 to Kuala Lumpa. This flight was on time and we arrived in KL an hour after we took off. We collected our luggage at KL, cleared immigration, and then queued to check in for the 3:40 flight to Tawau. When we had done all this we had a couple of hours to kill so had lunch (McDonalds) and then walked around getting information about buses for when we get back here in three weeks time.

The second flight was also on time and uneventful. We arrived in Tawau just after 6:30 and cleared Sabah immigration. We were met outside the airport by the taxi from Scuba Junkie and were in Semporna by 8:30. We checked into a room at the Scuba Junkie Lodge, dropped off our luggage and went out to eat in one of the nearby Indian restaurants. We went back to the room for showers after eating and also to prepare our diving equipment for tomorrow when we will do our first dives of the trip.

Friday, 13th July After a quick breakfast, we went across the road to Scuba Junkie at 7:15 and completed the required paperwork. We had booked a few days diving at the sites around Semporna as we had not visited these before. Today we were booked on a three dive trip to Mataking. We left Semporna at about 8:30 and had a one hour boat trip to Mataking. There were about 12 customers on the boat including four snorkelers as well as three dive staff. We were put in a group with three others, all from England and were guided by Edgar.

The first dive was at a small wreck and was a lot better than expected. We saw our first turtle after four minutes and saw 13 in total on this dive. We were not expecting that many in this area. The second dive was not so good - a similar site to the Kepali House reef but with no-where near as much to see. The third was very good again with another 8 turtles and four frog fish. All in all, a very good days diving to begin the trip with.

We were back in Semporna by 5:30 and after showers, we spent an hour or so trying to sort out our Santander bank accounts which Santander appear to have blocked for some reason. Roz was able to unblock hers but I could not unblock mine as I apparently could not answer my security questions correctly! We ate at our usual restaurant and went back to the hotel to do log books etc before a relatively early night.

Saturday, 14th July Another day of diving from Semporna, this time to Mantabuan, another island to the North of Semporna. Today, apart from the guide Nas, we were the only divers on the boat although there were three snorkelers and Shane, their guide. (One of the snorkelers was a German who had been at the England v Italy game in Kiev three weeks ago!) The dive sites were similar to yesterdays but not as good. It was not bad diving, just not quite as good as Mataking. We still saw another frog fish and six turtles as well as plenty of other stuff and it was another enjoyable day.

The best dive of the day was when we returned to Semporna and we did the night dive in the harbour. We returned to the dive shop at 6:00 and left about 15 minutes later, getting into the water at 6:45. We were the only two customers doing this dive with Jake (or Jack?) but the owner of the dive shop was also diving. We had been told about this dive last year but had been unable to fit it in. It was one of the best night dives we have ever done. I am not normally a big fan of night dives but there was loads to see here in amongst all the rubbish in the harbour. We saw three sea-horses and an electric clam which we have never seen before. There were a few cuttle fish of various types and sizes, octopus, crabs, a Spanish dancer, etc. Our longest dive of the day at 77 minutes and it went remarkably quickly.

We were back in Semporna by 8:30 and, after washing the gear and having showers, we ate in our usual restaurant and were back in the hotel by 10:00.

Sunday, 15th July Our third and final days diving from Semporna. This time, we went to Sibuan, about 30 minutes away from Semporna by boat. Three more dives with Nas and Ted, a former Scuba Junkie DMT. It was the same boat as we were on two days ago but with less people - four other customers - so we had plenty of space. The dives were very good again - with plenty of turtles. Roz counted 12 making a total of 40 for our three days of diving. Also plenty of frog-fish again as well as a devil ray which is quite rare to see here.

We were back in Semporna relatively early today by about 4:30. We washed our gear and had showers before settling our bill at the dive centre. We walked to a different cash-point machine and found that we could both get money out which was a surprise but good news. Did not do a lot for the rest of the day apart from another Indian meal and had another early night.
