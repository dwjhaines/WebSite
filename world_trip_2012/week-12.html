dwjhaines - 2012 week 12

Monday, 13th August Our Cebu Pacific flight left Singapore twenty minutes early at almost exactly midnight. It was a budget airline and was cramped with uncomfortable seats and limited legroom. We did however manage to get about an hours sleep during the 3.5 hour flight. We got through immigration in Cebu without any problems and collected our luggage by 4am. We had arranged a transfer through Thresher Shark Divers (TSD) and there was someone waiting outside the airport for us. We had a three hour car journey to Maya and then a 30 minute boat ride out to Malapascua. The sea was too rough for the boat to moor on Bounty Beach so we were dropped on the opposite side of the island and completed our journey with the two of us on the back of a moped. We were eventually dropped at the TSD dive centre just after 8am.

We left all our gear at the dive centre and walked to the resort (Tepanee) that we had booked. Our room was not going to be available until midday but they let us have a fan room so we could shower and get a few hours sleep while we waited. We returned to the dive centre to fill in the forms etc at 11:30 and had lunch in Oscar's Pub/Restaurant upstairs.

By the time we were back at Tepanee our room was ready. The room was very nice but certainly, the balcony did not have the sea view that we were promised. Roz went back to the dive centre to complain and also looked at other options but there are no sea view rooms available where we are and the TSD did not know if they were able to do anything about it (despite the fact that it was them who booked the room).

We went out for a pizza in the evening. Malapascua is a lot smaller than Tioman but has lots more eating options. It was nice to have the option of a few places to eat again and the restaurant right next to our hotel does superb Italian food. After eating, we went back to Oscar's for a beer and were told that if we wanted to, we could find another room somewhere else and TSD would give us the money back for the room we had booked. However, by the time we returned to Blue Corals, the room that Roz had seen earlier was no longer available so it looks as if we are staying where we are for the next week!

Tuesday, 14th August The number one attraction of Malapascua is the thresher sharks that can be viewed on early morning dives. We were at the dive centre by 4:40 for our first trip out to Monad Shoal the location of the cleaning stations where they can be viewed. We boarded the boat with about 20 other divers and staff for the 20 minute journey out to the dive site and we were in the water just before 6:00. At the dive site, you usually have to go down to about 30m and wait for them to turn up. We had hoped to see maybe one or two of the sharks but saw the first at about 15m after less than three minutes. We saw another three more at 18m and then descended to the cleaning station where there were two more. A very good start to our diving here!

We were back at Oscar's for breakfast and headed off for the second dive at about 9:30 (after showing a member of TSD staff round our resort to show that not all rooms have sea view!). The next dive was at Deep Rock a deep macro site. There was loads to see here including frog fish and lots of crabs and shrimps that we have not seen before. I also found a flamboyant cuttle fish - only the second one of these that we have ever seen. Third dive was at a site close to the previous one. Another muck dive but not quite as much to see. As with Mabul, we have been unlucky with the weather here. There is a tropical storm hitting the Philippines at the moment and we are just about close enough to it to feel its effect. This means that a lot of the island can not be dived due to the rough sea and there are only a few sites that we can dive. It also means a 10 - 15 minute walk around the island to get to and from the boat every time we dive.

By the time we were back from the third dive, it was 5ish. We had a few drinks on our balcony while planning what to do in the two weeks we have when we leave Malapascua and then went out for a pizza again. A couple more drinks when we got back and we were in bed by about 11.

Wednesday, 15th August A slightly later start this morning! We were in Oscar's for breakfast by 8 and downstairs at the dive centre in time for the 9:30 dive. We had to walk across the island again as it is still very windy. This also meant that we are still limited on dive sites. We dived at Bantigue and Deep Rock again. Bantigue is in the same corner of the island as the other sites we dived yesterday and was a similar type of dive. The dive at Deep Rock was excellent again. We failed to find the flamboyant cuttle fish but Roz found a similar sized cuttle fish and once again, there was loads to see.

We were back slightly earlier today but did not do too much for the rest of the afternoon/evening. We were both worn out after the last few days and are diving early again tomorrow. We went out to La Isla Bonita, a restaurant recommended to us by someone at TSD and had really good curries. Were back in the room by 8:30.

Thursday, 16th August Second trip to see the thresher sharks at Monad Shoal so another early start. We went to a slightly different part of the site - (old) instead of (new) - and the dive was not as good. It was more like how we were expecting it to be here i.e. about 40 minutes of waiting around looking for the sharks and then seeing one for less than a minute as it swam past. At least our group did see one - we were the only group on the boat that did.

Two more local dives during the day. The first at Quiliano was OK and the third at Deep Rock was very good again. The good news was that by the end of the third dive, the wind had dropped and the sea was calm meaning that we could be dropped by boat right outside the dive shop. It also means that we will be able to get to some of the further dive sites tomorrow.

In between the second and third dives, we booked a room for two nights at another resort for next Monday and Tuesday. We had planned to leave Malapascua after seven nights but will stay another two. We both like it here and it gives us more time to complete the 15 dive package that we have paid for without having to do dives that we may not want to.

We went for a couple of beers at Oscars before eating at La Isla Bonita again. We returned to the room for a few more drinks on the balcony and seemed to get quite drunk without drinking that much. Got to bed sometime around 11.

Friday, 17th August We were able to board the dive boat in front of the shop for today's day trip, an improvement on having to walk half way round the island. The day trip was a two-tank trip with one dive at the Dona Marilyn wreck, about 90 minutes North of Malapascua and the second at Gato Island on the way back. Both dives were very good. The 98m wreck was in good condition with plenty of coral on it and Gato Island was another very nice site. Again, lots of coral, a couple of large white tip sharks and a sea-horse.

We were back at the dive centre by 4ish and went out again at 5:30 to do the "Mandarin Dive" which turned out to be the best dive of the day. We have done these before. Mandarin fish hide in the coral and mate at dusk by floating up in the water for a few seconds. The previous times we have been to see them, there have been very few of them and they have been hard to see. Tonight there were loads of them and we saw several mating pairs. After watching them for about 30 minutes, we did the second half of the dive (by now in darkness) in an area famous for sea-horses. We saw seven of them. far more than we have ever seen on a single dive before.

Once again, we were worn out after the the three dives so after eating at La Isla Bonita again, we had an early night.

Saturday, 18th August Another day trip - this time with both dives at Gato Island. We dived on our own with Danx as Balt, our guide from the last few days has had to take time off. Two good dives again. The first was very similar to the dive we did here yesterday but without the strong currents. For the second dive, we went through the 45m tunnel that goes all the way under the island. It starts at about 3m on one side of the island and emerges on the other side at about 9m. On both dives we saw large white tip reef sharks, frog fish and loads of nudis. We were back at the dive centre by 2:30 - the good weather and the fact that the boats can go right up to the dive centre has made the diving here a lot more enjoyable for the last couple of days! It means that the whole day is not taken up by two hours of diving.

Did not do a lot for the rest of the afternoon. We went to Oscar's for a couple of beers in the evening and had a few more with our pizza at Angelina's. We returned to the balcony by 9:45 so we could follow the first day of the football season on the laptop while drinking our remaining Bacardi (QPR 0, Swansea 5!!!).

Sunday, 19th August We had not arranged any diving for today and spent most of the day recovering from last night. We went for breakfast at 11 but spent most of the rest of the day in the room. We did do the sunset dive at Lighthouse again. Another very good dive with plenty more mandarin fish and sea-horses. After showering, we returned to the dive centre and had tea at Oscar's. Back in the room before 9pm.
