dwjhaines - 2012 week 54

Monday, 3rd June Back to doing three dives today. We were the only people diving again and this time, we were the only ones on the boat for both dives. The first dive was back at Pescador Island and another 35 - 40 minutes with the sardines. |They really are superb to watch. We went round the East side of the island when we left them and saw two more turtles by the end of the dive.

The afternoon dive started about 100 metres from the dive centre and ended in front of Chili Bar. We were led by Roly, the other dive guide here for this dive. We went in search of pygmy sea-horses again without success but did find two leaf scorpion fish and another turtle. We dived the house reef on our own again just after 4:30. We did the Southern part of the site again where there seems to be a lot more fish and we also saw three turtles.

After a day without alcohol, we had a few drinks again tonight. We ate at Chili Bar again and then came back to the room and drank a half bottle of rum on the balcony.

Tuesday, 4th June Another excellent dive at Pescador to start the day. Again we were the only two on the boat and we were led by Roly. We entered at the cathedral and swam all the way back round to the area with the sardines. We saw the electric clam again, the two frog fish, a yellow box fish and a turtle on the way. By the time we reached the sardines, I was down to 50 bar but still spent 15 minutes watching them before surfacing (on 20 bar). Roz spent 5 - 10 minutes longer watching them.

The afternoon dive was at Kasay where we saw seven turtles. We also saw another three turtles on our evening dive on the house reef. One of these was a hawksbill, the first we have seen since getting to The Philippines.

We ate at Chili Bar again in the evening. The food in there is excellent and I don't think we will be trying anywhere else for the rest of our stay here. We had slightly more rum than usual when we got back to the room and don't clearly remember going to bed.

Wednesday, 5th June Probably the best days diving we have had here. There were two other divers on the boat today but we were still diving on our own with Dhodho. Pescador seems to get better every day that we go there. The sardines were as good as ever today and there was a huge amount of fish along the wall after we had left them.

The afternoon dive was at Talisay. We saw two ornate ghost pipe fish here, something we have not seen many of recently, and eleven turtles. On our evening dive on the house reef, there was quite strong current but we saw another seven turtles. At one point when we turned round, there seemed to be turtles everywhere. There seem to be a lot more around in the last couple of days due to the low tide.

Beer on the balcony while doing log books, pizzas at Chili Bar and a half bottle of rum when we got back. Another enjoyable evening!

Thursday, 6th June Our last days diving in Moalboal - we head to Malaysia tomorrow. We just did the two boat dives today. The resort is starting to get busier and there were four other divers on the boat but once again we were diving on our own with Dhodho. Pescador was as good as ever and we had our last look at the sardines.

We were back at Kasay for the afternoon dive and Roz counted another ten turtles so a good dive to finish with (this bought the total number of turtles seen in The Philippines to 109!).

In the evening we went to Chili Bar for the final pizzas of our stay in The Philippines. We ate with Andrew and Chris, a couple of Canadians who have been on the boat with us for the last couple of days. We had a few beers there and did not leave until after 11. We had a few more drinks back at the room before getting to bed sometime around 1:00.

Friday, 7th June Our flights out of Cebu had been moved so that instead of flying at midday, we were not leaving until 20:15. We had arranged an airport transfer with the dive centre and also a late check-out for 3pm. We did not have much to do all day apart from paying our bill and drying all dive gear.

We were picked up at exactly 3:00 for the three hour journey back to Cebu Airport. The nine days here has gone incredibly quickly and we have both really enjoyed staying here for a second time. The diving has been excellent again and we have really enjoyed the food - even if it has been mainly pizzas. With very cheap alcohol as well it really is an ideal place to stay.

We were at the airport just after 6:00. We were heading for Kota Kinabalu via Manila and checked in for both flights without any problems. The first flight to Manila was a bit late but we were still in Manila by 10pm. We cleared immigration and went through to international departures to wait for our 11:40 flight to Kota Kinabalu. There were quite a few problems at Manila due to an earlier storm. Lots of planes had been diverted from Manila to Clark and these had to be moved back to Manila before anyone could go anywhere. We were initially told we would have a 45 minute delay but we did not take off until after 2am - a delay of about two and a half hours.

Saturday, 8th June We eventually landed at K.K. at 4ish. We had booked a cheap hotel two minutes from the airport for one night and there was someone from the hotel to meet us after we had collected our luggage. By the time we had checked into our room it was about 4:30am. We both went straight to sleep.

We slept until 9:30 when we got up for breakfast. As soon as we had eaten, we checked out of the hotel and got a taxi into K.K. where we had booked two nights at the Le Meridian, a slightly nicer hotel. We were able to check straight into a room which is probably the nicest we will stay in now for the rest of the trip. It is certainly luxurious compared to where we will be staying next!

We spent an hour or so by the pool and then walked out to the Scuba Junkie office to sort out our diving for tomorrow. We had pre-booked a days diving so we took all our gear over there and filled out the paperwork. We stopped and had roti canai for lunch on the way back to the hotel before going to sleep for a couple of hours.

We stayed in K.K. thirteen years ago with Dave but a lot of it is unrecognisable from the place we stayed then. It is now quite a modern city with plenty more building work going on. We went for a bit of a walk round the night market before eating in the evening. This is probably one of the places that has not changed much since we were here last. It was all very colourful and interesting but we ended up eating in an Indian restaurant across the  road from our hotel.

Sunday, 9th June Three dives in the "Tunku Abdul Rahman National Park" today. We walked to the dive centre stopping for roti canai for breakfast on the way. We were the only two customers on the boat and were guided by James from Newcastle. The dives were nothing special. Kota Kinabalu is not known for great diving and we did not have high expectations and the sites were certainly no-where near as good as where we are heading tomorrow. They were all still quite enjoyable and we did see a few big schools of fish, a few more bat fish and two more turtles.

We were back in K.K. by about 3:30 and carried all our wet gear back to the hotel to wash.

We had a few drinks in the evening. Our room had good views of both the town and the sea so we bought a selection of drinks from the supermarket and spent most of the evening sat by our window. We only left the room briefly to eat at another Indian restaurant.
