dwjhaines - 2012 week 25

Monday, 12th November Overnight, we travelled to Kwatisore Bay. This area, still within the larger Cenderawasih Bay is famous for whale sharks. Whale sharks are the worlds biggest fish, growing up to 10 - 12m in length. We have only ever seen two, once when we were diving and once when we were on the way to a dive site. The two we have seen have both been small, about 2.5m and we only had a few minutes with each one.

At Cenderawasih, the fishermen fish from floating platforms called bagans. Whale sharks started coming up and trying to eat the catch through the fishermen's nets. To prevent damage to the nets, the fishermen started feeding the whale sharks small fish. They also believe it is lucky if the whale sharks are present while they are fishing. It is one of the few places in the world where you are almost guaranteed to see the whale sharks and is one of the main reasons we have come on this trip.

The day started early again for Cedric as he had to find a bagan where the whale sharks were present. Apparently there are a total of about 14 whale sharks in the area and they move around the different fishing platforms. Once he had found one, at the third attempt, a price was negotiated with the fisherman for us all to dive underneath the platform. The fisherman have to stop fishing while we are in the water and they also provide the bait fish used to keep the whale sharks there. A price of Rp1,600,000 (just over £100) was agreed and by 7:30 we were able to start diving.

The three dives we did here were excellent. The second in particular was one of the best dives we have ever done. On each dive, we got in and stayed under or near the bagan while two or three whale sharks circled around us the whole time. These were much bigger than the ones we have seen previously. The two that were there all of the time were about 5 - 7m and the third one which only appeared briefly on the second dive was slightly bigger. There was slight current on the first and third dive which made staying near the bagan a bit of an effort but there was none at all on the second. We spent almost four hours in total watching them - there was hardly any time that we were in the water without at least one of them within sight. Definitely one of the highlights of the whole trip.

We had the option of staying with the whale sharks for a second day but everyone agreed that one day was the right amount of time to spend here so we started moving again after the third dive. We anchored for the night in a bay and did another exploratory night dive. It was OK but a bit of an anticlimax after the rest of the days diving.

Tuesday, 13th November Four more dives today. The diving in Cenderawasih is not as good as in some places we have been recently. The reason to come here is to see the whale sharks. The dive guides do not know the other sites as they have not been here before and do not really know which are the best places to dive. The first dive at "Weka's Surprise" was another ridge similar to the ones two days ago. This site was probably quite nice once but a lot of the coral was dead due to the large numbers of crown of thorns all over it.

We moved on to Mangguar Point which was a lot better and stayed there for the rest of the day. Dives two and three were around a small group of islands and were very good dives with plenty of fish and lots of nice coral. The night dive was in the sheltered bay and was OK without being too spectacular.

Wednesday, 14th November The first dive today was a muck dive but there was not a lot to see. The second dive was better. As well as a decent reef, we saw two turtles - both very scared of divers - and a white-tip reef shark. Third dive started at the wreck of a small Japanese plane. Again, the reef was OK in places and there were a few fish to see. The night dive was better than usual. There were just three of us doing the night dive tonight - numbers have been dropping each evening - but it was probably the best one of the trip. Still not particularly brilliant but there were enough crabs and shrimps around to make it worth doing.

Thursday, 15th November Two dives today to complete the diving for this trip. We did two dives at the same site at Waimundi Island. Both dives were excellent - really nice hard coral formations again and plenty to see. Over the two dives we saw a turtle, an eagle ray, a black-tip reef shark and a school of 15 bump-heads. There were plenty of schools of fish around, hardly any current and both dives were very enjoyable.

We had finished the second dive by midday. Time to wash all the gear and hang it up to dry while we begun the nine hour trip back to Manokwari. Did not do too much during the trip back. As we got closer to Manokwari we were able to get mobile phone coverage for the first time in almost a week and were finally able to find out last Saturday's football results.

In the evening, Joey put her photos on the television in the saloon so we had a few hours in there while drinking another bottle of rum. Most people had gone to bed by about 9:30. We finished the remainder of our rum in our cabin before going to sleep about an hour later.

Friday, 16th November Woke up in the harbour at Manokwari to find it was raining heavily. The weather on this trip has been OK but there have been a few days of rain or cloud. All the other group were flying out of Manokwari this morning but we have a night here before flying out tomorrow. We said goodbye to everyone at 8:00 and had a few hours waiting on the boat before Cedric called for a taxi to take us to our hotel. We had booked a night at the Swiss-Belhotel and Cedric came with us to the hotel at about 11:00. We were pleased to find that we had free Wi-Fi included. There is not a lot to do in Manokwari. The only reason we are staying here is that the flights to Makassar were cheaper if we fly tomorrow. It was quite nice to be able to spend most of the day in an air-conditioned room catching up with e-mail, etc after 10 days on the boat.

We did go out for a walk around Manokwari for an hour or so. We tried to reconfirm tomorrow's flights at the Batavia office but the woman there did not seem to know what we were talking about. We stopped for lunch and bought a few things from the supermarket next to the hotel. I went for a 5km run in the hotel gym but that was about all we did all day.

We decided to eat at the KFC next to the hotel in the evening. It had been raining on and off all day and we could not be bothered to walk any further. We met Cedric there, ate with him and returned to the hotel bar for a drink (non-alcoholic as Manokwari is completely dry). We said our good-byes again and were back in the room by 9:30.

Saturday, 17th November We were up at 5:00 as we had to get a 7:00 flight to Makassar. We had booked the taxi that bought us to the hotel to pick us up again but he was no-where to be seen at 5:30. I phoned him and it sounded as if he had just woken up but we managed to get another taxi for the same price and were still at the airport by 6:00. We checked in without any problems and then had about an hour to wait in one of the smallest airports we have ever been to.

The flight was only a few minutes late leaving and we arrived in Makassar just about on time at about 9:30. We got a taxi into the centre of the city where we had booked a hotel for the night. Makassar is another place without a lot to see or do but we fly out of Indonesia from here tomorrow and it is not too bad a place for a one night stop-over. We went out for a walk after checking into the hotel.

We wanted to buy something to drink during the football tonight but although beer was widely available, we could not find anywhere selling spirits. There were plenty of supermarkets for everything else that we wanted but no sign of anything stronger than beer. Makassar is quite strongly Muslim and selling alcohol is not really encouraged. We had just about given up when we found a Chinese supermarket round the corner from the hotel that sold bottles of vodka.

We went from there to wander round the few attractions of the town - a pretty grubby looking sea-front and a Dutch fort that we could not be bothered to go and walk round when we got there.

For the second day running we ended up eating in a KFC due to the weather. Makassar seems to be the hottest place we have been so far and after a couple of hours of walking we were in need of somewhere with air-conditioning. We were back in the hotel by 1:30 and had another afternoon of not doing a lot.

We had a couple of drinks in the room before going out to eat. We ate at Kios Semarang which was recommended in Lonely Planet. This was an excellent place with good food, cheap beer and a good atmosphere. There were plenty of Indonesians in there drinking as well and they showed Arsenal v Spurs which was a bonus. We spent half our time in there talking to a group of locals who were all keen to practice their English and we were already quite pissed when we left. We returned to the hotel and continued drinking the vodka while listening to Soccer Saturday and watching Liverpool v Wigan. Swansea beat Newcastle 2 - 1 but we only vaguely remember the end of the game. A very enjoyable evening on our last night in Indonesia.

Sunday, 18th November Slept for most of the morning and checked out of the hotel at 1pm. We got a taxi to the airport and checked in for the 4:15 flight to Kuala Lumpa. This had been a very cheap way of leaving Indonesia as the three hour flight with Air Asia had only cost us about £30 each. The flight was just about on time and we landed at LCCT at 7:45. We collected our luggage and walked to the Tune Hotel for the second time on this trip. We were both totally worn out by the time we got to the hotel - we had been awake for about 22 - 23 hours yesterday. We went back to the airport for a McDonalds meal and were in bed by 10:00.
