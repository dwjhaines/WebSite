dwjhaines - 2012 week 8

Monday, 16th July Yesterday, we had booked tickets for the 9:30 bus to Tawau. There is nothing to see or do in Tawau but it is one of the easiest places in the world to pick up Indonesian visas which we need for later in the year. We left the hostel after breakfast and left a lot of our luggage, together with all of our dive gear, across the road with Scuba Junkie (we will be back here in a weeks time). It was a 10 minute walk to the bus station and the bus left a few minutes late just after 9:30. We got into Tawau at about 11:15 and walked to the MB Hotel which we had booked for two nights. We considered going straight to the consulate but decided to have lunch first and set off at about 12:30.

We started walking to the consulate but when we had to stop and ask directions, we found that it had moved from the location stated in Lonely Planet and we had to get a taxi out to it. We got there just before it reopened after lunch and found about 100 people sat around waiting. When it did open, we were shown straight to the visa counter and after filling out all the paperwork and paying about £35 each, we were told that the visas would be issued today. We had expected to have to come back tomorrow at the earliest so this was a bit of a bonus. We sat/stood around waiting for just over an hour but by 3pm, we had our passports back together with the visas. We got a bus back into Tawau and were back at the hotel by 4.

The downside to us getting the visas so quickly is that we have booked two nights in Tawau and there really is nothing to do here. Lonely Planet describes Tawau as Sabah's least memorable city and our hotel is paid for until Wednesday! It is a very Muslim city so there are no bars (although we are still on our week without alcohol so this is probably a good thing) and there are no sights to see at all. Did not do a lot for the rest of the day. The one good thing about Tawau is that good hotels are very cheap so spent most of the rest of the day in our room. Went out for another Indian meal at 7:30 but were back in the hotel an hour later.

Tuesday, 17th July Quite an enjoyable day spent doing very little. We left the hotel to walk to the bus station after breakfast. We wanted to buy tickets for tomorrow's bus to Sandakan but were told you could not buy tickets in advance and should just turn up in the morning. This was a bit disappointing as it was the only thing we had to do today. We wandered round Tawau and posted some postcards before returning to the hotel. We went out again for lunch before walking to the highlight of Tawau - a mosque with a giant crab in front of it! The roundabout with the giant crab also had a giant lobster and a giant fish. Exciting stuff!

Spent most of the rest of the day reading in the room while catching up with e-mails and sorting out underwater photos, etc. Like last night, we went out to eat at 7:30 and were back in the room an hour later.

Wednesday, 18th July Left the hotel at 7:30 and walked to the bus station where we got the 8:00 bus to Sandakan. The bus was old and not quite as luxurious as some buses in Malaysia but was less than half full so was reasonably comfortable. The journey took almost seven hours which went pretty quickly. I read for the whole journey and finished my book about 10 minutes before we arrived. The bus station is 2.5 miles outside Sandakan. We were quoted RM15 by a taxi driver which seemed too much so got a minibus instead for RM1 each.

The minibus dropped us in the centre and we had a five minute walk to our hotel. We had booked two nights at the Sheraton. This hotel only opened two months ago and is not yet finished.

There is still building work going on and some parts of it - e.g. the gym, the gift shop and the top six floors - are not yet open. However, the pool is finished, some of the rooms are finished and the views are superb. At £40 a night, it is probably the best value place we will stay on the entire trip. It is certainly an improvement on the place we stayed in last time we were here. It will be well outside our budget when it is completed!

We had time for about an hour by the pool before heading out to look around the town. We had stayed here 12 years ago with Dave and remember that everything shut at about 6pm. Things have changed a bit since then but it is still not the most lively of places. We did find a few Chinese restaurants by the waterfront that were quite nice to sit and have a beer in (our alcohol free week is now over). We had one drink before returning to the hotel for showers and then went back to the same restaurant to eat. We had a few more beers with our meal and in another place closer to the hotel. We then returned to our room  where we got through a couple of bottles of local "apple rum".

Thursday, 19th July Another day of not doing a lot. Had a very good breakfast in the hotel (included in the price) and then spent a few hours by the pool. Left the hotel briefly for lunch and again in the evening to go to the same Chinese restaurant as last night. Had a few beers again but gave the apple rum a miss tonight.

Friday, 20th July We checked out of the hotel just before 11 after explaining to the receptionist how to use the credit card machine! We had booked a two day trip to a lodge on the Kinabatangan River and the minibus from Barefoot Lodge was waiting for us by the time we had got outside the hotel. We stopped in Sandakan to pick up a Dutch family doing the same trip as us and then had a 2.5 hour journey to Sukau. The lodge is fairly basic, not quite up to the Sheraton standard, but has a really nice eating area overlooking the river and will be fine for a couple of days. We had lunch and then had a couple of hours to kill before our first river cruise at 4pm.

The reason to come to this area is to view the wildlife and the package we have booked includes four river cruises at  various different times. The first cruise was excellent. We saw four orang-utans which we were not really expecting to see. There were two on their own and one mother and baby. This was more than some people on our trip had seen when visiting Sepilok. We also saw a family of about 20 proboscis monkeys that can only be seen in Borneo.

The highlight of the trip was twenty minutes viewing a herd of about 25 - 30 pygmy elephants. As the name suggests, these are slightly smaller than other species of elephants but were still a fair size. We had hoped to catch a glimpse of these animals and had been led to believe that sightings were quite rare. However, we were able to watch them eating at the side of the river from a couple of metres away, not in the slightest bit bothered about us being there. We had been unsure about how much we would enjoy doing this trip but this experience on its own made it worth coming.

We were back at the lodge for tea at 7 but the planned night river cruise get postponed until tomorrow due to the amount of debris floating down the river following recent storms. Spent the rest of the evening in the eating area and had an early night.

Saturday, 21st July Three more cruises today, the first before breakfast at 6am. This one was no-where near as good as the one yesterday. We did see another orang-utan and more monkeys but not too much else apart from a few birds. We did a 30 minute "jungle trek" during which we saw two leaches and not a lot else. We had been advised that we needed walking boots and leach resistant socks for this walk. Roz borrowed a pair of shoes from another guest and I did it in sandals and British Airways socks. We both seemed to survive!

After breakfast, we had quite a bit of time to kill as the next cruise was not until 4pm. We spent the time sat around in the dining area and briefly lying in the sun. The second cruise was better. We saw mainly monkeys - both proboscis monkeys and long tailed lemurs. However, unlike yesterday, we were on a small tributary of the main river so were much closer to them and were able to get some reasonable pictures even with our cameras. We were back at the lodge for tea and went for the night cruise just after 8. This was quite good again. We saw a few owls and some other birds which were not that spectacular but also saw a couple of tiny kingfishers. One of them  let us get to within about a metre of before it flew off.

We were back at the lodge by 9:30 by which time the restaurant area was closed off so had showers and went to bed.

All the cruises today were unlikely to match the experience with the elephants yesterday but there has been enough to see to keep us interested and it has been an enjoyable couple of days. We had been unsure how long to stay here but the two nights and four cruises was probably about right. What we have seen has been very good but there is probably no reason to have stayed here any longer.

Sunday, 22nd July Up at 7:15 for breakfast and we were then transferred by minibus, together with a Dutch couple to the main road between Sandakan and Semporna (everyone at the lodge was Dutch for some unknown reason). We had expected to be the only ones there but there were about 15 - 20 people there by 9:00 all waiting for the bus to Semporna. The bus arrived at 9:00 and was almost full already but the four of us had places reserved so all got seats without any problems. The journey was quicker than expected and we got back into Semporna just before 1. We checked back in to the Scuba Junkie backpackers to find there was a power cut so no fan or air-con.

We had lunch in a Chinese restaurant. All the Indian ones are closed during the during daylight hours day at the moment as Ramadan started yesterday. We had a few things to do in Semporna before we head to Mabul tomorrow for 10 days. We got more cash out so we could pay Scuba Junkie another load of money. We will owe them a lot when we get back from Mabul and giving them cash saves on the credit card fees. We retrieved our dive bag so we could sort out our equipment and I also went out and got my hair cut. Did not do a lot else for the rest of the day. The electric came back on at about 4:30. We went back to our usual Indian restaurant for tea  and walked to the supermarket to stock up on diet coke for our stay in Mabul. We were back in our room by 9.00.
