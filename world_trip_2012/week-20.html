dwjhaines - 2012 week 20

Monday, 8th October We are staying just across the road from the U.S.A.T. Liberty ship wreck. This was an American army transport ship that was torpedoed by the Japanese during World War II and towed to the beach in Bali for unloading. When Mt Agung erupted in 1963, the ship was pushed off the beach and now sits in about 30m of water, a few minutes away from the shore. This is one of the best and easiest wreck dives in the world due to its proximity to the beach and the fact that the top of the wreck is only about 5m deep. It is also one of the most popular so we were up at 6:15 to try and complete the dive before the large number of divers start arriving on day-trips from other parts of Bali.

We got ready to dive at the dive centre while a porter carried our tanks and BCD's down to the beach. It was a two minute walk down to the entry point. All we had to do was get our gear on and walk into the sea. This was a repeat of the very first dive we did after qualifying almost exactly 12 years ago. We were in the water before 7:00 and although there were a few other divers around, the wreck was still reasonably quiet. We had a very enjoyable 75 minute dive before heading back over the road to the resort for breakfast.

The porter system is quite hard to get used to here. When you get back from a dive, you just leave all your equipment lying on the beach and walk back to the dive centre. There is a co-operative system of dive porters and someone will make sure that your tank gets taken back to where-ever it should go. Although it is often easier to carry the gear yourself, this is how a lot of people in the area make a living and carrying your own stuff is definitely not the done thing. The system seems to work but leaving almost a thousand pounds worth of dive equipment lying on a public beach does not seem right. Traditionally, the porters are female, although this seems to be changing, and seeing an Indonesian girl carry two tanks on her head is also a bit strange!

For our second dive, we went to Drop-Off. This involved a 10 minute walk from the dive centre. Our gear was taken by moped so again was waiting for us when we got there (again, the porters know where you want to dive and just leave your gear lying on the beach for you). The start of the dive was delayed as my o-ring was leaking so I had to carry the tank back for a replacement while Roz waited in the water. Again this a very nice dive of just over 70 minutes. For our third dive, we were back at the Liberty. This is the easiest site to get to and the best dive. It was very good again. By the time we were in the water at 4ish, the day-trippers were gone and we just about had the wreck to ourselves. A very good days diving.

Did not do too much for the rest of the day. By the time we had showered and cleaned our equipment after the third dive it was 6:30. We filled out log books and went out to eat at the TWD restaurant about two minutes walk down the road. Back in the room by 8:30.

Tuesday, 9th October A similar days diving to yesterday. We did the wreck again at 6:45 - there is such a lot to see there that we can easily dive here a couple of times a day without getting bored. After breakfast, I walked to the TWD Villas which is about 1km away. We can not keep our room beyond Thursday but they are upgrading us to one of their villas. I think the rooms will be very nice but we will lose the convenience of being 20m from the dive centre.

For the second dive we went to Coral Gardens, which is the site in between the two we dived yesterday. This was a bit boring for the first 10 - 15 minutes but suddenly seemed to get loads better. We found a small mound of tyres and other rubbish that had a huge amount of fish around it. As we left that area there was a black tip reef shark circling around and the amount of fish on the second half of the dive made it probably a better dive than the second one yesterday.

For the third dive it was back to the Liberty again. This time we headed South from the entry point and spent about 30 minutes swimming over the black sand. Yesterday we had seen a wunderpus on the way to the wreck and the whole area is good for muck diving. Roz managed to find an ornate ghost pipe fish before we got back to the wreck.

The wreck was excellent again. We only saw a small part of it tonight but you do not need to move around it. You can wait in one spot and watch everything going on around you for ages without needing to go anywhere.

We were back slightly earlier tonight. We ate at the Safety Stop again and were back by about 9.

Wednesday, 10th October We went a bit further afield to dive today. There are a couple of sites about 10 minutes drive from Tulamben that had been recommended to us. We had not dived one of them and the other we had dived once five years ago, so we went with a guide for these dives. The dives were both very good. The first at Kubu was supposed to be a reef dive but was mainly a muck dive.

The second, at Seraya, was purely a muck dive, spending the whole time over black sand. We saw all the usual stuff - frog fish, ghost pipe fish, leaf scorpion fish, etc - as well as a few things we have never seen before. Wayan, our guide found a hairy shrimp on the first dive causing him much excitement. This is a shrimp that is about 5mm long and was only recently discovered as a new species. He had never seen one before either. On the second we saw boxer crabs which were also new to us.

For the third dive, we were back to diving un-guided. It as a very similar dive to yesterday afternoon's. We did not see the ghost pipe fish again but did find a ribbon eel.

We ate at the Safety Stop again and once again, we were back by about 9.

Thursday, 11th October Three more dives today and a repeat of the dives we did two days ago - i.e. two dives at the wreck again and the one in between at Coral Garden. The wreck dives were both excellent again. We saw fifteen bump-head parrot fish on the first dive. We have rarely seen these except for here and at Sipadan and I don't think we have ever been as close to them as we were this morning.

At coral garden we returned to the cleaning station at the pile of tyres. The shrimps there will come onto your fingers and clean bits of dead skin from under the nails. They usually clean bits of dead skin and algae from fish but will do the same to your hands in some places. On the third dive, we  tried without success to find the frog fish that lives on the wreck. However, we did manage to photograph the two long-nosed hawk fish that we spotted two days ago.

We checked out of our room before the second dive and, after the dive, we were taken by moped to the villas which are about 1km away. The villas are very nice. We have a bedroom with our own bathroom and toilet just outside the room. There is one other bedroom and a huge outdoor living area with tables and chairs. There is a very small plunge pool and a large garden leading down to the sea. We also have the use of a swimming pool in front of the next villa along. It is nicer than the room we have just left but not as convenient for the dive centre. The staff there are happy to take us back and fore on mopeds but we ended up walking down for the third dive as the mopeds did not turn up! It is only a fifteen minute walk so it is not a huge problem.

We walked down to Safety Stop to eat in the evening and were bought back by moped by the dive guides again.

Friday, 12th October We were collected by moped for our usual early morning dive on the wreck. We managed to find the frog fish this time. We were only 120m out when we were looking for it yesterday!! Plenty more bump-heads again - probably about 20 today but none as close as yesterday. Vis was better today than it has been so another very good dive. Second dive was at drop-off. Very nice again - more enjoyable than the first time we did it a few days ago, probably because of the better vis. We had a couple of hours back at the villa before being picked up for the third dive. This time we spent about 40 minutes over the sand before getting to the wreck. Loads to see including two ghost pipe fish and a leaf scorpion fish. This was our longest dive here so far and meant that we had been underwater for more than four hours today.

We walked down into the village to eat at the TWD restaurant and were bought back by moped again.

Saturday, 13th October The pick-up was slightly earlier than expected this morning meaning that we were in the water by 6:30. This turned out very well as we found a group of about 30 bump-heads before we got to the wreck. (They all sleep on the wreck and move off as the sun rises). We were able to stay with them for about 20 minutes as they woke up and started moving away. Again, the fact that we were so close to them made this an incredible experience. We did not bother going back to the villa between the first and second dive - we had breakfast at the dive centre resort and stayed by the pool for an hour instead. The second dive was at coral garden again. We went the other way to usual today and covered some of the area between this site and the wreck. There was not so much to see here but we still found three ribbon-eels and saw the black-tip reef shark again so it was still a good dive.

We were driven back to the villa just after midday so had a few extra hours until the third dive. The sea had started to get quite rough when we did the second dive and by the time we went in for the third, it was quite an effort to get in and out of the water. We just about managed it and once again it was a very good dive. We did our usual afternoon dive with half on the sand and half on the wreck. The vis was down because of the rougher sea but still saw ghost pipe fish, leaf scorpion fish, a ribbon eel as well as everything on the wreck. Had another close encounter with a bump-head as we were leaving the wreck to complete another very good days diving.

Before going out to eat, we contacted the owner to book a room here at the end of the month for a few more days. The diving has been excellent and there is no way we are going to be able to dive anywhere else for such low prices. We will come back here before going back to Kuta at the end of our stay in Bali. There was a buffet meal at the villa next to ours tonight so we ate there instead of walking into the village. It was supposed to be a bit of a social event but although just about everyone who was diving with TWD was there, no-one was really talking to anyone else so we left after we had eaten and were back at our own villa by 8:30. We phoned Ayla as it is her birthday today but did not do much else for the rest of the evening.

Sunday, 14th October Another early start - we were in the water slightly earlier than yesterday. The dives here seem to get better each day. There were bump-heads everywhere on this dive. We think that due to the rougher sea, there were more of them sheltering on the wreck. There were about 20 hanging around the rudder of the wreck as we got there. We spent about 15 - 20 minutes with them before continuing onto the wreck and be kept meeting others as we swam round. Despite diving the same site 12 times in a week, we are still enjoying it and look forward to coming back in a couple of weeks.

The conditions today were still bad. Roz had to be helped into and out of the water at the wreck by guides from TWD who were there with other divers. We had planned to do the second dive at Coral Garden but were advised that the waves there would be even bigger so we did Drop-off again instead. This did not really matter and it was another very good dive. This was our last dive here as we leave tomorrow. We washed our equipment back at the dive centre and were taken back to the villa by about 12:30.

We spent the afternoon lazing around by the villa. I spent a couple of hours editing photos from the last few days. We walked back to the dive centre to pay our bill for the last nine days. It came to just over $800 for accommodation, diving, transfers, drinks and a repair to Roz's BCD. It is incredible value for where we have been staying and the amount of diving we have done here.

After walking back to the villa, we had a few drinks on our terrace. There was someone else staying in the other room in our villa tonight so we did not have it all to ourselves but he disappeared at about 7:30. We walked down into the village about an hour later and ate at Safety Stop again. Back at the villa we continued drinking and got through most of a bottle of rum. Got to bed sometime after 1am.
