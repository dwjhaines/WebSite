dwjhaines - 2012 week 17

Tuesday, 18th September We left Kingsclere at 4:30pm and were at Heathrow just before 6. The car was collected from Terminal 3 so within about 10 minutes of getting there, we had checked in and were on our way to the BA lounge again. We had three and a half hours in the lounge, the second half of which we spent watching the Man City champions league game.

We had boarded the plane by 10 and it took off a few minutes late at about 10:30.No upgrades this time meaning that we were sat in economy, but the flight was quite empty and we had plenty of space so it was still quite comfortable. The service seemed to be a lot better than it had been in business class and we got through quite a few bottles of wine before falling asleep.

Wednesday, 19th September We landed at Bangkok at 3:30pm and, after getting through immigration and collecting our luggage, we got a taxi to the Holiday Inn Express. We got a room on the 18th floor so had an excellent view of Bangkok including the National Stadium.

We also had a perfect view of another football ground that is opposite the hotel. We went out to buy crisps and drinks (from Tesco!) and then returned to the room to watch บีอีซี-เทโรศาสน เอฟซี V บุรีรัมย์ ยูไนเต็ด BEC - (Tero Sasana FC. V Buriram United.) in the Toyota League Cup quarter final 2nd leg. The game finished 2 - 2 meaning that บีอีซี-เทโรศาสน เอฟซี (managed by Sven-Goran Eriksson) were knocked out on away goals.

By the time the game had finished, it was almost 9:00. We walked out to Siam Square and ate at Ban Khun Mae - probably our favourite restaurant in Thailand. We drank a bottle of Sang Som (Thai whiskey) with the meal and returned to the room where we drank another couple of bottles. Got to bed sometime around 2am.

Thursday, 20th September Did not do a lot today. We got up for breakfast at 10 but then slept until about 12:30. We went out for a walk to Panthip Plaza looking at cameras again and then walked back to the MBK for lunch in the food hall. It rained quite heavily for a couple of hours in the afternoon. By the time we went out in the evening, the Siam Square area was completely flooded. We needed to buy malaria tablets but getting to the pharmacist took a lot longer than expected due to having to wade through puddles. By the time we got to Watsons, the pharmacist had left for the day so we had to return to the MBK where we got what we needed from Boots. We thought about eating at MBK again but the food hall was closing up as we got there so we returned to Siam Square (by which time a lot of the water had gone) and ate at Ban Khun Mae for the second night in a row.

Friday, 21st September After what seemed like only a few hours sleep, we were up at 4am as we had booked ourselves onto the 6:15 flight to Bali. We checked out of the hotel and got a taxi to the airport without any problems and had checked in and gone through security by about 5am. We had breakfast before boarding the Air Asia plane which left on time. The flight took just over four hours and we landed in Bali at 11:30 local time. Everything went reasonably smoothly at the airport and within an hour we got got through immigration, collected our luggage and had managed to withdraw money from both of our accounts.

We were staying the night at a Holiday Inn that is only about 500m from the airport. This was booked using the loyalty points that I get for using my credit card. We had planned to walk to the hotel but realised that we would have to go quite a long way round and ended up getting a taxi for about £2. We had to wait about 20 minutes for our room to be ready but it is an excellent hotel with a very nice pool right next to the beach. Spent the rest of the afternoon lying by the pool.

At 5:30, we walked into the centre of Kuta which is about 1.5 miles away from where we are staying. There is a footpath that goes all the way along the top of the beach so we were able to take in the views of the sun setting on the way.

We left the beach when the sun disappeared and wandered round the Poppies lanes for half an hour or so looking for bars or restaurants that we used to visit when we have stayed here previously. A lot of places have changed beyond recognition or been completely demolished and rebuilt. We managed to find a pizza place that we have been going to for years and ate there again before walking back to the hotel.

By the time we were back at the hotel we were both totally worn out. The combination of the alcohol consumed in the last week together with the travelling and the lack of sleep seems to have caught up with us. We tried (without success) to book ourselves somewhere to stay for tomorrow and had an early night.

Saturday, 22nd September We had breakfast at 9ish at a restaurant about 5 minutes walk away along the beach and spent an hour or so back at the hotel before checking out at 11. We got a taxi back to the airport and checked in for the 1:35 Lion Air flight to Labuan Bajo (from where we get on our live-aboard on Sunday). The flight left about 40 minutes late which was not too bad and we landed in Labuan Bajo just before 4pm. We got another taxi into the centre of town and were dropped off at the Gardena Hotel which was where we stayed when we were here 12 years ago. Some of the rooms at the Gardena are quite nice and most of the really basic ones have superb views. Unfortunately, the only room they had was very basic and had no view at all. We decided not to stay so I waited on the main road with all our luggage while Roz went in search of a better option. After about an hour she returned in a bemo having found a room further up the hill from the town. I got into the bemo with the luggage and it took us back up to "Seirama Villa Alam".

The room was still quite a long walk up the hill from the road but once there, the view looking down at the harbour and across to Komodo was excellent. The view we had from the Gardena 12 years ago was one of the best we have ever had but this one probably beat it. Our "room" room consisting of a small air conditioned bedroom and a large open terrace/balcony with an open air kitchen and a bathroom  We sat on the terrace and watched the sunset before having showers. We then had a couple of drinks on the balcony before walking back into town.

We had hoped to find somewhere showing the Swansea v Everton game but the only place  showing football was showing a game from Scotland and they did not have the required channel. After we had given up on that, we walked to another restaurant to eat before returning to the balcony to follow the rest of the football with the laptop and a very intermittent internet connection. We had a few more drinks and got to bed just after 1am.

Sunday, 23rd September Another day of not doing a lot. Breakfast was bought to our balcony at 9 and apart from going into town for meals, we spent the whole day lazing around on the balcony or sleeping. We walked into town at 1ish and ate at the Gardena's restaurant. This was the place where 12 years ago we had got drunk and were talked into learning to dive by Steve and Lilian!

We went back into town in the evening and both had Italian meals at The Lounge. By 9pm we were back on the balcony and had a slightly earlier night than yesterday.
