dwjhaines - 2012 week 15

Monday, 3rd September We did not have much that we wanted to do in Hong Kong but headed out at about 10:00 when we had both got up to have a look around the sights. We had enjoyed the bus journey in last night so instead of going on the MTR (underground) we got a couple of trams which took us to the Central area along the same route. At Central, we had a trip up to Mid Levels on the escalators before walking back down to the Star Ferry terminal. We took the ferry across the harbour to Kowloon and had a look round a couple of camera shops (Roz's underwater camera needs replacing) before getting the ferry back to Hong Kong Island. We got the MTR back to the hotel and were back in the room by 3:00. It was a very enjoyable few hours but one day here was probably enough.

We had been due to fly home via Helsinki tomorrow morning but our flights had been cancelled. We found this out by chance just before leaving home - BA had not notified us. After a lot of phone calls and e-mails, we had been put on a direct flight to London tonight at 11:40. We had also been upgraded to Economy Plus as an apology for there lack of communication.

We left the hotel at 6:30 (the room was booked and paid for until tomorrow) and walked across to the bus stop where we got the 6:45 bus to the airport. Again, it was an enjoyable one hour journey. At the airport, we could check in immediately and were given a second upgrade to business class - not bad considering we paid just over Â£100 each for these flights.  We went straight through passport control and into the lounge for a few hours before the flight. We had a few drinks in the lounge and boarded the plane at about 11:30. The business class section was very good with loads more room than we needed and seats that recline to form flat beds. All very nice but not worth the sort of money that people pay for these seats!

We spent a few more hours drinking and I think we got to sleep sometime around 10pm UK time.

Tuesday, 4th September Were woken for breakfast at about 3am and arrived at Heathrow slightly early just after 5. We collected the car and were back in Kingsclere just before 7.

Thursday, 6th September After 48 hours at home, we were on our way back to Heathrow at 10:45 as we headed out to Moldova for the England game tomorrow night. We met up with Mark and Steve in the bar at Terminal 1 for a pint before getting on the 1:15 Lufthansa flight to Munich.

We were in Munich before 4:00 which meant that we had four and a half hours in AirBrau - the airport brewery. This was an excellent place to drink with cheap beer and good food. We left the brewery at 8:30 and went back into Terminal 2 for the 9:25 flight to Chisinau. On this flight we were sat just behind quite a lot of the commentators flying out for the game (Andy Townsend, Danny Mills and Gareth Southgate). We arrived on time in Chisinau, just after 1am and were met by a taxi to take us to Hotel Lidia.

Friday, 7th September We were all up for breakfast at 9 before returning to our rooms for showers. We left the hotel just at 11ish and walked over to the hotel where the FA are based so that we could pick up our tickets for tonight's game. There are not a lot of spectacular sights to see in Chisinau and we saw a lot of what there is to see (a few big buildings) on the way to the hotel. We also picked up our copies of Free Lions on the way and got our tickets with no problems.

We sat reading Free Lions in a nearby park for 20 minutes or so and then went for our first beer of the day. By this time, we had realised that with the lack of things to do here, we needed to find something to occupy us for tomorrow. We had heard about trips out to wine cellars that this area was famous for and after having a drink, we returned to our hotel to look into the options. On the internet we found the address of a travel agent that organised tours and went out to try and find it. We did not make it to the planned travel agent but found another one on the way. We spent more than an hour in the office and eventually came away having booked a trip for tomorrow starting at 11:00 from our hotel.

By the time we had paid for the tour and had our vouchers printed, it was getting on for 4:00. We got a taxi out to The Beer House (recommended by Lonely Planet) where we had a late lunch and a few more beers. After leaving there, we wandered towards the railway station in search of cheaper bars. We found one right outside the station which had beer for 55p a pint and stayed there until about 8:45 - an hour before kick-off. We got a taxi to the ground and were dropped outside the ground at 9:10.

Getting into the ground was all a bit chaotic. The ground only holds 10,000 but only has one entrance with about 8 turnstiles. Everyone was trying to squeeze through one narrow entrance and the huge number of police there seemed to be making things worse. Despite the queues, we did get into the ground on time and were stood together just in time for kick-off. England won the game fairly comfortably 5 - 0. Inside the ground the policing was as bad as it had been outside with 100's of police, mostly in full riot gear stood in front of about 800 England fans who did not show any signs at all of causing any trouble. Just going to the toilets involved going through lines of police and being searched in both directions. They inevitably held the England fans in the ground after the final whistle but it was not for too long and we managed to get out by about 11:45.

As soon as we got out of the ground, we managed to get a taxi to take us back into the centre. We returned to the  same pub that we had been to for our first drink of the day before returning to the hotel just after 2am.

Saturday, 8th September We had breakfast at 9:30 and were collected for our tour at 11:15.

We were picked up by a car and driven for about 25 minutes to Milestii Mici, home of the "State Enterprise Quality Wines Industrial Complex". This is in the Guinness Book of Records as being the largest wine collection in the world. Wine is produced here and it is home to 1.5 million bottles stored in 200km of underground tunnels.

The tour involved driving round 4km of the tunnels with a guide. The tunnels and collections were pretty spectacular and the whole trip was well worth doing. It took about an hour to get round and we then had about 30 minutes in the tasting room. We could of done with longer here as there was still plenty of wine left when we were told we had to leave. We bought a couple of bottles from the gift shop as we left and were driven back to our hotel by about 2pm.

By this time, the weather had improved quite a bit so we bought food from a shop near the hotel and spent a few hours sat in the hotel garden drinking the wine that we had just bought. We left the hotel just after 5 and went out for the rest of the evening. We had one beer in "The Carlsberg Pub" while watching Scotland v Serbia, and then had a meal in the slightly more Moldavian restaurant next door to it. After eating, we returned to the terrace bar we had been to yesterday and stayed there until we walked home just before midnight.

Sunday, 9th September A fairly uneventful journey home. We checked out of the hotel at 11 and walked to the main road to get a taxi. The taxi to the airport involved us all being a bunch of grapes by the driver for some reason. We were at the airport by 11:30 and the flight back to Munich left on time at 1:00. We had less than an hour in Munich this time and were back in Heathrow on time at 4:30. We drove Steve back to Woking and were back in Kingsclere by 6ish.
