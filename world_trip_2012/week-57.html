dwjhaines - 2012 week 57

Monday, 24th June Three more relatively local dives today. Roz managed to get her high pressure hose changed before the first dive.  She has the same problem with the swivel joint that I had in Mexico but at least the hose is not leaking any more. We were diving on our own with Dave again today with quite a few other people on the boat. The first dive was at Black Ray Channel. This dive was OK and we did see two hawksbills but not too much else. Another group saw a manta or devil ray which we must of missed by about a minute.

Dive two was at Kapalai House Reef. This is a site we have dived a lot in the past but had not been to yet this year. There was still plenty to see. No ornates but four "leafies" (as leaf scorpion fish now appear to be known) and another turtle on one of the artificial houses.

After lunch it was back to Awas for a 107 minute dive. We pretty much saw the same things as a few days ago but it was still a really enjoyable dive. Not as many turtles today but a tiny cockatoo wasp fish at the end of the dive. We went to the Scuba Junkie bar for a beer after eating and had one more beer on the balcony while doing log books but it was still an early night as we have another early start tomorrow.

Tuesday, 25th June Our final day of diving at Sipadan. We were supposed to be diving with Khai but he was not feeling well so he took the two OW divers instead. We dived with Stu together with two Danish blokes and Hannah from Holland. It was another excellent day's diving. We dived South Point first followed by Mid Reef, a site we have not dived this year, and then Barracuda Point for the third. We were all given the option of where we wanted to do the final dive and just about unanimously agreed to do Barracuda again.

All the dives were excellent. There was a lot more current than on previous days here but it was not too bad and everyone was able to cope. We did not se anything that we have not seen already but there were another 51 sharks and 87 turtles. It had been another very good day out and it was very sad to be leaving the island for the last time.

We had a few drinks in the bar in the evening and a few more on our balcony after eating.

Wednesday, 26th June Three more local dives today to complete our diving for this trip. We checked out of our room after breakfast, left our luggage in the restaurant and were on the jetty by 9:00. We were guide by Nas again today and also had Sara and Andreas with us. First dive was at Cleaning Station on Kapalai. This was Roz's favourite site here last year due to the turtles that were always sleeping in the structures at the start of the site. There were none there today which was disappointing but we did see a hawksbill and it was still a good dive. We did Artificial Reef for the second dive. We have done this site quite a lot in previous years but this was the first time here on this visit. Quite a bit to see on this dive frog fish, cuttle fish, etc and one more green turtle.

Our last dive, after lunch back at the resort was at Panglima Reef. This was not an ideal choice of dive site as there was strong currents and crap vis. It was not the best dive of the trip but still enjoyable enough and we saw another eight turtles. This bought the total number of turtles that Roz has counted to 451 in the sixteen days since we arrived in Semporna!

Within about 15 minutes of getting out of the water, we had returned to the jetty, washed all our gear and were leaving Mabul for the last time. We were back in Semporna by 5ish and after sorting out a few things at the Dive Shop, we walked back to the Seafest Hotel where we had booked a room for the night.

We had a couple of drinks in the room before going out to eat and then called into the Scuba Junkie bar for a beer before walking back to the hotel. We spent the rest of the evening looking at photos from earlier in the trip while drinking our Philippine rum.

Thursday, 27th June We walked into Semporna for breakfast when we woke up - roti canai in Al Nasifah. Roz stayed in there using their Wi-Fi while I went and got my hair cut. We walked to the cash-point for a final time and then returned to Scuba Junkie to pay the remainder of our bill. We said goodbye to Nic and Nas who were both in the shop due to ear problems and walked back to the Seafest.

We checked out of the hotel at 1 but stayed up at the pool until 2:30. We returned to Al Nasifah for a second round of roti canai for lunch and then waited in the hotel reception area until just before 4:00 for our taxi to Tawau airport. We were at the airport by 5:00 and checked in for the 7:15 Air Asia flight to Kuala Lumpa. The flight was slightly late leaving but was still in KL by 10:00. We collected our luggage, got a burger from McDonalds and checked into the Tune hotel for the third time on this trip.

Friday, 28th June We checked out of the Tune Hotel at 8ish and walked back to the LCCT. We checked in for the 10:40 flight to Bangkok and then went to find somewhere for breakfast. We had hoped to have our last meal of roti canai but a lot of food places had closed down as the airport is moving soon so we ended up with our second meal in a row at McDonalds. We cleared immigration, bought a few things from the duty free shop and had about an hour to wait for our flight. The flight was just about on time and by 12:00 local time, we landed at Don Muang airport in Bangkok.

We got through the airport fairly quickly and had about an hours taxi journey to the Asia Hotel. We have stayed here several times before but not since 2007. It was good to find that the reception staff are just as incompetent, inefficient and impolite as they were six years ago. It took over an hour before we were finally in our room after a few arguments about whether or not the room was ready and then being given a room that had a wall in front of the window. By 3pm, we were in our room with a view of the sky-train station. Not quite up to the standard of some of the views we have had in the last couple of months but better than the wall!

Once we were in our room, we were able to go out for lunch. We walked to the MBK shopping centre and ate in the food court upstairs. We had a quick look round the shops and walked back to the hotel via the 7-11.

We had a couple of drinks in our room in the evening before walking down to Siam Square to eat. We ate at Ban Khun Mae which is one of our favourite restaurants anywhere. We have been coming here for years but had to queue for a table tonight for the first time. We had a bottle of Sang Som with our meal and then walked back to the hotel to continue looking through our photos.

Saturday, 29th June Did not do a lot all day today. We had planned to go out and do some sight-seeing but put that off until tomorrow when we realised that the second rugby test between the Lions and Australia was kicking off  at 5:00. We spent a bit of time at the pool and went to MBK for lunch but did not do much else.

At 4:30, we got the sky-train from the hotel to Sukumvit Road. This area has a lot of English type pubs. We ended up in The Huntsman which had several televisions showing the rugby and sold wheat beer. We had a few beers in there during the game and then got a tuk-tuk back to Siam Square at about 7:30. We had another bottle of sang som with our meal at Ban Khun Mae and only vaguely remember walking back to the hotel.

Sunday, 30th June The last day of the trip! We did not do a lot in the morning but headed out for our sight-seeing at 12:30.. We got a boat along the canal from near the hotel and then walked to Khao San Road. After a few photos here, we walked down to the main river that runs through the middle of Bangkok. We got on another boat here and travelled down the river past a lot of the main sights. There was nothing that we particularly wanted to see but the boat journeys themselves are quite enjoyable. We got off the boat at Central Pier and then got the sky-train back to MBK for lunch.

We were back at the hotel by about 3:30. We had booked a late check-out which meant that we had the room until 6:00. Just after 6:00, we checked out of the hotel and got a taxi to the airport. We had about 90 minutes to wait at the airport before we could check-in. By about 8:45, we were in the BA lounge, probably for the last time! We had three hours or so in there before boarding the plane. As we went to board, we were told that we had been up-graded to economy plus so had bigger seats for the flight home. We had a few more drinks on the plane before falling asleep.

We arrived on time in Heathrow at 6am on Monday morning. After collecting our luggage we phoned the car-park company to find they had gone bankrupt and had been taken over by Purple Parking who had no details of the car. It took about 10 - 15 minutes for them to find it and we left the airport by 7:30. Traffic not too bad so were back in Kingsclere by 8:30
