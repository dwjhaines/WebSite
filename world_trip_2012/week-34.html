dwjhaines - 2012 week 34

Wednesday, 16th After nearly four weeks at home over Christmas and New Year, it was time to begin the next leg of the trip. We had stayed with Alex and Andi in Farnborough overnight and were up at six (after about three hours sleep!) for our lift to a very cold North Camp station. We were at the station by about 6:50 in time for the 6:57 train to Gatwick. We were at Gatwick by 8 and checked in for the 10:00 BA flight to Cancun, Mexico. Before check-in, I realised that I had left the key to my luggage in Farnborough which was not good news (although by the time we got to Cancun, Roz found that she did have a spare). After getting rid of our luggage, we headed to the BA lounge for breakfast. We were on the plane by 9:45 but had to wait for the plane to be de-iced so eventually took off at about 10:40.

The flight was half empty so we had plenty of space to spread out again. We had a few glasses of wine with the first meal and then managed to get a few hours sleep. The flight took about 10 hours and we landed in Cancun at 2:45pm (six hours behind UK time). There were long queues to get through immigration and customs but we were still outside the airport about an hour after landing. We walked to the bus station and got the 16:00 bus to Playa del Carmen where we will be staying for the next four nights. The bus took less than an hour. We walked from the bus station to Hotel Vista Caribe which was where we stayed when we came to Playa four or five years ago.

We had to change rooms due to the first one being above the main pedestrian street making it quite noisy. They eventually let us have the one next door which was a lot better. We are staying in one of their apartments again so have a large bedroom and kitchen/living area. We were both pretty worn out by the time we had got everything sorted out and were in need of sleep. We went straight out for a walk around Playa. The dive centre that we have used before was closed and it was not obvious whether or not it was closed permanently. Also, Dr Taco, the restaurant we ate in every night we were here last time had closed which was probably more disappointing! There  were loads of other small restaurants and market stalls selling cheap food. We had tacos from a couple of them and walked back to the hotel by about 8:30.

Thursday, 17th January We were up by 8ish and had the breakfast that is included in the price of the room. We went for a walk round a few dive centres enquiring about diving tomorrow but could not really make up our minds who to dive with. We could not find the Abyss Dive Centre that we had a few recommendations for (it had moved from where it used to be) so we returned to the room and sent them an e-mail. Spent the rest of the morning lying by the pool.

Just before we went out to lunch, we got an e-mail from a Cuban travel company. We are planning on visiting Cuba in a couple of weeks and had used this company to book our hotel. The e-mail told us that the booking had been unsuccessful despite the fact that we have confirmation and have paid up-front. We sent them an e-mail asking what they would do about it and went out for lunch. We ate  at a restaurant we had not been to before (tacos again) mainly due to the tortoise logo!

Did not do much for the rest of the afternoon. Back at the room we managed to get in touch with Abyss diving and decided we would dive with them tomorrow.

We also exchanged a few e-mails with Cuba Travel Network. They offered us a newer hotel that did not look anywhere near as good as the one we had originally booked and in a much worse location. Once Roz started complaining, they were suddenly able to confirm our original booking so that panic was over.

By the time we went out to eat it had become quite cold which we were not really expecting here. Not exactly freezing but cold enough to need layers other that t-shirts. We had another very good meal at the same place that we ate last night and had another early night. (We have decided that we are in need of a few alcohol free days after the last week or so at home!)

Friday, 18th January After breakfast, we made our way down to the place on the beach where we were due to meet Dave, the owner of Abyss Dive Centre (|They no longer have the shop in Playa which is why we could not find them yesterday!). Dave arrived at about 8:30 and after a bit of waiting around, we were on our way to the dive site just before 9:00. We were diving with Dave himself and one other diver. We dived at Tortuga Reef and Sabalos, two sites that we had dived last time we were here. Both dives were very good again. At the first we saw five or six turtles and also had two sightings on a bull shark. We were not that close to it and were not sure if it was the same one twice or not but we were close enough to see it clearly and make out that it was a pretty big shark. We had another very close up view of a large hawksbill turtle at the start of the second dive.

One of the things we had been asking dive centres about yesterday was the lengths of the dives as this has been an issue in Mexico in the past. Diving with Abyss proved to be a good move as our dive times today were 55 and 66 minutes. We will probably dive with them again later on during our stay in Mexico.

By the time we had got back to the room and started cleaning our gear, Roz was not feeling well. She spent the afternoon in bed feeling either too hot or too cold. The weather was still pretty bad here - it had been freezing on the boat (although not too bad underwater) and certainly not pool or beach weather. Apart from a walk around Playa I also spent most of the afternoon in the room.

Roz was feeling a bit better in the evening and was up to coming out. She was not however, up to eating anything so sat and watched while I ate an "Alhambra" (very similar to tacos again) at El Cahuamo.

Saturday, 19th January Roz, although still not 100%, was feeling much better when we woke up and was able to eat some breakfast. At 9 we were able to listen to Soccer Saturday on the laptop. Not quite as good a time for listening to football as when we are  in S. E. Asia but Swansea and Sunderland both won so it was an enjoyable couple of hours. We went to the beach when the football had finished but it had clouded over by about 12:30 and started to get cold again. By this time, Roz's temperature was back to normal and she was able to eat lunch.

We did not have a lot else to do so spent most of the rest of the afternoon in the room reading. In the evening we walked to the bus station where we bought tickets for tomorrow's 9:00 bus to Tulum and took out enough money to pay for the hotel when we get there. Although feeling better, Roz was still not up to eating Mexican food so we ate at the McDonalds opposite the bus station and then spent an hour or so wandering round the souvenir shops on 5th Avenue. Loads of stuff that we want to buy but no way of carrying any more than we already have. We were back in the room by 9ish.

Sunday, 20th January We checked out of the hotel at 8:45 and walked to the bus station. It was a one hour journey to the centre of Tulum. We got a taxi from outside the bus station to Piedra Escondida, the hotel at the beach where we will spend the next four nights. We were at the hotel by 10:30 and met Regan and Annette on the beach - they got here two days ago and are staying in the room next to us. After getting into our room, we spent most of the rest of the day lying on the beach. It was reasonably warm on the beach but still quite cold as soon as you got out of the sun.

At 4:00, I went for a 5.2 mile run, the furthest I have run since May. We started on the session at about 5:30. We had drinks on next door's balcony before heading just up the road to a bar/restaurant. Had an excellent meal and a few margaritas. I do not remember too much about the rest of the evening!
