dwjhaines - 2012 week 5

Monday, 25th June We were up by about 9 after getting about four hours sleep. We had breakfast at the hotel and then got a taxi out to the airport at about 10:30. We checked in without any problems - a relief after what happened with our tickets in Vienna. Once again we had business class tickets so made our way to the lounge where the complimentary vodka did seem to improve things after last night! Looking ahead, the weather does not seem to of got much better since we were last in Germany and still does not look good for camping. We had decided that if we were not camping we would try and stay in Bamberg on the way home so while in the lounge, we booked an apartment there for Wednesday and Thursday nights.  The flight was uneventful and we were back in Vienna by 3:00. We got the airport bus back to the Westbahnhof and just after 4, we were checked into the same Ibis we had been in a couple of weeks ago.

We decided to go straight out for a few drinks. We called into a bar next to the hotel for a couple and started heading towards the Sternbrau 7 area again. We did not get very far! Within a few hundred meters, we found "Känguruh" an excellent bar with a huge range of Belgium and local beers. There was a local "beer journalist" in there filming a television program when we arrived and he later told us about a beer festival in Antwerp which may also be an option on the way home. Spent the rest of the evening in the bar and do not remember leaving!

Tuesday, 26th June Got up and checked out of the hotel by 9:15 before walking over to the Westbahnhof. We bought food from a supermarket and had breakfast after we had got on the 10:14 train to Munich. This train was a lot more crowded than the one going in the opposite direction a couple of weeks ago but we still got seats and it was an fairly comfortable journey of just over four hours. We arrived at the main station at 2:30 and went for a beer in the Botanical Garden Beer Garden before getting the U-Bahn to our apartment. The apartment was one of the best places we have stayed at in Munich - a big room with balcony and only about 5 - 10 metres from the entrance to a U-Bahn station.

We went out for a few beers at 7ish. We got the U-Bahn back into Marienplatz and walked down to the Augustiner-Grossgastätte. We had a few in there with food and then walked to Donisl (big dog pub) for one. At 9ish we headed for the Wheat Beer House. We had planned to end up in the Hofbrauhaus but ended up staying where we were until about midnight. For the second night running, I do not remember getting back to the hotel.

Wednesday, 27th June Had breakfast on the balcony of the apartment before returning to the Mercure Hotel where we had left the car 18 days ago. The car was exactly as we had left it. It had acquired another noise by the time we set off but seemed to be still running OK. We drove North about 150 miles to Bamberg a journey of about 2.5 hours. We found the apartment with no problems and checked in just after 2pm. We bought lunch from a supermarket and ate it in the apartment before falling asleep for a couple of hours.

We went out for a walk just after 5. We had been to Bamberg before but not for about 15 years so could only vaguely remember the town. We picked up cycling maps for tomorrow from the tourist information office and spent an hour or so wandering round the old town. We were in need of a day off beer so we got a take-away pizza and returned to the apartment to watch Portugal v Spain.

Thursday, 28th June Breakfast in the apartment and then out on our bikes for about four hours. We did the "Stadt-Land-Fluss" Tour from the book we had got yesterday. This was a fairly easy circular route from Bamberg of about 32 miles. Although not that strenuous, it felt good to be doing some exercise again after spending a lot of the last three weeks doing nothing but eating and drinking!

We went out on our bikes again in the evening to visit a pub about a mile from where we were staying and then walked into the old town to watch the Germany v Italy semi-final. There were loads of people out and about for this game and it was a struggle to get a table anywhere but we eventually got seats outside a restaurant with a big screen. It was an enjoyable evening despite the fact that we had wanted Germany to win. Got soaked walking home again - our decision not to camp looks like the correct one.

Friday, 29th June We had booked to stay three nights in Bamberg. After breakfast we spent some time looking for places to stay on the way back to Bruges. The weather still does not look good for camping and the best option we could come up with was to stay here another night. Bamberg is an excellent town and we seem to be running out of time to do everything we want to. Also, the weather here is better here than it is further North. We found the owner who said our room was available so we are now staying here until Sunday.

We went for a walk into the old town at 11ish. Spent a couple of hours wandering around and bought some souvenirs. Were back in the apartment for lunch and went out on our bikes later than planned at about 3. We had decided to do another route from our book - the "Wasser und Bier" Tour. We had planned to do most of the route before heading to Schammelsdorf to visit the Knoblach Brewery which had been recommended to us by the bloke we had met in Vienna. As it was, we missed a turning on the tour meaning we left the route early. This did not work out too badly as it was very hot and it meant that we did not cycle as far as we had expected to. We ended up getting to the brewery slightly earlier than expected after a short beer stop on the way. The brewery was very good and after a beer there, we cycled about 12km back into Bamberg. This was slightly downhill all the way and was very enjoyable. Neither of us had realised how much we had climbed to get to Schammelsdorf.

After quick showers, we went out to eat at one of the breweries nearest to where we are staying (there are lots of breweries in Bamberg!). We had probably our best meal of the trip for abut €8 each at the Fässla Brewery - schnitzel and chips covered in beer and onion gravy as well as a few beers. We walked across the road the Spezial brewery where I sampled the Rauch Beir (smoked beer) that Bamberg is famous for. The two breweries both closed at 10:30 and we could to be bothered to walk into the old town so we had a relatively early night.

Saturday, 30th June We went out on our bikes earlier today, leaving the apartment not long after 10:00. We did a 38 mile round trip, cycling up the Main cycle path (as in the river Main) and back again on the opposite side of the river. Once again the route was not that strenuous but by about midday, it was getting very hot again and the ride back to Bamberg was hard work. We got back to the apartment de-hydrated and sun-burnt by about 2:30 and did not do a lot for the rest of the afternoon apart from buying a few local beers from the supermarket to take home with us.

We went out for a few beers again in the evening visiting both the Klosterbräu and Schlenkerla breweries. Schlenkerla is the home of Bamberg's most famous Rauch Bier and I was expecting this bar to be a bit touristy. However, it was another excellent place - one of the best bars we have been to on the trip so far. I enjoyed the beer a lot more than I did when trying it previously although Roz was not so impressed and switched to drinking wine.

We had a few in there before walking home, a journey interrupted by the worst thunder storm we have seen since we have been away. It was impossible to walk in the wind and rain so we had to shelter in a shop doorway for about 15 minutes until the worst of it had passed. We were still totally soaked by the time we were back at the apartment. Our tent would definitely not have survived if we had been camping!

Sunday, 1st July Checked out of the apartment just after 10 and continued our journey back towards England. We drove 275 miles to Wuppertal where we had booked a room at the Novotel for the night. Wuppertal had been chosen because the hotel was cheap with free parking and was close to the motorway. It should only take a couple of hours to get to Bruges tomorrow. We got to Wuppertal in about four hours, despite heavy rain for most of the journey and were checked into the room just after 2pm.

Did not do a lot for the rest of the day. Wuppertal is not big on tourist attractions! We were not bothered about going out in the evening so we got a take-away pizza again and watched the final of Euro2012 in our room.
